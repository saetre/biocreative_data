The NALP3/Cryopyrin-Inflammasome Complex is Expressed in LPS-Induced Ocular Inflammation

###begin p 3
###xml 99 103 99 100 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">&#946;</italic>
###xml 170 174 167 168 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">&#946;</italic>
###xml 486 490 480 481 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">&#946;</italic>
###xml 555 559 546 547 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">&#946;</italic>
###xml 681 685 669 670 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">&#946;</italic>
###xml 108 114 <span type="species:ncbi:10090">murine</span>
###xml 388 392 <span type="species:ncbi:10090">mice</span>
###xml 813 817 <span type="species:ncbi:10090">mice</span>
###xml 962 968 <span type="species:ncbi:10090">murine</span>
In the inflammosome complex, NALP3 or NALP1 binds to ASC and activates caspase-1 which induces IL-1beta. In murine LPS-induced ocular inflammation, the production of IL-1beta is increased. We suggest that NALP3- or NALP1-inflammasome complex can be participating in the LPS-induced ocular inflammation. In this work, eye, brain, testis, heart, spleen, and lung were obtained from C3H/HeN mice treated with LPS for 3 to 48 hours, and the expression of NALP1b, NALP3, ASC, caspase-1, IL-1beta, and IL-18 was determined. Infiltrated leukocytes producing IL-1beta in the anterior chamber were found at 12-hour posttreatment. A high upregulated expression of NALP3, ASC, caspase-1, IL-1beta, and IL-18 was found at the same time when infiltrated leukocytes were observed. NALP1b was not detected in the eye of treated mice. NALP3 was also overexpressed in heart and lung. These results suggest that NALP3-, but not NALP1-inflammosome complex, is participating in the murine LPS-induced ocular inflammation.
###end p 3
###begin title 4
1. INTRODUCTION
###end title 4
###begin p 5
###xml 874 875 874 875 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="B1">1</xref>
###xml 431 436 <span type="species:ncbi:9606">human</span>
The innate immunity is the first barrier in the protection against ocular infections. The Toll-like receptors (TLRs), that belong to pattern recognition receptors (PRRs) family, have been the molecules involved in the recognition of pathogen-associated molecular patterns (PAMPs) that lead to ocular inflammatory process. Nevertheless, other PRRs such as Nod-like receptor (NLR) could also be participating in this phenomenon. The human genome database reveals that NLR family consist of at least 22 genes. Alternative names for this family include CATERPILLER, NOD, and NOD-LRR. The NLRs have three structural domains, LRR domain (carboxy-terminal), an intermediary NACHT (NBS; NOD) domain, and an effector domain (amino-terminal), which can be a pyrin domain (PYD), a caspase recruitment domain (CARD), or a baculovirus inhibitor of apoptosis protein repeat (BIR) domain [1].
###end p 5
###begin p 6
###xml 429 430 429 430 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="B1">1</xref>
###xml 432 433 432 433 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="B2">2</xref>
###xml 415 420 <span type="species:ncbi:9606">human</span>
Based on the phylogenetic history of NACHT domain (which is only present in NLR), and on the particular type of effector domain, the 22 NLR family members can be classified in two large subfamilies: the NODs (NLR family, CARD domain containing) also called NLRCs, and the NALPs (NLR family, pyrin domain containing) also called NLRPs. Through genomic database searches, 14 NALPs members have been identified in the human genome [1, 2]. As NALPs have been recently discovered, the participation of these molecules in the ocular inflammation remains unknown.
###end p 6
###begin p 7
###xml 618 619 618 619 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="B3">3</xref>
###xml 621 622 621 622 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="B4">4</xref>
###xml 704 708 704 705 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">&#946;</italic>
###xml 759 760 756 757 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="B5">5</xref>
###xml 886 887 883 884 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="B1">1</xref>
###xml 889 890 886 887 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="B2">2</xref>
One well-established feature of NALPs is their ability to interact with apoptosis-associated speck-like protein (ASC) through a PYD-PYD interaction. ASC encodes a 22-kDa protein that contains a carboxy-terminal CARD and an amino-terminal PYD, suiting as an adaptor molecule between PYD- and CARD-containing proteins. An interaction between ASC and NALPs was shown initially for NALP1 (Nlrp1/DEFCAP/NAC/CARD7) and subsequently also found for NALP2 and NALP3 (Nlrp3/PYPAF1/Cryopyrin/CIAS1). The PYD of ASC interacts with the PYD of several NALPs, whereas ASC's CARD recruits the CARD of procaspase-1 for its activation [3, 4]. The active caspase-1 was found to be required for the production of active IL-1beta and IL-18 in activated macrophages and monocytes [5]. When NALP and ASC interact with activate caspase-1 or caspase-5, they create an intracellular complex termed inflammosome [1, 2].
###end p 7
###begin p 8
###xml 281 282 281 282 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="B1">1</xref>
###xml 284 285 284 285 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="B2">2</xref>
###xml 470 471 470 471 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="B6">6</xref>
More than one type of inflammasomes have been identified up to date. NALP1-inflammasome is composed of NALP1, ASC, caspase-1, and caspase-5, whereas the NALP2/3-inflammasomes contain NALP2 or NALP3, ASC and caspase-1. A fourth inflammasome is composed of IPAF, ASC, and caspase-1 [1, 2]. NALP12 does not form inflammasome complex but it participate in inflammation as a negative regulator interfering with IRAK-1 function, resulting in the repression of TLR signalling [6].
###end p 8
###begin p 9
###xml 163 167 163 164 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">&#946;</italic>
###xml 172 177 169 170 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">&#945;</italic>
###xml 200 201 193 194 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="B7">7</xref>
###xml 395 400 388 389 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">&#945;</italic>
###xml 417 421 406 407 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">&#946;</italic>
###xml 85 91 <span type="species:ncbi:10090">murine</span>
###xml 274 280 <span type="species:ncbi:10090">murine</span>
###xml 436 440 <span type="species:ncbi:10090">mice</span>
LPS-induced ocular inflammation is a model to study molecular mechanisms involved in murine uveitis, where the expression of proinflammatory cytokines such as IL-1beta, TNFalpha, and IL-6 is present [7]. We suggest that an inflammasome complex could be participating in the murine LPS-induced ocular inflammation. In this work, mRNA expression of NALP1b, NALP3, NALP12, ASC, caspase-1, IL-6, TNFalpha, IL-18, and IL-1beta in the eye of mice treated with LPS was studied.
###end p 9
###begin title 10
2. MATERIALS AND METHODS
###end title 10
###begin title 11
###xml 5 10 <span type="species:ncbi:10090">Mouse</span>
2.1. Mouse strain
###end title 11
###begin p 12
###xml 8 12 <span type="species:ncbi:10090">mice</span>
###xml 106 110 <span type="species:ncbi:10090">mice</span>
###xml 195 199 <span type="species:ncbi:10090">mice</span>
C3H/HeN mice (Harlan, Mexico City, Mexico) susceptible to ocular inflammation by endotoxin were used. All mice were healthy, 6-8 weeks old, and did not present any ophthalmologic alteration. All mice were maintained in aseptic conditions during the treatment.
###end p 12
###begin p 13
The experimental protocols were performed in accordance to the Instituto Politecnico Nacional statements for the use of animals in research.
###end p 13
###begin title 14
2.2. LPS-induced ocular inflammation
###end title 14
###begin p 15
###xml 94 96 94 95 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">&#956;</italic>
###xml 101 117 100 116 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">Escherichia coli</italic>
###xml 168 170 167 168 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">&#956;</italic>
###xml 446 448 444 445 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">&#956;</italic>
###xml 53 57 <span type="species:ncbi:10090">mice</span>
###xml 101 117 <span type="species:ncbi:562">Escherichia coli</span>
###xml 221 225 <span type="species:ncbi:10090">mice</span>
###xml 382 387 <span type="species:ncbi:10090">mouse</span>
###xml 406 410 <span type="species:ncbi:10090">mice</span>
###xml 509 513 <span type="species:ncbi:10090">mice</span>
###xml 565 570 <span type="species:ncbi:10090">mouse</span>
To generate LPS-induced ocular inflammation, C3H/HeN mice received a footpad injection of 200 mug of Escherichia coli K-235 LPS (Sigma Chemical Company, Mo, USA) in 50 muL of phosphate-buffered saline solution (PBS). The mice were killed by cervical dislocation at 3, 6, 12, 24, and 48 hours posttreatment and the eye, brain, testis, heart, spleen, and lung were obtained from each mouse. As control, five mice received a footpad injection of 50 muL sterile PBS and killed after 48 hours. Groups of 5 treated mice were included in each time of treatment. From each mouse, one eye was fixed in formaldehyde for histology analysis and the other one was treated with Trizol (Invitro, Carlsbad, Calif, USA) reagent for RT-PCR analysis.
###end p 15
###begin title 16
2.3. Histological analysis
###end title 16
###begin p 17
###xml 139 140 139 140 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">2</sub>
###xml 142 143 142 143 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">4</sub>
###xml 164 165 164 165 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">2</sub>
###xml 167 168 167 168 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">4</sub>
###xml 248 250 248 249 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">&#956;</italic>
###xml 63 67 <span type="species:ncbi:10090">mice</span>
To verify if infiltration of leukocytes in the eyes of treated mice occurred, fixation of the whole eye in buffered formaldehyde (3.5 g NaH2PO4 anhydrous, 6.5 g NaH2PO4, and 40% formaldehyde) was carried out and embedded in paraffin. Sections of 3 mum slides were stained with haematoxylin-eosin. For quantification of infiltrated cells, the observed cells per field of view (40X) were counted, and the mean value was reported.
###end p 17
###begin title 18
2.4. Immunohistochemical analysis
###end title 18
###begin p 19
###xml 54 56 54 55 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">&#956;</italic>
###xml 166 170 165 166 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">&#946;</italic>
###xml 130 136 <span type="species:ncbi:9986">Rabbit</span>
###xml 237 243 <span type="species:ncbi:9986">rabbit</span>
The immunohistochemical analysis was developed in 3.0 mum sections of ocular and testis tissues fixed with buffered formaldehyde. Rabbit polyclonal IgG antimouse IL-1beta (Santa Cruz Biotechnology, Santa Cruz, Calif, USA) and irrelevant rabbit IgG (Santa Cruz Biotechnology) were used to assess expression of the proteins, followed by a secondary biotinylated antibody and streptavidin/peroxidase complex (LSAB+ Kit peroxidase, Dako Cytomation, Carpinteria, Calif, USA), and visualized with TACS-Blue Label (R&D System, Minn, USA) as chromogen and counterstained with Nuclear Fast Red counterstain (Sigma-Aldrich, Mo, USA).
###end p 19
###begin title 20
2.5. RNA isolation and RT-PCR analysis
###end title 20
###begin p 21
###xml 220 222 220 221 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">&#956;</italic>
###xml 233 235 232 233 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">&#956;</italic>
###xml 250 255 248 253 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">15-18</sub>
###xml 359 361 351 352 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">&#956;</italic>
###xml 550 552 535 536 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">&#956;</italic>
###xml 587 588 571 572 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">2</sub>
###xml 594 596 578 579 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">&#956;</italic>
###xml 646 648 629 630 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">&#956;</italic>
###xml 685 689 667 668 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">&#946;</italic>
###xml 702 706 681 682 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">&#946;</italic>
###xml 726 733 702 709 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="table" rid="tab1">Table 1</xref>
All studied organs were thoroughly washed in D-PBS to eliminate blood. Total RNA extraction was performed with TRIzol reagent and treated with DNAse I free of RNAse. For reverse transcriptase (RT) reaction, total RNA (3 mug) and 0.5 mug of oligo-(dT)15-18 (Invitrogen) were denatured at 70degreesC for 10 minutes, and 1X single strand buffer, 0.5 mM DTT, 500 muM of each dNTPs, and 200U of MMLV reverse transcriptase (Invitrogen, Ohio, USA) were added. The RT reactions were performed at 42degreesC for 1 hour. The PCR reactions were prepared with 1 muL of the cDNA, 1X buffer, 1 mM MgCl2, 200 muM of each dNTPs, 1U of TaqDNA polymerase, and 0.2 muM of each NALPs, ASC, caspase-1, IL-1beta, IL-18, and beta-actin primers (see Table 1). Optimal PCR conditions were 30 cycles of 92degreesC 30 seconds, 60degreesC 30 seconds, and 72degreesC 30 seconds.
###end p 21
###begin p 22
###xml 169 173 169 170 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">&#946;</italic>
###xml 208 212 205 206 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">&#946;</italic>
###xml 224 228 218 219 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">&#946;</italic>
###xml 246 250 237 238 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">&#946;</italic>
###xml 276 280 264 265 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">&#946;</italic>
###xml 352 358 <span type="species:ncbi:9103">Turkey</span>
To semiquantitate PCR products, the intensity of the amplified bands was analyzed with the AlphaImager software. Band intensities were normalized with the corresponding beta-actin signal, and relations NALPs/beta-actin, ASC/beta-actin, caspase-1/beta-actin, and interleukines/beta-actin were calculated. The results were analyzed by Kruskal-Wallis and Turkey statistical tests.
###end p 22
###begin title 23
3. RESULTS
###end title 23
###begin title 24
###xml 40 44 <span type="species:ncbi:10090">mice</span>
3.1. Analysis of ocular inflammation in mice treated with LPS
###end title 24
###begin p 25
###xml 353 361 353 361 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="fig" rid="fig1">Figure 1</xref>
###xml 461 462 461 462 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">P</italic>
###xml 474 482 474 482 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="fig" rid="fig1">Figure 1</xref>
###xml 85 89 <span type="species:ncbi:10090">mice</span>
###xml 252 256 <span type="species:ncbi:10090">mice</span>
Histological and molecular analyses were done to evaluate the ocular inflammation of mice treated with LPS, therefore leukocytes infiltrated in the anterior chamber and proinflammatory cytokines expression were determinated. In the eyes of LPS-treated mice, there were leukocytes infiltrated in the anterior chamber from 3 to 48 hours of treatment (see Figure 1(a)) and at 12 hours the mean value of infiltrated leukocytes was the highest (ten cells per field; P < .05; see Figure 1(b)).
###end p 25
###begin p 26
###xml 124 129 124 125 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">&#945;</italic>
###xml 306 314 302 310 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="fig" rid="fig1">Figure 1</xref>
###xml 366 371 362 363 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">&#945;</italic>
###xml 407 415 399 407 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="fig" rid="fig1">Figure 1</xref>
###xml 521 525 513 514 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">&#946;</italic>
###xml 531 539 520 528 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="fig" rid="fig1">Figure 1</xref>
###xml 53 57 <span type="species:ncbi:10090">mice</span>
###xml 192 196 <span type="species:ncbi:10090">mice</span>
###xml 331 335 <span type="species:ncbi:10090">mice</span>
To confirm ocular inflammation in the eye of treated mice, the mRNA expression of the proinflammatory cytokines IL-6 and TNFalpha was determined. IL-6 mRNA was highly expressed in LPS-treated mice and semiquantitative analysis showed a significant increase of IL-6 at 3, 6, and 12 hours posttreatment (see Figure 1(c)). In treated mice, the highest expression of TNFalpha mRNA was observed at 12 hours (see Figure 1(c)). At this time, it was also observed that the infiltrated leukocytes were producing and secreting IL-1beta (see Figure 1(d)). These results demonstrate that LPS induced ocular inflammation.
###end p 26
###begin title 27
###xml 48 52 <span type="species:ncbi:10090">mice</span>
3.2. Expression analysis of NALP1b and NALP3 in mice treated with LPS
###end title 27
###begin p 28
###xml 215 223 215 223 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="fig" rid="fig2">Figure 2</xref>
###xml 364 365 364 365 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">P</italic>
###xml 377 385 377 385 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="fig" rid="fig2">Figure 2</xref>
###xml 74 78 <span type="species:ncbi:10090">mice</span>
###xml 188 192 <span type="species:ncbi:10090">mice</span>
###xml 291 295 <span type="species:ncbi:10090">mice</span>
To analyse the type of inflammasome complex present in the eye of treated mice, expression of NALP1b and NALP3 was determined. NALP1b mRNA was not found, neither in the eye of the treated mice, nor in controls (see Figure 2). In contrast, NALP3 expression was detected in the eye of treated mice, and its highest expression was observed at 12 hours posttreatment (P < .05; see Figure 2). These results demonstrate that there is a differential expression of NALP1b and NALP3 in the LPS-induced ocular inflammation.
###end p 28
###begin p 29
###xml 171 179 171 179 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="fig" rid="fig3">Figure 3</xref>
In the other studied organs, NALP3 mRNA was detected in heart (from 3 hours posttreatment) and lung (from 6 hours posttreatment) but not in testis, brain, and spleen (see Figure 3). Although the highest NALP3 expression occurred in the eye at 12 hours posttreatment, NALP3 arose earlier in heart and lung (3 and 6 hours, resp.).
###end p 29

3.3. Expression analysis of ASC, caspase-1, IL-1beta, and IL-18 in the eye of LPS-treated mice
###end title 30
###begin p 31
###xml 171 175 171 172 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">&#946;</italic>

###xml 682 683 676 677 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="fig" rid="fig1">1</xref>
###xml 749 757 743 751 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="fig" rid="fig4">Figure 4</xref>
###xml 286 290 <span type="species:ncbi:10090">mice</span>
As NALP3 expression occurred in the eye, and as an increase of its expression was induced by LPS, the expression of the other inflammasome components (ASC, caspase-1, IL-1beta, and IL-18) in this organ was determined. The ASC mRNA expression was observed in both nontreated and treated mice but the highest expression was found at 12 hours posttreatment (see Figure 4). The expression of caspase-1 was gradually increased from 3 hours, and at 12 hours it showed its highest expression (see Figure 4). The expression of IL-1beta was upregulated from 3 hours posttreatment, and its highest expression happened at 12 hours posttreatment as occurred with the protein (see Figures 4 and 1(d)). Similar results were observed for the IL-18 expression (see Figure 4).
###end p 31
###begin title 32
###xml 71 75 <span type="species:ncbi:10090">mice</span>
3.4. Expression of NALP12 (Monarch 1) and NALP5 in the eye LPS-treated mice
###end title 32
###begin p 33
###xml 319 320 319 320 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">P</italic>
###xml 332 343 332 343 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="fig" rid="fig5">Figure 5(a)</xref>
As NALP12 (Monarch 1) is a molecule that can negatively regulate the inflammasome complex, the participation of this molecule in the LPS-induced ocular inflammation was investigated. A slight induction in the expression of NALP12 due to LPS-treatment was observed at 12 and 24 hours, but without statistical relevance (P > .05; see Figure 5(a)).
###end p 33
###begin p 34
###xml 354 355 354 355 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">P</italic>
###xml 367 378 367 378 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="fig" rid="fig5">Figure 5(b)</xref>
###xml 214 218 <span type="species:ncbi:10090">mice</span>
To assess if the LPS treatment can also modify the expression of other NALPs that are not involved in the inflammasome complex, such as NALP5, the expression of this molecule was determinated. The eye of untreated mice did not express NALP5, and the LPS induced slight expression of it at 12 hours posttreatment, but again without statistical relevance (P > .05; see Figure 5(b)).
###end p 34
###begin title 35
4. DISCUSSION
###end title 35
###begin p 36
###xml 70 74 70 71 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">&#946;</italic>
###xml 86 91 83 84 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">&#945;</italic>
###xml 135 139 128 129 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">&#946;</italic>
###xml 365 369 355 356 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">&#946;</italic>
In this work, we observed that LPS induced the mRNA expression of IL-1beta, IL-6, TNF-alpha, and IL-18 as well as the secretion of IL-1beta by the leukocyte infiltration in the anterior chamber of the eye, confirming an ocular inflammation process. Therefore, we searched if the expression of NALP3, NALP1b, ASC, and caspase-1 correlates with the expression of IL-1beta and IL-18 in the LPS-induced ocular inflammation model.
###end p 36
###begin p 37
###xml 182 186 182 183 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">&#946;</italic>
###xml 222 223 219 220 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="B2">2</xref>
###xml 656 657 653 654 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="B8">8</xref>
###xml 873 874 870 871 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="B9">9</xref>
###xml 1138 1142 1135 1136 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">&#946;</italic>
###xml 1296 1298 1290 1292 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="B10">10</xref>
###xml 294 298 <span type="species:ncbi:10090">mice</span>
###xml 398 402 <span type="species:ncbi:10090">mice</span>
###xml 664 670 <span type="species:ncbi:10090">murine</span>
###xml 866 871 <span type="species:ncbi:10090">mouse</span>
###xml 1192 1197 <span type="species:ncbi:9606">human</span>
NALP3 forms a multiprotein complex called "inflammasome," which contains ASC and caspase-1, and this complex promotes caspase-1 activation and consequently the processing of pro-IL-1beta and IL-18 in macrophages cultures [2]. We found that the NALP3 mRNA expression was increased in the eye of mice treated with LPS at 12 hours. The expression of NALP1b was found neither in treated nor in control mice, suggesting that the NALP3-inflammosome complex, but not the NALP1b-inflammosome complex, could be associated with the LPS-induced ocular inflammation. The NALP3 expression has been documented predominantly in monocytes, granulocytes, and chondrocytes [8]. The murine NALP3 mRNA expression was found initially in peripheral blood leukocytes, consistent with the postulation of an inflammatory function. NALP3 has also been detected in the eye and skin of healthy mouse [9]. Nevertheless, this is the first report that shows the increase of the NALP3 expression in the LPS-induced ocular inflammation. It is important to highlight that the overexpression of NALP3 correlated with the time leukocytes was found infiltrated producing IL-1beta. In vitro studies have demonstrated that primary human monocytes stimulated with LTA, LPS, or poly(I:C) elicited a robust induction of NALP3 expression [10]. With our results, we show that LPS can induce in vivo the expression of NALP3 in the eye.
###end p 37
###begin p 38
###xml 228 230 228 230 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="B11">11</xref>
###xml 407 409 407 409 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="B12">12</xref>
###xml 221 226 <span type="species:ncbi:10090">mouse</span>
###xml 479 483 <span type="species:ncbi:10090">mice</span>
This is also the first report that shows that LPS induces the expression of NALP3 in heart and lung but not in spleen, nevertheless it has been reported that NALP3 is not expressed in lymphoid and skin tissues of healthy mouse [11] but that NALP1 does. Chu et al. reported that NALP1 mRNA is expressed in healthy organs, such as heart, thymus, spleen, kidney, liver, lung, and peripheral blood lymphocytes [12]; we are reporting that NALP1 is not expressed in the eye of healthy mice, but even after treatment with LPS it remains absent.
###end p 38
###begin p 39
We found that the expression of NALP3 in the eye is delayed respect heart's and lung's. About it, we suggest that the hemato-ocular barrier that could delay the LPS arrival to the ocular tissues interfering in the time NALP3 is induced. The early expression of NALP3 in heart and lung could contribute to the effect of septic shock by LPS.
###end p 39
###begin p 40
###xml 185 189 185 186 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">&#946;</italic>
###xml 237 282 234 279 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">Staphylococcus aureus, Listeria monocytogenes</italic>
###xml 284 286 281 283 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="B13">13</xref>
###xml 379 381 376 378 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="B14">14</xref>
###xml 483 487 480 481 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">&#946;</italic>
###xml 509 519 503 513 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">Salmonella</italic>
###xml 585 589 579 580 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">&#946;</italic>
###xml 757 758 748 749 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="B4">4</xref>
###xml 760 762 751 753 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="B15">15</xref>
###xml 876 878 867 869 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="B16">16</xref>
###xml 938 940 929 931 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="B17">17</xref>
###xml 16 20 <span type="species:ncbi:10090">mice</span>
###xml 237 258 <span type="species:ncbi:1280">Staphylococcus aureus</span>
###xml 260 282 <span type="species:ncbi:1639">Listeria monocytogenes</span>
NALP3-deficient mice have been generated to investigate the role of NALP3 in inflammatory responses to pathogen-derived molecules. NALP3-deficient macrophages do not produce active IL-1beta in response to LPS, Pam3 CSK4 (TLR2 agonists), Staphylococcus aureus, Listeria monocytogenes [13], bacterial RNA, and the imidazoquinoline compounds R837 and R848 (TLR7 and TLR8 agonists) [14]. Nevertheless, NALP3-deficient macrophages, but not the IPAF-deficient macrophages, can produce IL-1beta when stimulated with Salmonella. On the other hand, ASC-deficient macrophages do not produce IL-1beta with any of the TLR agonists and cited bacteria. These results indicate that the adaptor ASC is the principal molecule for both NALP- and IPAF-inflammasome complexes [4, 15]. ASC is abundantly expressed in epithelial cells, leukocytes, hair follicles, and peripheral blood lymphocytes [16], and in neutrophils located in severe inflammatory sites [17]. In the eye, we found that ASC is expressed, and that the LPS can increase its expression.
###end p 40
###begin p 41
###xml 92 96 92 93 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">&#946;</italic>
###xml 170 174 167 168 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">&#946;</italic>
###xml 261 263 255 257 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="B18">18</xref>
###xml 338 340 332 334 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="B14">14</xref>
###xml 460 464 454 455 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">&#946;</italic>
###xml 600 604 591 592 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">&#946;</italic>
###xml 118 122 <span type="species:ncbi:10090">mice</span>
###xml 222 226 <span type="species:ncbi:10090">mice</span>
###xml 400 404 <span type="species:ncbi:10090">mice</span>
###xml 646 652 <span type="species:ncbi:10090">murine</span>
Caspase-1 is another component of the inflammasome complex useful for the activation of IL-1beta. Caspase-1-deficient mice carry a defect in the production of mature IL-1beta after stimulation with LPS. Furthermore, these mice are resistant to endotoxic shock [18]. Bacterial RNA can also induce the activation of caspase-1 trough NALP3 [14]. We found that caspase-1 was increased in the eyes of the mice treated with LPS at the same time when NALP3, ASC, IL-1beta, and IL-18 showed their highest expression (at 12 hours). These results suggest that the high expression of NALP3, caspase-1, ASC, IL-1beta, and IL-18 could be participating in the murine LPS-induced ocular inflammation.
###end p 41
###begin p 42
###xml 393 398 393 394 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">&#945;</italic>
###xml 423 424 419 420 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="B6">6</xref>
###xml 169 173 <span type="species:ncbi:10090">mice</span>
In this report, we also analyzed the NALP12 expression in LPS-induced ocular inflammation. Although a slight increase in the expression of NALP12 in the eyes of treated mice was detected, statistically it was not relevant. This suggests that this molecule does not participate in the regulation of the LPS-induced ocular inflammation as occurs in monocytes and granulocytes stimulated with TNFalpha and other TLRs ligands [6].
###end p 42
###begin p 43
###xml 138 140 138 140 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="B19">19</xref>
###xml 142 144 142 144 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="B20">20</xref>
###xml 285 289 <span type="species:ncbi:10090">mice</span>
###xml 345 349 <span type="species:ncbi:10090">mice</span>
NALP5 has not been involved in the inflammasome complex, but it has been related to reproduction because it has been detected in oocytes [19, 20]. So far, there are no reports about the expression of NALP5 in the eye. Although we did not find NALP5 expressed in the eye of the control mice, we found it barely detectable in some eyes of treated mice.
###end p 43
###begin p 44
In summary, these results suggest that LPS induced a significant expression of NALP3-inflammasomme complex in the endotoxin-induced ocular inflammation.
###end p 44
