alpha5 Subunit-containing GABAA receptors mediate a slowly decaying inhibitory synaptic current in CA1 pyramidal neurons following Schaffer collateral activation

###begin title 2
Abstract
###end title 2
###begin p 3
###xml 4 5 4 5 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">A</sub>
###xml 59 60 51 52 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">A</sub>

###xml 599 615 583 599 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">stratum radiatum</italic>

###xml 920 936 888 904 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">stratum radiatum</italic>
###xml 1100 1101 1063 1064 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">A</sub>
###xml 1396 1397 1355 1356 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">A</sub>
###xml 1401 1406 1360 1365 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">gabra</italic>
###xml 1407 1410 1366 1369 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">&#8722;/&#8722;</sup>
###xml 1452 1453 1407 1408 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">A</sub>
###xml 1609 1610 1560 1561 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">A</sub>

###xml 1411 1415 <span type="species:ncbi:10090">mice</span>
GABAA receptors that contain the alpha5 subunit (alpha5GABAARs) are highly expressed in the hippocampus, and have been implicated in learning and memory processes. They generate a tonic form of inhibition that regulates neuronal excitability. Recently it was shown that alpha5GABAARs also contribute to slow phasic inhibition of CA1 pyramidal neurons following local stimulation in the stratum lacunosum moleculare. However, it is unknown whether alpha5GABAARs can also be recruited indirectly by stimulation of Schaffer collaterals. Here, we studied GABAergic currents evoked by stimulation in the stratum radiatum of CA1 in the presence and absence of CNQX to block AMPA receptor-mediated excitation. We tested their sensitivity to gabazine and two drugs acting at the benzodiazepine site of alpha1/alpha2/alpha3 or alpha5GABAARs (400 nM zolpidem and 20 nM L-655,708, respectively). IPSCs evoked by stimulation in the stratum radiatum in the presence of CNQX were potentiated by zolpidem, blocked by 1 muM gabazine and were relatively insensitive to L-655,708 consistent with the lack of alpha5GABAARs. In contrast, IPSCs evoked by stimulation of Schaffer collaterals had a significant gabazine-insensitive component. This component was attenuated by L-655,708 and enhanced by burst stimulation. Furthermore, the L-655,708-sensitive current was absent in recordings from mice lacking alpha5GABAARs (gabra5-/- mice). These results show that alpha5GABAAR-mediated phasic inhibition is activated by the Schaffer collateral pathway and provide evidence for activity pattern-dependent participation of alpha5GABAARs in inhibition.

###begin title 5
Introduction
###end title 5
###begin p 6
###xml 206 207 202 203 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">A</sub>
###xml 223 224 219 220 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">A</sub>
###xml 383 405 379 401 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" rid="bib19" ref-type="bibr">Mann and Paulsen, 2007</xref>
###xml 412 413 408 409 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">A</sub>
###xml 422 423 418 419 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">&#8722;</sup>
###xml 708 721 665 678 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" rid="bib42" ref-type="bibr">Wafford, 2005</xref>
Synaptic inhibition in the hippocampus plays a crucial role in balancing and synchronising the activity of excitatory cells. gamma-aminobutyric acid (GABA) released by inhibitory interneurons activates GABAA receptors (GABAARs), and in most mature neurons, GABA causes a reduction of the postsynaptic cell excitability via hyperpolarising and/or shunting inhibition (for review, see Mann and Paulsen, 2007). GABAARs are Cl- permeable, pentameric ionic channels that are formed from the combination of distinct subunits (alpha1-6, beta1-3, gamma1-3, delta, epsilon, theta, pi, rho1-3), and the majority of native combinations identified to date have a common 2:2:1 alpha/beta/gamma stoichiometry (reviewed in Wafford, 2005).
###end p 6
###begin p 7
###xml 224 253 224 253 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" rid="bib17" ref-type="bibr">Klausberger and Somogyi, 2008</xref>
###xml 290 291 290 291 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">A</sub>
###xml 489 490 489 490 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">A</sub>
###xml 510 551 510 551 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" rid="bib24 bib39" ref-type="bibr">Nusser et&#160;al., 1996; Thomson et&#160;al., 2000</xref>
###xml 777 778 773 774 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">A</sub>
###xml 834 835 826 827 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">A</sub>
###xml 839 860 831 852 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" rid="bib2" ref-type="bibr">Ali and Thomson, 2008</xref>
The targeting of pyramidal cells by inhibitory interneurons follows a highly organised pattern, and the vast majority of GABAergic interneurons target either the perisomatic or specific dendritic domains of pyramidal cells (Klausberger and Somogyi, 2008). However, the role of specific GABAAR subtypes expressed in distinct CA1 pyramidal cell compartments is still poorly understood. There is some evidence of a high correlation between presynaptic interneuron type and their specific GABAARs subunit targets (Nusser et al., 1996; Thomson et al., 2000). For example, in the neocortex, recordings from synaptically-connected pairs between GABAergic interneurons and pyramidal cells have demonstrated that dendritic targeting inhibitory neurons preferentially activate alpha5GABAARs, whereas those targeting the soma activate alpha1GABAARs (Ali and Thomson, 2008).
###end p 7
###begin p 8
###xml 23 24 23 24 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">A</sub>
###xml 227 228 227 228 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">A</sub>
###xml 484 497 476 489 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" rid="bib42" ref-type="bibr">Wafford, 2005</xref>
###xml 539 540 531 532 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">A</sub>
###xml 748 749 732 733 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">A</sub>
###xml 885 928 869 912 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" rid="bib21 bib30" ref-type="bibr">McKernan et&#160;al., 2000; Rudolph et&#160;al., 1999</xref>
###xml 995 1017 979 1001 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" rid="bib38" ref-type="bibr">Tenen and Hirsch, 1980</xref>
###xml 1245 1263 1221 1239 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" rid="bib4" ref-type="bibr">Atack et&#160;al., 2005</xref>
###xml 704 708 <span type="species:ncbi:10090">mice</span>
By identifying the GABAAR subtypes in different inhibitory pathways, it may be possible to pharmacologically target specific GABAergic networks in the hippocampus. Recent studies have started to dissect the contribution of GABAARs subtypes to different behaviours. Pharmacological tools in this area include the use of benzodiazepine derivatives or genetic modifications targeted at the benzodiazepine site, which is located at the interface of gamma2 and alpha subunits (reviewed in Wafford, 2005). By altering the kinetics of single GABAAR channels, benzodiazepines enhance the effect of GABA and a behavioural readout can be obtained to interpret the function of targeted alpha subunits. For example, mice with a point mutation in the alpha1GABAAR subunit (His101Arg), which rendered it insensitive to diazepam, did not display the sedative and amnestic effects of benzodiazepines (McKernan et al., 2000; Rudolph et al., 1999). Conversely, inverse agonists acting at the benzodiazepine site (Tenen and Hirsch, 1980) inhibit the effect of GABA, and have effects opposite to those of the classical benzodiazepines. Using this approach, systemic application of the alpha3 inverse-agonist alpha3IA promoted anxiety-related behaviours in rodents (Atack et al., 2005).
###end p 8
###begin p 9
###xml 10 11 6 7 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">A</sub>

###xml 630 649 610 629 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" rid="bib20" ref-type="bibr">Martin et&#160;al., 2009</xref>
###xml 765 766 741 742 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">A</sub>
###xml 789 808 765 784 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" rid="bib15" ref-type="bibr">Howell et&#160;al., 2000</xref>
###xml 906 907 878 879 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">A</sub>
###xml 385 389 <span type="species:ncbi:10090">mice</span>
###xml 739 747 <span type="species:ncbi:9606">patients</span>
alpha5GABAARs are of particular interest as they are highly expressed in the adult hippocampus both at synaptic and extrasynaptic sites (Sperk et al., 1997; Sur et al., 1999), in stark contrast to low expression levels in other brain areas. Consistent with their hippocampal localisation, behavioural studies using alpha5 subunit-specific inverse agonists and alpha5 subunit knock out mice strongly implicated alpha5GABAARs in the modulation of learning and memory (Collinson et al., 2002; Atack et al., 2006; Ballard et al., 2009). Therefore, alpha5GABAARs are currently considered as relevant targets for memory blocking drugs (Martin et al., 2009) and cognitive enhancing drugs with clinical applications such as in Alzheimer's disease patients, whose alpha5GABAARs are well preserved (Howell et al., 2000). However, the precise mechanisms underlying the regulation of hippocampal function by alpha5GABAARs are not known.

###xml 839 867 819 847 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">stratum lacunosum moleculare</italic>
###xml 894 959 874 939 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" rid="bib26 bib25 bib43" ref-type="bibr">Pearce, 1993; Ouardouz and Lacaille, 1997; Zarnowska et&#160;al., 2009</xref>
###xml 972 978 952 958 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">A,slow</sub>
###xml 1218 1236 1198 1216 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" rid="bib9" ref-type="bibr">Banks et&#160;al., 2000</xref>
###xml 374 378 <span type="species:ncbi:10090">mice</span>
###xml 432 436 <span type="species:ncbi:10090">mice</span>
It is well established that extrasynaptic alpha5GABAARs can mediate a large component of tonic inhibition in the hippocampus (Caraiscos et al., 2004; Scimemi et al., 2005; Glykys and Mody, 2006; Prenosil et al., 2006). In contrast, the role of alpha5GABAARs in phasic inhibition remains poorly understood. Studies comparing spontaneous and locally-evoked inhibition between mice lacking alpha5GABAARs (gabra5-/-) and wild type (WT) mice suggested a negligible contribution of alpha5GABAARs to phasic inhibition (Collinson et al., 2002; Glykys and Mody, 2006). Other studies have suggested that alpha5GABAARs mediate a slowly decaying component of synaptic inhibition (GABAA,slow) (Prenosil et al., 2006; Zarnowska et al., 2009). Evoked GABAA,slow potentials have only been observed following local extracellular stimulation at or near the stratum lacunosum moleculare (SLM) of the hippocampus (Pearce, 1993; Ouardouz and Lacaille, 1997; Zarnowska et al., 2009). Thus, GABAA,slow has been proposed to modulate the activity of distal dendrites in hippocampal CA1 pyramidal neurons, and to mediate a component of synaptic inhibition activated by the direct input from the entorhinal cortex to the hippocampus at the SLM (Banks et al., 2000).
###end p 10
###begin p 11
###xml 62 63 58 59 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">A</sub>
###xml 192 193 184 185 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">A</sub>

###xml 470 471 450 451 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">A</sub>
###xml 475 495 455 475 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" rid="bib39" ref-type="bibr">Thomson et&#160;al., 2000</xref>
###xml 763 785 743 765 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" rid="bib1" ref-type="bibr">Alger and Nicoll, 1982</xref>
###xml 1074 1075 1050 1051 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">A</sub>
###xml 1159 1164 1135 1140 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">gabra</italic>
###xml 1165 1168 1141 1144 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">&#8722;/&#8722;</sup>

In order to understand the underlying mechanisms of alpha5GABAARs targeting cognitive enhancing drugs, it becomes important to establish whether in addition to their SLM activation, alpha5GABAARs are recruited by CA3 output via Schaffer collateral activity. Local stimulation at the stratum radiatum (SR) in CA1 has been reported to produce fast decaying IPSCs via perisomatic targeting inhibitory cells (Ouardouz and Lacaille, 1997) mediated by alpha1/alpha2/alpha3GABAARs (Thomson et al., 2000). However, under conditions of local stimulation, excitatory synaptic transmission is usually blocked with glutamate receptor antagonists. Feed-forward inhibition requires activation of afferent fibres to interneurons which in turn release GABA onto pyramidal cells (Alger and Nicoll, 1982). The Schaffer collaterals are likely to stimulate directly or indirectly a wide variety of interneurons that would not be reached by local stimulation. In the present study, we compared locally-evoked and Schaffer collateral-stimulated inhibitory currents. To determine whether alpha5GABARs contribute to the evoked IPSCs we used the inverse-agonist L-655,708 in rats and gabra5-/- mice. The results show that stimulation of Schaffer collaterals can activate a slowly decaying component of GABAergic inhibition, mediated by alpha5 subunit-containing GABAA receptors, particularly following bursts of high-frequency stimulation of Schaffer collateral afferent input.

Parasagittal slices containing the hippocampus were obtained from male Sprague Dawley rats (supplied by Harlan, Bicester, UK), or from gabra5-/- mice (Collinson et al., 2002) and wild type (WT) littermates ranging from postnatal day 14 to 28. Rodents were anaesthetized with 5% isofluorane until breathing slowed down to approximately one breath per second, and stimulation of the limb withdrawal reflex no longer elicited a response. After decapitation, the brain was quickly removed into ice-cold artificial cerebrospinal fluid (aCSF), containing (in mM): NaCl, 126; KCl, 2.5; NaHCO3, 26; CaCl2, 2; MgCl2, 2; NaH2PO4, 1.25; glucose, 10, saturated with 95% O2/5% CO2, with a final pH of 7.2-7.4. Slices were prepared at 350 mum thickness using a Leica VT1000S microtome. Slices containing the hippocampal formation were trimmed from other brain regions and were maintained and recorded at room temperature (22-27 degreesC).

###begin title 16
Electrophysiological recordings
###end title 16
###begin p 17
###xml 210 228 209 227 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">stratum pyramidale</italic>
###xml 353 360 352 359 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">A,local</sub>

After transferring a single slice to a submerged-style recording chamber, a monopolar stainless steel stimulation electrode (A-M Systems, Sequim, WA, USA) was placed into the SR of CA1 50-100 mum away from the stratum pyramidale (SP) for synaptic stimulation. Stimulation in the SR was carried out under two different conditions: firstly, to record GABAA,local, AMPA receptor-mediated excitation was blocked with CNQX while recording from a pyramidal cell. The stimulation electrode was placed approximately 100 mum lateral to the recorded cell to ensure stimulation of local interneurons. Secondly, to record inhibition elicited by the Schaffer collaterals (GABAA,SC) CNQX was not included. The stimulation electrode was placed approximately 300 mum lateral to the recorded cell to reduce local stimulation of GABAergic neurons in addition to afferent stimulation.

Experiments were performed in voltage clamp mode. ###end p 18
###begin p 19
Whole-cell patch clamp recordings were obtained with 2-4 MOmega borosilicate pipettes from putative CA1 pyramidal cells identified by their location in the SP and by their shape.
###end p 19
###begin p 20
###xml 16 18 16 18 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">Cl</sub>
The calculated ECl at room temperature was -56 mV, and AMPA receptor-mediated currents reversed near 0 mV. For this reason GABAergic currents were recorded at 0 mV, both for local and Schaffer collateral stimulation, so as to isolate them from AMPA receptor-mediated currents in the latter case. For recordings at voltages other than -70 mV, a voltage step from -70 mV to the test potential started 5 s before synaptic stimulation. Whole-cell recordings were made using an Axon Multiclamp 700B amplifier (Molecular Devices, Union City, CA, USA). Recordings were low-pass filtered at 2 kHz and digitised at 20 kHz with a National Instruments A/D board (Austin, TX, USA) using Ginj 1.0 software (courtesy of Hugh P. C. Robinson) for acquisition from within Matlab (Mathworks Ltd, Natick, MA, USA). Postsynaptic currents were evoked using a stimulus isolator unit (ISO-flex, A.M.P.I. Jerusalem, Israel) which delivered pulses of 100 mus duration in current mode; stimulation intensities ranged between 20 muA and 70 muA and the computer-controlled stimulation interval was 60 s. Drugs were applied after a stable baseline of 6-10 min (<10% drift allowed). Series resistance was not compensated during recordings. Series resistance was measured before each stimulation with a 5 mV, 50 ms step pulse. Recordings were terminated if series resistance (16 +/- 6 MOmega) changed by more than 20%.

###begin title 38
Discussion
###end title 38
###begin p 39
###xml 68 69 68 69 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">A</sub>
###xml 283 289 283 289 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">versus</italic>
###xml 325 326 325 326 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">A</sub>
###xml 342 349 342 349 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">A,local</sub>
###xml 452 456 451 455 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">A,SC</sub>
###xml 560 564 558 562 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">A,SC</sub>
###xml 701 702 695 696 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">A</sub>
###xml 884 888 878 882 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">A,SC</sub>
###xml 952 957 946 951 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">gabra</italic>
###xml 958 961 952 955 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">&#8722;/&#8722;</sup>
###xml 962 966 <span type="species:ncbi:10090">mice</span>
Previous work investigating the subunit composition of synaptic GABAA receptors has measured spontaneous or stimulus-evoked GABAergic inhibition in the hippocampus while blocking glutamatergic excitation. In the present study, we describe the distinct pharmacology of locally-evoked versus Schaffer collateral-stimulated GABAAR currents. GABAA,local currents were eliminated by 1 muM gabazine and were markedly enhanced by 400 nM zolpidem, whereas GABAA,SC currents were less sensitive to 1 muM gabazine and zolpidem. The gabazine-insensitive component of GABAA,SC showed the greatest reduction by L-655,708 and was relatively insensitive to zolpidem, suggesting a significant proportion of alpha5GABAAR. Furthermore, the gabazine-insensitive current was markedly enhanced following burst stimulation of Schaffer collaterals. Finally, we confirmed the specificity of L-655,708 on GABAA,SC and the gabazine-insensitive currents by recording from WT and gabra5-/- mice.
###end p 39
###begin p 40
###xml 163 190 163 190 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" rid="bib25" ref-type="bibr">Ouardouz and Lacaille, 1997</xref>
###xml 202 209 202 209 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">A,local</sub>
###xml 224 227 224 225 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">&#964;</italic>
###xml 227 232 225 230 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">decay</sub>
###xml 395 402 393 400 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">A,local</sub>
###xml 411 415 409 413 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">A,SC</sub>
###xml 591 623 589 621 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" rid="bib26 bib7" ref-type="bibr">Pearce, 1993; Banks et&#160;al., 1998</xref>
###xml 693 700 691 698 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">A,local</sub>
###xml 709 713 707 711 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">A,SC</sub>
###xml 811 815 809 813 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">A,SC</sub>
IPSCs recorded in CA1 pyramidal neurons evoked by local stimulation at the SR were likely generated by perisomatic targeting interneurons, including basket cells (Ouardouz and Lacaille, 1997). This GABAA,local current had a taudecay of 163 ms at 0 mV. In experiments designed to study Schaffer collateral-stimulated currents, the IPSCs decayed with a strikingly slow time constant (277 ms). GABAA,local and GABAA,SC also showed significantly different time-to-peak, and 10-90% rise time. Previous studies have already shown evidence for such fast and slow components of synaptic inhibition (Pearce, 1993; Banks et al., 1998). Whereas there was no significant difference in latency between GABAA,local and GABAA,SC, suggesting that stimulation at the SR could also trigger direct activation of interneurons, GABAA,SC(gz) also showed a longer latency, suggesting that under these conditions, a delay in activation of presynaptic interneurons contributed to the longer time-to-peak.
###end p 40
###begin p 41
###xml 97 101 97 101 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">A,SC</sub>
###xml 118 125 118 125 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">A,local</sub>
###xml 287 325 287 325 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" rid="bib40 bib10" ref-type="bibr">Tia et&#160;al., 1996; Burgard et&#160;al., 1999</xref>
###xml 568 588 568 588 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" rid="bib31" ref-type="bibr">Scimemi et&#160;al., 2004</xref>
###xml 787 815 787 815 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" rid="bib29" ref-type="bibr">Roepstorff and Lambert, 1994</xref>
###xml 1060 1105 1060 1105 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" rid="bib37 bib2" ref-type="bibr">Szabadics et&#160;al., 2007; Ali and Thomson, 2008</xref>
###xml 1354 1358 1354 1358 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">A,SC</sub>
###xml 1556 1587 1556 1587 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" rid="bib18" ref-type="bibr">Maccaferri and Dingledine, 2002</xref>
###xml 1694 1715 1694 1715 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" rid="bib14" ref-type="bibr">Hefft and Jonas, 2005</xref>
###xml 287 290 <span type="species:ncbi:72648">Tia</span>
Several possible explanations could account for the late peak and surprisingly slow decay of GABAA,SC compared to GABAA,local. Firstly, using fast perfusion on excised patches from cells expressing recombinant receptors, it has been shown that subunit composition can affect decay time (Tia et al., 1996; Burgard et al., 1999). Secondly, the time course of GABA concentration at the release sites would affect the response kinetics, for example the rise time responses for extrasynaptic receptors could be slowed down as has been suggested for glutamatergic synapses (Scimemi et al., 2004). Thirdly, long exposure to GABA, either in the synaptic cleft or in extrasynaptic space, could produce the reactivation of synaptic receptors as previously shown by modifying GABA uptake kinetics (Roepstorff and Lambert, 1994). Recent studies using somatic recordings in anatomically-identified connected cell pairs suggest that the time course of inhibitory responses can indeed be determined both by subunit composition and by distinct transmitter release transients (Szabadics et al., 2007; Ali and Thomson, 2008), but we cannot exclude the possibility that dendritic filtering contributes to the slow kinetics as recorded at the soma. A further possibility with potential physiological significance is that the slow rise and decay kinetics we observed for GABAA,SC is due to the pattern of activation of GABAergic interneurons. For example, late-firing dendritic targeting interneurons have been shown to generate slow GABAergic events in CA1 pyramidal neurons (Maccaferri and Dingledine, 2002). Furthermore, bursts of GABA release produced by several action potentials or slow asynchronous release (Hefft and Jonas, 2005) could contribute to slow kinetics.
###end p 41
###begin p 42
###xml 4 8 4 8 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">A,SC</sub>

###xml 1555 1562 1525 1532 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">A,local</sub>
###xml 1763 1764 1733 1734 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">A</sub>
###xml 1831 1835 1801 1805 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">A,SC</sub>
###xml 1941 1945 1911 1915 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">A,SC</sub>

###xml 1954 1957 <span type="species:ncbi:10116">rat</span>
###xml 1962 1967 <span type="species:ncbi:10090">mouse</span>
GABAA,SC and GABAA,local showed different sensitivities to gabazine. We consistently found that approximately 20% of GABAA,SC charge transfer evoked by single stimulation was insensitive to 1 muM gabazine, and this fraction increased to approximately50% when using burst stimulation. We interpreted this finding as an indication of a component mediated by alpha5GABAARs as it has been previously shown that the tonic current mediated by these is not sensitive to 1 muM gabazine (Bai et al., 2001; Caraiscos et al., 2004). We obtained further evidence that alpha5GABAARs are activated by Schaffer collateral stimulation by studying the effects of L-655,708 on the isolated GABAAR components described above. GABAA,SC(gz) produced either by single or burst stimulation was reduced by 30% and there was no significant difference in the amount of reduction using either type of stimulation (P = 0.27) as would be expected if in both cases the synaptically evoked current is mediated by a similar proportion of alpha5GABAARs. L-655,708 also produced a significant but smaller effect on GABAA,SC and GABAA,local. In previous studies, spontaneous GABAA,local currents were found not to contain an alpha5GABAAR-mediated component (Caraiscos et al., 2004; Glykys and Mody, 2006; Zarnowska et al., 2009), however, it is likely that the sorting of populations of spontaneous IPSCs by their decay time for analysis could limit the detection of a slow component in GABAA,local currents. Furthermore, it is likely that the extracellular stimulation used to produce GABAA,local under our experimental conditions would recruit interneurons other than perisomatic targeting interneurons. It is also possible that gabazine, as a competitive antagonist, could be displaced from GABAA receptors by released GABA under our recording conditions for GABAA,SC(gz). Although we do not have evidence to discard this possibility, the effects of 20 nM L-655,708 on GABAA,SC in both rat and mouse recordings without the use of gabazine strongly support a specific alpha5GABAAR-mediated component.

###begin p 43

###xml 390 395 <span type="species:ncbi:9606">human</span>
###xml 527 530 <span type="species:ncbi:10116">rat</span>
###xml 535 540 <span type="species:ncbi:10090">mouse</span>
We corroborated the specificity of 20 nM L-655,708 on GABAA,SC and GABAA,SC(gz) currents using gabra5-/- mice and WT littermate controls. There was a one third reduction in the GABAA,SC and GABAA,SC(gz) currents from initial control values following application of L-655,708 in WT mice, and no significant reduction in gabra5-/- mice. In vitro analysis of L-655,708 activity on recombinant human GABAARs showed a maximum inhibition of 20% of the alpha5GABAAR current (Atack et al., 2006). Consistent with this we found in both rat and mouse recordings that 20 nM L-655,708 inhibited approximately 30% of GABAA,SC(gz).
###end p 43
###begin p 44
###xml 48 49 48 49 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">A</sub>
###xml 201 202 197 198 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">A</sub>
###xml 234 235 226 227 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">A</sub>
###xml 238 239 230 231 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">K</italic>
###xml 263 277 254 268 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" rid="bib44" ref-type="bibr">Sieghart, 1995</xref>
###xml 284 291 275 282 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">A,local</sub>
###xml 406 410 397 401 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">A,SC</sub>
###xml 435 439 426 430 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">A,SC</sub>
###xml 488 492 479 483 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">A,SC</sub>
###xml 723 724 702 703 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">A</sub>
###xml 728 748 707 727 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" rid="bib39" ref-type="bibr">Thomson et&#160;al., 2000</xref>
###xml 930 931 905 906 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">A</sub>
###xml 1026 1030 1001 1005 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">A,SC</sub>
###xml 1078 1079 1049 1050 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">A</sub>
###xml 1159 1160 1126 1127 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">A</sub>
###xml 1264 1268 1231 1235 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">A,SC</sub>
As a final test to probe the composition of GABAARs mediating the early and late components of inhibition, we used 400 nM zolpidem which is a highly potent and selective benzodiazepine, with alpha5GABAAR sparing properties (alpha5GABAAR, Ki > 15 muM, reviewed in Sieghart, 1995). GABAA,local was markedly enhanced after application of zolpidem with a three fold increase in charge transfer compared to GABAA,SC(gz). In comparison, GABAA,SC showed only a two fold increase compared to GABAA,SC(gz). We interpret this result to suggest that local stimulation in SR near SP in the absence of fast synaptic excitation mostly activates perisomatic targeting interneurons, which selectively activate alpha1, alpha2 and alpha3GABAARs (Thomson et al., 2000), and that activation of Schaffer collaterals not only stimulates perisomatic targeting cells but also dendritic targeting interneurons, some of which specifically target alpha5GABAARs. Therefore, the combined results using 400 nM zolpidem and 20 nM L-655,708 suggest that GABAA,SC contains a significant population of alpha5GABAARs. Thus, the present observations highlight the strong activation of alpha5GABAARs following Schaffer collateral stimulation in the hippocampus and the pharmacological analysis of GABAA,SC(gz) suggests a particularly robust activation during bursting activity.
###end p 44
###begin p 45
###xml 166 222 166 222 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" rid="bib7 bib26 bib43" ref-type="bibr">Banks et&#160;al., 1998; Pearce, 1993; Zarnowska et&#160;al., 2009</xref>
###xml 266 267 262 263 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">A</sub>
###xml 290 308 286 304 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" rid="bib33" ref-type="bibr">Sperk et&#160;al., 1997</xref>
###xml 483 490 479 486 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">in vivo</italic>
###xml 511 531 507 527 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" rid="bib16" ref-type="bibr">Kamondi et&#160;al., 1998</xref>
###xml 581 585 577 581 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">A,SC</sub>
###xml 594 598 590 594 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">A,SC</sub>
###xml 644 663 640 659 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" rid="bib41" ref-type="bibr">Vicini et&#160;al., 1998</xref>
###xml 810 831 806 827 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" rid="bib34" ref-type="bibr">Staley and Mody, 1992</xref>
Our results are consistent with previous suggestions that slow inhibition is mediated by a specific subpopulation of interneurons and molecularly distinct receptors (Banks et al., 1998; Pearce, 1993; Zarnowska et al., 2009). The widespread localisation of alpha5GABAARs at dendritic sites (Sperk et al., 1997) suggests that they are involved in gating dendritic excitability, for example they could be involved in regulating the generation of dendritic spikes such as those observed in vivo during sharp waves (Kamondi et al., 1998). Furthermore, the time course similarity of GABAA,SC and GABAA,SC(gz) to the decay kinetics of NMDA receptors (Vicini et al., 1998) makes them well suited for inhibition of the induction of long-term potentiation by providing a shunting inhibitory effect on the NMDAR current (Staley and Mody, 1992).
###end p 45
###begin p 46
###xml 139 144 139 144 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">gabra</italic>
###xml 145 148 145 148 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">&#8722;/&#8722;</sup>
###xml 354 355 350 351 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">A</sub>
###xml 468 469 460 461 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">A</sub>
###xml 711 712 699 700 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">A</sub>
###xml 753 754 737 738 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">A</sub>
###xml 942 943 922 923 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">A</sub>
###xml 966 986 946 966 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" rid="bib5" ref-type="bibr">Ballard et&#160;al., 2009</xref>
###xml 1028 1029 1004 1005 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">A</sub>
###xml 1054 1061 1030 1037 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">in vivo</italic>
###xml 68 72 <span type="species:ncbi:10090">mice</span>
###xml 149 153 <span type="species:ncbi:10090">mice</span>
The higher acquisition rate in associative memory tasks observed in mice after systemic application of the inverse-agonist L,655,708 or in gabra5-/- mice could be the expression of plasticity produced by enhanced dendritic excitation. In order to understand the cellular and network mechanisms that lead to enhanced learning after reduction of alpha5GABAARs function it will be necessary to study the requirement for activation/silencing of inputs targeting alpha5GABAARs on hippocampal pyramidal neurons by afferent inputs to the hippocampus and their timing in relation to the timing of somatic and dendritic spikes. Here we provide evidence of activity pattern-dependent feed-forward activation of alpha5GABAARs in CA1. Testing the role of alpha5GABAARs in controlling the generation of dendritic spikes or in the fine tuning of long-term potentiation awaits the broader availability of more potent and selective drugs acting on alpha5GABAARs, such as RO4938581 (Ballard et al., 2009) or the specific activation of alpha5GABAAR-targeting interneurons in vivo.
