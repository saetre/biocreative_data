A missense mutation in the nuclear gene coding for the mitochondrial aspartyl-tRNA synthetase suppresses a mitochondrial tRNAAsp mutation

###begin p 2
###xml 30 34 30 34 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">NSM3</italic>
###xml 129 132 129 132 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">Asp</sup>
###xml 141 165 141 165 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">Saccharomyces cerevisiae</italic>
###xml 209 213 209 213 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">NSM3</italic>
###xml 406 409 406 409 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">Asp</sup>
###xml 629 633 629 633 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">MSD1</italic>
###xml 658 662 658 662 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">NSM3</italic>
###xml 998 1002 998 1002 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">NSM3</italic>
###xml 1282 1285 1282 1285 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">Asp</sup>
###xml 105 110 <span type="species:ncbi:4932">yeast</span>
###xml 141 165 <span type="species:ncbi:4932">Saccharomyces cerevisiae</span>
###xml 531 536 <span type="species:ncbi:4932">yeast</span>
The nuclear suppressor allele NSM3 in strain FF1210-6C/170-E22 (E22), which suppresses a mutation of the yeast mitochondrial tRNAAsp gene in Saccharomyces cerevisiae, was cloned and identified. To isolate the NSM3 allele, a genomic DNA library using the vector YEp13 was constructed from strain E22. Nine YEp13 recombinant plasmids were isolated and shown to suppress the mutation in the mitochondrial tRNAAsp gene. These nine plasmids carry a common 4.5-kb chromosomal DNA fragment which contains an open reading frame coding for yeast mitochondrial aspartyl-tRNA synthetase (AspRS) on the basis of its sequence identity to the MSD1 gene. The comparison of NSM3 DNA sequences between the suppressor and the wild-type version, cloned from the parental strain FF1210-6C/170, revealed a G to A transition that causes the replacement of amino acid serine (AGU) by an asparagine (AAU) at position 388. In experiments switching restriction fragments between the wild type and suppressor versions of the NSM3 gene, the rescue of respiratory deficiency was demonstrated only when the substitution was present in the construct. We conclude that the base substitution causes the respiratory rescue and discuss the possible mechanism as one which enhances interaction between the mutated tRNAAsp and the suppressor version of AspRS.
###end p 2
###begin title 3
INTRODUCTION
###end title 3
###begin p 4
###xml 246 247 246 247 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="gkd279c1">1</xref>
###xml 442 466 442 466 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">Saccharomyces cerevisiae</italic>
###xml 554 557 554 557 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">tsl</italic>
###xml 593 597 593 597 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">var1</italic>
###xml 649 650 649 650 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="gkd279c2">2</xref>
###xml 651 652 651 652 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="gkd279c3">3</xref>
###xml 436 441 <span type="species:ncbi:4932">yeast</span>
###xml 442 466 <span type="species:ncbi:4932">Saccharomyces cerevisiae</span>
Mitochondria have their own translational machinery to synthesize the proteins encoded by their DNA. Production of these proteins is coordinated with the production of proteins from nuclear genes, which are required for mitochondrial biogenesis (1). Although most protein components of the translational machinery are encoded in the nucleus, the mitochondrial genome encodes a complete set of the RNAs necessary for translation. In the yeast Saccharomyces cerevisiae, the mitochondrial genome encodes 24 tRNAs, the large and small subunits of rRNAs, the tsl (tRNA synthesis locus) RNA and the var1 protein (a subunit of the mitochondrial ribosome) (2,3).
###end p 4
###begin p 5
###xml 424 425 424 425 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="gkd279c3">3</xref>
###xml 439 442 439 442 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">tsl</italic>
###xml 487 488 487 488 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="gkd279c4">4</xref>
###xml 679 680 679 680 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="gkd279c5">5</xref>
Several steps are involved in expression of mitochondrial tRNA genes: (i) transcription initiation to synthesize the primary RNA molecules; (ii) maturation of tRNAs by 5'- and 3'-processing enzymes, modification enzymes, and CCA terminal transferase; (iii) recognition of tRNAs by mitochondrial aminoacyl-tRNA synthetases; and (iv) interaction of amino-acylated tRNAs with mitochondrial ribosomes and translational factors (3). Except the tsl RNA for the 5'-processing of tRNA molecule (4), the genes encoding the enzymes that catalyze these steps are located in nucleus. These enzymes are synthesized on cytoplasmic ribosomes and subsequently transported into the mitochondria (5).
###end p 5
###begin p 6
###xml 249 250 249 250 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">&#8211;</sup>
###xml 252 253 252 253 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="gkd279c6">6</xref>
###xml 277 278 277 278 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">&#8211;</sup>
###xml 415 416 415 416 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="gkd279c7">7</xref>
###xml 417 419 417 419 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="gkd279c10">10</xref>
###xml 433 434 433 434 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">&#8211;</sup>
###xml 488 490 488 490 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="gkd279c11">11</xref>
###xml 507 508 507 508 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">&#8211;</sup>
###xml 650 653 650 653 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">Asp</sup>
###xml 780 783 780 783 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">Asp</sup>
###xml 841 843 841 843 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="gkd279c12">12</xref>
###xml 903 906 903 906 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">Asp</sup>
###xml 982 983 982 983 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">&#8211;</sup>
###xml 1090 1092 1090 1092 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="gkd279c13">13</xref>
###xml 17 22 <span type="species:ncbi:4932">yeast</span>
###xml 268 273 <span type="species:ncbi:4932">yeast</span>
Mutations in the yeast mitochondrial tRNA genes result in the loss of mitochondrial protein synthesis and a phenotype of respiratory deficiency that is unable to use glycerol as a carbon source. This type of mitochondrial mutant is designated as syn- (6). A number of yeast syn- mutants have been characterized at the molecular level, and in most cases, a single base substitution in the tRNA genes has been found (7-10). In most syn- mutants, the mitochondrial genome is gradually lost (11). However, a syn- mutant FF1210-6C/170 contains a stable mitochondrial genome. It has a single base substitution (C to T transition) at position 72 in the tRNAAsp gene, resulting in two adjacent non-Watson-Crick base pairings at the acceptor stem. This mutant has a decreased level of tRNAAsp and shows no detectable mitochondrial protein synthesis (12). To understand the structural features of the mutated tRNAAsp that are responsible for the respiratory deficiency, revertants of this syn- mutant were isolated, including four independent nuclear suppressors and three other types of revertants (13).
###end p 6
###begin p 7
###xml 33 36 33 36 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">Asp</sup>
###xml 177 179 177 179 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="gkd279c14">14</xref>
###xml 180 182 180 182 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="gkd279c19">19</xref>
###xml 251 254 251 254 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">Asp</sup>
###xml 389 391 389 391 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="gkd279c20">20</xref>
###xml 392 394 392 394 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="gkd279c21">21</xref>
###xml 562 566 562 566 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">NSM3</italic>
###xml 575 576 575 576 <underline xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">n</underline>
###xml 583 584 583 584 <underline xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">s</underline>
###xml 599 600 599 600 <underline xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">m</underline>
###xml 743 746 743 746 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">Asp</sup>
###xml 871 875 871 875 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">NSM3</italic>
###xml 699 704 <span type="species:ncbi:4932">yeast</span>
The original mutation of the tRNAAsp gene was located at the acceptor stem which was demonstrated to play a vital role in the recognition by cognate aminoacyl tRNA synthetases (14-19). Several suppression mechanisms are possible, i.e. the mutated tRNAAsp is recognized by a mutated aspartyl-tRNA synthetase or by proteins involved in the processing of pre-tRNAs or translation elongation (20,21). To distinguish between these possibilities, we characterized one of these suppressor strains, FF1210-6C/170-E22 (hereafter referred to as strain E22) which contains NSM3 allele (nuclear suppressor of a mitochondrial mutation). A genomic DNA library was constructed from strain E22 and transformed into yeast strain H95 containing the mutated tRNAAsp gene to isolate recombinant plasmids that are able to suppress respiratory deficiency in strain H95. Here, we show that the NSM3 allele is a mutated mitochondrial aspartyl-tRNA synthetase gene and that a G to A transition, which results in an amino acid substitution, is responsible for respiratory rescue.

Strains. Escherichia coli strain YMC9 [F-, endA1, hsdR17 (r-, m+), supE44, thia-1, DeltalacU169] (23) was used for the construction of the genomic library. The strain E22 was grown in 500 ml of YPG medium (1% yeast extract, 1% Bacto-peptone, 50 mM sodium phosphate buffer pH 6.5 and 2% glycerol). The chromosomal DNA was isolated as described in Sherman et al. (24) and partially digested with Sau3AI. DNA fragments >3 kb were purified using a 10-40% sucrose density gradient, and ligated with BamHI-digested YEp13 plasmid DNA. The ligated DNA was then transformed into E.coli strain YMC9. All ampicillin resistant transformants were pooled and the recombinant plasmid DNA was isolated as described in Sambrook et al. (26).

###xml 274 277 262 265 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">Hin</italic>

###begin title 26
RESULTS
###end title 26
###begin title 27
###xml 15 19 15 19 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">NSM3</italic>
Cloning of the NSM3 allele
###end title 27
###begin p 28
###xml 13 17 13 17 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">NSM3</italic>
###xml 126 129 126 129 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">Bam</italic>
###xml 205 206 205 206 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">r</sup>
###xml 301 302 301 302 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">s</sup>
###xml 338 339 338 339 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">r</sup>
###xml 401 407 401 407 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">E.coli</italic>
###xml 430 431 430 431 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">s</sup>
###xml 528 529 528 529 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">+</sup>
###xml 401 407 <span type="species:ncbi:562">E.coli</span>
###xml 459 464 <span type="species:ncbi:4932">yeast</span>
To clone the NSM3 allele, a chromosomal DNA library using the YEp13 shuttle vector was constructed from strain E22. Since the BamHI site in YEp13 is located in a gene giving resistance to tetracycline (Tetr), transformants harboring plasmids with chromosomal inserts are sensitive to tetracycline (Tets), and those without inserts are Tetr. Plasmid DNA isolated from about 16 000 ampicillin resistant E.coli transformants (60% Tets) was used to transform the yeast leucine auxotroph, H95, to leucine prototroph. About 32 000 LEU+ transformants were obtained and transferred to YPG plates by replica plating to select for respiration positive colonies. Nine respiration positives were obtained.
###end p 28
###begin p 29
###xml 103 106 103 106 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">Asp</sup>
To eliminate the possibility that the respiratory rescue was due to reversion of the mitochondrial tRNAAsp mutation, each respiratory positive transformant was grown in YPD medium without selection for overnight and spread onto YPD plates to obtain single colonies. These colonies were subsequently transferred onto both minimal medium and YPG plates to examine leucine auxotroph and respiratory deficiency, respectively. Fifteen percent of colonies showed leucine auxotroph, indicating the loss of YEp plasmid. All of these leucine auxotrophs were also respiration deficient. These results indicated that the respiratory rescue in the nine transformants is due to existence of the YEp13-recombinant plasmids.
###end p 29
###begin p 30
###xml 59 60 59 60 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">E</italic>
###xml 61 65 61 65 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">coli</italic>
###xml 207 208 207 208 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">+</sup>
###xml 464 465 464 465 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="fig" rid="gkd279f1">1</xref>
###xml 59 65 <span type="species:ncbi:562">E.coli</span>
Plasmid DNAs from the nine transformants were amplified in E.coli before they were transformed back into strain H95 to re-confirm that the YEp13-recombinant plasmids contained the suppressor alleles. All LEU+ transformants were shown to be respiration positive. The restriction enzyme maps of the recombinant DNA fragments in these nine transformants were determined and shown to be identical. Each plasmid contained the same 4.5-kb chromosomal DNA fragment (Fig. 1). This recombinant plasmid was designated as YEp13-NSM3.
###end p 30
###begin title 31
Gene amplification is not required for suppression
###end title 31
###begin p 32
###xml 282 284 282 284 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="gkd279c33">33</xref>
To determine if the respiratory rescue resulted from amplification of a wild-type gene due to the high copy number of YEp plasmids and not from a mutation, the 4.5-kb chromosomal DNA fragment was subcloned into vectors YCp and YIp, which are single-copy plasmid per haploid genome (33), and the resulting plasmid DNAs were transformed into strain H95. In both cases, the transformants were respiratory competent, indicating that a high copy number of plasmid is not necessary for the rescue.
###end p 32
###begin title 33
###xml 14 18 14 18 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">NSM3</italic>
The wild-type NSM3 allele is required for maintenance of the mitochondrial DNA (mtDNA)
###end title 33
###begin p 34
###xml 4 8 4 8 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">URA3</italic>
###xml 26 29 26 29 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">Hin</italic>
###xml 83 86 83 86 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">Hin</italic>
###xml 103 104 103 104 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="fig" rid="gkd279f1">1</xref>
###xml 147 151 147 151 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">NSM3</italic>
###xml 302 304 302 304 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="gkd279c34">34</xref>
###xml 478 479 478 479 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">+</sup>
###xml 610 611 610 611 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">+</sup>
###xml 699 700 699 700 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="fig" rid="gkd279f2">2</xref>
###xml 746 750 746 750 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">NSM3</italic>
The URA3 gene in a 1.1-kb HindIII DNA fragment was inserted at the two neighboring HindIII sites (Fig. 1), resulting in a disrupted version of the NSM3 allele. This disrupted allele was used to substitute for the wild-type allele in strain XRD9 by a double crossover event of homologous recombination (34). The substitution was confirmed by Southern blot hybridization (data not shown). In contrast to the recipient strain XRD9 that is able to grow stably on YPG medium, the URA+ transformants rapidly became unable to grow on YPG medium, indicating the loss of mitochondrial function. Hybridization of the URA+ colonies with radiolabeled mtDNA showed that these colonies had lost their mtDNA (Fig. 2). These results indicated that the wild-type NSM3 allele is essential for maintenance of mtDNA.
###end p 34
###begin title 35
###xml 50 54 50 54 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">NSM3</italic>
Identification of the suppression mutation in the NSM3 allele
###end title 35
###begin p 36
###xml 32 35 32 35 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">Hin</italic>
###xml 132 135 132 135 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">Hin</italic>
###xml 254 255 254 255 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="fig" rid="gkd279f1">1</xref>
Since vector YEp13 contains two HindIII sites, the chromosomal DNA fragment of plasmid YEp13-NSM3 was divided into three regions by HindIII digestion. The two large fragments, containing either the left or the right end of chromosomal DNA fragment (Fig. 1), were subcloned into YEp13 and the resulting plasmid DNAs were transformed into strain H95 to test for respiratory rescue. The results showed that neither region was able to rescue the respiratory deficiency.
###end p 36
###begin p 37
###xml 76 79 76 79 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">Hin</italic>
###xml 275 276 275 276 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="fig" rid="gkd279f1">1</xref>
###xml 362 365 362 365 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">Nco</italic>
###xml 433 436 433 436 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">Cla</italic>
###xml 513 514 513 514 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="fig" rid="gkd279f1">1</xref>
To identify the suppression mutation, the 3.5 kb of DNA surrounding the two HindIII sites in the chromosomal insert was sequenced. Among the six possible reading frames, only one was found to be significant, as indicated by an open box below the restriction enzyme map (Fig. 1). The first AUG codon of the open reading frame is 228 nucleotides downstream of the NcoI site. The open reading frame ends 121 nucleotides upstream of the ClaI site. This open reading frame codes for a protein of 658 amino acids (Fig. 1).
###end p 37
###begin p 38
###xml 464 465 452 453 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="fig" rid="gkd279f1">1</xref>
###xml 657 658 645 646 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="fig" rid="gkd279f1">1</xref>
The expression of this open reading frame was examined by northern blot hybridization and S1 nuclease protection experiments. A single transcript of approximately2000 nucleotides in length was detected (data not shown), slightly larger than the size of the open reading frame. The results of S1-nuclease protection experiments revealed three possible initiation sites at the 5' end of the open reading frame (indicated by filled circles with vertical bars in Fig. 1). The middle site is about five times stronger than the other two, suggesting that this may represent the major site for initiation of transcription, 251 bp upstream of the start codon (Fig. 1).
###end p 38
###begin p 39
###xml 236 240 236 240 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">MSD1</italic>
###xml 247 249 247 249 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="gkd279c35">35</xref>
###xml 168 173 <span type="species:ncbi:4932">yeast</span>
The deduced amino acid sequence was used to search for homology with known proteins. Except one amino acid difference at position 388, this protein is identical to the yeast mitochondrial aspartyl-tRNA synthetase (AspRS) encoded by the MSD1 gene (35). At the nucleotide level, these two proteins have only one base difference, a G to A transition, 1160 bp downstream of the start codon.
###end p 39
###begin p 40
###xml 252 256 252 256 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">MSD1</italic>
To distinguish the base difference as the suppression mutation from DNA sequence polymorphism, the wild-type allele in this region was cloned from strain FF1210-6C/170 and its DNA sequence was determined, which was found to be identical to that of the MSD1 gene. Thus, the G to A transition results in the substitution of asparagine (AAU) for a serine (AGU), (S388N).
###end p 40
###begin p 41
###xml 198 202 198 202 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">NSM3</italic>
###xml 216 217 216 217 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="fig" rid="gkd279f3">3</xref>
###xml 296 299 296 299 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">Hin</italic>
###xml 307 310 307 310 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">Hin</italic>
###xml 327 328 327 328 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="fig" rid="gkd279f3">3</xref>
###xml 479 482 479 482 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">Nco</italic>
###xml 484 487 484 487 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">Hin</italic>
###xml 495 498 495 498 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">Hin</italic>
###xml 503 506 503 506 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">Bgl</italic>
###xml 594 597 594 597 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">Hin</italic>
###xml 601 604 601 604 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">Hin</italic>
###xml 648 649 648 649 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="fig" rid="gkd279f3">3</xref>
###xml 838 841 838 841 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">Asp</sup>
To confirm that this G to A transition is the cause of the suppression, four chimeric YCp plasmids were constructed with the DNA fragments from either the wild-type or the suppressor version of the NSM3 allele (Fig. 3B). The base substitution, as indicated by an asterisk, is located between the HincII and HindIII sites (Fig. 3A). YCpGL852, carrying the entire suppressor DNA fragment, caused suppression and served as a positive control. YCpGL1104-1, substitution of wild-type NcoI-HincII and HindIII-BglII fragments, did not affect suppression, but YCpGL901-4, substitution of the wild-type HincII-HindIII fragment, eliminated suppression (Fig. 3C). Since the mutation encoding the Ser to Asn substitution is located in this fragment, we conclude that the S388N substitution is responsible for the suppression of the mitochondrial tRNAAsp mutation.
###end p 41
###begin p 42
###xml 50 70 50 70 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">Thermus thermophilus</italic>
###xml 132 134 128 130 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="gkd279c36">36</xref>
###xml 312 313 304 305 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="fig" rid="gkd279f4">4</xref>
###xml 50 70 <span type="species:ncbi:274">Thermus thermophilus</span>
Based on the structural information of AspRS from Thermus thermophilus, the S388N mutation is located at the end of alpha-helix H7 (36). An amino acid sequence alignment among 21 sequenced AspRSs revealed that positively charged amino acids at the third position of the alpha-helix H7 are highly conserved (Fig. 4). An implication that the alpha-helix H7 contacts the tRNA molecule and the possible suppression mechanism are discussed below.
###end p 42
###begin title 43
DISCUSSION
###end title 43
###begin p 44
###xml 61 65 61 65 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">NSM3</italic>
###xml 157 161 157 161 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">MSD1</italic>
###xml 230 232 230 232 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="gkd279c35">35</xref>
###xml 239 243 239 243 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">MSD1</italic>
###xml 319 323 319 323 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">NSM3</italic>
###xml 516 519 516 519 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">Asp</sup>
###xml 184 189 <span type="species:ncbi:4932">yeast</span>
In the present work, we characterized the nuclear suppressor NSM3 allele. It appears to encode the mitochondrial AspRS based on its sequence identity to the MSD1 gene, which codes for yeast mitochondrial AspRS in strain D272-10B (35). The MSD1 gene activity is required for maintenance of the mitochondrial genome. The NSM3 allele contains a single amino acid substitution from serine to asparagine at position 388 in the mitochondrial AspRS, S388N, which is responsible for the suppression of the mitochondrial tRNAAsp mutation in strain E22.
###end p 44
###begin p 45
###xml 47 50 47 50 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">Asp</sup>
###xml 148 149 148 149 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="gkd279c9">9</xref>
###xml 150 152 150 152 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="gkd279c37">37</xref>
###xml 276 279 276 279 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">Asp</sup>
###xml 514 517 514 517 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">Asp</sup>
###xml 550 552 550 552 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="gkd279c37">37</xref>
###xml 553 555 553 555 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="gkd279c38">38</xref>
###xml 639 642 639 642 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">Asp</sup>
###xml 766 768 766 768 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="gkd279c21">21</xref>
###xml 769 771 769 771 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="gkd279c37">37</xref>
###xml 772 774 772 774 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="gkd279c38">38</xref>
###xml 812 815 812 815 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">Asp</sup>
###xml 868 870 868 870 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="gkd279c39">39</xref>
###xml 934 937 934 937 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">Asp</sup>
The original mutation of the mitochondrial tRNAAsp gene in strain FF1210-6C/170 was a C to T transition at position 72 (C72T) in the acceptor stem (9,37), resulting in two consecutive non-Watson-Crick base-pairings and causing mitochondrial defect. Since a lower level of tRNAAsp was observed in strain FF1210-6C/170, this mutation might affect the numerous post-transcriptional modifications. The results of both primer-extension and S1 nuclease protection experiments indicated that the 5' end of the mutant tRNAAsp molecule is properly processed (37,38). The results of northern blot hybridization further revealed that the mutated tRNAAsp appears to be the same size as that of the wild-type molecules. No accumulation of larger pre-tRNA molecules was observed (21,37,38), in contrast to a mitochondrial tRNAAsp mutant in which the 3' end processing is abolished (39). These results suggested that a certain amount of mutated tRNAAsp molecules are properly processed at both the 5' and 3' ends.
###end p 45
###begin p 46
###xml 162 164 162 164 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="gkd279c14">14</xref>
###xml 165 167 165 167 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="gkd279c16">16</xref>
###xml 168 170 168 170 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="gkd279c18">18</xref>
###xml 171 173 171 173 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="gkd279c19">19</xref>
###xml 174 176 174 176 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="gkd279c40">40</xref>
###xml 177 179 177 179 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="gkd279c41">41</xref>
###xml 326 328 326 328 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="gkd279c12">12</xref>
###xml 361 364 361 364 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">Asp</sup>
###xml 566 569 566 569 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">Asp</sup>
###xml 571 573 571 573 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="gkd279c13">13</xref>
The acceptor stem where the C72U mutation is located has been proven to play a critical role in recognition of tRNAs by their cognate aminoacyl-tRNA synthetases (14-16,18,19,40,41). AspRS is required for maintenance of the mitochondrial genome (this study), therefore, the stable mitochondrial genome in strain FF1210-6C/170 (12) suggested that the mutated tRNAAsp was probably recognized by AspRS at a lower efficiency. This is consistent with the observation that the defective phenotype of strain FF1210-6C/170 was corrected by over-production of the mutated tRNAAsp (13).
###end p 46
###begin p 47
###xml 113 116 113 116 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">Phe</sup>
###xml 124 127 124 127 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">Asp</sup>
###xml 180 183 180 183 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">Phe</sup>
###xml 204 206 204 206 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="gkd279c13">13</xref>
###xml 207 209 207 209 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="gkd279c20">20</xref>
###xml 210 212 210 212 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="gkd279c21">21</xref>
###xml 416 420 416 420 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">NSM3</italic>
The mitochondrial function in two temperature-sensitive mutants, caused by mutations at either mitochondrial tRNAPhe or tRNAAsp, was restored by over-expression of the mutated tRNAPhe or wild-type AspRS (13,20,21). In this work, one copy of a mutated version of the gene coding for mitochondrial AspRS per haploid cell was sufficient to recover the mitochondrial function. Thus, the respiratory rescue is not due to NSM3 over-production.
###end p 47
###begin p 48
###xml 133 135 133 135 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="gkd279c42">42</xref>
###xml 136 138 136 138 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="gkd279c44">44</xref>
###xml 361 363 354 356 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="gkd279c45">45</xref>
###xml 364 366 357 359 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="gkd279c46">46</xref>
###xml 465 471 458 464 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">E.coli</italic>
###xml 473 487 466 480 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">T.thermophilus</italic>
###xml 492 504 485 497 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">S.cerevisiae</italic>
###xml 676 690 669 683 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">T.thermophilus</italic>
###xml 698 700 691 693 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="gkd279c36">36</xref>
###xml 767 769 760 762 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="gkd279c47">47</xref>
###xml 946 948 939 941 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="gkd279c36">36</xref>
###xml 949 951 942 944 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="gkd279c47">47</xref>
###xml 974 988 967 981 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">T.thermophilus</italic>

###xml 1322 1325 1311 1314 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">Asp</sup>
###xml 0 5 <span type="species:ncbi:4932">Yeast</span>
###xml 465 471 <span type="species:ncbi:562">E.coli</span>
###xml 473 487 <span type="species:ncbi:274">T.thermophilus</span>
###xml 492 504 <span type="species:ncbi:4932">S.cerevisiae</span>
###xml 676 690 <span type="species:ncbi:274">T.thermophilus</span>
###xml 974 988 <span type="species:ncbi:274">T.thermophilus</span>
###xml 1009 1015 <span type="species:ncbi:562">E.coli</span>
Yeast mitochondrial AspRS is a member of class II aminoacyl-tRNA synthetases, containing characteristic conserved motifs 1, 2 and 3 (42-44). Built on these motifs is the active site, six antiparallel beta-sheets surrounded by three alpha-helices, which promotes aminoacylation by activating and transferring amino acid onto the acceptor stem of tRNA molecules (45,46). A comparison of amino acid sequences among seven cloned AspRSs first revealed that enzymes from E.coli, T.thermophilus and S.cerevisiae mitochondria have an insertion sequence of about 100 amino acids located between motifs 2 and 3. This insertion sequence forms an extra domain in the crystal structure of T.thermophilus AspRS (36). This domain seems to be a characteristic of prokaryotic AspRSs (47). The function of the extra domain is unknown, however, it has been suggested that the extra domain modulates function of the active site by stabilizing the complex with tRNA (36,47). The extra domain of T.thermophilus AspRS is similar to E.coli ThrRS in its N-terminal domain from which two alpha-helices contact tRNAThr in the ThrRS:tRNAThr complex (48). One helix, H7 in the extra domain, at a similar position as one of these two helices (36) implicates that the C-terminus of the helix H7 likely contacts the D arm and/or the acceptor stem of tRNAAsp.

###xml 64 78 64 78 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">T.thermophilus</italic>

###xml 1000 1003 1000 1003 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">Asp</sup>
###xml 1151 1154 1151 1154 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">Asp</sup>
###xml 1419 1422 1419 1422 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">Asp</sup>
###xml 64 78 <span type="species:ncbi:274">T.thermophilus</span>
Based on the amino acid sequence alignment and the structure of T.thermophilus AspRS (36), the amino acid substitution in the NSM3 allele, S388N, is mapped to the 13th position of the helix H7 (Fig. 4). This is the first reported mutation in the extra domain, indicating a possible biological function of this domain. An alignment among 21 sequenced AspRSs revealed that the amino acid residue at the third position of H7 is highly conserved. The S388N mutation resides on the same side of H7 as this conserved residue, presumably facing tRNA toward the active site (see figure 5C in ref. 36). This amino acid substitution results in four consecutive asparagine residues at the C-terminus of the helix H7. Amino acid asparagine with a longer side chain than serine might push the acceptor stem of mutant tRNAAsp closer to the active site of AspRS, restoring aminoacylation of the mutated tRNAAsp and resulting in the respiratory rescue. The examination of the aminoacylation efficiency of mutant tRNAAsp by the mutant AspRS would be required to support this explanation. Either a crystal structure or molecular modeling of the mutant AspRS:mutant tRNAAsp complex should reveal the proposed contacts. In addition, a systemic analysis of mutations in the helix H7 in the presence or absence of mutations in the acceptor stem of tRNA would bring better insights into the interactions between the helix H7 of AspRS and tRNAAsp.
