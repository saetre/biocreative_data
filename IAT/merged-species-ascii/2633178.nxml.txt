###xml 28 29 25 26 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">1</sub>
Aldosterone-induced TGF-beta1 Expression is Regulated by Mitogen-Activated Protein Kinases and Activator Protein-1 in Mesangial Cells
###end article-title 0
###begin p 1
###xml 54 55 51 52 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">1</sub>
###xml 124 125 118 119 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">1</sub>
###xml 366 367 357 358 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">1</sub>
###xml 411 412 399 400 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">1</sub>
###xml 521 522 506 507 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">1</sub>
###xml 729 730 711 712 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">1</sub>
###xml 762 763 741 742 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">1</sub>
###xml 1056 1057 1032 1033 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">1</sub>
###xml 1321 1322 1294 1295 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">1</sub>
###xml 1394 1395 1364 1365 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">1</sub>
###xml 382 385 <span type="species:ncbi:10116">rat</span>
###xml 461 464 <span type="species:ncbi:10116">rat</span>
Aldosterone has been shown to stimulate renal TGF-beta1 expression. However, the mechanisms for aldosterone-induced TGF-beta1 expression have not been clearly determined in mesangial cells. We examined the role of extracellular-signal regulated kinase 1 and 2 (ERK1/2), c-Jun N-terminal kinase (JNK) and activator protein-1 (AP-1) in the aldosterone-induced TGF-beta1 expression in rat mesangial cells. TGF-beta1 protein in the conditioned medium released from rat mesangial cells was measured by sandwich ELISA, TGF-beta1 mRNA expression was analyzed by Northern blotting, AP-1 DNA binding activity was measured by EMSA and the ERK1/2, JNK activity was analyzed by western blotting. Aldosterone significantly stimulated TGF-beta1 protein production and TGF-beta1 mRNA expression in mesangial cells in a dose-dependent manner. Aldosterone significantly increased AP-1 DNA binding activity in mesangial cells. Pre-treatment of cells with AP-1 inhibitor, curcumin, blocked aldosterone-induced AP-1 DNA binding activity as well as aldosterone-induced TGF-beta1 production. Aldosterone increased phosphorylation of ERK1/2 and JNK in mesangial cells. Pre-treatment of cells with ERK1/2 inhibitor, PD98059, or JNK inhibitor, SP600125 significantly inhibited aldosterone-induced ERK1/2 and JNK activity and subsequently TGF-beta1 production, respectively. We conclude that aldosterone-induced TGF-beta1 expression in mesangial cells is regulated by the ERK1/2, JNK and AP-1 intracellular signaling pathways.
###end p 1
###begin title 2
INTRODUCTION
###end title 2
###begin p 3
###xml 446 447 439 440 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="B1">1</xref>
###xml 449 450 442 443 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="B2">2</xref>
###xml 656 657 649 650 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="B1">1</xref>
In the renin-angiotensin-aldosterone systems, angiotensin II is well known to mediate progressive renal disease not only by elevating glomerular pressure, but also by non-hemodynamic effects on the kidney, such as proliferation of renal cells including mesangial cells, recruitment of mononuclear cells into the kidney by NF-kappaB-mediated transcription of chemokines, and stimulating renal TGF-beta, fibronectin and type I collagen expression (1, 2). Angiotensin converting enzyme inhibitors and angiotensin receptor blockers have shown efficacy in slowing the progression of diabetic and non-diabetic renal disease as well as in decreasing proteinuria (1).
###end p 3
###begin p 4
###xml 237 238 237 238 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="B3">3</xref>
###xml 435 436 435 436 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="B4">4</xref>
###xml 593 594 593 594 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="B5">5</xref>
###xml 720 721 720 721 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="B6">6</xref>
###xml 162 165 <span type="species:ncbi:10116">rat</span>
###xml 541 545 <span type="species:ncbi:10116">rats</span>
###xml 714 718 <span type="species:ncbi:10116">rats</span>
Recently, emerging and convincing evidences have shown the independent role of aldosterone in the progression of renal disease. In the remnant kidney model using rat, aldosterone induced proteinuria, hypertension and glomerulosclerosis (3). In addition, inhibition of aldosterone by spironolactone not only slowed the development of glomerulosclerosis, but also induced the regression of existing glomerulosclerosis in the same model (4). Aldosterone infusion developed malignant nephrosclerosis in saline-drinking stroke-prone hypertensive rats, independent of the effects of blood pressure (5) and induced severe inflammation and fibrosis by macrophage infiltration and cytokine up-regulation in saline-drinking rats (6).
###end p 4
###begin p 5
###xml 351 352 345 346 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="B7">7</xref>
###xml 449 450 443 444 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="B8">8</xref>
###xml 705 706 696 697 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="B9">9</xref>
###xml 863 865 854 856 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="B10">10</xref>
###xml 1061 1063 1049 1051 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="B11">11</xref>
###xml 129 132 <span type="species:ncbi:10116">rat</span>
###xml 699 703 <span type="species:ncbi:10116">rats</span>
###xml 857 861 <span type="species:ncbi:10116">rats</span>
###xml 955 959 <span type="species:ncbi:10116">rats</span>
The role of TGF-beta in the aldosterone-induced renal injury has been suggested in animal models of various renal diseases. In a rat model of chronic cyclosporine A (CsA) nephrotoxicity, spironolactone treatment reduced arteriolopathy and tubulointerstitial fibrosis by prevention of CsA-induced up-regulation of TGF-beta, collagen I and fibronectin (7), and also prevented the progression of renal injury in preexisting chronic CsA nephrotoxicity (8). Aldosterone also played a role in diabetic renal injury, which was demonstrated by increasing glomerular and tubulointerstitial macrophage infiltration, plasminogen activator inhibitor-1 and TGF-beta expression in streptozotocin-induced diabetic rats (9), and by increasing renal tissue and urinary levels of monocyte chemoattractant protein-1 as well as renal macrophage infiltration in type 2 diabetic rats (10). Juknevicius and colleagues demonstrated that aldosterone infusion for 3 days in normal rats increased urinary TGF-beta excretion without changes in blood pressure or evidence of kidney damage (11).
###end p 5
###begin p 6
###xml 429 431 429 431 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="B12">12</xref>
###xml 491 493 491 493 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="B13">13</xref>
###xml 104 112 <span type="species:ncbi:9606">patients</span>
###xml 197 205 <span type="species:ncbi:9606">patients</span>
The renal protective effects of inhibiting aldosterone were also demonstrated in chronic kidney disease patients. In a prospective randomized open-label study, spironolactone treatment for 1 yr in patients with chronic kidney disease already treated with angiotensin-converting enzyme inhibitors and/or angiotensin type 1 receptor antagonists significantly decreased proteinuria without any change in glomerular filtration rate (12). Similar results were observed in another clinical study (13).
###end p 6
###begin p 7
###xml 200 202 197 199 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="B10">10</xref>
###xml 759 760 746 747 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">1</sub>
###xml 775 778 <span type="species:ncbi:10116">rat</span>
Putting these results from various experimental studies together, it is positive that aldosterone stimulates renal TGF-beta expression in the course of progressive renal injury. Recently, Han et al. (10) described the aldosterone stimulated NF-kappaB DNA binding activity and subsequently monocyte chemoattractant protein-1 expression in mesangial cells. However, it has not been clearly determined if aldosterone-induced TGF-beta expression was mediated through the extracellular-signal regulated-kinase 1 and 2 (ERK1/2), c-Jun N-terminal kinase (JNK) and activator protein-1 (AP-1) pathways in mesangial cells. In this study, we examined the role of ERK1/2, JNK and activator protein-1 in the intracellular signaling pathways of aldosterone-induced TGF-beta1 expression in rat mesangial cells.
###end p 7

###begin title 11
Cell culture
###end title 11
###begin p 12
###xml 97 99 97 99 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="B14">14</xref>

Rat mesangial cells were isolated from glomeruli of normal renal cortex as described previously (14). Mesangial cells were grown in RPMI1640 medium supplemented with 10% heat-inactivated FBS, 10 mM HEPES, and an antibiotic/antifungal solution.

###begin title 13
###xml 8 9 5 6 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">1</sub>
TGF-beta1 ELISA
###end title 13
###begin p 14
###xml 396 397 393 394 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">1</sub>
###xml 492 493 486 487 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">1</sub>
###xml 543 544 534 535 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">1</sub>
###xml 592 593 576 577 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">1</sub>
###xml 808 811 792 795 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">450</sub>
After a 24 hr starvation, confluent cells were stimulated with aldosterone (0.2 ng/mL, 1 ng/mL, 5 ng/mL) for 24 hr. The conditioned medium was collected, centrifuged at 10,000 xg (4℃) for 3 min and stored at -70℃ until assayed. For the time course, confluent cells incubated in the serum free medium were incubated with or without aldosterone (5 ng/mL) for the indicated time points. For TGF-beta1 ELISA, the conditioned medium was incubated with 1N HCl for 60 min to activate latent TGF-beta1, and then neutralized with 1N NaOH. Anti-TGF-beta1 Ab (1 microg/mL) and biotinylated Anti-TGF-beta1 Ab (300 ng/mL) were used. The TMB microwell peroxidase substrate system (Kirkegaard & Perry Laboratories, Gaithersburg, MD, U.S.A.) was used as an enzyme substrate and the reaction was followed by measurement of OD450.
###end p 14
###begin title 15
###xml 29 30 26 27 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">1</sub>
Isolation of RNA and TGF-beta1 Northern blot analysis
###end title 15
###begin p 16
###xml 548 549 545 546 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">1</sub>
###xml 643 645 640 642 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">32</sup>
###xml 805 806 802 803 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">6</sup>
###xml 1130 1131 1124 1125 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">1</sub>
###xml 536 539 <span type="species:ncbi:10116">rat</span>
###xml 1118 1121 <span type="species:ncbi:10116">rat</span>
Serum-starved confluent cells were stimulated with aldosterone (5 ng/mL) for the indicated time points. RNA was isolated by one-step guanidium-thiocyanatephenol-chloroform extraction. RNA quantification was determined by absorbance at 260 nm. Total RNA was fractionated by electrophoresis on a 1% agarose-formaldehyde gel, blotted by capillary transfer on a nylon membrane (Gene Screen; NEN, Boston, MA, U.S.A.), and cross-linked by UV irradiation (UV Strata-linker 1800; Stratagene, La Jolla, CA, U.S.A.). The membrane was probed with rat TGF-beta1 cDNA labeled by random priming using a commercial kit (Amersham, Buckinghamshire, U.K.) and [32P]dCTP. The blot was prehybridized at 42℃ for 2 hr in 50% formamide, 0.1% SDS, 2xDenhardt's solution, 5xstandard SSPE, and 0.1 mg/mL salmon sperm DNA. Probe (106 cpm/mL) was added to the prehybridization solution, and the blot was hybridized overnight at 42℃. The blot was then washed twice for 10 min each at 55℃ in 2xstandard saline citrate and 0.1% SDS, and autoradiography was performed with Fuji radiograhpy film (Tokyo, Japan) and an intensifying screen at -70℃. The rat TGF-beta1 probe was then removed by boiling, and the same blot was rehybridized to a cDNA probe encoding for the GAPDH. The intensity of the blots was quantified by densitometric analysis.
###end p 16
###begin title 17
Electrophoretic mobility shift assay (EMSA)
###end title 17
###begin p 18
###xml 996 998 992 994 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">32</sup>
###xml 1468 1470 1452 1454 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">32</sup>
Confluent cells were starved for 24 hr and then were stimulated with aldosterone (5 ng/mL) for 15 min. Nuclear extracts were prepared from the treated cells that were pelleted (-500 xg, 10 min, 4℃) and resuspended in hypotonic buffer (10 mM HEPES, pH 7.9, 10 mM KCl, 0.1 mM EDTA, 0.1 mM EGTA, 1 mM DTT, 0.5 mM PMSF, complete protease inhibit mixture tablets) and incubated for 10 min on ice. Cells were lyzed by the addition of Nonidet P-40 (final concentration of 0.1%). The nuclear pellet was resuspended in extraction buffer (20 mM HEPES, pH 7.9, 0.4 mM KCl, 1 mM EDTA, 1 mM EGTA, 1 mM DTT, 1 mM PMSF, complete protease inhibit mixture tablets) and incubated for 10 min on ice, and the tubes were centrifuged at 14,000 xg (4℃) for 10 min. The supernatants were stored at -70℃ until used. The protein content in nuclear extracts was measured by the Bradford method (Bio-Rad, Hercules, CA, U.S.A.). The oligonucleotide containing the AP-1 consensus binding sequence was radiolabeled with [gamma-32P]ATP by T4 polynucleotide kinase (Roche, Mannheim, Germany) for 30 min at room temperature. Unincorporated label was removed with a Chroma spin STE-10 column (Clontech Laboratories, Palo Alto, CA, U.S.A.). The binding reaction was performed for 30 min at room temperature in a total volume of 20 microL containing binding buffer (50 mM Tris, pH 7.5, 250 mM NaCl, 2.5 mM EDTA, 20% glycerol, 2.5 mM DTT, and 5 mM MgCl2), 5 microg of nuclear protein extracts, 1 microL of 32P-labeled oligonucleotide, and 50 microg/mL poly (dI-dC) (Roche). The DNA-protein complexes were fractionated by electrophoresis over 6% polyacrylamide gels in 1x Tris-boric acid-EDTA buffer. The gels were dried and exposed to radiograhy film for autoradiography.
###end p 18
###begin title 19
Western blot analysis
###end title 19
###begin p 20
Confluent cells were starved for 24 hr and then were stimulated with aldosterone (5 ng/mL) for 10 min. The treated cells were lyzed for 10 min on ice in lysis buffer (50 mM Tris, pH 7.5, 40 mM NaCl, 1% Triton X-100, 2 mM EDTA, 1 microg/mL leupeptin, 2 mM DTT, and 1 mM PMSF). Lysates were cleared by centrifugation at 14,000 xg (4℃) for 10 min. Total protein was quantified by the Bradford method. Equal amounts of lysates were fractionated by 10% SDS-PAGE and electrotransferred to Bio-Blot nitrocellulose membranes (Bio-Rad). The membranes were blocked with TBS (pH 7.6)/5% nonfat dry milk/0.05% Tween 20, and were blotted with the indicated antibodies at 4℃ overnight. The membranes were incubated with 1/1,000 diluted HRP-conjugated secondary antibody (Amersham) at room temperature for 1 hr and visualized by an ECL kit (Amersham). The membranes were stripped in solution (62.5 mM Tris-HCl, pH 7.5, 20 mM DTT, and 1% SDS) for 5 min at 50℃ with agitation and reprobed with anti-total JNK or anti-total ERK1/2 antibody.
###end p 20

###begin title 24

Aldosterone Stimulates TGF-beta1 Production
###end title 24
###begin p 25
###xml 8 9 5 6 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">1</sub>
###xml 130 131 124 125 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">1</sub>

###xml 287 294 281 288 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="fig" rid="F1">Fig. 1A</xref>
###xml 322 323 313 314 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">1</sub>

###xml 752 753 734 735 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">1</sub>
###xml 812 813 794 795 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">p</italic>
###xml 820 827 802 809 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="fig" rid="F1">Fig. 1B</xref>
###xml 918 919 897 898 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">1</sub>
###xml 991 993 970 972 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">-9</sup>
###xml 1119 1120 1095 1096 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">1</sub>
###xml 1138 1139 1114 1115 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">p</italic>
###xml 1146 1153 1122 1129 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="fig" rid="F1">Fig. 1C</xref>
###xml 54 57 <span type="species:ncbi:10116">rat</span>
###xml 163 166 <span type="species:ncbi:10116">rat</span>
TGF-beta1 protein was measured in the supernatants of rat mesangial cells stimulated with aldosterone (5 ng/mL) by ELISA. TGF-beta1 was constitutively produced by rat mesangial cells and was stimulated by aldosterone, which was statistically significant at 24 hr and 48 hr (n=5, p<0.05; Fig. 1A). We then measured TGF-beta1 protein in the supernatants of cells incubated with various concentration of aldosterone with or without angiotensin II (10-7 M) for 24 hr. Aldosterone stimulated TGF-beta1 production in a dose-dependent manner. Angiotensin II also stimulated TGF-beta1 production as previously described in the same cells (15). Furthermore, a combination treatment of the cells with aldosterone and angiotensin II additively stimulated TGF-beta1 production significantly more than single treatment (n=5, p<0.05; Fig. 1B). To determine if the mineralocorticoid receptor mediates the aldosterone-induced TGF-beta1 production, cells were pre-incubated with or without spironolactone (10-9 M) for 60 min and then stimulated with aldosterone (5 ng/mL) for 24 hr. Spironolactone abolished aldosterone-induced TGF-beta1 production (n=5, p<0.05, Fig. 1C).

###begin p 26
###xml 29 30 26 27 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">1</sub>
###xml 195 196 189 190 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">1</sub>
###xml 312 314 306 308 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">-9</sup>
###xml 324 325 318 319 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">p</italic>
###xml 332 339 326 333 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="fig" rid="F1">Fig. 1D</xref>
We also examined the TGF-beta1 protein expression in the lysates of cells, which were stimulated with aldosterone for 24 hr by western blot analysis. Aldosterone significantly stimulated TGF-beta1 protein expression in a dose-dependent manner, which was blocked by pre-incubation of cells with spironolactone (10-9 M) (n=5, p<0.01, Fig. 1D).
###end p 26
###begin title 27
###xml 31 32 28 29 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">1</sub>
Aldosterone stimulates TGF-beta1 mRNA expression
###end title 27
###begin p 28
###xml 20 21 17 18 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">1</sub>
###xml 126 128 123 125 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">-7</sup>
###xml 188 189 182 183 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">1</sub>
###xml 337 338 328 329 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">1</sub>
###xml 402 403 393 394 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">p</italic>
###xml 410 417 401 408 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="fig" rid="F2">Fig. 2A</xref>
###xml 473 475 464 466 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">-9</sup>
###xml 576 577 564 565 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">1</sub>
###xml 614 615 602 603 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">p</italic>
###xml 622 629 610 617 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="fig" rid="F2">Fig. 2B</xref>
We measured TGF-beta1 mRNA in the cells incubated with various concentration of aldosterone with or without angiotensin II (10-7 M) for 16 hr. Aldosterone significantly stimulated TGF-beta1 mRNA expression in a dose-dependent manner. A combination treatment of the cells with aldosterone and angiotensin II additively stimulated TGF-beta1 mRNA expression significantly more than single treatment (n=5, p<0.05, Fig. 2A). When cells were pre-incubated with spironolactone (10-9 M) for 60 min and then stimulated with aldosterone (5 ng/mL) for 16 hr, aldosterone-induced TGF-beta1 mRNA expression was abolished (n=5, p<0.05, Fig. 2B).
###end p 28
###begin title 29
###xml 60 61 57 58 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">1</sub>
Role for AP-1 activation in the aldosterone-induced TGF-beta1 expression
###end title 29
###begin p 30
###xml 41 42 38 39 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">1</sub>
###xml 76 78 73 75 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="B16">16</xref>
###xml 147 148 141 142 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">1</sub>
###xml 313 314 304 305 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">1</sub>
###xml 595 596 586 587 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">p</italic>
###xml 603 610 594 601 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="fig" rid="F3">Fig. 3A</xref>
###xml 726 733 717 724 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="fig" rid="F3">Fig. 3B</xref>
###xml 1441 1442 1428 1429 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">p</italic>
###xml 1449 1456 1436 1443 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="fig" rid="F3">Fig. 3B</xref>
###xml 1558 1559 1542 1543 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">1</sub>
###xml 1710 1711 1687 1688 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">1</sub>
###xml 1822 1823 1796 1797 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">1</sub>
###xml 1841 1842 1815 1816 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">p</italic>
###xml 1849 1856 1823 1830 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="fig" rid="F3">Fig. 3C</xref>
###xml 29 32 <span type="species:ncbi:10116">rat</span>
###xml 386 389 <span type="species:ncbi:10116">rat</span>
The 5'-flanking sequences of rat TGF-beta1 gene contains AP-1 binding site (16), suggesting a potential role for AP-1 in the regulation of TGF-beta1 expression. However, it has not been clearly determined whether aldosterone stimulates AP-1 activity and whether aldosterone-induced AP-1 activity triggers TGF-beta1 expression in mesangial cells. Therefore, we examined AP-1 activity in rat mesangial cells stimulated with aldosterone by EMSA using AP-1 consensus oligonucleotides. AP-1 DNA binding activity was significantly increased by aldosterone (5 ng/mL), which was maximal at 15 min (n=5, p<0.05, Fig. 3A). Supershift analysis indicated that the AP-1 complexes induced by aldosterone contained c-Jun and c-Fos proteins (Fig. 3B). The specificity of the AP-1 binding activity was tested using both an unlabelled consensus AP-1 oligonucleotiedes (cold probe) and a mutant AP-1 oligonucleotides, which has two base changes within the AP-1 binding motif (CA to TG), at the same molar quantity (50-fold excess) for competition. The specific band induced by aldosterone was deleted by preincubation of nuclear extracts with excessive amounts of unlabelled AP-1 oligonucleotides, but not by preincubation of nuclear extracts with mutant AP-1 oligonucleotides (data not shown). When the cells were preincubated with c-Jun/AP-1 inhibitor, curcumin (12.5 microM), for 15 min, the aldosterone-induced AP-1 activity was completely abolished (n=3, p<0.05, Fig. 3B). To examine whether aldosterone-induced activation of AP-1 is responsible for the increased TGF-beta1 production, cells were pre-incubated with curcumin (12.5 microM) for 15 min and then were stimulated with aldosterone (5 ng/mL) for 24 hr and TGF-beta1 was measured in the cell supernatants by ELISA. Curcumin completely inhibited the aldosterone-induced TGF-beta1 production (n=5, p<0.05, Fig. 3C).
###end p 30
###begin title 31
###xml 56 57 53 54 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">1</sub>
Role for JNK pathway in the aldosterone-induced TGF-beta1 expression
###end title 31
###begin p 32
###xml 113 115 113 115 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="B17">17</xref>
###xml 375 376 375 376 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">p</italic>
###xml 383 390 383 390 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="fig" rid="F4">Fig. 4A</xref>
###xml 450 452 450 452 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">-9</sup>
###xml 561 562 561 562 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">p</italic>
###xml 569 576 569 576 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="fig" rid="F4">Fig. 4B</xref>
###xml 731 732 724 725 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">1</sub>
###xml 786 787 779 780 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">p</italic>
###xml 794 801 787 794 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="fig" rid="F4">Fig. 4C</xref>
The activation of JNK has been shown to be linked to AP-1 activity particularly to the phosphorylation of c-Jun (17). We examined the phosphorylation of JNK in the cells stimulated with aldosterone by western blot analysis. Aldosterone (5 ng/mL) phosphorylated both 46 and 54 kDa JNKs in mesangial cells from 2 to 10 min and the peak phosphorylation was seen at 10 min (n=5, p<0.05, Fig. 4A). When the cells were pre-incubated with spironolactone (10-9 M) and then stimulated with aldosterone, aldosterone-induced JNK activity was significantly inhibited (n=5, p<0.05, Fig. 4B). When the cells were pre-incubated with JNK inhibitor, SP600125 (20 microM), and then stimulated with aldosterone (5 ng/mL), aldosterone-induced TGF-beta1 protein production was significantly inhibited (n=5, p<0.05, Fig. 4C).
###end p 32
###begin title 33
###xml 59 60 56 57 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">1</sub>
Role for ERK1/2 pathway in the aldosterone-induced TGF-beta1 expression
###end title 33
###begin p 34
###xml 262 263 262 263 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">p</italic>
###xml 270 277 270 277 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="fig" rid="F5">Fig. 5A</xref>
###xml 442 443 442 443 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">p</italic>
###xml 450 457 450 457 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="fig" rid="F5">Fig. 5B</xref>
###xml 627 628 620 621 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">1</sub>
###xml 668 669 661 662 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">p</italic>
###xml 676 683 669 676 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="fig" rid="F5">Fig. 5C</xref>
We examined the phosphorylation of ERK1/2 in the cells stimulated with aldosterone by western blot analysis. Aldosterone (5 ng/mL) phosphorylated both 42 and 44 kDa ERK1/2 in mesangial cells from 2 to 60 min and the peak phosphorylation was seen at 10 min (n=3, p<0.05, Fig. 5A). When the cells were pre-incubated with spironolactone and then stimulated with aldosterone, aldosterone-induced ERK1/2 activity was significantly decreased (n=3, p<0.05, Fig. 5B). In addition, when the cells were pre-incubated with ERK1/2 inhibitor, PD98059 (50 microM), and then stimulated with aldosterone (5 ng/mL), aldosterone-induced TGF-beta1 protein production was inhibited (n=5, p<0.05, Fig. 5C).
###end p 34
###begin title 35
DISCUSSION
###end title 35
###begin p 36
###xml 74 75 71 72 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">1</sub>
###xml 90 93 <span type="species:ncbi:10116">rat</span>
###xml 375 380 <span type="species:ncbi:9606">human</span>
In the present study, we demonstrated that aldosterone stimulated TGF-beta1 expression in rat mesangial cells, partly through the induction of ERK1/2 and JNK phosphorylation and AP-1 DNA binding activity. These results suggested a mechanism involved in the anti-fibrotic effect of spironolactone which was demonstrated in the animal models of various renal injury as well as human chronic kidney diseases.
###end p 36
###begin p 37
###xml 104 105 101 102 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">1</sub>
###xml 207 208 201 202 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">1</sub>
###xml 316 317 307 308 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">1</sub>
###xml 390 391 378 379 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">1</sub>
###xml 465 466 450 451 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">1</sub>
###xml 509 511 494 496 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="B16">16</xref>
###xml 545 547 530 532 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="B18">18</xref>
###xml 688 689 670 671 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">1</sub>
###xml 895 896 874 875 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">1</sub>
###xml 803 806 <span type="species:ncbi:10116">rat</span>
In our study, aldosterone induced transcription factor, AP-1 DNA binding activity, which led to TGF-beta1 up-regulation in mesangial cells. Recently, the role of AP-1 activation in the regulation of TGF-beta1 expression in mesangial cells has been described in a few studies. A positive regulatory region in TGF-beta1 5'-flanking sequence, which is responsible for the induction of TGF-beta1, has an AP-1 binding site. Oxidized LDL has been shown to induce TGF-beta1 expression through the AP-1 binding site (16). In a study done by Ahn et al. (18), transfection of mesangial cells with AP-1 decoy oligodeoxynucleotides strongly inhibited high glucose- and angiotensin II-induced TGF-beta1 expression. In addition, administration of AP-1 decoy oligodeoxynucleotides into streptozotocin-induced diabetic rat kidney using the hemagglutinating virus of Japan-liposome method also abolished TGF-beta1 and plasminogen activator inhibitor-1 expression.
###end p 37
###begin p 38
###xml 145 147 145 147 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="B19">19</xref>
###xml 266 268 266 268 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="B19">19</xref>
###xml 415 417 415 417 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="B20">20</xref>
###xml 659 660 656 657 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">1</sub>
###xml 789 790 783 784 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">1</sub>
c-Jun contains a docking site for the JNK, which phosphorylate serines 63 and 73, thereby enhancing its transcriptional potential and stability (19). Activation of JNK results in greatly enhanced c-Jun transcriptional activity and induction of the AP-1 target gene (19). Most cells express two JNK isoforms, 45 kDa and 54 kDa in size and termed JNK1 and JNK2, which are highly similar in their modes of regulation (20). In our study, aldosterone rapidly induced phosphorylation of JNK and spironolactone abolished aldosterone-induced phosphorylation of JNK. In addition, both JNK inhibitor, SP600125, and spironolactone suppressed aldosterone-induced TGF-beta1 production, suggesting that blocking JNK activation contributed to the inhibition of c-jun/AP-1 activation, and finally TGF-beta1 expression.
###end p 38
###begin p 39
###xml 116 117 113 114 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">1</sub>
###xml 360 362 357 359 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="B21">21</xref>
###xml 404 406 401 403 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="B22">22</xref>
###xml 513 515 510 512 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="B23">23</xref>
###xml 587 588 581 582 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">1</sub>
###xml 828 829 819 820 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">1</sub>
###xml 1001 1003 992 994 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="B24">24</xref>
###xml 1075 1076 1063 1064 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">1</sub>
###xml 1184 1185 1169 1170 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">1</sub>
###xml 1271 1273 1256 1258 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="B25">25</xref>
###xml 1479 1480 1461 1462 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">1</sub>
###xml 1513 1515 1495 1497 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="B26">26</xref>
###xml 1629 1630 1608 1609 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">1</sub>
###xml 718 722 <span type="species:ncbi:10116">rats</span>
###xml 1329 1332 <span type="species:ncbi:10116">rat</span>
In our study, aldosterone rapidly stimulated ERK1/2 phosphorylation which led to subsequent increase in the TGF-beta1 production. Aldosterone-induced ERK1/2 activation has been demonstrated in different in vitro and in vivo studies. Aldosterone-induced ERK1/2 activation has been shown to stimulate collagen gene expression and synthesis in renal fibroblasts (21) to induce mesangial cell proliferation (22), and to increase mytogenic response synergistically with angiotensin II in vascular smooth muscle cells (23). However, a direct relationship between ERK1/2 activation and TGF-beta1 up-regulation in mesangial cells has not been clearly determined. In an animal study using aldosterone/salt-induced hypertensive rats, chronic administration of spironolactone inhibited ERK1/2 activity and suppressed expression of TGF-beta1, type I and III collagen, and monocyte chemoattractant protein-1 mRNA, which resulted in the amelioration of perivascular fibrosis and coronary microvascular hyperplasia (24). However, a direct relationship between ERK1/2 activation and TGF-beta1 up-regulation was not confirmed in that study. Recently, the role for ERK1/2 in the stimulation of TGF-beta1 expression was demonstrated in two different cells. In a study done by Huang et al. (25), renin induced rapid activation of cellular ERK1/2 in rat mesangial cells and an ERK kinase inhibitor significantly reduced the renin-induced ERK1/2 phosphorylation and the subsequent increase in TGF-beta1 mRNA expression. Otsuka et al. (26) has reported that liposomes composed of phosphatidylserine activated ERK which led to the expression of TGF-beta1 in macrophages.
###end p 39
###begin p 40
###xml 77 78 74 75 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">1</sub>
###xml 93 96 <span type="species:ncbi:10116">rat</span>
In summary, our data provide an evidence that aldosterone stimulates TGF-beta1 expression in rat mesangial cells partly by enhancing ERK1/2 and JNK activities and subsequent AP-1 activity. This study also provides an understanding of a part of intracellular mechanisms for proliferative and fibrotic effect of aldosterone in the inflammatory renal diseases.
###end p 40
