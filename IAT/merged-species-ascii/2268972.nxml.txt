Pharmacological separation of early afterdepolarizations from arrhythmogenic substrate in DeltaKPQ Scn5a murine hearts modelling human long QT 3 syndrome

###begin title 3
Aim
###end title 3
###begin p 4
###xml 179 184 <span type="species:ncbi:10090">mouse</span>
###xml 201 206 <span type="species:ncbi:9606">human</span>
To perform an empirical, pharmacological, separation of early afterdepolarizations (EADs) and transmural gradients of repolarization in arrhythmogenesis in a genetically modified mouse heart modelling human long QT syndrome (LQT) 3.
###end p 4
###begin title 5
Methods
###end title 5
###begin p 6
###xml 146 151 146 151 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">Scn5a</italic>
###xml 192 193 187 188 <sc xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">m</sc>
Left ventricular endocardial and epicardial monophasic action potentials and arrhythmogenic tendency were compared in isolated wild type (WT) and Scn5a+/Delta hearts perfused with 0.1 and 1 mum propranolol and paced from the right ventricular epicardium.
###end p 6
###begin title 7
Results
###end title 7
###begin p 8
###xml 4 25 4 25 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">spontaneously beating</italic>
###xml 38 43 38 43 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">Scn5a</italic>
###xml 123 124 119 120 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">n</italic>
###xml 163 164 159 160 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">n</italic>
###xml 198 199 193 194 <sc xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">m</sc>
###xml 223 226 218 221 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">all</italic>
###xml 416 421 411 416 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">Scn5a</italic>
###xml 465 466 455 456 <sc xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">m</sc>
###xml 612 614 598 600 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">90</sub>
###xml 635 636 620 621 <sc xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">m</sc>
###xml 691 693 676 678 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">90</sub>
###xml 750 752 727 729 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">90</sub>
###xml 794 795 767 768 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">n</italic>
###xml 835 836 807 808 <sc xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">m</sc>
###xml 873 875 845 847 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">90</sub>
###xml 924 926 894 896 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">90</sub>
###xml 984 986 946 948 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">90</sub>
All spontaneously beating bradycardic Scn5a+/Delta hearts displayed EADs, triggered beats and ventricular tachycardia (VT; n = 7), events never seen in WT hearts (n = 5). Perfusion with 0.1 and 1 mum propranolol suppressed all EADs, triggered beats and episodes of VT. In contrast, triggering of VT persisted following programmed electrical stimulation in 6 of 12 (50%), one of eight (12.5%), but six of eight (75%) Scn5a+/Delta hearts perfused with 0, 0.1 and 1 mum propranolol respectively in parallel with corresponding alterations in repolarization gradients, reflected in action potential duration (DeltaAPD90) values. Thus 0.1 mum propranolol reduced epicardial but not endocardial APD90 from 54.7 +/- 1.6 to 44.0 +/- 2.0 ms, restoring DeltaAPD90 from -3.8 +/- 1.6 to 3.5 +/- 2.5 ms (all n = 5), close to WT values. However, 1 mum propranolol increased epicardial APD90 to 72.5 +/- 1.2 ms and decreased endocardial APD90 from 50.9 +/- 1.0 to 24.5 +/- 0.3 ms, increasing DeltaAPD90 to -48.0 +/- 1.2 ms.
###end p 8
###begin title 9
Conclusion
###end title 9
###begin p 10
###xml 249 255 <span type="species:ncbi:10090">murine</span>
###xml 273 278 <span type="species:ncbi:9606">human</span>
These findings empirically implicate EADs in potentially initiating spontaneous arrhythmogenic phenomena and transmural repolarization gradients in the re-entrant substrate that would sustain such activity when provoked by extrasystolic activity in murine hearts modelling human LQT3 syndrome.
###end p 10
###begin p 11
###xml 199 205 199 205 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">et al.</italic>
###xml 192 210 192 210 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="b19">Kannel <italic>et al.</italic> 1987</xref>
###xml 220 226 220 226 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">et al.</italic>
###xml 212 231 212 231 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="b51">Willich <italic>et al.</italic> 1987</xref>
###xml 277 287 277 287 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="b32">NICE, 2000</xref>
###xml 618 623 618 623 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">Scn5a</italic>
###xml 642 643 642 643 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">+</sup>
###xml 723 724 723 724 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">+</sup>
###xml 734 735 734 735 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">I</italic>
###xml 735 737 735 737 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">Na</sub>
###xml 745 751 745 751 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">et al.</italic>
###xml 740 757 740 757 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="b48">Wang <italic>et al.</italic> 1995a</xref>
###xml 757 758 757 758 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="b49">b</xref>
Sudden cardiac death (SCD) attributable to ventricular arrhythmogenesis is one of the major causes of mortality in the developed world, accounting for over 300 000 deaths per year in the USA (Kannel et al. 1987, Willich et al. 1987) and up to 70 000 deaths per year in the UK (NICE, 2000). Cardiac arrhythmias are clinically associated with a condition known as long QT syndrome, which is characterized by prolonged ventricular repolarization and a tendency to ventricular tachycardia, particularly torsades de pointes (TdP), leading to syncope and SCD. The LQT3 subtype results from gain of function mutations in the Scn5a gene, affecting Na+ channel inactivation and leading to an arrhythmogenic persistent late inward Na+ current (INa) (Wang et al. 1995ab).
###end p 11
###begin p 12
###xml 458 464 458 464 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">et al.</italic>
###xml 450 469 450 469 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="b12">Eckardt <italic>et al.</italic> 1998</xref>
###xml 479 485 479 485 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">et al.</italic>
###xml 471 491 471 491 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="b21">Killeen <italic>et al.</italic> 2007a</xref>
###xml 826 848 826 848 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="b29">Milberg &amp; Eckardt 2005</xref>
###xml 873 879 873 879 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">et al.</italic>
###xml 865 885 865 885 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="b21">Killeen <italic>et al.</italic> 2007a</xref>
###xml 1008 1010 1008 1010 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">90</sub>
###xml 1020 1022 1016 1018 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">90</sub>
The induction of ventricular arrhythmias has been attributed to the following, not necessarily exclusive electrophysiological events: (1) impaired cardiac repolarization leads to small, premature depolarizations (early afterdepolarizations, EADs), interrupting the smooth repolarization phase of the action potential (AP). If EADs reach a sufficient amplitude they may initiate a premature AP (triggered beat) which may in turn precipitate into TdP (Eckardt et al. 1998, Killeen et al. 2007a). (2) Alterations in the repolarization gradients have been attributed to heterogeneous ion channel expression through the thickness of the ventricular wall. Thus, selective action potential duration (APD) prolongation or reduction within localized regions of the ventricle can alter the dispersion of repolarization; both increases (Milberg & Eckardt 2005) and decreases (Killeen et al. 2007a) in such ventricular transmural repolarization gradients, often quantified as the epicardial-endocardial difference in APD90 (DeltaAPD90), have been associated with arrhythmogenesis at the whole heart level.
###end p 12
###begin p 13
###xml 469 475 469 475 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">et al.</italic>
###xml 460 480 460 480 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="b17">Huelsing <italic>et al.</italic> 2000</xref>
Previous isolated tissue and single cell experiments have indicated that most cardiac cell types are capable of generating EADs. However, arrhythmogenesis at the whole heart level may involve a differential expression of contrasting electrophysiological characteristics in a population of intercellularly coupled cells not detectable in single cell studies. Such intercellular coupling has been previously shown to either facilitate or suppress EAD induction (Huelsing et al. 2000). Furthermore, single cell studies and studies confined to isolated tissue exclude examination of important myocardial properties essential for the study of arrhythmogenesis including re-entry mechanisms and transmural gradients of repolarization. Consequently, they have failed to clarify the relationship between EADs, transmural gradients of repolarization and arrhythmogenesis at the whole heart level. The use of an intact, working myocardium for the study of arrhythmogenesis permits the measurement of multicellular parameters involving ventricular transmural gradients of repolarization and EADs, triggered beats and arrhythmias that cannot be assessed in single cell or isolated tissue preparations. The use of an intact, beating heart model thus includes all myocardial cell types and maintains intercellular coupling and thus provides more physiologically relevant information regarding the induction and propagation of arrhythmias.
###end p 13
###begin p 14
###xml 163 165 159 161 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">90</sub>
###xml 495 501 491 497 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">et al.</italic>
###xml 487 507 483 503 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="b22">Killeen <italic>et al.</italic> 2007b</xref>
###xml 677 682 669 674 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">Scn5a</italic>
###xml 684 689 676 681 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">Scn5a</italic>
###xml 819 820 807 808 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">+</sup>
###xml 953 971 938 956 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="b36">Rauls &amp; Baker 1979</xref>
###xml 973 994 958 979 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="b28">Matthews &amp; Baker 1982</xref>
###xml 1042 1043 1026 1027 <sc xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">m</sc>
###xml 1150 1156 1134 1140 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">et al.</italic>
###xml 1145 1161 1129 1145 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="b11">Duff <italic>et al.</italic> 1983</xref>
###xml 1256 1283 1240 1267 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="b41">Shimizu &amp; Antzelevitch 2000</xref>
###xml 197 203 <span type="species:ncbi:10090">murine</span>
###xml 227 232 <span type="species:ncbi:9606">human</span>
###xml 432 438 <span type="species:ncbi:10090">murine</span>
We explored for empirical means of resolving the causal relationship between EADs and abnormal transmural gradients of repolarization reflected in altered DeltaAPD90 values in genetically modified murine whole hearts modelling human long QT syndrome type 3 for the first time. This study was directly prompted by a recent report describing a similarly empirical pharmacological separation of EADs from arrhythmogenic substrate in a murine model of hypokalaemia-induced arrhythmogenesis (Killeen et al. 2007b). Accordingly, it made endocardial and epicardial monophasic action potential (MAP) recordings from the left ventricle of isolated, intact Langendorff-perfused DeltaKPQ Scn5a (Scn5a+/Delta) and wild type (WT) hearts, exposed to a range of propranolol concentrations. Propranolol is well known to exert potent Na+ channel blockade occurring independently of its beta-adrenoceptor blocking actions, thought important in its antiarrhythmic action (Rauls & Baker 1979, Matthews & Baker 1982). Propranolol concentrations used (0.1 and 1 mum) were based on values known to cause electrophysiological effects in clinical dose-response studies (Duff et al. 1983). Indeed, they are close to previous values used in earlier studies of LQTS in animal models (Shimizu & Antzelevitch 2000).
###end p 14
###begin p 15
###xml 22 27 22 27 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">Scn5a</italic>
###xml 168 181 164 177 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">spontaneously</italic>
###xml 190 195 186 191 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">Scn5a</italic>
###xml 232 233 223 224 <sc xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">m</sc>
###xml 458 463 449 454 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">Scn5a</italic>
###xml 573 574 559 560 <sc xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">m</sc>
###xml 626 628 612 614 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">90</sub>
###xml 648 650 630 632 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">90</sub>
###xml 685 690 667 672 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">Scn5a</italic>
###xml 749 750 726 727 <sc xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">m</sc>
###xml 791 796 768 773 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">Scn5a</italic>
###xml 914 916 887 889 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">90</sub>
###xml 944 946 917 919 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">90</sub>
###xml 982 984 951 953 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">90</sub>
Spontaneously beating Scn5a+/Delta hearts following AV node ablation showed EADs and episodes of ventricular tachycardia (VT) never observed in WT hearts. Perfusion of spontaneously beating Scn5a+/Delta hearts with both 0.1 and 1 mum propranolol suppressed both EADs and episodes of VT in all hearts, and had no effect in WT hearts. In contrast, programmed electrical stimulation (PES) protocols applying premature stimuli successfully provoked VT in 50% of Scn5a+/Delta hearts at baseline, events never recorded from WT hearts. The lower propranolol concentrations (0.1 mum) both abolished this VT and selectively reduced APD90, restoring DeltaAPD90 close to WT control values in all Scn5a+/Delta hearts. In contrast, at higher concentrations (1 mum), PES successfully induced VT in 75% of Scn5a+/Delta hearts. This proarrhythmic effect was specifically associated with a combination of a prolonged epicardial APD90 and reduced endocardial APD90, resulting in an increased DeltaAPD90 and exacerbated arrhythmogenic substrate.
###end p 15
###begin p 16
###xml 346 350 346 350 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">both</italic>
###xml 553 558 553 558 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">Scn5a</italic>
###xml 694 700 694 700 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">et al.</italic>
###xml 686 706 686 706 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="b22">Killeen <italic>et al.</italic> 2007b</xref>
###xml 820 826 820 826 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">et al.</italic>
###xml 813 831 813 831 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="b43">Stokoe <italic>et al.</italic> 2007</xref>
###xml 225 231 <span type="species:ncbi:10090">murine</span>
###xml 266 271 <span type="species:ncbi:9606">human</span>
###xml 492 497 <span type="species:ncbi:9606">human</span>
###xml 759 764 <span type="species:ncbi:10090">mouse</span>
###xml 785 790 <span type="species:ncbi:9606">human</span>
###xml 962 967 <span type="species:ncbi:10090">mouse</span>
###xml 984 989 <span type="species:ncbi:9606">human</span>
We achieve an empirical pharmacological separation of EADs from arrhythmic substrate through the empirical use of propranolol, resolving the causal relationship between EADs and arrhythmic substrate in a genetically modified murine whole heart model of an inherited human arrhythmic syndrome. These results thus (1) demonstrate the importance of both EADs and arrhythmic substrate in the initiation of arrhythmias in a whole heart model of arrhythmogenecity which directly corresponds to the human clinical phenotype through genetic modification of the Scn5a gene, (2) provide independent corroboration of such important relationships seen using alternative pharmacological strategies (Killeen et al. 2007b) and (3) add to recent arguments for the utility of mouse hearts in modelling human arrhythmia syndromes (Stokoe et al. 2007). Such a causal relationship between EADs and arrhythmic substrate as demonstrated in the present study in a genetically modified mouse heart modelling human LQT3 syndrome at the very least merits further testing.
###end p 16
###begin title 17
Methods
###end title 17
###begin title 18
Preparation of Langendorff-perfused hearts
###end title 18
###begin p 19
###xml 38 43 38 43 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">Scn5a</italic>
###xml 155 161 151 157 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">et al.</italic>
###xml 150 166 146 162 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="b16">Head <italic>et al.</italic> 2005</xref>
###xml 51 55 <span type="species:ncbi:10090">mice</span>
Techniques used for the generation of Scn5a+/Delta mice and the preparation of whole hearts for Langendorff perfusion have been previously described (Head et al. 2005). Healthy, viable hearts suitable for experimentation regained a homogeneous pink coloration and spontaneous rhythmic contraction with warming. Hearts not demonstrating these features were instantly discarded prior to experimentation, to avoid false-positive results during PES (2 hearts discarded out of a total of 25). Hearts were perfused with physiological perfusion buffer for 30 min prior to experimentation, to minimize any residual effects of endogenous catecholamine release.
###end p 19
###begin title 20
Programmed electrical stimulation
###end title 20
###begin p 21
###xml 503 509 503 509 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">et al.</italic>
###xml 495 515 495 515 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="b21">Killeen <italic>et al.</italic> 2007a</xref>

###xml 691 710 691 710 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="b27">Maguire <italic>et al.</italic> 2003</xref>
###xml 880 886 880 886 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">et al.</italic>
###xml 872 891 872 891 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="b27">Maguire <italic>et al.</italic> 2003</xref>
###xml 481 487 <span type="species:ncbi:10090">murine</span>
###xml 536 540 <span type="species:ncbi:10090">mice</span>
###xml 604 608 <span type="species:ncbi:10090">mice</span>
Programmed electrical stimulation of the intact hearts used paired (1 mm inter-pole spacing) platinum stimulating and recording electrodes, positioned on the basal epicardial surface of the right ventricle. The pacing protocols used 2 ms square-wave stimuli with amplitudes three times excitation threshold (Grass S48 stimulator; Grass-Telefactor, Slough, UK). PES is an established method of arrhythmia provocation that has been previously used to assess arrhythmogenecity in the murine heart (Killeen et al. 2007ab). All experimental mice were bred from a 129 genetic background, which, along with C57 mice, are less susceptible to PES-induced arrhythmias than FBV or Black Swiss animals (Maguire et al. 2003). Nevertheless, complex pacing protocols involving double/triple extra-stimuli and rapid burst pacing were avoided to reduce the risk of false-positive results (Maguire et al. 2003). PES protocols comprised a drive train of eight paced S1 beats at 125 ms basic cycle length (BCL), followed by premature S2 extrastimuli every ninth beat. S1S2 intervals first equalled the pacing interval and were then successively reduced by 1 ms with each nine beat cycle until ventricular refractoriness was reached, whereupon the S2 stimuli elicited no response. All PES experiments were performed in hearts in which AV node block had not been induced.
###end p 21
###begin title 22
Monophasic action potential recordings
###end title 22
###begin p 23
###xml 1463 1469 1463 1469 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">et al.</italic>
###xml 1453 1474 1453 1474 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="b23">Knollmann <italic>et al.</italic> 2001</xref>
###xml 1007 1013 <span type="species:ncbi:10090">murine</span>
Monophasic action potentials were recorded from the left ventricular epicardium using a spring-loaded, Ag-Cl contact (2 mm tip diameter) MAP electrode (Linton Instruments; Harvard Apparatus, Edenbridge, UK) which was positioned manually. Left ventricular endocardial recordings were obtained using a custom-built electrode, constructed from two twisted strands of Teflon-coated (0.25 mm diameter) silver wire (99.99% purity) (Advent Research Materials Ltd, Oxford, UK), galvanically chlorided and introduced into the left ventricular cavity through a small access window created in the interventricular septum and rotated such that the tip came to rest against the free wall. The endocardial electrode was initially positioned by hand, and the contact maintained by custom-designed magnetic grips positioned on a metallic platform. All recordings were performed during steady state pacing at 125 ms to negate rate-dependent differences in APD. Signals were amplified and low-pass filtered appropriately for murine recordings (0.1-300 Hz) (Gould 2400S; Gould-Nicolet Technologies, Ilford, Essex, UK) then digitized using a 1401plus analogue-to-digital converter (Cambridge Electronic Design, Cambridge, UK). All electronically stored traces were analysed using Spike II software (Cambridge Electronic Design) according to previously documented criteria of a stable baseline and triangular MAP morphology, rapid upstroke phase and a consistent amplitude (Knollmann et al. 2001). The point of maximum positive deflection was considered to be the point of 0% repolarization, and return to baseline the point of 100% repolarization.
###end p 23
###begin title 24

###end title 24
###begin p 25
###xml 78 79 77 78 <sc xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">m</sc>

###xml 353 354 351 352 <sc xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">m</sc>
###xml 357 384 355 382 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="b41">Shimizu &amp; Antzelevitch 2000</xref>

The concentrations of propranolol used in the present experiment (0.1 and 1 mum) were based on values known to cause electrophysiological effects from corresponding human, dose-response studies (Duff et al. 1983), and were in keeping with previously published studies of animal models of LQTS whereby a range of concentrations have been used (0.1-3.0 mum) (Shimizu & Antzelevitch 2000). Propranolol (Sigma-Aldrich, Poole, UK) was stored at -20 degreesC as a 1 mm stock solution in distilled water before dilution to the final drug concentrations in physiological buffer solution.

###begin title 28
Results
###end title 28
###begin p 29
###xml 79 84 79 84 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">Scn5a</italic>
###xml 114 115 114 115 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">+</sup>
###xml 183 184 183 184 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">+</sup>
###xml 200 206 200 206 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">et al.</italic>
###xml 195 211 195 211 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="b16">Head <italic>et al.</italic> 2005</xref>
###xml 308 309 308 309 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">+</sup>
###xml 592 598 592 598 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">et al.</italic>
###xml 587 603 587 603 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="b16">Head <italic>et al.</italic> 2005</xref>
###xml 612 618 612 618 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">et al.</italic>
###xml 605 624 605 624 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="b44">Thomas <italic>et al.</italic> 2007a</xref>
###xml 244 250 <span type="species:ncbi:10090">murine</span>
###xml 553 558 <span type="species:ncbi:9606">human</span>
Long QT syndrome type 3 (LQT3) is associated with genetic modifications of the Scn5a gene, which interfere with Na+ channel inactivation and lead to persistent, arrhythmogenic late Na+ currents (Head et al. 2005). Recent reports have described murine hearts with such a genetic modification of the cardiac Na+ channel: these show a prolonged repolarization phase, altered transmural gradients of repolarization and EADs. These yield arrhythmic preparations showing triggered activity and episodes of non-sustained VT that thereby fully recapitulate the human clinical phenotype of LQT3 (Head et al. 2005, Thomas et al. 2007a).
###end p 29
###begin p 30
###xml 333 337 333 337 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">both</italic>
###xml 542 548 542 548 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">et al.</italic>
###xml 536 553 536 553 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="b7">Cosio <italic>et al.</italic> 1991</xref>
###xml 564 570 564 570 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">et al.</italic>
###xml 556 576 556 576 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="b13">Fabritz <italic>et al.</italic> 2003a</xref>
###xml 576 577 576 577 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="b14">b</xref>
###xml 579 601 579 601 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="b29">Milberg &amp; Eckardt 2005</xref>
###xml 683 688 683 688 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">Scn5a</italic>
###xml 25 31 <span type="species:ncbi:10090">murine</span>
###xml 241 246 <span type="species:ncbi:9606">human</span>
###xml 703 709 <span type="species:ncbi:10090">murine</span>
We accordingly used this murine model to explore the causal relationship between EADs, transmural gradients of repolarization and the initiation and maintenance of arrhythmias in an experimental model system that directly corresponds to the human clinical phenotype of LQT3. Previous clinical and experimental studies had implicated both EADs and altered transmural gradients of repolarization in the initiation of arrhythmogenesis at the whole heart level but have not yet attempted to organize these phenomena into a causal sequence (Cosio et al. 1991, (Fabritz et al. 2003ab, Milberg & Eckardt 2005). The experiments recorded left ventricular epicardial and endocardial MAPs from Scn5a+/Delta and WT murine hearts in the absence and presence of varying concentrations of propranolol.
###end p 30
###begin p 31
###xml 240 268 240 268 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="b24">Krishnan &amp; Antzelevitch 1991</xref>
###xml 351 352 351 352 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">+</sup>
###xml 593 594 590 591 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">+</sup>
###xml 790 796 787 793 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">et al.</italic>
###xml 779 801 776 798 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="b50">Weselcouch <italic>et al.</italic> 1995</xref>
###xml 815 834 812 831 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="b42">Simaan &amp; Fawaz 1970</xref>
###xml 999 1018 996 1015 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="b42">Simaan &amp; Fawaz 1970</xref>
###xml 1031 1037 1028 1034 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">et al.</italic>
###xml 1020 1042 1017 1039 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="b50">Weselcouch <italic>et al.</italic> 1995</xref>
###xml 1343 1354 1340 1351 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">empirically</italic>
###xml 96 102 <span type="species:ncbi:10090">murine</span>
###xml 774 777 <span type="species:ncbi:10116">rat</span>
###xml 977 980 <span type="species:ncbi:10116">rat</span>
###xml 1163 1169 <span type="species:ncbi:10090">murine</span>
###xml 1412 1418 <span type="species:ncbi:10090">murine</span>
###xml 1428 1433 <span type="species:ncbi:9606">human</span>
In keeping with earlier studies using the canine ventricular wedge preparation, we perfused all murine hearts with buffer solution for a period of 30 min in order to washout residual catecholamines that may be leaking from nerve terminals (Krishnan & Antzelevitch 1991). Propranolol has well documented nonspecific pharmacological actions, blocking Na+ channels in addition to its beta-adrenoceptor blocking effects. Therefore propranolol would be expected to antagonize any residual adrenergic tone caused by spontaneous release of catecholamines from nerve endings in addition to blocking Na+ channels. Nevertheless, the role of endogenous catecholamines in isolated hearts is unclear. Indeed, the presence of endogenous catecholamines has been previously reported in the rat (Weselcouch et al. 1995) and canine (Simaan & Fawaz 1970) isolated hearts. However, depletion of endogenous catecholamines with reserpine did not affect coronary flow or cardiac function in both the rat and canine heart (Simaan & Fawaz 1970, Weselcouch et al. 1995). With this in mind we acknowledge that propranolol may antagonize residual adrenergic tone in the Langendorff-perfused murine whole heart in the present study. Nonetheless, irrespective of the mechanisms of action of propranolol in the present study, we used this agent as a pharmacological tool to empirically separate out EADs from the arrhythmogenic substrate in a murine model of human LQT3 syndrome for the first time.
###end p 31
###begin title 32
###xml 77 82 77 82 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">Scn5a</italic>
Inhibition of EADs and spontaneous arrhythmogenesis in intrinsically beating Scn5a+/Delta hearts
###end title 32
###begin p 33
###xml 50 55 50 55 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">Scn5a</italic>
###xml 375 386 371 382 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">spontaneous</italic>
###xml 576 582 572 578 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">et al.</italic>
###xml 568 588 564 584 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="b13">Fabritz <italic>et al.</italic> 2003a</xref>
###xml 598 604 594 600 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">et al.</italic>
###xml 590 610 586 606 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="b21">Killeen <italic>et al.</italic> 2007a</xref>
###xml 737 743 733 739 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">et al.</italic>
###xml 724 748 720 744 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="b6">van den Berg <italic>et al.</italic> 2001</xref>
###xml 759 765 755 761 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">et al.</italic>
###xml 750 770 746 766 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="b40">Schwartz <italic>et al.</italic> 2001</xref>
###xml 974 980 970 976 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">et al.</italic>
###xml 966 986 962 982 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="b13">Fabritz <italic>et al.</italic> 2003a</xref>
###xml 70 76 <span type="species:ncbi:10090">murine</span>
###xml 631 639 <span type="species:ncbi:9606">patients</span>
Following isolation, cannulation and perfusion of Scn5a+/Delta and WT murine hearts, left ventricular epicardial and endocardial MAPs possessing a stable waveform morphology, amplitude and duration were achieved after a 10 min equilibration period. Following this, MAP waveforms remained highly reproducible throughout all experiments. Bradycardia is a known risk factor for spontaneous arrhythmogenesis at the whole heart level and earlier studies have reported an increased incidence of EADs and consequent ventricular arrhythmogenesis under bradycardic conditions (Fabritz et al. 2003a, Killeen et al. 2007a). Furthermore, LQT3 patients predominantly experience arrhythmic episodes at rest, under bradycardic conditions (van den Berg et al. 2001, Schwartz et al. 2001). To investigate the effects of propranolol upon EADs and spontaneous arrhythmogenesis we therefore induced AV block through crush-ablation of the AV node, in keeping with earlier methodologies (Fabritz et al. 2003a).
###end p 33
###begin p 34
###xml 29 34 29 34 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">Scn5a</italic>
###xml 95 97 79 81 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">&#8722;1</sup>
###xml 200 209 184 193 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="fig" rid="fig01">Fig. 1a,b</xref>
###xml 224 229 208 213 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">Scn5a</italic>
###xml 377 378 357 358 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">n</italic>
###xml 385 392 365 372 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="fig" rid="fig01">Fig. 1b</xref>
###xml 509 510 489 490 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">n</italic>
Crush ablation rendered both Scn5a+/Delta and WT hearts bradycardic (approximately100 beats min-1) but nevertheless still spontaneously beating, compared to hearts in which AV block was not induced, (Fig. 1a,b). Bradycardic Scn5a+/Delta hearts spontaneously showed multiple EADs, triggered beats and episodes of non-sustained VT lasting on average 15 beats (range 7-33 beats) (n = 7) (Fig. 1b). In contrast, WT hearts never showed such events (whether EADs or polymorphic VT) following induction of AV block (n = 5).
###end p 34
###begin p 35
###xml 123 128 123 128 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">Scn5a</italic>
###xml 503 504 498 499 <sc xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">m</sc>

All such features are suppressed following perfusion with physiological buffer solution containing propranolol (1 mum) (c).
###end p 35
###begin p 36
###xml 126 131 126 131 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">Scn5a</italic>
###xml 195 206 191 202 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">spontaneous</italic>
###xml 284 285 279 280 <sc xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">m</sc>
###xml 381 386 376 381 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">Scn5a</italic>
###xml 402 409 393 400 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="fig" rid="fig01">Fig. 1c</xref>
###xml 477 478 468 469 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">n</italic>
###xml 631 636 622 627 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">Scn5a</italic>
###xml 793 798 780 785 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">Scn5a</italic>
###xml 644 650 <span type="species:ncbi:10090">murine</span>
###xml 813 819 <span type="species:ncbi:10090">murine</span>
We then investigated the empirical effects of a range of propranolol concentrations in such spontaneously beating bradycardic Scn5a+/Delta and WT hearts to explore a correlation between EADs and spontaneous arrhythmogenesis. Perfusion with physiological buffer containing 0.1 and 1 mum propranolol suppressed not only EADs, but also triggered beats and spontaneous VT in all seven Scn5a+/Delta hearts (Fig. 1c), and had no further effects upon spontaneously beating WT hearts (n = 5). These data therefore associate EADs with spontaneous arrhythmogenesis, and their abolition with its absence in spontaneously beating, bradycardic Scn5a+/Delta murine hearts. This then led to experiments that next explored the effects of propranolol upon arrhythmogenesis provoked by extrasystolic stimuli in Scn5a+/Delta and WT murine hearts.
###end p 36
###begin title 37
###xml 41 46 41 46 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">Scn5a</italic>
Paradoxical arrhythmogenic properties in Scn5a+/Delta hearts following programmed electrical stimulation
###end title 37
###begin p 38
###xml 132 137 132 137 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">Scn5a</italic>
###xml 193 198 189 194 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">Scn5a</italic>

###xml 379 380 367 368 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">n</italic>

###xml 1042 1046 <span type="species:ncbi:10090">mice</span>
The present PES results thus demonstrated that low concentrations of propranolol (0.1 mum) exerted an antiarrhythmic effect in Scn5a+/Delta hearts, findings that did correlate with the antiarrhythmic properties in spontaneously beating hearts described above. However, a higher concentration of propranolol (1 mum) reversed this correlation by exerting a proarrhythmic effect in Scn5a+/Delta hearts. This was in direct contrast to findings in the spontaneously beating Scn5a+/Delta hearts in which both the 0.1 and 1 mum concentrations of propranolol suppressed unprovoked arrhythmogenesis. This prompted us to explore whether these paradoxical, even if empirical, effects of propranolol in Scn5a+/Delta hearts might correlate with similar paradoxical alterations in the transmural gradient of repolarization. Accordingly, we investigated the effects of propranolol upon the transmural repolarization gradients known to exist within the left ventricle of WT (Anumonwo et al. 2001, Knollmann et al. 2001, Dilly et al. 2006) and DeltaKPQ Scn5a mice (Thomas et al. 2007a). These measurements permit the quantification of the transmural gradient of repolarization, alterations which have been recently associated with arrhythmogenecity at the whole heart level (Killeen et al. 2007ab, Thomas et al. 2007a).

However, increased propranolol concentrations (1.0 mum) caused progressive prolongation of epicardial APD90 in WT hearts to 61.6 +/- 1.8 ms (n = 5, P < 0.05). In corresponding endocardial recordings, however, perfusion of WT hearts with propranolol did not significantly affect endocardial APD90 (51.4 +/- 0.7 vs. 47.5 +/- 1.5 ms, respectively, n = 5). These effects of 1 mum propranolol in WT hearts thus significantly reduced DeltaAPD90 from 1.0 +/- 8.0 to -10.2 +/- 1.9 ms (P < 0.05). In contrast, perfusion of Scn5a+/Delta hearts with 1.0 mum propranolol significantly increased epicardial APD90 to 72.5 +/- 1.2 ms (n = 5, P < 0.05), and significantly shortened endocardial APD90 to 24.5 +/-0.3 ms. These marked, contrasting effects of 1 mum propranolol upon epicardial and endocardial APD90, significantly altered DeltaAPD90 to -48.0 +/- 1.2 ms (n = 5, P < 0.05).
###end p 48
###begin p 49
###xml 111 113 107 109 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">90</sub>
###xml 117 122 113 118 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">Scn5a</italic>
###xml 286 288 272 274 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">90</sub>
###xml 366 367 351 352 <sc xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">m</sc>
###xml 583 585 564 566 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">90</sub>
###xml 628 630 609 611 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">90</sub>
###xml 663 665 644 646 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">90</sub>
###xml 753 755 730 732 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">90</sub>
###xml 779 784 756 761 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">Scn5a</italic>
###xml 810 811 782 783 <sc xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">m</sc>
###xml 1134 1140 <span type="species:ncbi:10090">murine</span>
These findings precisely parallel the findings from the PES studies described above. Thus the baseline DeltaAPD90 in Scn5a+/Delta hearts of -3.8 +/- 1.6 ms corresponded to an induction of VT by PES in 6 of 12 preparations (50% incidence of arrhythmogenesis). The restoration of DeltaAPD90 values close to control WT values at baseline following application of 0.1 mum propranolol was reflected in the PES studies, in which premature stimuli induced VT in only one of eight preparations (12.5% incidence of arrhythmogenesis). Finally the increased arrhythmogenic substrate of DeltaAPD90 produced by prolongation of epicardial APD90 and reduction in endocardial APD90 bringing about a significantly increased arrhythmogenic substrate reflected in DeltaAPD90 following perfusion of Scn5a+/Delta hearts with 1.0 mum propranolol corresponded to the increased arrhythmic substrate suggested by the induction of VT in six of eight preparations (75% incidence of arrhythmogenesis). Collectively these findings demonstrate the pharmacological separation of EADs from altered transmural gradients of repolarization in this genetically modified murine whole heart model of arrhythmogenesis for the first time through the application of propranolol at differential concentrations.

###begin title 50
Discussion
###end title 50
###begin p 51
###xml 207 208 207 208 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">+</sup>
###xml 218 223 218 223 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">Scn5a</italic>

###xml 432 438 428 434 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">et al.</italic>

###xml 470 476 466 472 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">et al.</italic>
###xml 463 481 459 477 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="b43">Stokoe <italic>et al.</italic> 2007</xref>
###xml 490 496 486 492 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">et al.</italic>
###xml 483 502 479 498 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="b44">Thomas <italic>et al.</italic> 2007a</xref>
###xml 704 710 700 706 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">et al.</italic>
###xml 696 715 692 711 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="b47">Volders <italic>et al.</italic> 2000</xref>
###xml 859 865 855 861 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">et al.</italic>
###xml 855 870 851 866 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="b53">Yan <italic>et al.</italic> 2001</xref>
###xml 880 886 876 882 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">et al.</italic>
###xml 872 891 868 887 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="b37">Restivo <italic>et al.</italic> 2004</xref>
###xml 32 38 <span type="species:ncbi:10090">murine</span>
###xml 110 115 <span type="species:ncbi:9606">human</span>
###xml 244 250 <span type="species:ncbi:10090">Murine</span>
###xml 1131 1137 <span type="species:ncbi:10090">murine</span>
###xml 1189 1194 <span type="species:ncbi:9606">human</span>
We have previously engineered a murine whole heart model of arrhythmogenesis that directly corresponds to the human clinical phenotype of LQT3 through genetic modification of the gene encoding the cardiac Na+ channel, Scn5a (Head et al. 2005). Murine hearts heterozygous for this mutation (Scn5a+/Delta hearts) have prolonged epicardial APDs, altered transmural gradients of repolarization, and show EADs and episodes of VT (Nuyens et al. 2001, Head et al. 2005, Stokoe et al. 2007, Thomas et al. 2007a). The mechanisms responsible for cardiac arrhythmogenesis in LQT3 are not fully understood, although it is believed that heterogeneous AP prolongation both facilitates the development of EADs (Volders et al. 2000) and creates a transmural dispersion of repolarization across the ventricular wall, capable of maintaining TdP via a re-entrant mechanism (Yan et al. 2001, Restivo et al. 2004). We took advantage of this model to explore, for the first time, the causal relationship between EADs and arrhythmic substrate of transmural gradients of repolarization, through the empirical use of propranolol, in a genetically modified murine heart which fully recapitulates the arrhythmogenic human clinical phenotype.
###end p 51
###begin p 52
###xml 262 263 261 262 <sc xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">m</sc>
###xml 371 373 366 368 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">90</sub>
###xml 533 539 525 531 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">et al.</italic>
###xml 526 544 518 536 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="b34">Priori <italic>et al.</italic> 2004</xref>
###xml 427 435 <span type="species:ncbi:9606">patients</span>
###xml 516 524 <span type="species:ncbi:9606">patients</span>
We empirically demonstrate a paradoxical situation in which it was possible to suppress EADs and spontaneous arrhythmias on the one hand, yet exacerbate provoked arrhythmias on the other with the same empirical manoeuvre of propranolol at a concentration of 1 mum. The latter finding correlated with increased transmural gradients of repolarization, expressed as DeltaAPD90, and translates into clinical findings in which LQT3 patients appear to derive less benefit from beta-blocker therapy than other LQT syndrome patients (Priori et al. 2004).
###end p 52
###begin p 53
###xml 137 142 137 142 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">Scn5a</italic>
###xml 191 196 187 192 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">Scn5a</italic>
###xml 276 278 268 270 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">&#8722;1</sup>
###xml 306 311 298 303 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">Scn5a</italic>
###xml 422 428 410 416 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">et al.</italic>
###xml 415 434 403 422 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="b44">Thomas <italic>et al.</italic> 2007a</xref>
###xml 455 475 443 463 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="b8">Damiano &amp; Rosen 1984</xref>
###xml 477 493 465 481 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="b54">Zeng &amp; Rudy 1995</xref>
###xml 524 526 512 514 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">2+</sup>
###xml 808 829 796 817 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="b18">January &amp; Riddle 1989</xref>
###xml 1011 1013 999 1001 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">2+</sup>
###xml 1026 1031 1014 1019 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">Scn5a</italic>
###xml 1061 1067 1045 1051 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">et al.</italic>
###xml 1054 1073 1038 1057 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="b44">Thomas <italic>et al.</italic> 2007a</xref>
###xml 1103 1104 1086 1087 <sc xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">m</sc>
###xml 1208 1213 1191 1196 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">Scn5a</italic>
###xml 1039 1045 <span type="species:ncbi:10090">murine</span>
First, the present study has shown, for the first time, that propranolol suppressed EADs and spontaneous arrhythmogenesis in bradycardic Scn5a+/Delta hearts. Crush-ablation of the AV node in Scn5a+/Delta hearts resulted in a bradycardic heart rate (approximately 100 beats min-1). At baseline, bradycardic Scn5a+/Delta hearts frequently displayed EADs and episodes of VT, closely correlating with earlier findings (Thomas et al. 2007a). Previous studies (Damiano & Rosen 1984, Zeng & Rudy 1995) have implicated the L-type Ca2+ channel (LTCC) as a necessary depolarizing charge carrier for EAD induction at slow stimulation rates. The mechanism for EAD induction is generally considered to involve the recovery from inactivation of LTCCs due to prolongation of APD within their critical window voltage range (January & Riddle 1989). This theory is further supported by our recent finding in which the specific LTCC blocker nifedipine suppressed EADs and arrhythmogenesis through selective inhibition of inward Ca2+ currents in Scn5a+/Delta murine hearts (Thomas et al. 2007a). Perfusion with 0.1 and 1 mum propranolol not only suppressed all EADs but also episodes of VT in spontaneously beating, bradycardic Scn5a+/Delta hearts.
###end p 53
###begin p 54
###xml 40 53 40 53 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">spontaneously</italic>
###xml 114 119 114 119 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">Scn5a</italic>
###xml 242 253 238 249 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">provocation</italic>
###xml 350 355 346 351 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">Scn5a</italic>
###xml 506 511 498 503 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">Scn5a</italic>
###xml 583 584 570 571 <sc xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">m</sc>
###xml 654 655 640 641 <sc xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">m</sc>
###xml 688 693 674 679 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">Scn5a</italic>
###xml 808 809 789 790 <sc xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">m</sc>
###xml 835 840 816 821 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">Scn5a</italic>
Secondly, in contrast to experiments in spontaneously beating hearts to assess unprovoked arrhythmogenecity, both Scn5a+/Delta and WT hearts were subject to premature extrasystolic ventricular depolarizations using the established arrhythmia provocation protocol of PES. Under control conditions, premature stimuli successfully induced VT in 6 of 12 Scn5a+/Delta hearts (50% arrhythmia incidence) but never in WT hearts. Following subsequent perfusion with propranolol, PES only induced VT in one of eight Scn5a+/Delta hearts (12.5% arrhythmia incidence) at a concentration of 0.1 mum. However, following the increase in propranolol concentration to 1 mum, PES induced VT in six of eight Scn5a+/Delta hearts (75% arrhythmia incidence). This finding is in sharp contrast to the antiarrhythmic findings of 1 mum in spontaneously beating Scn5a+/Delta hearts.
###end p 54
###begin p 55

###xml 785 791 781 787 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">et al.</italic>
###xml 778 796 774 792 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="b43">Stokoe <italic>et al.</italic> 2007</xref>
###xml 805 811 801 807 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">et al.</italic>
###xml 798 817 794 813 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="b44">Thomas <italic>et al.</italic> 2007a</xref>
###xml 846 852 842 848 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">et al.</italic>
###xml 839 858 835 854 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="b45">Thomas <italic>et al.</italic> 2007b</xref>
###xml 742 748 <span type="species:ncbi:10090">murine</span>
###xml 766 771 <span type="species:ncbi:9606">human</span>
Thirdly, the findings that a given manoeuvre can prevent both EADs and VT in spontaneously beating hearts yet induce VT in provoked hearts suggests that it may be exerting additional proarrhythmic effects that only are unmasked under arrhythmia provocation protocols. To explain differential antiarrhythmic effects in Scn5a+/Delta hearts exposed to propranolol we measured the corresponding epicardial and endocardial APD, thus permitting the subsequent quantification of transmural gradients of repolarization. Previously, alterations in ventricular transmural gradients of repolarization have been associated with arrhythmogenicity at the whole heart level in the setting of hypokalaemia (Killeen et al. 2007ab) and in genetically modified murine hearts modelling human LQT3 (Stokoe et al. 2007, Thomas et al. 2007a) and LQT5 syndromes (Thomas et al. 2007b).

###xml 1143 1144 1143 1144 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="b14">b</xref>
###xml 1144 1148 1144 1148 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="b15">2005</xref>
###xml 1157 1163 1157 1163 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">et al.</italic>
###xml 1150 1168 1150 1168 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="b25">London <italic>et al.</italic> 2007</xref>
###xml 132 138 <span type="species:ncbi:10090">murine</span>
###xml 946 952 <span type="species:ncbi:10090">murine</span>
In the present study we measured transmural repolarization gradients under conditions of steady-state pacing. The quantification of murine APD and transmural repolarization gradients in the spontaneously beating heart is problematic. The intrinsic variability in heart rate as observed in spontaneously beating hearts can in turn alter APDs. It is well recognized that mammalian cardiac repolarization exhibits a cycle-length dependence, with shorter APDs recorded at rapid rates and longer APDs recorded at slow rates (Sabir et al. 2007). These effects could have important implications when determining APDs and transmural repolarization gradients. Thus APD and repolarization gradient measurements obtained from the spontaneously beating heart may not fully represent the observed experimental conditions but may instead reflect spontaneous intrinsic increases or decreases in heart rate. Therefore, in keeping with recent studies correlating murine ventricular repolarization gradients and arrhythmogenicity reported from our laboratory (Killeen et al. 2007ab, Stokoe et al. 2007, Thomas et al. 2007ab) and elsewhere (Fabritz et al. 2003a,b2005, London et al. 2007) we recorded APDs and repolarization gradients in the presence of an extrinsic pacing protocol that eliminated any intrinsic variability in heart rate which may in turn alter APD and repolarization gradients.

###xml 1215 1221 1192 1198 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">et al.</italic>
###xml 1210 1226 1187 1203 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="b1">Aiba <italic>et al.</italic> 2005</xref>
###xml 1228 1250 1205 1227 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="b29">Milberg &amp; Eckardt 2005</xref>
###xml 1259 1265 1236 1242 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">et al.</italic>
###xml 1252 1271 1229 1248 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="b44">Thomas <italic>et al.</italic> 2007a</xref>
The present experiments showed that administration of 0.1 mum propranolol to Scn5a+/Delta hearts produced selective abbreviation of epicardial APD90, as opposed to endocardial APD90, thus restoring DeltaAPD90 to positive values close to those recorded from control WT hearts. This finding is in agreement with earlier reports demonstrating selective abbreviation of epicardial over endocardial APD90 in response to a variety of pharmacological agents in a range of arrhythmogenic cardiac models, restoring altered transmural gradients of repolarization and exerting an antiarrhythmic effect (Aiba et al. 2005, Thomas et al. 2007b). However, perfusion of Scn5a+/Delta hearts with 1 mum propranolol produced significant prolongation of epicardial APD90 alongside significant shortening of endocardial APD90. These opposite effects of 1 mum propranolol on epicardial and endocardial APD90 in Scn5a+/Delta hearts produced a significant increase in the arrhythmogenic substrate of DeltaAPD90, resulting in a higher incidence of provoked arrhythmogenesis. Increases in transmural repolarization gradients have been previously correlated with arrhythmogenicity in a range of cardiac models (Antzelevitch et al. 1998, Aiba et al. 2005, Milberg & Eckardt 2005, Thomas et al. 2007a).
###end p 57
###begin p 58
###xml 66 68 62 64 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">90</sub>
###xml 297 299 287 289 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">90</sub>
###xml 320 321 308 309 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">P</italic>
###xml 235 241 <span type="species:ncbi:10090">murine</span>
###xml 420 426 <span type="species:ncbi:10090">murine</span>
###xml 436 441 <span type="species:ncbi:9606">human</span>
Following the administration of propranolol to WT hearts, DeltaAPD90 was reduced to -10.2 +/- 1.9 ms, however, neither spontaneous nor provoked arrhythmias were recorded under these conditions. Under baseline conditions, however, LQT3 murine hearts displayed a significantly less negative DeltaAPD90 of -3.8 +/- 1.6 ms (P < 0.05) alongside a 50% incidence of arrhythmogenesis. These data suggest that the DeltaKPQ Scn5a murine model of human LQT3 syndrome is significantly more sensitive to changes in the transmural repolarization gradient which may precipitate arrhythmogenesis compared to WT hearts. To the best of our knowledge, this finding has not been previously reported in the setting of LQT3 syndrome.
###end p 58
###begin p 59
###xml 338 366 338 366 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="b24">Krishnan &amp; Antzelevitch 1991</xref>

###xml 793 794 793 794 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">I</italic>
###xml 794 796 794 796 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">to</sub>
###xml 896 902 896 902 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">et al.</italic>
###xml 888 908 888 908 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="b21">Killeen <italic>et al.</italic> 2007a</xref>
###xml 727 733 <span type="species:ncbi:10090">murine</span>
These findings in the present study correlate well with earlier reports suggesting differential epicardial and endocardial effects of propranolol alongside an increase in arrhythmogenicity. Firstly, a study in the canine ventricular wedge preparation demonstrated that propranolol increased epicardial APD, yet shortened endocardial APD (Krishnan & Antzelevitch 1991). Such differential effects were also observed in canine tissue under conditions of simulated ischaemia (Lukas & Antzelevitch 1993). Electrophysiologically, endocardial and epicardial ventricular regions are primarily characterized by their relative densities of Ito: epicardial myocytes have been reported to have a significantly higher density of Ito in the murine heart (Killeen et al. 2007a). These differing densities of Ito are in part responsible for the shorter APD in epicardial compared to endocardial regions (Killeen et al. 2007a).

Blockade of Na+ channels with propranolol would be expected to reduce the amplitude of phase 0. Additionally, blockade of the Na+ window/late Na+ current by propranolol would act to shorten endocardial repolarization times. Reductions in epicardial INa and ICa, due to a direct effect of propranolol on the Na+ channel and indirect effects due to a reduction in the amplitude of phase 0, respectively, would be considerably overwhelmed by the prominent epicardial Ito component, which would open at more negative potentials. In keeping with the study in the canine ventricular wedge preparation, we documented a prolongation of epicardial APD in response to propranolol administration (Krishnan & Antzelevitch 1991).

###xml 0 1 0 1 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">I</italic>

###xml 702 707 702 707 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">Kslow</sub>
###xml 931 959 931 959 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="b24">Krishnan &amp; Antzelevitch 1991</xref>
###xml 644 650 <span type="species:ncbi:10090">murine</span>
Ito would bring the membrane potential to a more negative potential which would in turn affect ICa. Hyperpolarization would be expected to shift the membrane potential outside the activation range of ICa, which would in turn reduce ICa. However, if the Ca2+ channels are already activated, these would be expected to carry more inward Ca2+ current as hyperpolarization would increase the electrical gradient acting on the Ca2+ ions. Inactivation of Ito would reduce outward current and would give rise to a net inward current, although ICa may be small and slow in onset. This delay in the onset of ICa would in turn delay the opening of other murine delayed rectifier repolarizing components such as IKslow, causing prolongation of epicardial APD. Such a scheme of events has been previously proposed to take place in the canine myocardium and account for the contrasting effects of propranolol on epicardial and endocardial APD (Krishnan & Antzelevitch 1991).

###xml 1231 1259 1229 1257 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="b24">Krishnan &amp; Antzelevitch 1991</xref>
###xml 957 965 <span type="species:ncbi:9606">patients</span>
###xml 1078 1084 <span type="species:ncbi:10090">murine</span>
In the canine wedge preparation administration of propranolol prolonged epicardial APD, however, when a higher concentration of propranolol was used a shortening effect upon epicardial APD was observed. Higher concentrations of propranolol reduced phase 0 amplitude to a critical level as to permit premature repolarization due to an overwhelming Ito (Krishnan & Antzelevitch 1991). Premature epicardial repolarization achieved through significant reductions in the amplitude of phase 0 is primarily responsible for the appearance of the epicardial extrasystole and the subsequent phase 2 re-entry mechanism which is thought to underlie arrhythmogenesis in the Brugada syndrome (Antzelevtich & Yan 2000). We did not increase propranolol concentrations beyond 1 mum in the present study due to the fact that plasma concentrations of propranolol beyond 1 mum are not clinically relevant and are therefore not likely to occur in the treatment of LQT3 syndrome patients. Higher concentrations of propranolol may have reduced phase 0 amplitude to an even greater extent in the LQT 3 murine heart which would lead to premature epicardial repolarization, in keeping with previous findings performed in the canine ventricular wedge model (Krishnan & Antzelevitch 1991).

###xml 1929 1940 1929 1940 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">apico-basal</italic>
###xml 2033 2039 2033 2039 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">et al.</italic>
###xml 2028 2044 2028 2044 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="b46">Tian <italic>et al.</italic> 2007</xref>
###xml 110 116 <span type="species:ncbi:10090">murine</span>
###xml 434 439 <span type="species:ncbi:10090">mouse</span>
###xml 987 993 <span type="species:ncbi:10090">murine</span>
###xml 1482 1487 <span type="species:ncbi:10090">mouse</span>
###xml 1741 1745 <span type="species:ncbi:10090">mice</span>
###xml 1991 1997 <span type="species:ncbi:10090">murine</span>
###xml 2007 2012 <span type="species:ncbi:9606">human</span>
We have previously correlated alterations in the transmural repolarization gradient with arrhythmogenicity in murine models of LQT3 syndrome (Thomas et al. 2007a), LQT5 syndrome (Thomas et al. 2007b) and hypokalaemia-induced arrhythmogenesis (Killeen et al. 2007ab). However, we fully acknowledge that additional factors beyond transmural repolarization gradients may indeed account for the observed incidences of arrhythmias in LQT3 mouse hearts in the present study. In mammalian hearts, cardiac repolarization follows tightly regulated patterns, from epicardium to endocardium and from apex to base, which are considered to maintain cardiac pump function and to provide a safeguard against arrhythmias. In addition to transmural repolarization gradients, studies have suggested that apico-basal repolarization gradients also play an important role in arrhythmia induction. In a recent study, London et al. (2007) measured apex to base repolarization gradients in genetically modified murine hearts harbouring deletions of components for Ito. Increases in the apex to base repolarization gradient were associated with arrhythmogenesis. Alterations in this gradient can lead to disrupted patterns of depolarization and repolarization, which may in turn lead to the development of regions of conduction block which can facilitate arrhythmogenesis through re-entrant mechanisms (Killeen & Sabir 2007). Such alterations in depolarization and repolarization patterns in an alternative mouse model of LQT3 syndrome have been recently reported (Tian et al. 2007). We therefore cannot exclude the fact that similar mechanisms may be taking place in the present study, which may also account for the incidences of arrhythmogenesis observed in LQT3 mice. Nevertheless, these findings in the present study demonstrating alterations in the transmural repolarization gradient are in agreement with recent findings describing alterations in apico-basal repolarization patterns in a genetically modified murine model of human LQT3 syndrome (Tian et al. 2007).
###end p 63
###begin p 64
###xml 666 672 666 672 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">et al.</italic>
###xml 660 677 660 677 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="b4">Baker <italic>et al.</italic> 2000</xref>
###xml 904 910 904 910 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">et al.</italic>
###xml 896 916 896 916 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="b13">Fabritz <italic>et al.</italic> 2003a</xref>
###xml 916 917 916 917 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="b14">b</xref>
###xml 927 933 927 933 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">et al.</italic>
###xml 919 939 919 939 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="b21">Killeen <italic>et al.</italic> 2007a</xref>
###xml 252 257 <span type="species:ncbi:10090">mouse</span>
###xml 621 627 <span type="species:ncbi:10090">murine</span>
###xml 1125 1130 <span type="species:ncbi:10090">mouse</span>
Measuring cardiac repolarization through MAP recordings helps to quantify changes in AP shape and duration that may correspond to an arrhythmogenic phenotype. In the present study, continuous recording of epicardial and endocardial APs in the isolated mouse heart enabled us to monitor changes in transmural gradients of repolarization throughout the duration of experiments and to correlate these changes with an altered arrhythmic tendency. Similarly, optical mapping of the isolated heart can provide important information regarding dispersion of repolarization alongside activation and repolarization patterns in the murine ventricle at a high resolution (Baker et al. 2000). However, optical mapping studies in the intact heart are limited to recording signals from only the epicardial surface. MAPs can be recorded continuously and simultaneously from multiple sites in the isolated heart (Fabritz et al. 2003ab, Killeen et al. 2007a). In the present study, recording from ventricular epicardial and endocardial sites served as an accurate and sensitive marker for changes in transmural repolarization gradients in the mouse heart and as a useful indicator of proarrhythmia.
###end p 64
###begin p 65
###xml 59 60 58 59 <sc xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">m</sc>
###xml 255 260 254 259 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">Scn5a</italic>
###xml 281 287 276 282 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">et al.</italic>
###xml 276 292 271 287 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="b16">Head <italic>et al.</italic> 2005</xref>
###xml 380 386 375 381 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">et al.</italic>
###xml 375 391 370 386 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="b16">Head <italic>et al.</italic> 2005</xref>
###xml 548 554 543 549 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">et al.</italic>
###xml 539 559 534 554 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="b39">Saumarez <italic>et al.</italic> 1992</xref>
###xml 595 601 590 596 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">et al.</italic>
###xml 579 606 574 601 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="b5">Balasubramaniam <italic>et al.</italic> 2003</xref>
At the whole heart level we have previously shown that 1 mum propranolol increases electrogram duration (EGD) and increases the dispersion of ventricular conduction curves as measured through paced electrogram fractionation analysis, when administered to Scn5a+/Delta hearts (Head et al. 2005). These latter effects were also accompanied by an increase in arrhythmogenecity (Head et al. 2005). Increases in EGD have been previously associated with increased re-entrant substrate and a higher incidence of arrhythmogenesis at the clinical (Saumarez et al. 1992) and experimental (Balasubramaniam et al. 2003) levels.
###end p 65
###begin p 66
###xml 224 229 224 229 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">Scn5a</italic>
###xml 405 411 398 404 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">et al.</italic>
###xml 397 416 390 409 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="b52">Woosley <italic>et al.</italic> 1979</xref>
###xml 474 475 467 468 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">+</sup>
###xml 572 590 565 583 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="b9">Davis &amp; Temte 1968</xref>
###xml 599 605 592 598 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">et al.</italic>
###xml 592 610 585 603 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="b35">Pruett <italic>et al.</italic> 1980</xref>
###xml 612 633 605 626 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="b28">Matthews &amp; Baker 1982</xref>
###xml 644 667 637 660 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="b28">Matthews &amp; Baker (1982)</xref>
###xml 753 754 746 747 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">+</sup>
###xml 870 888 863 881 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="b36">Rauls &amp; Baker 1979</xref>
###xml 933 939 926 932 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">et al.</italic>
###xml 928 946 921 939 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="b11">Duff <italic>et al.</italic> (1983)</xref>
###xml 1178 1180 1166 1168 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">&#8722;1</sup>
###xml 1202 1203 1177 1178 <sc xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">m</sc>
###xml 1291 1297 1266 1272 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">beyond</italic>
###xml 1366 1368 1339 1341 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">&#8722;1</sup>
###xml 1390 1391 1350 1351 <sc xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">m</sc>
###xml 1399 1405 1359 1365 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">et al.</italic>
###xml 1394 1410 1354 1370 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="b11">Duff <italic>et al.</italic> 1983</xref>
###xml 1497 1498 1457 1458 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">+</sup>
###xml 856 862 <span type="species:ncbi:9986">rabbit</span>
###xml 1010 1018 <span type="species:ncbi:9606">patients</span>
The aim of this study was to use propranolol as an empirical means of separating conditions known to contribute to arrhythmogenesis rather than to elucidate the anti and pro-arrhythmic mechanisms of action of propranolol in Scn5a+/Delta hearts. Nevertheless, it has been known for some time that the antiarrhythmic effects of propranolol may be unrelated to antagonism of the beta-adrenoreceptor (Woosley et al. 1979). Additional local anaesthetic actions of propranolol, Na+ channel blockade, leading to reductions in APD have previously been observed in cardiac tissue (Davis & Temte 1968, Pruett et al. 1980, Matthews & Baker 1982). Indeed, Matthews & Baker (1982) concluded that concentrations of propranolol required to produce a 50% blockade of Na+ channels closely correlated with concentrations required to exert antiarrhythmic effects on isolated rabbit atria (Rauls & Baker 1979). Additionally, at the clinical level, Duff et al. (1983) reported that suppression of ventricular arrhythmias in 40% of patients necessitates a plasma propranolol concentration in excess of that producing substantial beta-receptor blockade. At high plasma concentrations (472 +/- 68 ng ml-1 approximately 1.82 mum), additional shortening of the endocardial MAP duration and QTc interval was observed, beyond that seen at low plasma propranolol concentrations (104 +/- 17 ng ml-1 approximately 0.57 mum) (Duff et al. 1983). These findings suggest that propranolol possesses an intrinsic efficacy of potent Na+ channel blockade, irrespective of its beta-adrenoceptor blocking actions.
###end p 66
###begin p 67
###xml 119 146 116 143 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="b41">Shimizu &amp; Antzelevitch 2000</xref>
###xml 153 159 150 156 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">et al.</italic>
###xml 148 164 145 161 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="b16">Head <italic>et al.</italic> 2005</xref>
###xml 441 468 438 465 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="b41">Shimizu &amp; Antzelevitch 2000</xref>
###xml 619 646 616 643 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="b41">Shimizu &amp; Antzelevitch 2000</xref>
###xml 959 965 953 959 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">et al.</italic>
###xml 954 970 948 964 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="b16">Head <italic>et al.</italic> 2005</xref>
###xml 1050 1077 1044 1071 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="b41">Shimizu &amp; Antzelevitch 2000</xref>
###xml 1227 1233 1218 1224 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">et al.</italic>
###xml 1220 1238 1211 1229 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="b34">Priori <italic>et al.</italic> 2004</xref>
###xml 1341 1347 1332 1338 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">et al.</italic>
###xml 1336 1352 1327 1343 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="b16">Head <italic>et al.</italic> 2005</xref>
###xml 839 843 <span type="species:ncbi:10090">mice</span>
###xml 1125 1133 <span type="species:ncbi:9606">patients</span>
###xml 1210 1218 <span type="species:ncbi:9606">patients</span>
###xml 1322 1328 <span type="species:ncbi:10090">murine</span>
###xml 1466 1472 <span type="species:ncbi:10090">murine</span>
Previous reports have attempted to examine the effects of beta-adrenergic stimulation and antagonism in LQT3 syndrome (Shimizu & Antzelevitch 2000, Head et al. 2005). Administration of isoproterenol to a surrogate, pharmacological model of LQT3 syndrome in the canine ventricular wedge preparation reduces transmural repolarization gradients through preferential abbreviation of the M-cell APD and reduces the incidence of arrhythmogenesis (Shimizu & Antzelevitch 2000). Propranolol appears to antagonize this protective effect of isoproterenol and confers a higher degree of arrhythmogenesis in the wedge preparation (Shimizu & Antzelevitch 2000). These results suggest that beta-adrenoceptor stimulation may be antiarrhythmic in the setting of LQT3 syndrome. However, results from our previous study suggest otherwise: perfusion of LQT3 mice with identical isoproterenol concentrations used in the above study did not exert any antiarrhythmic effects (Head et al. 2005). In keeping with earlier results in the canine wedge ventricular preparation (Shimizu & Antzelevitch 2000) and clinical findings which suggest that LQT3 patients derive less benefit from beta-adrenoceptor blockade therapy than other LQTS patients (Priori et al. 2004), administration of propranolol did not exert an antiarrhythmic effect in the LQT3 murine heart (Head et al. 2005). These results are in keeping with the present study in which propranolol exerted proarrhythmic effects in LQT3 murine hearts through amplification of left ventricular transmural repolarization gradients.
###end p 67
###begin p 68
###xml 309 311 305 307 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">90</sub>
###xml 577 582 573 578 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">Scn5a</italic>
###xml 748 753 740 745 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">Scn5a</italic>
###xml 131 136 <span type="species:ncbi:10090">mouse</span>
###xml 146 151 <span type="species:ncbi:9606">human</span>
###xml 396 401 <span type="species:ncbi:9606">human</span>
In conclusion, we report, for the first time, the empirical separation of EADs from arrhythmic substrate in a genetically modified mouse model of human LQT3 syndrome. In this study we used propranolol at different concentrations as a pharmacological tool to separate EADs from arrhythmic substrate of DeltaAPD90 in an intact genetically modified whole heart model of arrhythmogenesis that models human LQT3 syndrome. Regardless of the underlying mechanism, we have demonstrated that low and high concentrations of propranolol reduced arrhythmogenecity in spontaneously beating Scn5a+/Delta hearts by removing the trigger for the arrhythmia, the EAD. However, high concentrations of propranolol exacerbated transmural gradients of repolarization in Scn5a+/Delta hearts, which resulted in a proarrhythmic effect unmasked in provoked arrhythmogenesis studies in which premature stimuli, acting as surrogate EADs, imposed upon an arrhythmic substrate.
###end p 68
###begin p 69
###xml 385 394 382 391 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="b30">Moss 1998</xref>
###xml 401 407 398 404 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">et al.</italic>
###xml 396 412 393 409 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="b31">Moss <italic>et al.</italic> 2000</xref>
###xml 427 433 424 430 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">et al.</italic>
###xml 414 438 411 435 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="b6">van den Berg <italic>et al.</italic> 2001</xref>
###xml 449 455 446 452 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">et al.</italic>
###xml 440 460 437 457 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="b40">Schwartz <italic>et al.</italic> 2001</xref>
###xml 469 475 466 472 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">et al.</italic>
###xml 462 480 459 477 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="b34">Priori <italic>et al.</italic> 2004</xref>
###xml 142 148 <span type="species:ncbi:10090">murine</span>
###xml 343 351 <span type="species:ncbi:9606">patients</span>
We also show, for the first time, that higher concentrations of propranolol can exert a proarrhythmic effect in a genetically modified intact murine model of LQT3 syndrome. These findings correspond with clinical observations in which beta-adrenoceptor blocker therapy has proved considerably less effective in reducing cardiac events in LQT3 patients than in the other LQTS subtypes (Moss 1998, Moss et al. 2000, van den Berg et al. 2001, Schwartz et al. 2001, Priori et al. 2004).
