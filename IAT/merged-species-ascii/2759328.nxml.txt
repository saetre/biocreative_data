###begin article-title 0
###xml 44 48 44 48 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">IRF5</italic>
Lack of association of two polymorphisms of IRF5 with Behcet's disease
###end article-title 0
###begin title 1
Purpose
###end title 1
###begin p 2
###xml 350 358 <span type="species:ncbi:9606">patients</span>
Interferon regulation factor 5 (IRF5) is a member of the IRF family of transcription factors that control the transactivation of type I interferon system-related genes as well as the expression of several other genes involved in immune response. Here, we investigated its association with Behcet's disease (BD) in a well defined group of Chinese Han patients.
###end p 2
###begin title 3
Methods
###end title 3
###begin p 4
###xml 98 102 98 102 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">IRF5</italic>
###xml 377 381 377 381 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">IRF5</italic>
###xml 466 467 464 465 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">2</sup>
###xml 33 41 <span type="species:ncbi:9606">patients</span>
###xml 423 431 <span type="species:ncbi:9606">patients</span>
A total of 152 unrelated Chinese patients with BD and 149 healthy blood donors were genotyped for IRF5 and  polymorphisms. Genomic DNA was isolated from peripheral blood mononuclear cells. Genotyping of each single nucleotide polymorphism (SNPs) was performed by polymerase chain reaction restriction fragment length polymorphism (PCR-RFLP). Allele and genotype frequencies of IRF5 and  polymorphisms were compared between patients and controls using a two-sided chi2 test.
###end p 4
###begin title 5
Results
###end title 5
###begin p 6
###xml 409 413 409 413 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">IRF5</italic>
###xml 118 126 <span type="species:ncbi:9606">patients</span>
###xml 259 267 <span type="species:ncbi:9606">patients</span>
###xml 436 444 <span type="species:ncbi:9606">patients</span>
The results showed no significant difference concerning the frequency of the allele of  and  polymorphisms between BD patients and the normal controls (p=0.647 and p=0.105, respectively). The frequencies of the genotype of  and  were not different between BD patients and the normal controls (p=0.233 and, p=0.266, respectively). Clinical manifestation stratification analysis did not show any association of IRF5 polymorphisms with BD patients (p>0.05).
###end p 6
###begin title 7
Conclusions
###end title 7
###begin p 8
###xml 148 152 148 152 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">IRF5</italic>
Our study revealed that the  and  SNPs were not associated with the susceptibility to BD. There was no association between the two polymorphisms of IRF5 and any extraocular clinical manifestations in BD.
###end p 8
###begin title 9
Introduction
###end title 9
###begin p 10
###xml 194 195 194 195 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="r1">1</xref>
###xml 289 290 289 290 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="r2">2</xref>
Behcet's disease (BD) is a chronic autoimmune disease in which both genetic and environmental factors are involved. It is a common uveitis entity in China, Japan, and the Middle East countries [1]. BD is an autoimmune disease that results from damage to blood vessels throughout the body [2]. However, what triggers this reaction is unclear.
###end p 10
###begin p 11
###xml 140 141 140 141 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="r3">3</xref>
###xml 280 281 280 281 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="r4">4</xref>
###xml 282 283 282 283 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="r7">7</xref>
###xml 317 321 317 321 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">IRF1</italic>
###xml 359 360 359 360 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="r8">8</xref>
###xml 370 374 370 374 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">IRF5</italic>
###xml 484 485 484 485 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="r9">9</xref>
###xml 486 488 486 488 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="r10">10</xref>
###xml 525 527 525 527 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="r11">11</xref>
###xml 557 559 557 559 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="r12">12</xref>
###xml 591 593 591 593 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="r13">13</xref>
###xml 631 635 631 635 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">IRF5</italic>
###xml 699 703 699 703 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">IRF5</italic>
The family of interferon regulatory factors (IRF) had been implicated in providing a principal basis for host resistance against pathogens [3]. The family also plays a role in the regulation of the development of the immune system and the production of pro-inflammatory cytokine [4-7]. Studies have demonstrated that IRF1 is associated with Behcet's disease [8] whereas IRF5 has been found to be associated with certain autoimmune diseases such as systemic lupus erythematosus (SLE) [9,10], inflammatory bowel disease (IBD) [11], rheumatoid arthritis (RA) [12], and multiple sclerosis (MS) [13]. Whether there is an association of IRF5 with BD is not yet clear. Here, we examined the association of IRF5 single polymorphisms ( and ) with BD in a Chinese Han population.
###end p 11
###begin title 12
Methods
###end title 12
###begin title 13
###xml 0 8 <span type="species:ncbi:9606">Patients</span>
Patients and normal controls
###end title 13
###begin p 14
###xml 103 105 103 105 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="r14">14</xref>
###xml 250 254 250 254 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">IRF5</italic>
###xml 33 41 <span type="species:ncbi:9606">patients</span>
###xml 209 217 <span type="species:ncbi:9606">patients</span>
A total of 152 unrelated Chinese patients with BD according to the international study group criteria [14] and 149 age-, sex-, and ethnically-matched healthy controls were included in this study. Samples from patients and controls were genotyped for IRF5 and  polymorphisms after they all gave their informed consents. All study subjects were recruited from Zhongshan Ophthalmic Center (Sun Yat-sen University, Guangzhou, P.R. China), the First Affiliated Hospital, Chongqing Medical University (Chongqing, P.R. China), and Daping Hospital (the Third Military Medical University, Chongqing, P.R. China). The study was approved by the local ethics committees of the Third Military Medical University and Chongqing Medical University.
###end p 14
###begin title 15
Genomic DNA extraction and PCR
###end title 15
###begin p 16
###xml 120 122 114 116 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="r15">15</xref>
###xml 365 367 359 361 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="r16">16</xref>
###xml 401 405 395 399 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">IRF5</italic>
###xml 689 696 683 690 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="table" rid="t1">Table 1</xref>
###xml 48 53 <span type="species:ncbi:42554">tetra</span>
Blood samples were collected in ethylenediamine tetra acetic acid (EDTA) tubes and kept at -70 degreesC until required [15]. Genomic DNA was extracted from peripheral blood mononuclear cells, which was performed by conventional salting of cellular protein, an alcohol precipitation using standard proteinase K digestion, and phenol chloroform extraction procedure [16]. We amplified the target DNA in IRF5 by polymerase chain reaction (PCR) using the relevant primers. The primers of the two remaining sites were designed using Primer Premier 5.0 software (Premier Biosoft International, Palo Alto, CA). The details of the primers and enzymes used for PCR-RFLP genotyping are presented in Table 1.
###end p 16
###begin title 17
The primers and enzymes used for PCR-RFLP genotyping.
###end title 17
###begin p 18
The details of the primers and enzymes used for PCR-RFLP genotyping.
###end p 18
###begin p 19
Each PCR was performed in 10 mul of solution containing 5 mul premix Taq (Takara Biotechnology Co. Ltd, Dalian, P.R. China), 10 pmol primers, and 0.2 mug of genomic DNA. The cycling profile was: initial denaturation at 94 degreesC for 4 min, 45 cycles consisting of 94 degreesC for 5 s, proper annealing temperature 63 degreesC for 30 s, 72 degreesC for 30 s, and a final extension for 4 min at 72 degreesC.
###end p 19
###begin title 20
Genotyping
###end title 20
###begin p 21
###xml 396 398 394 396 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">TM</sup>
Genotyping of each single nucleotide polymorphism (SNP) was performed by PCR restriction fragment length polymorphism (PCR-RFLP). PCR products of  and  were respectively digested with 2 mul of BSMFI, BCEAI restriction enzymes (Fermentas, MBI, Vilnius, Lithuania) in a 10 mul reaction volume overnight. Digestion products were visualized on agarose of 2%-3% concentration and stained with GoldviewTM (SBS Genetech, Beijing, P.R. China). BSMFI digestion of 130 bp PCR product of AA genotype gives two fragments, the GG gives one fragment, and the AG gives three fragments. BCEAI digestion of 120 bp PCR product of AA genotype gives two fragments, the GG gives one fragment, and the AG gives three fragments. To confirm the accuracy of the method employed, randomly selected subjects (20% of all samples) were analyzed by direct sequencing (Invitrogen Biotechnology Co., Guangzhou, P.R. China). An appropriate control was included in each typing run.
###end p 21
###begin title 22
Statistical analysis
###end title 22
###begin p 23
###xml 35 39 35 39 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">IRF5</italic>
###xml 283 284 281 282 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">2</sup>
###xml 81 89 <span type="species:ncbi:9606">patients</span>
Allele and genotype frequencies of IRF5 and  polymorphisms were compared between patients and controls. Statistical analysis was performed with the SPSS version 10.0 for Windows (SPSS Inc., Chicago, IL). We evaluated the frequency of genotypes and alleles in this study using the chi2 test. The association of  and  with the extraocular findings was evaluated by logistic regression and statistical significance was taken when p<0.05.

###begin title 31
Discussion
###end title 31
###begin p 32
###xml 108 110 108 110 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="r17">17</xref>
###xml 163 167 163 167 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">IRF5</italic>
###xml 332 336 332 336 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">IRF5</italic>
It has been demonstrated that both genetic and environmental factors are involved in the development of BD [17]. In this study, we investigated the association of IRF5 polymorphisms with BD in the Chinese Han population. Our study showed that  and  were not associated with BD. There was no association between the polymorphisms of IRF5 with any extraocular clinical manifestations.
###end p 32
###begin p 33
###xml 140 142 140 142 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="r18">18</xref>
###xml 143 145 143 145 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="r19">19</xref>
###xml 196 200 196 200 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">IRF5</italic>
###xml 308 310 308 310 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="r10">10</xref>
###xml 311 313 311 313 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="r11">11</xref>
###xml 314 316 314 316 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="r20">20</xref>
###xml 317 319 317 319 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="r21">21</xref>
###xml 386 388 386 388 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="r22">22</xref>
###xml 657 661 657 661 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">IRF5</italic>
###xml 560 568 <span type="species:ncbi:9606">patients</span>
###xml 685 693 <span type="species:ncbi:9606">patients</span>
###xml 813 821 <span type="species:ncbi:9606">patients</span>
BD is a common multisystem inflammation disorder characterized by recurrent oral and genital ulceration, multiple skin lesions and uveitis [18,19]. It has a distinctive geographical distribution. IRF5 has been shown to be strongly associated with certain autoimmune diseases in different ethnic populations [10,11,20,21] and to play a critical role in the pathogenesis of autoimmunity [22]. Therefore, this gene has been considered as a general susceptible gene for autoimmune diseases. However, the genetic factors associated with this disease in Chinese Han patients have been scarcely studied. In this study, we focused on the association of two SNPs of IRF5 with BD in the Chinese patients. As population stratification may lead to a false-positive result in a case-control study, we strictly selected the BD patients and controls with Chinese Han nationality. To validate the result of genotyping by PCR-RFLP, 20% of the samples were directly sequenced.
###end p 33
###begin p 34
###xml 309 311 309 311 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="r10">10</xref>
###xml 312 314 312 314 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="r12">12</xref>
###xml 315 317 315 317 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="r20">20</xref>
###xml 318 320 318 320 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="r21">21</xref>
###xml 462 463 462 463 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="r2">2</xref>
###xml 464 466 464 466 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="r18">18</xref>
###xml 467 469 467 469 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="r19">19</xref>
###xml 598 599 598 599 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="r2">2</xref>
###xml 600 602 600 602 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="r19">19</xref>
###xml 645 649 645 649 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">IRF5</italic>
###xml 520 526 <span type="species:ncbi:9606">people</span>
Unfortunately, in this study, we did not find any association of  and  SNPs with BD. A similar result was observed after stratification analysis based on extraocular features. These results are not consistent with those seen in other autoimmune diseases reported in European and some other Asian populations [10,12,20,21]. This could be explained by the fact that the etiology and pathogenesis of BD may be different from those of the other autoimmune diseases [2,18,19]. The other possibility could be that Chinese Han people may have a different genetic background from other ethnic populations [2,19]. Additionally, association of some other IRF5 SNPs should be tested in BD.
###end p 34
###begin p 35
###xml 43 47 43 47 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">IRF1</italic>
###xml 95 96 95 96 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="r8">8</xref>
###xml 142 146 142 146 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">IRF5</italic>
Lee and coworkers found the association of IRF1 polymorphism with Behcet's disease in Koreans [8]. Here, we failed to find the association of IRF5 with BD. The inconsistent result can be explained by the different immune function of IRF1 from IRF5. Both of them interact with MyD88, an adaptor for multiple innate immune receptors, but induce a distinctively different set of pro-inflammatory cytokines. These results suggest that IRF1 and IRF5 play different roles in the production of pro-inflammatory cytokine and the regulation of the development immune system, that is to say, different mechanisms may be induced by IRF1 than by IRF5 in the development of BD disease.
###end p 35
###begin p 36
###xml 163 167 163 167 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">IRF5</italic>
###xml 277 281 277 281 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">IRF5</italic>
In conclusion, our study revealed that the  and  SNPs were not associated with the susceptibility to BD. There was no association between the two polymorphisms of IRF5 with any clinical manifestations. Further studies are needed to investigate the association of other SNPs in IRF5 with BD.
