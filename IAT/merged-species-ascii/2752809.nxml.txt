###begin article-title 0
Catechol-O-Methyltransferase Expression and 2-Methoxyestradiol Affect Microtubule Dynamics and Modify Steroid Receptor Signaling in Leiomyoma Cells
###end article-title 0
###begin p 1
Conceived and designed the experiments: SAS SMS GSK CRDA. Performed the experiments: SAS MWK MAB MS MYW. Analyzed the data: SAS SKB SMS AAH MYW CRDA. Contributed reagents/materials/analysis tools: SAS MS CRDA. Wrote the paper: SAS SKB SMS MAB AAH GSK MYW CRDA.
###end p 1
###begin p 2
Current address: Department of Obstetrics & Gynecology, Division of Gynecologic Oncology, Baylor College of Medicine, Houston, Texas
###end p 2
###begin title 3
Context
###end title 3
###begin p 4
###xml 382 387 <span type="species:ncbi:9606">human</span>
Development of optimal medicinal treatments of uterine leiomyomas represents a significant challenge. 2-Methoxyestradiol (2ME) is an endogenous estrogen metabolite formed by sequential action of CYP450s and catechol-O-methyltransferase (COMT). Our previous study demonstrated that 2ME is a potent antiproliferative, proapoptotic, antiangiogenic, and collagen synthesis inhibitor in human leiomyomas cells (huLM).
###end p 4
###begin title 5
Objectives
###end title 5
###begin p 6
Our objectives were to investigate whether COMT expression, by the virtue of 2ME formation, affects the growth of huLM, and to explore the cellular and molecular mechanisms whereby COMT expression or treatment with 2ME affect these cells.
###end p 6
###begin title 7
Results
###end title 7
###begin p 8
###xml 28 29 28 29 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">2</sub>
Our data demonstrated that E2-induced proliferation was less pronounced in cells over-expressing COMT or treated with 2ME (500 nM). This effect on cell proliferation was associated with microtubules stabilization and diminution of estrogen receptor alpha (ERalpha) and progesterone receptor (PR) transcriptional activities, due to shifts in their subcellular localization and sequestration in the cytoplasm. In addition, COMT over expression or treatment with 2ME reduced the expression of hypoxia-inducible factor -1alpha (HIF-1 alpha) and the basal level as well as TNF-alpha-induced aromatase (CYP19) expression.
###end p 8
###begin title 9
Conclusions
###end title 9
###begin p 10
###xml 80 81 80 81 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">2</sub>
###xml 188 193 <span type="species:ncbi:9606">human</span>
COMT over expression or treatment with 2ME stabilize microtubules, ameliorates E2-induced proliferation, inhibits ERalpha and PR signaling, and reduces HIF-1 alpha and CYP19 expression in human uterine leiomyoma cells. Thus, microtubules are a candidate target for treatment of uterine leiomyomas. In addition, the naturally occurring microtubule-targeting agent 2ME represents a potential new therapeutic for uterine leiomyomas.
###end p 10
###begin title 11
Introduction
###end title 11
###begin p 12
###xml 273 276 273 276 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0007356-Hoekstra1">[1]</xref>
###xml 463 466 463 466 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0007356-Farquhar1">[2]</xref>
###xml 588 591 588 591 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0007356-Murray1">[3]</xref>
###xml 663 666 663 666 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0007356-AlHendy1">[4]</xref>
###xml 87 92 <span type="species:ncbi:9606">women</span>
###xml 183 188 <span type="species:ncbi:9606">women</span>
###xml 218 223 <span type="species:ncbi:9606">women</span>
Uterine leiomyomas are the most common benign gynecological tumors in reproductive age women. It is estimated that the incidence of uterine leiomyomas is over 80% in African-American women by age 50, whereas Caucasian women have an incidence of almost 70% at a similar age [1]. Although uterine leiomyomas are benign tumors, they have a tremendous medical and economical impact. Uterine leiomyomas are the leading indication for hysterectomy in the United States [2]. Myomectomy and uterine artery embolization are also common treatments; however, hysterectomy may be eventually required [3]. To date, medical treatments for leiomyomas are limited and suboptimal [4].
###end p 12
###begin p 13
###xml 382 385 382 385 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0007356-Okolo1">[5]</xref>
###xml 415 418 415 418 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0007356-Ishikawa1">[6]</xref>
###xml 723 726 719 722 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0007356-Englund1">[7]</xref>
###xml 728 731 724 727 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0007356-Nisolle1">[8]</xref>
The development of medical treatment for uterine leiomyomas is hampered by the fact that molecular mechanisms underlying the development and progression of leiomyomas are elusive. Substantial experimental and clinical evidence indicates that steroid hormones (estrogens and progesterone) and their cognate receptors are important etiologic factors in the pathogenesis of leiomyomas [5]. Recently, Ishikawa H, et al [6] reported that estrogen receptor subtype alpha (ERalpha) mRNA levels were 1.8 to 2.6-fold higher in uterine leiomyomas compared with adjacent myometrium. Similarly, progesterone receptors A and B (PRA & PRB) are expressed at significantly higher levels in uterine leiomyomas compared to normal myometrium [7], [8].
###end p 13
###begin p 14
###xml 125 128 125 128 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0007356-Manavathi1">[9]</xref>
###xml 309 313 302 306 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0007356-Jordan1">[10]</xref>
###xml 315 319 308 312 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0007356-Mitchison1">[11]</xref>
###xml 435 439 428 432 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0007356-Nogales1">[12]</xref>
###xml 569 573 562 566 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0007356-Lee1">[13]</xref>
###xml 713 717 695 699 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0007356-Azuma1">[14]</xref>
###xml 881 884 855 858 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0007356-Manavathi1">[9]</xref>
The transcriptional activities of estrogen and progesterone receptors are inherently regulated by microtubules (MT) dynamics [9]. MT are highly dynamic polymers comprised of alpha/beta-tubulin heterodimers and their biological functions are largely regulated by their polymerization/depolymerization dynamics [10], [11]. By providing scaffolding, sequestering, and delivery functions, MT regulates nuclear receptors signaling pathways [12]. MT tether steroid receptors and regulate their nuclear translocation as well as the translocation of their signaling components [13]. It has been reported that activation function 1 (AF1) domain of ERalpha binds to alpha- and beta-tubulins in MCF-7 breast carcinoma cells [14]. Indeed, it has been reported that destabilization of MT activates ERalpha transcriptional activity, whereas stabilization of MT represses ERalpha transactivation [9].
###end p 14
###begin p 15
###xml 62 63 59 60 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">2</sub>
###xml 207 211 204 208 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0007356-Salama1">[15]</xref>
###xml 280 284 277 281 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0007356-LaVallee1">[16]</xref>
###xml 185 190 <span type="species:ncbi:9606">human</span>
###xml 426 431 <span type="species:ncbi:9606">human</span>
2-Methoxyestradiol (2ME) is an endogenous 17 beta-estradiol (E2) metabolite with antimitotic, antiangiogenic, and proapoptotic effects. It is also an inhibitor of collagen synthesis in human leiomyoma cells [15] and has been described as MT targeting agent in experimental models [16]. In this study, we tested the hypothesis that 2ME or COMT gene over expression, by the virtue of 2ME formation, inhibit the proliferation of human leiomyoma cells by affecting MT dynamic which in turn regulate the nuclear receptors signaling pathways. Our results suggest that 2ME, a naturally-occurring MTA, is a promising and safe medical treatment of uterine leiomyomas.
###end p 15
###begin title 16
Materials and Methods
###end title 16
###begin title 17
Cell culture and establishing huLM cells expressing different levels of COMT
###end title 17
###begin p 18
###xml 466 467 466 467 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">W</sup>
###xml 517 519 517 519 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">KI</sup>
###xml 555 557 555 557 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">KD</sup>
###xml 582 584 582 584 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">KI</sup>
###xml 1072 1074 1068 1070 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">KD</sup>
###xml 1130 1131 1126 1127 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">W</sup>
###xml 1155 1159 1151 1155 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0007356-Salama2">[17]</xref>
###xml 1224 1228 1220 1224 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0007356-Carney1">[18]</xref>
###xml 1230 1234 1226 1230 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0007356-Salama1">[15]</xref>
###xml 1286 1287 1282 1283 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">2</sub>
###xml 17 22 <span type="species:ncbi:9606">human</span>
###xml 673 678 <span type="species:ncbi:9606">human</span>
The immortalized human uterine leiomyoma cell line (huLM), which expresses both estrogen receptors (ERs) and progesterone receptors (PRs), was a gift from Dr. Darlene Dixon (National Institute of Environmental Health Sciences, Research Triangle Park, NC). To evaluate the functional significance of COMT gene expression on estrogen or progesterone and their respective receptors signaling in huLM cells, we generated stable subcell-lines from the parental huLM (huLMW) cells that either overexpressing COMT (huLM-COMTKI) or underexpressing COMT (huLM-COMTKD). To establish huLM-COMTKI cells, the huLM were stably transfected with the pcDNA 3.1 (FLAG) vector expressing the human membrane-bound isoform of COMT cDNA using Fugene 6 Transfection Reagent (Roche Applied Science, Indianapolis, IN) according to the manufacturer's protocol. Stable clones were selected in the presence of 1 microg/ml of puromycin. Colonies resistant to puromycin appeared within 2 weeks, and the cells were then expanded for another 3 weeks to make the original stock cells. Similarly, huLM-COMTKD cells were generated by stable transfection of the huLMW as we described before [17]. The cells were cultured and maintained as previously described [18], [15]. For hypoxic exposure, cells were treated with CoCl2 (100 microM) for 8 hours.
###end p 18

###xml 15 16 15 16 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">2</sub>

###xml 390 392 390 392 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">&#8722;8</sup>
###xml 399 400 399 400 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">2</sub>
###xml 578 582 578 582 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0007356-Salama2">[17]</xref>
###xml 103 108 <span type="species:ncbi:9606">human</span>
We studied the effect of COMT expression or treatment with 2ME (500 nM) on E2-induced proliferation of human leiomyoma cells. huLMW, huLM-COMTKD, EM-COMTKI, or huLM cells treated with 500 nM of (huLM2ME) were plated in triplicate in 96-well microplates at a density of 1x104 cell/well in phenol-red free media supplemented with 10% charcoal-stripped FCS. The cells were then treated with 10-8 M of E2. After 24, 48, 72 and 96 hr, the cell numbers were determined using colorimetric (3-[4,5-dimethylthiazol-2-yle] 2,5-diphenyltetrazolium bromide) (MTT) assay as described before [17].

Transient transfection and luciferase reporter gene assay

###begin p 24
###xml 225 229 225 229 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0007356-Hassan1">[19]</xref>

###xml 800 802 796 798 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">&#8722;8</sup>
###xml 806 807 802 803 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">2</sub>
###xml 1037 1041 1033 1037 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0007356-Salama2">[17]</xref>
The effect of COMT gene expression or treatment with 2ME (500 nM) on the estrogen receptor signaling in huLM was determined using an estrogen-responsive reporter in the adenoviral vector (Ad-ERE-luc), as described previously [19]. Similarly, progesterone receptor signaling was assessed by transfecting cells with 1 microg of the progesterone-responsive reporter plasmid pPRE/GRE.E1b.Luc (kindly provided by Dr. Ming-Jer Tsai, Baylor College of Medicine, Houston, TX) using Fugene 6 transfection reagents as described before [17]. Briefly, huLMW, huLM-COMTKI, huLM-COMTKD, or huLM 2ME were grown in charcoal-stripped, phenol red-free medium for 2 days. The cells were then transfected with Ad-ERE-Luc (50 PFU/cell) or pPRE/GRE.E1b.Luc. The next day, media were replaced with fresh media containing 10-8 M E2 (for estrogen receptor signaling) or 100 nM of progesterone (P4) (for progesterone receptor signaling). Forty-eight hours later, luciferase activities were determined using luciferase enzyme assay systems as previously described [17]. The luciferase activity was normalized against protein concentrations.

###begin title 27
Microtubule assay
###end title 27
###begin p 28
###xml 145 146 145 146 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">W</sup>
###xml 157 159 157 159 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">KD</sup>
###xml 170 172 170 172 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">KI</sup>
###xml 181 184 181 184 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">2ME</sup>
###xml 887 888 883 884 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">2</sub>
###xml 934 935 926 927 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">2</sub>
###xml 981 985 973 977 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0007356-ReiterFunk1">[20]</xref>
To assess the effect of COMT expression or 2ME (500 nM) on the microtubule dynamics in huLM, we measured the free and polymerized tubulin in huLMW, huLM-COMTKD, huLM-COMTKI, or huLM2ME using microtubules/tubulin assay kit (Cytoskeleton, Inc, Denver, CO), according to the manufacturer's instruction. Briefly, cells were homogenized via syringe trituration and incubated for 10 minutes in lysis buffer. A volume of 10 microL of cell homogenates was saved for protein measurement using the Bradford Assay. Homogenized cells were then centrifuged at 100,000xg for 30 minutes to separate microtubules from free-tubulin. The polymerized microtubules settle in the pellet, while the free-tubulin remains in the supernatant. Following centrifugation, the supernatant (free-tubulin) was removed and frozen until Western blot analysis. The pellet was resuspended in ice-cold water containing CaCl2 (200 microM) and incubated for one hour. CaCl2 acts to enhance microtubule depolymerization [20]. Thus, the microtubules remaining in the pellet were depolymerized to free-tubulin. The samples were then centrifuged at 14,000xg (4degreesC) for 10 minutes. The supernatant (containing free-tubulin representing the original microtubules) was collected and frozen. Tubulin concentrations in both fractions were measured using Western blotting

###begin title 29
Immunofluorescence and Confocal Microscopy
###end title 29
###begin p 30
###xml 4 5 4 5 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">W</sup>
###xml 16 18 16 18 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">KI</sup>
###xml 24 27 24 27 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">2ME</sup>
###xml 41 43 41 43 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">KD</sup>
###xml 102 103 102 103 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">2</sub>
###xml 539 544 <span type="species:ncbi:10090">mouse</span>
huLMW, huLM-COMTKI, huLM2ME, or huLM-COMTKD were grown on glass cover slips. Cells were treated with E2 (10 nM) or P4 (100 nM) for 30 min and then fixed in 4% formaldehyde, permeabilized with 0.2% Triton X-100 for 10 minutes, and incubated in 10% BSA/PBS for 1 hour to block nonspecific protein-binding sites. Subsequently, cells were incubated overnight at 4degreesC with primary antibodies against ERalpha (1:100 Ab-10, Thermo Fisher Scientific Inc. Fremont, CA) or PR (1:100 Ab-7 Thermo Fisher Scientific Inc. Fremont, CA), followed by mouse fluorescein isothiocyanate-labeled secondary antibodies (Jackson ImmunoResearch Laboratories, Inc. PA). ###begin title 47

###begin p 48

To understand how COMT/2ME affects proliferation and steroid receptors signaling in huLM, we assessed the total level and the subcellular localization of ERalpha and PRs in huLMW, huLM2ME huLM-COMTKI and huLM-COMTKD. Western blot analysis revealed that there was no significant difference in the total amount of ERalpha in huLMW, huLM2ME, huLM-COMTKI and huLM-COMTKD (Figure 5). However, our data revealed that COMT/2ME alters the subcellular localization of ERalpha. In huLMW or huLM-COMTKD cells treated with E2 (10 nM for 30 min), the vast majority of ERalpha was localized in the nuclear fraction, with minimal amount retained in the cytoplasmic fraction (Figure 5A). On the other hand, in huLM-COMTKI and huLM2ME treated with E2, the ERalpha in the nuclear fraction was reduced with parallel increase in ERalpha retained in the cytoplasmic fraction (Figure 5A). Thus, COMT/2ME reduces nuclear shuttling of ERalpha without any significant change in the expression level of total ERalpha. Similarly, we assessed the effect of COMT/2ME on the total level and the subcellular localization of PRs. As indicated in figure [6], COMT over expression or treatment with 2ME resulted in an apparent decrease in the total PRs level. This decrease in total PR level was associated with, a decrease in PR nuclear translocation, and increased retention PRs in the cytoplasmic fraction upon treatment with P4 (100 nM, for 30 min) (Figure 6). Thus, COMT/2ME decreases PRs expression and reduces their nuclear translocation as well. The effect of COMT/2ME on the subcellular localization of ERalpha and PRs was also confirmed by confocal microscopy (figures 5B and 6B).

###xml 535 537 519 521 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">KI</sup>

The activity of hypoxia inducible factor 1 alpha (HIF-1alpha), a hallmark of hypoxia, is blocked by 2ME via disruption of MT dynamics [22]. Accordingly, we investigated the effect of COMT/2ME on the basal as well as hypoxia-induced expression of HIF-1alpha in huLM. Our data demonstrated that HIF-1alpha is constitutively expressed in huLM under normoxic condition and is further increased by CoCl2-induced hypoxia (Figure 7). Interestingly, the basal level and hypoxia-induced HIF-1alpha are significantly low in huLM2ME and huLM-COMTKI compared with huLMW (Figure 7).

###begin title 67
COMT/2ME antagonizes TNF-alpha-induced aromatase (CYP19) expression in huLM
###end title 67
###begin p 68
###xml 163 167 159 163 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0007356-Purohit1">[23]</xref>
###xml 386 388 378 380 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">KI</sup>
###xml 397 400 389 392 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">2ME</sup>
###xml 463 464 455 456 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">W</sup>
###xml 473 474 465 466 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">W</sup>
###xml 566 569 554 557 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">2ME</sup>
###xml 583 585 571 573 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">KI</sup>
###xml 692 700 676 684 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="fig" rid="pone-0007356-g008">Figure 8</xref>
Experimental evidence suggests that microtubule stabilizers, including 2ME, inhibit the basal and TNF-alpha-induced CYP19 expression in breast stromal fibroblasts [23]. Thus, we investigated the effect of COMT/2ME on CYP19 expression in huLM. Our data demonstrated that COMT/2ME reduces both basal as well as TNF-alpha-induced CYP19 expression. CYP19 basal expression level in huLM-COMTKI and huLM2ME was reduced by 50% and 70%, respectively, compared to the huLMW. In huLMW, TNF-alpha (20 ng/ml) increases CYP19 expression by 190% from basal level. However, in huLM2ME and huLM-COMTKI, TNF-alpha increases CYP19 expression only by 50% and 40%, respectively, from corresponding basal levels (Figure 8).
###end p 68

###begin title 71
Discussion
###end title 71
###begin p 72
###xml 200 204 200 204 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0007356-Salama1">[15]</xref>
###xml 470 471 470 471 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">2</sub>
###xml 1022 1023 1014 1015 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">W</sup>
###xml 1073 1077 1065 1069 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0007356-Salama1">[15]</xref>
###xml 46 51 <span type="species:ncbi:9606">human</span>
Our previous findings showed that 2ME targets human uterine leiomyoma cells through multiple mechanisms, including its effects on proliferation, angiogenesis, apoptosis, as well as collagen synthesis [15]. The current study was undertaken to investigate the molecular mechanisms underlying these effects. We have further examined whether manipulation of COMT expression, by the virtue of 2ME formation, can affect leiomyoma cells. Our data show that 2ME (500 nM) halts E2-induced huLM proliferation, promotes microtubules polymerization, attenuates nuclear receptors signaling, antagonizes TNF-alpha-induced CYP19 expression, and decreases HIF-1alpha protein levels. Interestingly, our results suggest that COMT overexpression produces effects similar to 2ME treatment. Although COMT is constitutively expressed in huLM cells, this expression level and the resultant concentration of 2ME is lower than the minimum concentration that can affect proliferation of these cells. We previously demonstrated that 2ME affects huLMW proliferation at concentrations more than 200 nM [15]. Based on results from measuring 2ME level in huLM and other cell lines, the concentration of 2ME which can inhibit cell proliferation or affect the cytoskeleton is not attainable under the normal expression level of COMT. This finding may indicate COMT/2ME represents an integral system, with 2ME the proximal effector that regulates uterine leiomyomas growth.
###end p 72
###begin p 73
###xml 335 339 331 335 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0007356-Stewart1">[24]</xref>
###xml 390 393 386 389 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0007356-Manavathi1">[9]</xref>
###xml 395 399 391 395 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0007356-Vrzal1">[25]</xref>
Our study suggests that the effects of COMT/2ME on huLM are mediated by disturbing microtubule dynamics. Microtubules provide structural support and modulate signal transduction of several molecules involved in uterine leiomyomas development. For instance, ERalpha and PRs signaling, which are crucial for growth of uterine leiomyomas [24], are inherently regulated by microtubule dynamics [9], [25].
###end p 73
###begin p 74
###xml 130 134 130 134 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0007356-Nogales1">[12]</xref>
###xml 283 287 283 287 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0007356-Lee1">[13]</xref>
###xml 289 293 289 293 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0007356-Lombardi1">[26]</xref>
By providing scaffolding, sequestering, and delivery functions, microtubules are involved in nuclear receptors signaling pathways [12]. In addition, microtubules tether steroid receptors and regulate their nuclear shuttling as well as the translocation of their signaling components [13], [26]. Thus, COMT/2ME-induced microtubule stabilization leads to cytoplasmic retention and nuclear compartmentalization impairment of ERalpha and PRs, and consequently leiomyoma cells growth arrest.
###end p 74
###begin p 75
Interestingly, COMT/2ME exerts a dual effect on PRs as it inhibits both total protein expression and nuclear localization. The effect of COMT/2ME on PRs nuclear localization could be explained by microtubule stabilization. Meanwhile, reduction in total PRs expression may be a consequence of attenuation ERalpha signaling, which is a key regulator of PRs expression.
###end p 75
###begin p 76
###xml 276 280 272 276 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0007356-Sumitani1">[27]</xref>
###xml 410 414 406 410 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0007356-Imir1">[28]</xref>
###xml 416 420 412 416 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0007356-Bulun1">[29]</xref>
###xml 637 641 629 633 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0007356-Purohit1">[23]</xref>
###xml 797 798 789 790 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">2</sub>
###xml 854 857 846 849 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0007356-Ishikawa1">[6]</xref>
###xml 960 961 952 953 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">2</sub>
###xml 1002 1006 994 998 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0007356-Purohit1">[23]</xref>
###xml 1152 1156 1136 1140 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0007356-Salama3">[30]</xref>
###xml 1243 1247 1223 1227 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0007356-Mikenberg1">[31]</xref>
###xml 1303 1307 1279 1283 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0007356-Shrum1">[32]</xref>
In addition to the effect on steroid receptors signaling, our data demonstrated that COMT/2ME reduces both basal and TNF-alpha-induced CYP19 expression. CYP19 expression promotes the development and progression of uterine leiomyomas by mediating in situ estrogen biosynthesis [27]. Indeed, it has been established that CYP19 expression is strikingly higher in uterine leiomyoma compared to adjacent myometrium [28], [29]. The effect of COMT/2ME on CYP19 expression is consistent with a previous report that microtubule stabilizers, including 2ME, inhibits basal as well as TNF-alpha-induced CYP19 expression in estrogen responsive cells [23]. The ability of COMT/2ME to reduce basal (i.e. unstimulated) CYP19 expression may result from blocking the autocrine/paracrine actions of cytokines and PGE2, which are known inducers of CYP19 in leiomyomas cells [6]. In fact, it has been reported that 2ME, through microtubule stabilization, inhibits cytokines and PGE2-induced CYP19 expression in fibroblasts [23]. TNF-alpha-induced CYP19 expression in huLM is consistent with our previous finding that TNF-alpha induces CYP19 expression in endometrial cells [30]. It has been reported that microtubules play a role in nuclear transport of NF-kappaB [31], as well as NF-kappaB-dependent transcription activity [32]. It is likely, therefore, that COMT/2ME-induced microtubule stabilization inhibits the nuclear translocation of NF-kappaB in huLM and therefore, CYP19 expression.
###end p 76
###begin p 77
###xml 313 317 309 313 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0007356-Moon1">[33]</xref>
###xml 472 476 468 472 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0007356-Ding1">[34]</xref>
###xml 478 482 474 478 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0007356-Yu1">[35]</xref>
###xml 484 488 480 484 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0007356-Sourla1">[36]</xref>
###xml 490 494 486 490 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0007356-Hwu1">[37]</xref>
###xml 618 619 610 611 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">2</sub>
###xml 1011 1015 991 995 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0007356-Escuin1">[38]</xref>
Another critical factor for the uterine leiomyomas growth is the transcription factor HIF-1alpha which regulates the expression of growth factors and profibrotic mediators, such as platelet-derived growth factor, fibroblasts growth factor-2, plasminogen activator inhibitor-1, and connective tissue growth factor [33]. The products of these genes are known to influence cell proliferation and extracellular matrix turnover which are critical processes in leiomyoma growth [34], [35], [36], [37]. Our data demonstrated that huLM cells express a constitutive level of HIF-1alpha, which is significantly increased by CoCl2-induced hypoxia. Intriguingly, COMT/2ME inhibited both basal and hypoxia-induced HIF-1alpha expression in huLM. The effect of COMT/2ME on HIF-1 alpha is probably a consequence of microtubule stabilization. Indeed, it has been reported that disruption of microtubule dynamics, both by microtubule-stabilizing and microtubule-destabilizing agents, inhibits HIF-1 alpha expression and activity [38].
###end p 77
###begin p 78
###xml 455 460 <span type="species:ncbi:9606">human</span>
Thus, COMT/2ME exerts several therapeutic effects on huLM. It inhibits steroid nuclear receptor signaling, reduces aromatase gene expression, and down-regulates HIF-1 alpha expression. These pleiotropic therapeutic effects of COMT/2ME on huLM suggest that microtubule targeting agents could represent a potential new therapeutic for uterine leiomyomas. Considering the fact that it is an orally-active natural metabolite, with excellent safety profile in human, 2ME represents a potential medicinal treatment for uterine leiomyomas. Pre-clinical studies to support its use in uterine leiomyomas can be initiated
