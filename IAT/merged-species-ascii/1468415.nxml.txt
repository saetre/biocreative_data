###begin article-title 0
The interferon gamma gene polymorphism +874 A/T is associated with severe acute respiratory syndrome
###end article-title 0
###begin p 1
This is an Open Access article distributed under the terms of the Creative Commons Attribution License (), which permits unrestricted use, distribution, and reproduction in any medium, provided the original work is properly cited.
###end p 1
###begin title 2
Background
###end title 2
###begin p 3
###xml 89 98 89 94 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">IFN-&#947;</italic>
###xml 99 109 95 101 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">TNF-&#945; </italic>
###xml 113 119 105 111 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">IL-10 </italic>
Cytokines play important roles in antiviral action. We examined whether polymorphisms of IFN-gamma,TNF-alpha and IL-10 affect the susceptibility to and outcome of severe acute respiratory syndrome (SARS).
###end p 3
###begin title 4
Methods
###end title 4
###begin p 5
###xml 123 132 123 128 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">IFN-&#947;</italic>
###xml 133 143 129 135 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">TNF-&#945; </italic>
###xml 147 153 139 145 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">IL-10 </italic>
###xml 57 65 <span type="species:ncbi:9606">patients</span>
A case-control study was carried out in 476 Chinese SARS patients and 449 healthy controls. We tested the polymorphisms of IFN-gamma,TNF-alpha and IL-10 for their associations with SARS.
###end p 5
###begin title 6
Results
###end title 6
###begin p 7
###xml 0 10 0 6 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">IFN-&#947; </italic>
###xml 94 96 90 92 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">P </italic>
###xml 123 133 119 125 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">IFN-&#947; </italic>
###xml 318 324 310 316 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">IL-10 </italic>
###xml 328 338 320 326 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">TNF-&#945; </italic>
IFN-gamma +874A allele was associated with susceptibility to SARS in a dose-dependent manner (P < 0.001). Individuals with IFN-gamma +874 AA and AT genotype had a 5.19-fold (95% Confidence Interval [CI], 2.78-9.68) and 2.57-fold (95% CI, 1.35-4.88) increased risk of developing SARS respectively. The polymorphisms of IL-10 and TNF-alpha were not associated with SARS susceptibility.
###end p 7
###begin title 8
Conclusion
###end title 8
###begin p 9
###xml 0 10 0 6 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">IFN-&#947; </italic>
IFN-gamma +874A allele was shown to be a risk factor in SARS susceptibility.
###end p 9
###begin title 10
Background
###end title 10
###begin p 11
###xml 94 95 94 95 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="B1">1</xref>
###xml 147 148 147 148 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="B2">2</xref>
###xml 252 253 252 253 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="B3">3</xref>
###xml 371 372 371 372 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="B4">4</xref>
###xml 373 374 373 374 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="B6">6</xref>
###xml 572 573 572 573 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="B7">7</xref>
###xml 618 619 618 619 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="B8">8</xref>
###xml 648 655 648 655 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">CLEC4M </italic>
###xml 737 738 737 738 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="B9">9</xref>
###xml 76 92 <span type="species:ncbi:227859">SARS coronavirus</span>
###xml 196 212 <span type="species:ncbi:227859">SARS coronavirus</span>
Severe acute respiratory syndrome (SARS) is an infectious disease caused by SARS coronavirus [1] with >8000 cases and 774 deaths reported in 2003 [2]. Much progress has been made in understanding SARS coronavirus but the pathogenesis is still unclear [3]. It was reported that old age, diabetes mellitus and heart disease were risk factors for adverse prognosis of SARS [4-6], however, little is known about the contribution of genetic factors. We have demonstrated that genetic haplotypes associated with low serum mannose-binding lectin (MBL) were associated with SARS [7] and our findings were recently replicated [8]. Recently, homozygotes for CLEC4M tandem repeats were reported to be less susceptible to SARS in Hong Kong Chinese [9].
###end p 11
###begin p 12
###xml 374 376 366 368 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="B10">10</xref>
###xml 532 534 520 522 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="B11">11</xref>
###xml 535 537 523 525 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="B13">13</xref>
Cytokines are known to be important in antiviral action. Interferon (IFN)-gamma from T and natural killer (NK) cells is important in driving the T helper cell type 1 (Th1) responses. It also activates monocytes and macrophages, which in turn take part in antiviral responses by producing free radicals and pro-inflammatory cytokines like tumor necrosis factor (TNF)-alpha. [10]. TNF-alpha then regulates expression of neutrophil-endothelial cell adhesion molecules and chemokines, which recruit leukocytes to the site of infection [11-13]. Thus, IFN-gammaand TNF-alpha play important role in antiviral response and inflammation.
###end p 12
###begin p 13
###xml 148 150 148 150 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="B14">14</xref>
###xml 309 311 309 311 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="B15">15</xref>
###xml 488 490 484 486 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="B16">16</xref>
###xml 762 764 758 760 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="B17">17</xref>
###xml 765 767 761 763 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="B19">19</xref>
Interleukin 10 (IL-10) is an antiinflammatory cytokine that inhibits the activation and effector function of Th1 cells, monocytes, and macrophages [14]. IL-10 appears to limit and ultimately terminate inflammatory responses by blocking the expression of a number of pro-inflammatory cytokines and chemokines [15]. In animal model, IL10 counteracts the inflammatory response by inhibiting TNF-alpha production and neutrophil activation, and leads to a reduction of the lung tissue injury [16]. Thus, IL-10 plays an important role in regulating many immune and inflammatory processes. Various studies showed that a high IL-10 level would result in suppression of innate host defense and lead to increasing susceptibility of the host to various microbes and death [17-19].
###end p 13
###begin p 14
###xml 82 92 82 88 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">IFN-&#947; </italic>
###xml 101 111 97 103 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">TNF-&#945; </italic>
###xml 120 126 112 118 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">IL-10 </italic>
###xml 406 407 398 399 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="table" rid="T1">1</xref>
###xml 512 522 504 510 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">IFN-&#947; </italic>
###xml 447 455 <span type="species:ncbi:9606">patients</span>
In this study, we hypothesized that the polymorphisms of the cytokine genes, i.e. IFN-gamma +874A/T, TNF-alpha -308G/A, IL-10 -1082G/A and -592A/C, might be associated with SARS. These genes were chosen based on their functions in antiviral response and inflammation regulation that may be involved in SARS pathogenesis and their polymorphisms based on their potential regulation on gene expression (Table 1). We tested our hypotheses in 476 SARS patients and 449 healthy controls and found that polymorphism of IFN-gamma +874A allele was associated with susceptibility to SARS in a dose-dependent manner.
###end p 14

###begin title 16
Methods
###end title 16
###begin title 17
###xml 0 7 <span type="species:ncbi:9606">Patient</span>
Patient populations
###end title 17
###begin p 18
###xml 197 205 <span type="species:ncbi:9606">patients</span>
###xml 370 378 <span type="species:ncbi:9606">patients</span>
The study was approved by the Clinical Research Ethics Committee of the Institutional Review Board of the University of Hong Kong/Hospital Authority Hong Kong West Cluster and included 476 Chinese patients with SARS (201 male, mean age = 39.8 +/- 15.2) and 449 ethnically matched healthy controls from Red Cross (273 male, mean age = 29.1 +/- 10.4). At least 95% of the patients were documented with SARS-CoV antibody seroconversion and/or detectable SARS-CoV RNA in respiratory secretions by RT-PCR.
###end p 18
###begin title 19
Genotyping
###end title 19
###begin p 20
###xml 0 10 0 6 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">IFN-&#947; </italic>
###xml 19 25 15 21 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">IL-10 </italic>
###xml 146 148 142 144 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="B20">20</xref>
###xml 151 161 147 153 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">TNF-&#945; </italic>
###xml 373 374 365 366 <bold xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">G</bold>
###xml 413 414 405 406 <bold xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">A</bold>
IFN-gamma +874A/T, IL-10 -1082G/A and -592A/C were genotyped by TaqMan system (Applied Biosystems, Foster City, CA, USA) as described previously [20]. TNF-alpha -308 G/A was also genotyped by TaqMan system with same condition. The sequences of the primers were 5'-CCT GGT CCC CAA AAG AAA TG-3' and 5'-TCT TCT GGG CCA CTG ACT GA-3' and the probes were 6-FAM-TTG AGG GGC ATG GGG ACG G-TAMRA and VIC-TTG AGG GGC ATG AGG ACG GG-TAMRA.
###end p 20
###begin title 21
Statistical analysis
###end title 21
###begin p 22
###xml 302 304 302 304 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">P-</italic>
###xml 120 128 <span type="species:ncbi:9606">patients</span>
The frequencies of genotypes and alleles of the 4 single nucleotide polymorphisms (SNPs) were compared between the SARS patients and healthy controls by 3 x 2 and 2 x 2 chi square test respectively. In case of significance, logistic regression was used for calculating OR with 95% CI and corresponding P-values between groups by controlling age and sex as covariables. The genotypes of all SNPs were tested for Hardy-Weinberg equilibrium (HWE) by chi square test.
###end p 22
###begin title 23
Results and discussion
###end title 23
###begin p 24
###xml 44 54 44 50 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">IFN-&#947; </italic>
###xml 63 73 59 65 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">TNF-&#945; </italic>
###xml 82 88 74 80 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">IL-10 </italic>
###xml 255 256 247 248 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="table" rid="T2">2</xref>
###xml 262 272 254 260 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">IFN-&#947; </italic>
###xml 371 373 359 361 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">P </italic>
###xml 476 478 464 466 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">P </italic>
###xml 510 520 498 504 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">IFN-&#947; </italic>
###xml 717 723 701 707 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">IL-10 </italic>
###xml 727 736 711 716 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">TNF-&#945;</italic>
###xml 789 791 769 771 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">P </italic>
###xml 856 861 836 841 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">IL-10</italic>
###xml 124 132 <span type="species:ncbi:9606">patients</span>
###xml 313 321 <span type="species:ncbi:9606">patients</span>
###xml 807 815 <span type="species:ncbi:9606">patients</span>
Our case-control study genotyped the 4 SNPs IFN-gamma +874A/T, TNF-alpha -308G/A, IL-10 -1082G/A and -592A/C in 476 Chinese patients with SARS and 449 healthy controls. The genotype distributions and allele frequencies of these 4 SNPs were shown in Table 2. The IFN-gamma +874A allele was overrepresented in SARS patients (83.1%) when compared with the controls (66.3%) (P < 0.001). It was also significantly associated with susceptibility to SARS in a dose-dependent manner (P < 0.001), i.e. individuals with IFN-gamma +874 AA and AT genotype had an odds ratio (OR) of 5.19 (95% CI, 2.78-9.68) and 2.57 (95% CI, 1.35-4.88) in developing SARS respectively. However, no significant correlation was observed in SNPs of IL-10 and TNF-alpha. All SNPs were in Hardy-Weinberg equilibrium (HWE) (P > 0.05) in SARS patients and controls by chi square test, except IL-10-592A/C.
###end p 24

###begin p 28
###xml 0 10 0 6 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">IFN-&#947; </italic>
###xml 171 173 167 169 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="B20">20</xref>
###xml 174 176 170 172 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="B22">22</xref>
###xml 293 303 289 295 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">IFN-&#947; </italic>
###xml 438 448 426 432 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">IFN-&#947; </italic>
###xml 570 580 546 552 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">IFN-&#947; </italic>
###xml 592 594 564 566 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="B23">23</xref>
###xml 788 798 756 762 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">IFN-&#947; </italic>
###xml 1100 1109 1053 1062 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">in vitro </italic>
###xml 1110 1112 1063 1065 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="B24">24</xref>
###xml 1113 1115 1066 1068 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="B25">25</xref>
###xml 116 133 <span type="species:ncbi:10407">hepatitis B virus</span>
IFN-gamma +874A allele has been previously reported to be associated with infectious diseases such as tuberculosis, hepatitis B virus infection, and parvovirus infection [20-22], revealing its potential role of function in host defense against microbial infections. The mechanism by which the IFN-gamma +874A/T allele influences the susceptibility to SARS may depend on its role in the regulation of IFN-gamma production. The T allele of IFN-gamma +874A/T provides a binding site for the transcription factor nuclear factor-kappaB (NF-kappaB), which is able to regulate IFN-gamma expression [23]. It is possible that low IFN-gamma production may impair their anti-viral response against SARS-CoV, rendering these individuals more susceptible to this virus infection. Our observation that IFN-gamma +874A allele was significantly associated with SARS-CoV infection suggests a genetic risk factor for SARS. The role of IFN-gamma in antiviral response against SARS-CoV has also been supported by recent studies showing that IFN-gamma can inhibit the replication of SARS-CoV in combination with IFN-beta in vitro [24,25].
###end p 28
###begin p 29
###xml 0 6 0 6 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">IL-10 </italic>
###xml 10 20 10 16 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">TNF-&#945; </italic>
###xml 139 141 135 137 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="B26">26</xref>
###xml 142 144 138 140 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="B28">28</xref>
###xml 245 246 241 242 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="table" rid="T2">2</xref>
###xml 293 299 289 295 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">IL-10 </italic>
###xml 303 313 299 305 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">TNF-&#945; </italic>
###xml 572 578 564 570 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">IL-10 </italic>
###xml 582 592 574 580 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">TNF-&#945; </italic>
IL-10 and TNF-alpha SNPs were also included in this study. They were chosen due to their potential regulation on protein expression level [26-28]. However, our present data did not show any significant association of these SNPs with SARS (Table 2). Nevertheless, we cannot exclude the role of IL-10 and TNF-alpha as the susceptibility genes for SARS, because other SNPs in these 2 genes may also be involved in gene expression regulation. Further association studies on other SNPs, which could alter the gene expression level are required to ascertain the relationship of IL-10 and TNF-alpha in SARS.
###end p 29

###xml 154 155 154 155 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="table" rid="T3">3</xref>
###xml 138 146 <span type="species:ncbi:9606">patients</span>
We have also compared the genotype and allele frequencies of all the polymorphisms between the death group and survival group of the SARS patients (Table 3). However, no significant association was established.

###begin title 33
Conclusion
###end title 33
###begin p 34
###xml 21 31 21 27 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">IFN-&#947; </italic>
###xml 178 180 170 172 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="B15">15</xref>
We demonstrated that IFN-gamma +874A allele was significantly associated with SARS susceptibility in a dose dependent manner. Due to its role in regulating IFN-gamma expression [15], this allele may be involved in the pathogenesis of SARS by altering the IFN-gamma production.
