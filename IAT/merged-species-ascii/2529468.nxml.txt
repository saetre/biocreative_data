HSV-1 infection suppresses TGF-beta1 and SMAD3 expression in human corneal epithelial cells

###begin title 2
Purpose
###end title 2
###begin p 3
###xml 89 97 86 91 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">TGF-&#946;</italic>
###xml 157 162 142 147 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">SMAD2</italic>
###xml 167 172 152 157 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">SMAD3</italic>
###xml 190 217 <span type="species:ncbi:10298">herpes simplex virus type 1</span>
###xml 219 224 <span type="species:ncbi:10298?0.9377676277476449">HSV-1</span>
The present study was undertaken to investigate whether transforming growth factor-beta (TGF-beta) isoforms (TGF-beta1, TGF-beta2, and TGF-beta3) and SMADs (SMAD2 and SMAD3) are involved in herpes simplex virus type 1 (HSV-1) corneal infection.
###end p 3
###begin title 4
Methods
###end title 4
###begin p 5
###xml 192 200 192 197 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">TGF-&#946;</italic>
###xml 237 242 234 239 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">SMAD2</italic>
###xml 247 252 244 249 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">SMAD3</italic>
###xml 0 5 <span type="species:ncbi:9606">Human</span>
###xml 56 61 <span type="species:ncbi:10298?0.9377676277476449">HSV-1</span>
Human corneal epithelial cells (HCE) were infected with HSV-1 at a multiplicity of infection of 5. Cell morphological changes were observed under phase-contrast microscopy. Levels of mRNA for TGF-beta isoforms 1, 2, and 3 as well as for SMAD2 and SMAD3 were measured by reverse transcription polymerase chain reaction (RT-PCR) at 0 h, 4 h, 8 h, 12 h, and 24 h after infection. Protein expression of TGF-beta1, TGF-beta2, SMAD3, and phospho-SMAD3 were analyzed by indirect immunofluorescence at 0 h, 12 h, and 24 h post-infection (p.i.) in HCE cells. Protein expression of TGF-beta1 was also evaluated by ELISA.
###end p 5
###begin title 6
Results
###end title 6
###begin p 7
###xml 168 177 168 174 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">TGF-&#946;1</italic>
###xml 290 295 287 292 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">SMAD3</italic>
###xml 441 450 438 444 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">TGF-&#946;2</italic>
###xml 452 461 446 452 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">TGF-&#946;3</italic>
###xml 467 472 458 463 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">SMAD2</italic>
###xml 10 15 <span type="species:ncbi:10298?0.9377676277476449">HSV-1</span>
###xml 181 186 <span type="species:ncbi:10298?0.9377676277476449">HSV-1</span>
Following HSV-1 infection, a cytopathic effect in HCE cells was observed at 8 h p.i. and became significant at 24 h p.i. Compared with normal cells, the mRNA levels of TGF-beta1 in HSV-1 infected HCE cells decreased significantly at 8 h, 12 h, and 24 h p.i. (p<0.01), and the expression of SMAD3 was also dramatically decreased 12 h and 24 h p.i. (p<0.01). No noticeable changes were found as a result of infection with respect to levels of TGF-beta2, TGF-beta3, and SMAD2 in HCE cells. Protein expression of TGF-beta1, SMAD3, and phospho-SMAD3 decreased in infected cells at 12 h and 24 h p.i. compared with normal cells, but TGF-beta2 had no change.
###end p 7
###begin title 8
Conclusions
###end title 8
###begin p 9
###xml 0 9 0 6 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">TGF-&#946;1</italic>
###xml 14 19 11 16 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">SMAD3</italic>
###xml 89 94 <span type="species:ncbi:10298?0.9377676277476449">HSV-1</span>
TGF-beta1 and SMAD3 may be involved in the pathology of corneal diseases associated with HSV-1 infection.

###begin title 10
Introduction
###end title 10
###begin p 11
###xml 330 331 330 331 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="r1">1</xref>
###xml 332 333 332 333 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="r2">2</xref>
###xml 483 484 483 484 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="r3">3</xref>
###xml 821 822 805 806 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="r4">4</xref>
###xml 823 824 807 808 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="r6">6</xref>
###xml 0 27 <span type="species:ncbi:10298">Herpes simplex virus type 1</span>
###xml 29 34 <span type="species:ncbi:10298?0.9377676277476449">HSV-1</span>
###xml 125 130 <span type="species:ncbi:10298?0.9377676277476449">HSV-1</span>
###xml 152 157 <span type="species:ncbi:9606">human</span>
###xml 348 353 <span type="species:ncbi:10298?0.9377676277476449">HSV-1</span>
Herpes simplex virus type 1 (HSV-1) is a large, enveloped, double-stranded DNA virus with a genome of approximately 150 kbp. HSV-1 is widespread in the human population and commonly causes infections of the skin or mucosal surfaces. Occasionally, it can cause serious diseases such as sporadic encephalitis and ocular infections [1,2]. In the eye, HSV-1 infection usually results in blepharitis, conjunctivitis, corneal epithelial keratitis, and ulcerative and/or stromal keratitis [3]. The pathologies of these diseases are associated with several events such as the infiltration of neutrophils and mononuclear lymphocytes and the expression of growth factors, proinflammatory factors, and cytokines, which include transforming growth factor-beta (TGF-beta), IL-2, IL-6, IL-8, TNF-alpha, and interferon-beta (IFN-beta) [4-6]. These studies suggest that growth factors and cytokines are extremely important in regulating the body's reaction to viral infection.
###end p 11
###begin p 12
###xml 168 169 165 166 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="r7">7</xref>
###xml 341 342 335 336 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="r8">8</xref>
###xml 343 345 337 339 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="r11">11</xref>
###xml 793 794 766 767 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="r7">7</xref>
###xml 795 797 768 770 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="r12">12</xref>
###xml 798 800 771 773 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="r13">13</xref>
###xml 891 893 861 863 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="r14">14</xref>
###xml 894 896 864 866 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="r15">15</xref>
###xml 1023 1025 990 992 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="r16">16</xref>
###xml 1026 1028 993 995 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="r18">18</xref>
###xml 1174 1176 1138 1140 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="r16">16</xref>
###xml 1519 1520 1480 1481 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="r8">8</xref>
###xml 1521 1522 1482 1483 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="r9">9</xref>
###xml 1523 1525 1484 1486 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="r19">19</xref>
###xml 1526 1528 1487 1489 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="r22">22</xref>
###xml 877 882 <span type="species:ncbi:9606">human</span>
###xml 1394 1397 <span type="species:ncbi:12305?0.03296277145811789|species:ncbi:36410?3.8779731127197516E-4">CMV</span>
###xml 1400 1428 <span type="species:ncbi:12721">human immunodeficiency virus</span>
###xml 1430 1433 <span type="species:ncbi:12721?0.9873544761140104">HIV</span>
###xml 1436 1458 <span type="species:ncbi:11232">canine distemper virus</span>
###xml 1472 1477 <span type="species:ncbi:10298?0.9377676277476449">HSV-1</span>
###xml 1483 1488 <span type="species:ncbi:9606">human</span>
###xml 1874 1879 <span type="species:ncbi:10298?0.9377676277476449">HSV-1</span>
###xml 2019 2024 <span type="species:ncbi:10298?0.9377676277476449">HSV-1</span>
TGF-beta is a superfamily of cytokines, which affect a range of biological processes such as cell growth, differentiation, matrix production, migration, and apoptosis [7]. Furthermore, the TGF-beta pathway is an important target for several viral proteins that interfere with signal transduction and transcription control in infected cells [8-11]. Upon activation of the TGF-beta signaling pathway, TGF-beta family members bind to the TGF-beta type II receptor (TbetaR-II). TbetaR-II then recruits and phosphorylates TGF-beta type I receptors (TbetaR-I), which in turn phosphorylates the intracellular effectors (i.e., SMAD2 and SMAD3). Subsequently, SMAD2 and SMAD3 complexes, which are associated with SMAD4, are translocated into the nucleus and regulate the transcription of target genes [7,12,13]. A previous study demonstrated that TGF-beta isoforms are expressed in the human cornea [14,15], and TGF-beta is believed to be one of the major factors involved in cell migration in the cornea and corneal wound healing [16-18]. Furthermore, TGF-beta signaling through the SMAD pathway is activated in response to corneal wounds in which the basement membrane is removed [16]. Earlier studies suggested that TGF-beta might be important in the pathology of various disease processes involved with viral infection. This has been demonstrated for a variety of viruses including cytomegalovirus (CMV), human immunodeficiency virus (HIV), canine distemper virus, rhinovirus, HSV-1, and human T-cell leukemia virus (HTLV) [8,9,19-22]. Corneal epithelial cells are the first line of defense against microbial infection and against further damage to the underlying stroma. Therefore, we must understand the role of TGF-beta in the pathology of viral infection in the corneal epithelium. It is reasonable to suppose that TGF-beta and SMADs play a critical role in the pathology of HSV-1 infection in the cornea. The present study was undertaken to examine whether TGF-beta isoforms and SMADs (SMAD2 and SMAD3) are involved in HSV-1 corneal epithelial infection in vitro.
###end p 12
###begin title 13
Methods
###end title 13
###begin title 14
Cell culture
###end title 14
###begin p 15
###xml 85 87 85 87 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="r23">23</xref>
###xml 431 432 422 423 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">2</sub>
###xml 4 9 <span type="species:ncbi:9606">human</span>
###xml 159 165 <span type="species:ncbi:9913">bovine</span>
###xml 208 213 <span type="species:ncbi:9606">human</span>
###xml 293 298 <span type="species:ncbi:9606">human</span>
The human corneal epithelial cell line (HCEC) that we used was described previously [23]. Cells were cultured in DMEM/high glucose supplemented with 10% fetal bovine serum (FBS; Hyclone, Logan, UT), 10 ng/ml human epidermal growth factor (EGF; Sigma, St Louis, MO), 5 mug/ml insulin, 5 mug/ml human transferrin (Sigma), and 0.4 mug/ml hydrocortisone (Gibco BRL, Grand Island, NY). The cells were incubated at 37 degreesC in a 5% CO2-95% air incubator. Experiments were performed when cells were at 80%-90% confluence.
###end p 15
###begin title 16
Virus infection
###end title 16
###begin p 17
###xml 227 229 227 229 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="r24">24</xref>
###xml 636 640 630 634 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">VP16</italic>
###xml 753 760 747 754 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="table" rid="t1">Table 1</xref>
###xml 14 19 <span type="species:ncbi:10298?0.9377676277476449">HSV-1</span>
###xml 128 134 <span type="species:ncbi:9913">bovine</span>
###xml 645 650 <span type="species:ncbi:10298?0.9377676277476449">HSV-1</span>
###xml 789 794 <span type="species:ncbi:10298?0.9377676277476449">HSV-1</span>
###xml 870 875 <span type="species:ncbi:10298?0.9377676277476449">HSV-1</span>
Stocks of the HSV-1 (F strain) used in this study were propagated on HEp-2 cells grown in DMEM/F12, which contained 10% newborn bovine serum. The titer of virus stocks was determined according to a previously described method [24]. After cells were grown to 80%-90% confluence, cells were infected at a multiplicity of infection (MOI) of 5. After 1 h of adsorption at 37 degreesC with intermittent rocking, the inoculum was removed, and the medium was replaced with serum-free DMEM/high glucose. At the indicated times, cells were harvested for further experiments. To confirm virus infection, two virus genes (i.e., DNA polymerase and VP16) of HSV-1 were examined by reverse transcription polymerase chain reaction (RT-PCR) using the primers listed in Table 1. Two genes were detected in HSV-1 infected cells, which implied that HCE cells were successfully infected by HSV-1.

###begin title 19
RNA isolation and reverse transcription polymerase chain reaction analysis
###end title 19
###begin p 20
###xml 358 360 358 360 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">TM</sup>
###xml 536 543 534 541 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="table" rid="t1">Table 1</xref>
###xml 594 596 592 594 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">TM</sup>
###xml 910 915 908 913 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">GAPDH</italic>
Cells were harvested and washed with phosphate buffered saline (PBS). Total RNA was isolated with TRIzol reagent (Invitrogen, Carlsbad, CA) according to the manufacturer's instructions. The quantity and quality of total RNA were estimated by spectrophotometry and agarose electrophoresis. Subsequently, RNA was reverse-transcribed into cDNA using a RevertAidTM First Strand cDNA synthesis kit (Fermentas, Glen Burnie, MD). cDNA was then amplified by GoTaq(R) Green Master mix (Promega, Madison, WI) using the specific primers listed in Table 1. The PCR products were electrophoresed in GoldViewTM stained 2% agarose gels (SBS Genetech, Beijing, China). Quantification of the bands was performed using a BioImaging System (UVP, Upland, CA) and Gel-pro software (Media Cybernetics, Bethesda, MD), and the level of mRNA was expressed as the ratio of integrated optical density (IOD) of specific PCR products over GAPDH IOD.
###end p 20
###begin title 21
Indirect immunofluorescence
###end title 21
###begin p 22
###xml 340 342 340 342 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="r25">25</xref>
###xml 89 94 <span type="species:ncbi:10298?0.9377676277476449">HSV-1</span>
###xml 427 431 <span type="species:ncbi:9925">goat</span>
###xml 534 540 <span type="species:ncbi:9986">rabbit</span>
###xml 546 551 <span type="species:ncbi:9606">human</span>
###xml 816 820 <span type="species:ncbi:9925">goat</span>
###xml 826 832 <span type="species:ncbi:9986">rabbit</span>
HCE cells were cultured on a glass coverslip in 12 well chamber dishes and infected with HSV-1 as described above. At the indicated times, changes in cellular morphology were photographed using a phase-contrast microscope. Slide-mounted cells were used for indirect immunofluorescence analysis according to the method described previously [25]. The cells were blocked by endogenous peroxidase-blocking solution and followed by goat serum (each for 10 min at 37 degreesC). After blocking nonspecific binding, cells were incubated with rabbit anti-human monoclonal/polyclonal antibodies that recognize TGF-beta1 (Santa Cruz, Delaware Avenue, CA), TGF-beta2 (Santa Cruz), SMAD3, and phospho-SMAD3 (both from Cell Signaling, Danvers, MA) at 4 degreesC overnight. Cells were then incubated with FITC-conjugated secondary goat anti-rabbit IgG (Zhongshan Goldenbridge, Beijing, China) at 37 degreesC for 1 h. Prior to mounting, cells were stained with propidium iodide (PI) for 10 min. Cells were then observed using a confocal laser scanning microscope (Carl Zeiss, Jena, Germany). Cells incubated with PBS (instead of the first antibody) were used as negative controls.
###end p 22
###begin title 23
###xml 36 41 <span type="species:ncbi:9606">human</span>
Measurement of TGF-beta1 protein in human corneal epithelial cells by ELISA
###end title 23
###begin p 24
###xml 29 34 <span type="species:ncbi:10298?0.9377676277476449">HSV-1</span>
###xml 472 477 <span type="species:ncbi:9606">human</span>
At 0 h, 12 h, and 24 h p.i., HSV-1 infected HCE cells were lysed with lysate buffer (Pierce, Rockford, IL). The samples were sonicated and centrifuged at 12,000 rpm for 30 min at 4 degreesC to remove cellular debris. Protein content in the supernatant was determined by the bicinchoninic acid method using BSA as the standard. The TGF-beta1 levels of cell homogenate were assayed using a specific TGF-beta1 enzyme-liked immunosorbent assay kit (Boster, Wuhan, China), and human TGF-beta1 was used to construct a standard curve. The amount of TGF-beta1 protein in the cell was normalized to the total amount of cellular protein. Absorbance values were read at 450 nm by an ELISA enzyme-labeled device.
###end p 24
###begin title 25
Statistical analysis
###end title 25
###begin p 26
###xml 175 180 <span type="species:ncbi:10298?0.9377676277476449">HSV-1</span>
Statistical analysis of data was performed by one-way ANOVA and a Student-Newman-Keuls test to determine statistically significant differences (p<0.05) between uninfected and HSV-1 infected cells.

###begin title 27
Results
###end title 27
###begin title 28
###xml 25 30 <span type="species:ncbi:10298?0.9377676277476449">HSV-1</span>
###xml 40 45 <span type="species:ncbi:9606">human</span>
Morphological changes of HSV-1 infected human corneal epithelial cells
###end title 28
###begin p 29
###xml 135 144 135 144 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="fig" rid="f1">Figure 1A</xref>
###xml 377 388 377 388 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="fig" rid="f1">Figure 1B,C</xref>
###xml 638 647 638 647 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="fig" rid="f1">Figure 1D</xref>
###xml 157 162 <span type="species:ncbi:10298?0.9377676277476449">HSV-1</span>
Cell morphological changes were observed under phase-contrast microscopy. Normal HCE cells exhibited a typical cobblestone appearance (Figure 1A). Following HSV-1 infection and up to 8 h p.i., the cell morphology of infected groups was similar to the uninfected group. Compared with control cells, a cytopathic effect (CPE) in HCE cells could be observed at 8 h and 12 h p.i. (Figure 1B,C). The space between infected cells increased, and the cobblestone appearance disappeared. At 24 h p.i., most of the infected cells exhibited a CPE (dead cells were observed floating in the medium), and many giant multinucleated cells could be seen (Figure 1D).
###end p 29

###begin p 32

###begin title 41
Discussion
###end title 41
###begin p 42
###xml 215 217 215 217 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="r26">26</xref>
###xml 218 220 218 220 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="r27">27</xref>
###xml 239 241 239 241 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="r28">28</xref>
###xml 1130 1134 1130 1134 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">VP16</italic>
###xml 196 201 <span type="species:ncbi:10298?0.9377676277476449">HSV-1</span>
###xml 271 276 <span type="species:ncbi:10298?0.9377676277476449">HSV-1</span>
###xml 362 366 <span type="species:ncbi:10090">mice</span>
###xml 790 795 <span type="species:ncbi:10298?0.9377676277476449">HSV-1</span>
###xml 850 855 <span type="species:ncbi:10298?0.9377676277476449">HSV-1</span>
###xml 916 921 <span type="species:ncbi:10298?0.9377676277476449">HSV-1</span>
###xml 1184 1189 <span type="species:ncbi:10298?0.9377676277476449">HSV-1</span>
###xml 1274 1279 <span type="species:ncbi:10298?0.9377676277476449">HSV-1</span>
###xml 1350 1355 <span type="species:ncbi:10298?0.9377676277476449">HSV-1</span>
The cornea contains three principal cell types, epithelial cells, keratocytes, and endothelial cells. Previous studies have shown that corneal epithelial cells are capable of supporting efficient HSV-1 replication [26,27]. Balliet et al. [28] reported that a recombinant HSV-1 virus, KOS-CMVGFP, expressing enhanced green fluorescent protein (EGFP) could infect mice as efficiently as a wild-type virus. They found that fluorescence was observed in eyes as distinct small foci on the cornea at day 1 p.i., and that the fluorescence spread throughout the eye between days 1 and 3 p.i. Finally, the foci grew larger and coalesced, resulting in large, dendritic corneal lesions. Consistent with the studies described above, our work also demonstrated that the HCE cell is highly permissive to HSV-1 infection in vitro. When HCE cells were infected with HSV-1 at a MOI of 5, a cytopathic effect was observed at 8 h p.i. HSV-1 infection caused an increase in the number of dead cells, which may be the reason for the dendritic keratitis observed in vivo. Furthermore, we also observed expression of two viral genes (DNA polymerase and VP16) in infected cells by RT-PCR, which implies that HSV-1 caused a productive infection of HCE cells. Therefore, HCE cells are susceptible to HSV-1 infection, and it can provide a useful in vitro model for research of HSV-1 infection in the cornea.
###end p 42
###begin p 43
###xml 156 158 150 152 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="r14">14</xref>
###xml 159 161 153 155 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="r15">15</xref>
###xml 162 164 156 158 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="r29">29</xref>
###xml 315 317 309 311 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="r16">16</xref>
###xml 318 320 312 314 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="r30">30</xref>
###xml 486 488 477 479 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="r31">31</xref>
###xml 696 701 <span type="species:ncbi:10298?0.9377676277476449">HSV-1</span>
###xml 852 857 <span type="species:ncbi:10298?0.9377676277476449">HSV-1</span>
TGF-beta isoforms and SMAD family members have been identified in mammalian cells. In the eye, TGF-beta isoforms are expressed in different ocular tissues [14,15,29]. In the cornea, SMAD2 and SMAD4 were expressed and translocated into the nuclei, and SMAD7 was overexpressed during corneal epithelial wound repair [16,30]. In the cultured retinal pigment epithelial cell line (D407), TGF-beta can stimulate the translocation of SMAD2 (but not SMAD1) from the cytoplasm into the nuclei [31]. Therefore, TGF-beta isoforms and SMADs may play important roles in the pathogenesis of ocular diseases. However, there is limited research on the effect of TGF-beta isoforms and SMADs in cells infected by HSV-1. Accordingly, the objective of the present study was to investigate whether the expression of TGF-beta isoforms and SMADs in HCE cells is affected by HSV-1 infection in vitro.
###end p 43
###begin p 44
###xml 140 142 137 139 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="r19">19</xref>
###xml 143 145 140 142 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="r32">32</xref>
###xml 146 148 143 145 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="r33">33</xref>
###xml 379 381 370 372 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="r22">22</xref>
###xml 382 384 373 375 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="r33">33</xref>
###xml 544 546 532 534 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="r19">19</xref>
###xml 756 757 741 742 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="r4">4</xref>
###xml 781 789 766 771 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">TGF-&#946;</italic>
###xml 872 881 854 860 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">TGF-&#946;1</italic>
###xml 1059 1068 1038 1044 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">TGF-&#946;2</italic>
###xml 1073 1082 1049 1055 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">TGF-&#946;3</italic>
###xml 1161 1169 1134 1139 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">TGF-&#946;</italic>
###xml 102 105 <span type="species:ncbi:12721?0.9873544761140104">HIV</span>
###xml 107 110 <span type="species:ncbi:12305?0.03296277145811789|species:ncbi:36410?3.8779731127197516E-4">CMV</span>
###xml 116 121 <span type="species:ncbi:10298?0.9377676277476449">HSV-1</span>
###xml 154 157 <span type="species:ncbi:12305?0.03296277145811789|species:ncbi:36410?3.8779731127197516E-4">CMV</span>
###xml 226 231 <span type="species:ncbi:9606">human</span>
###xml 396 401 <span type="species:ncbi:10298?0.9377676277476449">HSV-1</span>
###xml 415 420 <span type="species:ncbi:9606">human</span>
###xml 711 716 <span type="species:ncbi:10090">mouse</span>
###xml 738 743 <span type="species:ncbi:10298?0.9377676277476449">HSV-1</span>
###xml 961 966 <span type="species:ncbi:10298?0.9377676277476449">HSV-1</span>
###xml 1021 1026 <span type="species:ncbi:10298?0.9377676277476449">HSV-1</span>
###xml 1196 1201 <span type="species:ncbi:10298?0.9377676277476449">HSV-1</span>
The effect of viral infection on TGF-beta expression has been described for several viruses including HIV, CMV, and HSV-1 in other tissues [19,32,33]. In CMV infection, TGF-beta1 was detected in increasing amounts in infected human foreskin fibroblast and astrocyte supernatants, and TGF-beta1 transcription was significantly increased when compared to that of uninfected cells [22,33]. In vitro HSV-1 infection of human mononuclear cells resulted in a significant time-dependent increase in the release of TGF-beta1 protein into supernatants [19]. These studies showed that virus infection could induce the overexpression of TGF-beta1 with respect to protein expression and mRNA levels. However, in a study on mouse cornea infected with HSV-1, Hu et al. [4] showed that levels of TGF-beta mRNA decreased in inflamed corneas. Our study demonstrated that the expression of TGF-beta1 at both the mRNA and protein level was down-regulated in HCE cells infected by HSV-1 at 8 h p.i. and beyond. However, during the course of HSV-1 infection, the transcription of TGF-beta2 and TGF-beta3 remained unchanged compared to uninfected cells. These results suggested that TGF-beta expression in response to HSV-1 infection is specific to cell type.
###end p 44
###begin p 45
###xml 92 97 92 97 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">SMAD3</italic>
###xml 150 155 150 155 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">SMAD2</italic>
###xml 520 525 520 525 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">SMAD3</italic>
###xml 556 561 556 561 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">SMAD3</italic>
###xml 35 40 <span type="species:ncbi:10298?0.9377676277476449">HSV-1</span>
###xml 207 212 <span type="species:ncbi:10298?0.9377676277476449">HSV-1</span>
###xml 607 612 <span type="species:ncbi:10298?0.9377676277476449">HSV-1</span>
The current study also showed that HSV-1 infection caused a decline in the transcription of SMAD3 in HCE cells but had no effect on the expression of SMAD2. Similarly, by confocal laser scanning microscopy, HSV-1 infected HCE cells displayed weak immunostaining for SMAD3 and phospho-SMAD3. Although measuring protein levels with a quantitative method such as western blot would provide more convincing evidence of protein expression change, the immunostaining result was consistent with the data of RT-PCR analysis for SMAD3. These results suggested that SMAD3 decreased in both mRNA and protein levels in HSV-1 infected HCE cells.
###end p 45
###begin p 46
###xml 51 59 51 56 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">TGF-&#946;</italic>
###xml 310 312 304 306 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="r10">10</xref>
###xml 483 485 477 479 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="r34">34</xref>
###xml 602 603 593 594 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="r8">8</xref>
###xml 664 666 655 657 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="r11">11</xref>
###xml 1045 1047 1033 1035 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="r35">35</xref>
###xml 1060 1065 1048 1053 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">SMAD3</italic>
###xml 1313 1321 1298 1303 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">TGF-&#946;</italic>
###xml 1360 1362 1342 1344 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="r36">36</xref>
###xml 834 839 <span type="species:ncbi:10298?0.9377676277476449">HSV-1</span>
###xml 1439 1444 <span type="species:ncbi:10298?0.9377676277476449">HSV-1</span>
It has been demonstrated that in virus infections, TGF-beta could be regulated by the SMAD subfamily. In HPV infected cells, viral E7 oncoprotein blocks through its constitutive interactions with SMAD2, SMAD3, and SMAD4, both SMAD transcriptional activity and the ability of TGF-beta to inhibit DNA synthesis [10]. E6 oncoprotein of HPV-5 inhibits SMAD3 transactivation by interacting with SMAD3, destabilizing the SMAD3/SMAD4 complex, and inducing the degradation of both proteins [34]. Virus proteins also interfere with TGF-beta signaling via SMAD proteins as observed in HTLV-1 infected ATL cells [8] and in Kaposi's sarcoma-associated herpes virus infection [11]. These results show that suppression of SMAD-mediated TGF-beta signaling in virus infected cells might contribute to the carcinogenesis. The present study focuses on HSV-1 infected corneal epithelial cells, which characterizes recurrent inflammation of the cornea in vivo. The fundamental physiologic roles of SMAD3 are involved in the processes of tissue repair and fibrosis [35]. Decreased SMAD3 expression could reduce formation and nuclear import of transcriptionally active SMAD heterocomplexes and decrease transcription of TGF-beta1 regulated target genes, which result in reduced inflammatory cell infiltrates, reduced auto-induction of TGF-beta, and reduced elaboration of collagen [36]. This may be the cause of the observed decreases of TGF-beta1 and SMAD3 in HSV-1 infected HCE cells in this study, which occurred as an in vivo inflammatory process.
###end p 46
###begin p 47
###xml 174 176 174 176 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="r37">37</xref>
###xml 193 202 193 199 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">TGF-&#946;1</italic>
###xml 207 212 204 209 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">SMAD3</italic>
###xml 22 27 <span type="species:ncbi:10298?0.9377676277476449">HSV-1</span>
###xml 216 221 <span type="species:ncbi:10298?0.9377676277476449">HSV-1</span>
###xml 353 358 <span type="species:ncbi:10298?0.9377676277476449">HSV-1</span>
###xml 418 423 <span type="species:ncbi:10298?0.9377676277476449">HSV-1</span>
The interplay between HSV-1 and its host involves numerous factors, and the virus employs several mechanisms to combat many antiviral responses enacted by the infected cell [37]. Expression of TGF-beta1 and SMAD3 in HSV-1 infected HCE cells decreased in this study, which suggested that they may be involved in corneal diseases that are associated with HSV-1 infection. The specific function of TGF-beta1 and SMAD3 in HSV-1 corneal infection requires further investigation.
