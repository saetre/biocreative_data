###begin article-title 0
beta-Selection: abundance of TCRbeta-/gammadelta- CD44-CD25- (DN4) cells in the foetal thymus
###end article-title 0
###begin title 1
Summary
###end title 1
###begin p 2
###xml 572 573 545 546 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">&#8722;</sup>
###xml 584 585 549 550 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">&#8722;</sup>
###xml 1196 1203 1136 1143 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">in vivo</italic>
###xml 235 239 <span type="species:ncbi:10090">mice</span>
###xml 508 512 <span type="species:ncbi:10090">mice</span>
###xml 789 793 <span type="species:ncbi:10090">mice</span>
###xml 1355 1359 <span type="species:ncbi:10090">mice</span>
Expression of TCRbeta and pre-TCR signalling are essential for differentiation of CD4-CD8- double negative (DN) thymocytes to the CD4+CD8+ double positive (DP) stage. Thymocyte development in adult Rag1, Rag2 or TCRbetadelta deficient mice is arrested at the DN3 stage leading to the assumption that pre-TCR signalling and beta-selection occur at and are obligatory for the transition from DN3 to DN4. We show that the majority of DN3 and DN4 cells that differentiate during early embryogenesis in wild type mice do not express icTCRbeta/gammadelta. These foetal icTCRbeta-/gammadelta- DN4 cells were T lineage as determined by expression of Thy1 and icCD3 and TCRbeta DJ rearrangement. In addition, in the foetal Rag1-/- thymus a normal percentage of DN4 cells were present. In wild type mice after thymocyte synchronisation by hydrocortisone, the majority of DN4 cells that first emerged did not express icTCRbeta/gammadelta, showing that adult thymocytes can also differentiate to the DN4 stage independently of pre-TCR signalling. Pre-TCR signalling induced expansion in the DN4 population, but lack of TCRbeta/gammadelta expression did not immediately induce apoptosis. Our data demonstrate in vivo differentiation from DN3 to DN4 cell in the absence of TCRbeta/gammadelta expression in the foetal thymus, and after hydrocortisone treatment of adult mice.
###end p 2
###begin title 3
Introduction
###end title 3
###begin p 4
###xml 170 171 170 171 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="R1">1</xref>
###xml 173 174 173 174 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="R2">2</xref>
###xml 487 488 487 488 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="R3">3</xref>
###xml 727 728 718 719 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="R4">4</xref>
###xml 882 883 870 871 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="R4">4</xref>
###xml 884 885 872 873 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="R6">6</xref>
###xml 901 902 889 890 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="R7">7</xref>
###xml 904 905 892 893 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="R8">8</xref>
###xml 1022 1023 1010 1011 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="R9">9</xref>
Signalling through the pre-T Cell Receptor (TCR) is essential for differentiation of CD4-CD8- double negative (DN) thymocytes to the CD4+CD8+ double positive (DP) stage [1, 2]. The DN population can be sub-divided into four subsets, DN1-DN4, depending on the expression of the cell surface molecules CD44 and CD25. The earliest CD44+CD25- (DN1) cells give rise to the CD44+CD25+(DN2) population that progresses to the CD25+CD44- (DN3) stage, and then to the CD44-CD25- (DN4) population [3]. Expression of rearranged TCRbeta and formation of the pre-TCR complex leads to a series of events collectively known as beta-selection, resulting in allelic exclusion of the TCRbeta locus, expansion and differentiation to the DP stage [4]. Several studies have indicated that thymocytes that successfully pass the beta-selection checkpoint receive pre-TCR induced signals for proliferation [4-6] and survival [7, 8]. Recently, BCL2A1 has been identified as an anti-apoptotic agent that is upregulated following pre-TCR signalling [9].
###end p 4
###begin p 5
###xml 182 184 176 178 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="R10">10</xref>
###xml 193 195 187 189 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="R11">11</xref>
###xml 214 216 201 203 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="R12">12</xref>
###xml 640 641 613 614 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="R7">7</xref>
###xml 686 687 656 657 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">&#8722;</sup>
###xml 698 699 660 661 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">&#8722;</sup>
###xml 796 797 755 756 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="R7">7</xref>
###xml 158 162 <span type="species:ncbi:10090">mice</span>
###xml 290 294 <span type="species:ncbi:10090">mice</span>
###xml 634 638 <span type="species:ncbi:10090">mice</span>
Rearrangement of the TCRbeta locus and expression of a functional TCRbeta chain are essential for differentiation, and the development of thymocytes in adult mice deficient in Rag1 [10], Rag2 [11] or TCRbetadelta [12] is arrested at the DN3 stage. The fact that T cell development in these mice is arrested at the DN3 stage has lead to the assumption that pre-TCR signalling and beta-selection occur at the transition from DN3 to DN4 and are obligatory for differentiation beyond the DN3 stage of development. Contrary to this assumption, rare DN4 cells that do not express TCRbeta or gammadelta have been detected in adult wild type mice [7]. As cell death is increased in this TCRbeta-/gammadelta- DN4 population, it has been suggested that cells that fail beta-selection die at the DN4 stage [7]. Here we show that in the wild type foetal thymus differentiation from DN3 to DN4 frequently occurs independently of TCRbeta/gammadelta expression (and hence normal pre-TCR signalling).
###end p 5
###begin p 6
###xml 205 207 205 207 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="R13">13</xref>
###xml 496 503 496 503 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">in vivo</italic>
In addition, in the foetal Rag1-/- thymus a normal percentage of DN4 cells were present, although no DP cells were observed. In the foetal thymus, T cell development occurs in a largely synchronized wave [13], allowing the study of thymocyte populations as they first emerge, and enabling the definition of requirements for the transition of cells from one population to the next. After synchronizing adult thymocyte development by hydrocortisone treatment, the majority of DN4 cells that emerge in vivo in the adult thymus do not express icTCRbeta/gammadelta.
###end p 6
###begin title 7
Results
###end title 7
###begin title 8
The majority of early foetal DN4 thymocytes do not express intracellular TCRbeta nor gammadelta
###end title 8
###begin p 9
###xml 545 551 536 542 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="fig" rid="F1">Fig 1a</xref>
###xml 661 668 649 656 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="fig" rid="F1">Fig. 1b</xref>
###xml 698 699 683 684 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">+</sup>
###xml 848 855 830 837 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="fig" rid="F1">Fig. 1c</xref>
###xml 898 899 877 878 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">&#8722;</sup>
###xml 961 968 932 939 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="fig" rid="F1">Fig. 1c</xref>
###xml 1156 1163 1113 1120 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="fig" rid="F1">Fig. 1d</xref>
Analysis of TCRbeta expression in foetal thymocytes revealed a large population of CD3-CD4-CD8- triple negative DN4 cells (CD44-CD25-) that did not express detectable intracellular (ic) TCRbeta. We analyzed the intracellular expression of TCRbeta in DN3 and DN4 cells of E15.5, E16.5 and adult wild type thymi. DN3 and DN4 subsets that fell within the live FSC/SSC gate were identified by positive staining for anti-Thy1.2, presence or absence, respectively, of cell surface expression of CD25 and the absence of CD44, CD3, CD4, CD8 expression (Fig 1a). In a typical experiment, on E15.5 12.66% of the DN3 cells and 15.23% of the DN4 cells expressed icTCRbeta (Fig. 1b). The percentage of icTCRbeta+ cells increased in both DN3 and DN4 subsets on E16.5 and in a typical experiment 18.41% of the DN3 and 54.59% of the DN4 cells expressed icTCRbeta (Fig. 1c). On E16.5, approximately 12% of icTCRbeta- DN4 cells stained positively for icTCRgammadelta expression (Fig. 1c). Thus, 40% of DN4 cells on E16.5 did not express either icTCRbeta/ or TCRgammadelta. As previously shown, in the adult thymus, 18.49% of DN3 and 90.45% of DN4 cells expressed icTCRbeta (Fig. 1d).
###end p 9
###begin p 10
###xml 76 77 73 74 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">&#8722;</sup>
###xml 129 130 126 127 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="R5">5</xref>
###xml 132 133 129 130 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="R7">7</xref>
###xml 135 137 132 134 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="R14">14</xref>
###xml 139 141 136 138 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="R15">15</xref>
###xml 207 208 201 202 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">+</sup>
###xml 265 270 259 264 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="fig" rid="F1">Fig 1</xref>
###xml 307 308 298 299 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">+</sup>
###xml 403 404 391 392 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">&#8722;</sup>
###xml 473 474 458 459 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">&#8722;</sup>
###xml 521 522 503 504 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">&#8722;</sup>
###xml 536 537 515 516 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">+</sup>
###xml 588 589 564 565 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">&#8722;</sup>
###xml 614 615 587 588 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">+</sup>
###xml 657 664 630 637 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="fig" rid="F1">Fig. 1e</xref>
The fact that the majority of the E15.5 foetal DN4 thymocytes were icTCRbeta- was surprising as in contrast to the adult thymus [5, 7, 14, 15] in the foetal E15.5 thymus there was no enrichment for icTCRbeta+ cells in the DN4 population compared to the DN3 subset (Fig 1), suggesting that the foetal TCRbeta+ DN3 cells did not preferentially differentiate to the DN4 stage compared to the foetal TCRbeta- DN3 cells. Analysis of icCD3 expression confirmed that the icTCRbeta- DN4 cells were T lineage cells. Both icTCRbeta- and icTCRbeta+ DN4 cells expressed icCD3 and 54% of the icTCRbeta- and 90% of the icTCRbeta+ DN4 cells expressed icCD3 at high level (Fig. 1e).
###end p 10
###begin p 11
###xml 50 51 47 48 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">&#8722;</sup>
###xml 145 146 139 140 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">&#8722;</sup>
###xml 272 273 263 264 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">&#8722;</sup>
###xml 287 288 275 276 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">+</sup>
###xml 381 382 366 367 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">&#8722;</sup>
###xml 477 478 459 460 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">+</sup>
###xml 542 549 524 531 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="fig" rid="F1">Fig. 1f</xref>
Given the reduction in the proportion of icTCRbeta- DN4 cells present in the thymus from E15.5 to E16.5 to adult, it seemed likely that icTCRbeta- DN4 cells are gradually removed from the thymus from E15.5 onwards. We therefore calculated the absolute numbers of icTCRbeta- and icTCRbeta+ DN4 cells in the thymus on E15.5, E16.5 and in adults. We found that the number of icTCRbeta- DN4 thymocytes in fact increases over time but to a lesser extent than the number of icTCRbeta+ DN4 cells, accounting for the reduction in overall percentage (Fig. 1f).
###end p 11
###begin title 12
DN4 cells develop in Rag1-/- foetal thymus
###end title 12
###begin p 13
###xml 25 26 22 23 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">&#8722;</sup>
###xml 366 379 363 376 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="fig" rid="F2">Fig. 2a and b</xref>
###xml 503 505 500 502 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="R10">10</xref>
###xml 746 753 743 750 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="fig" rid="F2">Fig. 2b</xref>
###xml 907 914 904 911 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="fig" rid="F2">Fig. 2b</xref>
###xml 1269 1276 1263 1270 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="fig" rid="F2">Fig. 2c</xref>
###xml 474 478 <span type="species:ncbi:10090">mice</span>
###xml 1045 1049 <span type="species:ncbi:10090">mice</span>
The presence of icTCRbeta- DN4 cells in the first wave of differentiation of wild type foetal thymocytes led us to ask whether DN4 cells are present in Rag1-/- foetal thymi. We analyzed Rag1-/- foetal thymi at embryonic days E15.5, E16.5, E17.5, juvenile (three week postnatal) and adult thymi and observed a substantial DN4 population on all embryonic days tested (Fig. 2a and b), whereas the percentage of DN4 thymocytes was extremely small in the adult thymus of Rag1-/- mice as previously reported [10]. In a typical experiment 31.12% of the thymocytes were DN4 on E15.5 compared to only 0.76% of the adult thymocytes. The percentage of DN4 thymocytes gradually decreased over time until they were virtually undetectable in the adult thymus (Fig. 2b). Correspondingly, the percentage of DN3 cells increased substantially from E15.5 to E16.5 and thereafter the thymus consisted mainly of DN3 thymocytes (Fig. 2b). As expected no TCRbeta expression nor DP thymocytes were observed at any stage of embryonic or postnatal development in Rag1-/- mice (data not shown). Although the percentage of Rag1-/- DN4 thymocytes gradually decreased over time after day E15.5, the absolute number of DN4 cells did not change appreciably, whereas the number of DN3 cells increased (Fig. 2c).
###end p 13
###begin p 14
###xml 237 244 237 244 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="fig" rid="F2">Fig. 2d</xref>
###xml 363 370 363 370 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="fig" rid="F2">Fig. 2d</xref>
The Rag1-/- DN4 cells observed, as defined by the absence of CD44 and CD25 cell surface markers, were true T lineage DN4 cells as the majority stained positive for cell surface expression of HSA (94.22%) and expressed intracellular CD3 (Fig. 2d). In contrast, in a typical experiment only 0.42% stained positive with antiNK1.1 and 8.58% with antiB220 antibodies (Fig. 2d).
###end p 14
###begin title 15
Transcription of Rag1 and Rag2 is downregulated in the foetal DN4 population
###end title 15
###begin p 16
###xml 132 134 132 134 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="R16">16</xref>
###xml 543 562 532 551 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="fig" rid="F3">Fig. 3a, left panel</xref>
###xml 935 955 921 941 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="fig" rid="F3">Fig. 3a, right panel</xref>
###xml 1222 1229 1208 1215 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="fig" rid="F3">Fig. 3b</xref>
It has been shown that transcription of Rag1 and to a lesser extent Rag2 is downregulated in adult DN4 cells relative to DN3 cells [16]. To test whether downregulation of the Rag genes in the DN4 relative to the DN3 population depended on the expression of TCRbeta/gammadelta we analyzed the transcription of Rag1 and Rag2 by quantitative reverse transcriptase (RT) PCR in DN3 and DN4 thymocytes sorted from E15.5, E16.5 and adult wild type thymi. Rag1 transcripts were two to four times less abundant in DN4 thymocytes compared to DN3 cells (Fig. 3a, left panel). More than 80% of DN4 thymocytes on day E15.5 did not express TCRbeta suggesting that downregulation of Rag1 in the DN4 subset on day E15.5 was independent of pre-TCR signalling. Rag2 transcription was also significantly decreased in foetal DN4 thymocytes compared to DN3 cells but the difference in Rag2 expression in the adult thymocyte population was not significant (Fig. 3a, right panel). When foetal and adult Rag1-/- thymocytes were treated with antiCD3 antibody for 24 hours to mimic the pre-TCR signal, downregulation of Rag2 transcription was even greater (six to twelve fold) compared to downregulation of Rag2 in foetal DN4 wild type thymocytes (Fig. 3b). The latter observation suggests that although pre-TCR was not necessary for downregulation of the Rag genes at the DN4 stage, a strong signal can accelerate downregulation of Rag2.
###end p 16

###xml 39 40 33 34 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">&#8722;</sup>

D to J TCRbeta rearrangement in TCRbeta-/gammadelta- DN4 thymocytes
###end title 17
###begin p 18
###xml 66 67 63 64 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">&#8722;</sup>
###xml 78 79 67 68 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">&#8722;</sup>
###xml 169 170 152 153 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">&#8722;</sup>

###xml 248 257 212 221 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="fig" rid="F3">Fig. 3c-d</xref>
###xml 392 394 353 355 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="R17">17</xref>

###xml 494 495 441 442 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">&#8722;</sup>

###xml 640 649 570 579 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="fig" rid="F3">Fig. 3f-g</xref>
###xml 681 682 608 609 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">&#8722;</sup>
###xml 693 694 612 613 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">&#8722;</sup>
To further assess the origin and lineage commitment of the TCRbeta-/gammadelta- DN4 cells we analysed the extent of their DJ TCRbeta rearrangement. We sorted DN4 TCRbeta-/gammadelta- and TCRbeta/gammadelta+ cells from E16.5 foetal wild type thymi (Fig. 3c-d) and performed a PCR-Southern assay to compare the extend of DJ TCRbeta rearrangement in the two cell types, as previously described [17]. We amplified the TCRbeta locus from equal amounts of DNA template from sorted TCRbeta-/gammadelta- and TCRbeta/gammadelta+ DN4 cells using Dbeta2 and Jbeta2.7 specific primers. We observed similar level of DJ rearrangement in both cell types (Fig. 3f-g) demonstrating that the TCRbeta-/gammadelta- DN4 cells are T lineage committed and suggesting that they originate from DN2/DN3 cells.

###begin title 19
TCRbeta expression promotes proliferation in foetal DN4 thymocytes
###end title 19
###begin p 20

A gradual decrease in the proportion of icTCRbeta- DN4 cells during embryogenesis suggests that the foetal DN4 TCRbeta+ subset expands faster than the DN4 TCRbeta- subset. To test whether this is due to increased proliferation we sorted E16.5 foetal DN3 and DN4 cells based on the intracellular expression of beta and gammadelta TCRs and analyzed proliferation. In a typical experiment, propidium iodide DNA staining showed that approximately four-times more TCRbeta+ DN3 (47.91%) and DN4 (41.06%) cells were in cell cycle than in the TCRbeta-/gammadelta- DN3(10.67%) or DN4 (11.22%) subset (Fig. 4a), consistent with a pre-TCR induced signal for proliferation [5, 14, 18-21].

###xml 926 927 873 874 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">+</sup>
###xml 939 946 886 893 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="fig" rid="F4">Fig. 4c</xref>

We also tested the possibility that the percentage of DN4 TCRbeta-/gammadelta- cells decreased due to active apoptosis in this subset. We analyzed the intracellular expression of the active form of caspase-3, as it has recently been shown that pre-TCR upregulates the anti-apoptotic gene BCL2A1 that rescues cells from caspase-3 mediated apoptosis [9]. We were able to detect induction of apoptosis in control, adult DP cells treated for two hours with antiCD3 (Fig. 4b), but the active form of caspase-3 was virtually absent in both icTCRbeta-/gammadelta- and icTCRbeta+ foetal DN4 thymocytes (Fig. 4c) and among DN3 thymocytes (data not shown). In contrast in the adult thymus we were able to detect more apoptotic cells amongst the icTCRbeta-/gammadelta- DN4 subset compared to TCRbeta+ DN4 subset. In a typical experiment 2.91% of icTCRbeta-/gammadelta- DN4 cells were caspase-3 positive compared to 0.25% of the icTCRbeta+ DN4 cells (Fig. 4c). Our data indicate that in the foetal thymus, clonotypic TCRbeta expression promotes proliferation of DN thymocytes. Lack, however, of TCRbeta expression did not immediately induce cell death in foetal DN4 cells, as evidenced by the absence of active caspase-3 expressing cells in the DN4 icTCRbeta-/gammadelta- population. The increased apoptosis that we observed in the adult DN4 icTCRbeta-/gammadelta- population might be attributed to the accumulation of ageing cells in this subset.

###begin title 22
After hydrocortisone treatment differentiation of adult thymocytes to the DN4 stage occurs in the absence of TCRbeta/gammadelta expression
###end title 22
###begin p 23
###xml 616 618 613 615 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="R22">22</xref>
###xml 620 622 617 619 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="R23">23</xref>
###xml 681 683 678 680 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="R24">24</xref>

###xml 923 924 906 907 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">+</sup>
###xml 1176 1178 1159 1161 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="R25">25</xref>
###xml 1603 1610 1586 1593 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="fig" rid="F5">Fig. 5a</xref>
###xml 1842 1849 1822 1829 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="fig" rid="F5">Fig. 5b</xref>

###xml 2050 2051 2024 2025 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">+</sup>
###xml 2147 2168 2121 2142 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="fig" rid="F5">Fig. 5c, middle panel</xref>
###xml 2223 2224 2194 2195 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">+</sup>
###xml 2400 2401 2368 2369 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">+</sup>
###xml 2403 2423 2371 2391 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="fig" rid="F5">Fig. 5c, lower panel</xref>
###xml 2580 2581 2545 2546 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">&#8722;</sup>
###xml 2592 2593 2549 2550 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">&#8722;</sup>
###xml 2609 2610 2563 2564 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">+</sup>
###xml 2691 2692 2642 2643 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">+</sup>
###xml 2710 2717 2661 2668 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="fig" rid="F5">Fig. 5d</xref>
###xml 1087 1091 <span type="species:ncbi:10090">mice</span>
###xml 2311 2315 <span type="species:ncbi:10090">mice</span>
Differentiation of foetal thymocytes occurs in a largely synchronized wave. In adults, however, thymocyte production is continuous and the adult thymus has reached steady state with pre-existing cells at all stages of development and homeostasis between thymocyte populations. The difference in the expression of TCRbeta in the DN4 subset that we observe between foetal and adult thymocytes could be due to a foetal restricted capability of thymocytes to differentiate to the DN4 stage independently of normal pre-TCR signalling, either as a result of differences between foetal and adult hematopoietic progenitors [22, 23] or between the foetal and adult thymic microenvironment [24]. Alternatively, it is possible that adult TCRbeta/gammadelta- thymocytes differentiate to the DN4 stage as efficiently as foetal thymocytes but that their percentage is very low due to the pre-existing high number of proliferating TCRbeta+ DN4 thymocytes that mask their presence. To distinguish between these two hypotheses we synchronized the differentiation of immature thymocytes by treating adult mice with hydrocortisone (HC). HC treatment induces apoptosis of immature DP thymocytes [25] but mature single positive cells survive treatment and the thymus then grows exponentially to recover its normal size and subset distribution a week following the treatment. HC enabled us to study the differentiation of thymocytes in the adult thymus in a synchronized wave and in a rapidly expanding thymus that had not yet reached homeostasis. Three days after HC treatment the thymus contained only 1.27% DP thymocytes (Fig. 5a). Analysis of intracellular expression of TCRbeta in DN3 and DN4 cells, identified by the absence of surface expression of CD3, CD4, CD8, CD44 and B220 markers, the presence of Thy1.2, and presence or absence respectively of CD25 (Fig. 5b) showed that the percentage of cells expressing TCRbeta was equivalent between DN3 (11.59%) and DN4 (10.13%) populations (Fig. 5c, top panel). Four days after HC treatment, the percentage of icTCRbeta+ cells in the DN4 population significantly increased to 48.49% compared to 13.32% of DN3 cells (Fig. 5c, middle panel). Seven days after treatment the percentage of TCRbeta+ DN4 cells had reverted back to the percentage observed in non treated wild type adult mice with typically 15.37% of DN3 and 90.02% of DN4 cells staining positive for icTCRbeta+ (Fig. 5c, lower panel), and 90.36% of thymocytes were CD4+CD8+DP indicating that the thymus had fully recovered (data not shown). Although the absolute number of DN3, DN4 TCRbeta-/gammadelta- and DN4 TCRbeta+ cells increased overtime there was preferential expansion of the DN3 and TCRbeta+ DN4 populations (Fig. 5d).

To assess cell cycle status in the DN4 population, we compared cyclin B1 expression in the TCRbeta-/gammadelta- and TCRbeta+ DN4 thymocytes at three and four days after HC treatment (Fig. 6a and b). Approximately four times more cells in the TCRbeta+ DN4 compartment showed high intensity cyclin B1 staining (Fig. 6b) indicative of cells in the S/G2/M stages of the cell cycle, than in the TCRbeta-/gammadelta- DN4 population, indicating that TCRbeta expression (and hence pre-TCR signalling) induces expansion.

In summary, in the synchronised and rapidly expanding HC treated adult thymus we were able to detect a large percentage of TCRbeta-/gammadelta- adult DN4 thymocytes, similar to that observed in the foetal thymus. Thus, the rarity of TCRbeta-/gammadelta- DN4 cells in the normal adult thymus cannot be attributed to an intrinsic inability of either adult lymphoid or stromal compartments to support pre-TCR independent differentiation to DN4. Our experiments suggest that the decrease in the ratio of TCRbeta-/gammadelta-:TCRbeta+ DN4 cells after treatment is due to pre-TCR induced expansion of the TCRbeta+ subset rather than apoptosis of the TCRbeta-/gammadelta- DN4 population. The detection of significantly more apoptotic cells in the TCRbeta-/gammadelta- DN4 subset in non treated adults and seven days after HC treatment seems to reflect ageing cells accumulating in this subset rather than immediate induction of apoptosis due to lack of pre-TCR signalling.

###begin title 27
Reduced proliferation and increased apoptosis in foetal Rag1-/- DN4 compared to DN3 cells
###end title 27
###begin p 28
###xml 555 562 555 562 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="fig" rid="F7">Fig. 7a</xref>
###xml 751 758 751 758 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="fig" rid="F7">Fig. 7b</xref>
To account for the virtual absence of DN4 cells in Rag1-/- adult thymus compared to the readily detectable population in the Rag1-/- foetal thymus, we analyzed proliferation and apoptosis of Rag1-/- DN3 and DN4 cells during embryonic development and in the adult Rag1-/- thymus. On all embryonic days tested the percentage of DN4 cells that were in cell cycle as assessed by the intensity of cyclin B1 staining, was lower than the corresponding percentage of DN3 cells, and the percentage of cycling cells decreased over time in both DN3 and DN4 subsets (Fig. 7a). Interestingly, the ratio of cycling cells in the DN3 relative to the DN4 population increased six fold between foetal and adult thymus, accounting for the decline in the DN4 population (Fig. 7b).
###end p 28
###begin p 29
###xml 238 251 238 251 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="fig" rid="F7">Fig. 7c and d</xref>
###xml 435 442 435 442 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="fig" rid="F7">Fig. 7c</xref>

Staining for the active form of caspase-3 revealed that contrary to the data obtained from the wild type foetal thymus, apoptotic cells were present in Rag1-/- foetal DN3 and DN4 subsets and their percentage was higher in the DN4 subset (Fig. 7c and d). In a typical experiment the percentage of apoptotic cells in the DN3 subset varied from 0.31% and 0.63% for embryonic days E15.5 to E17.5 and from 0.89% to 1.58% in the DN4 subset (Fig. 7c). In the adult Rag1-/- thymus we were only able to detect a very small percentage of active caspase-3 positive cells in both the DN3 (0.02%) and the DN4 (0.14%) subsets (Fig. 7c) and there was no significant difference in the percentage of apoptotic cells between the two subsets (Fig. 7 d). The observed increase in apoptosis in the foetal DN4 subset relative to the DN3 subset during ontogeny provides an additional explanation for the decline of the DN4 population.

###xml 442 443 406 407 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">&#8722;</sup>
###xml 448 449 412 413 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">&#8722;</sup>
###xml 453 454 417 418 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">&#8722;</sup>
###xml 246 250 <span type="species:ncbi:10090">mice</span>
Here we showed that in the foetal thymus downregulation of CD25 cell surface expression and differentiation to the DN4 stage can occur independently of TCRbeta expression and hence pre-TCR signalling. Rare TCRbeta-/gammadelta- DN4 cells in adult mice have been previously reported [7, 15] but we showed that in contrast to the adult thymus, most DN4 thymocytes in the E15.5 foetal thymus were icTCRbeta-/gammadelta-. These TCRbeta-/gammadelta- CD25-CD44- thymocytes were T lineage, based on expression of Thy1 and icCD3, and TCRbeta DJ rearrangement. Likewise, we found a normal percentage of DN4 cells in the early Rag1-/- foetal thymus, despite their inherent inability for TCRbeta/gammadelta rearrangement and expression.
###end p 31
###begin p 32
###xml 80 81 77 78 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">&#8722;</sup>
###xml 92 93 81 82 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">&#8722;</sup>
###xml 119 120 105 106 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">&#8722;</sup>
###xml 131 132 109 110 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">&#8722;</sup>
###xml 547 548 511 512 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">&#8722;</sup>
###xml 559 560 515 516 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">&#8722;</sup>
###xml 686 687 639 640 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">&#8722;</sup>
###xml 698 699 643 644 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">&#8722;</sup>
###xml 56 60 <span type="species:ncbi:10090">mice</span>
Most DN4 cells that emerged after HC treatment of adult mice were also icTCRbeta-/gammadelta-. The abundance of TCRbeta-/gammadelta- DN4 cells in the HC treated adult thymus demonstrated that their production was not foetal-restricted and that both adult lymphoid and stromal elements are capable of supporting differentiation to DN4 in the absence of TCRbeta/gammadelta expression. This similarity with the foetal thymus could reflect the synchronicity of the transition in both cases (enabling the identification of normally undetectable TCRbeta-/gammadelta- that would physiologically make the transition from DN3 to DN4 in the adult thymus). Alternatively, the production of TCRbeta-/gammadelta- DN4 cells in the adult HC-treated thymus could be a direct consequence of the treatment, as regeneration and rapid growth are induced. During embryogenesis the foetal thymus also expands exponentially. It therefore remains to be determined to what extent pre-TCR independent mechanisms of differentiation from DN3 to DN4 occur in the adult thymus under physiological conditions, but clearly TCRbeta/gammadelta expression independent differentiation from DN3 to DN4 is important in the foetal thymus. It likewise remains to be determined if low-level signalling through other components of the pre-TCR complex in the absence of TCRbeta expression, such as CD3 polypeptides, would be sufficient to drive downregulation of CD25.
###end p 32
###begin p 33
###xml 150 154 <span type="species:ncbi:10090">mice</span>
The fact that DN4 thymocytes are found in the foetal Rag1-/- thymus is important for the interpretation of the phenotype of other genetically altered mice that have arrested thymocyte development. For example, particularly in the foetus, the appearance of DN4 cells may have lead to the assumption that a mutation affected processes following pre-TCR signalling when in fact this might not necessarily be the case.
###end p 33
###begin p 34
###xml 102 103 99 100 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="R5">5</xref>
###xml 105 107 102 104 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="R14">14</xref>
###xml 109 111 106 108 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="R18">18</xref>
###xml 112 114 109 111 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="R21">21</xref>
###xml 215 216 209 210 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">+</sup>
###xml 308 309 299 300 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">&#8722;</sup>
###xml 320 321 303 304 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">&#8722;</sup>
###xml 394 395 374 375 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">+</sup>
###xml 407 408 384 385 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">&#8722;</sup>
###xml 419 420 388 389 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">&#8722;</sup>
###xml 676 677 642 643 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">&#8722;</sup>
###xml 688 689 646 647 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">&#8722;</sup>
###xml 807 808 765 766 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="R7">7</xref>
###xml 810 812 768 770 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="R26">26</xref>
There are several studies indicating that pre-TCR signalling and beta-selection induce proliferation [5, 14, 18-21]. We also demonstrated directly, both by PI and anti-cyclin B1 staining that four times more TCRbeta+ cells were dividing in both the foetal and HC treated adult thymus, compared to the TCRbeta-/gammadelta- DN4 population. We found more proliferating cells in both foetal TCRbeta+ and TCRbeta-/gammadelta- DN4 cells than in their adult counterparts, consistent with the exponential increase in thymocyte number during embryogenesis. In addition, it is worth noting that we detected some cell division in all thymocyte populations examined, including the TCRbeta-/gammadelta- DN4 population, suggesting the existence of pre-TCR independent mechanisms of proliferation, as previously reported [7, 26].
###end p 34
###begin p 35
###xml 100 101 100 101 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="R9">9</xref>
###xml 155 156 152 153 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">&#8722;</sup>
###xml 201 202 195 196 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">+</sup>
###xml 208 209 202 203 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="R7">7</xref>
###xml 248 249 242 243 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="R7">7</xref>
###xml 410 411 393 394 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">+</sup>
###xml 443 444 426 427 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">&#8722;</sup>
###xml 550 551 530 531 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">&#8722;</sup>
###xml 562 563 534 535 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">&#8722;</sup>
###xml 593 594 562 563 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">+</sup>
###xml 626 633 595 602 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="fig" rid="F4">Fig. 4c</xref>
###xml 886 887 855 856 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="R4">4</xref>
###xml 889 890 858 859 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="R6">6</xref>
###xml 1012 1013 967 968 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">&#8722;</sup>
###xml 1024 1025 971 972 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">&#8722;</sup>
###xml 1214 1215 1158 1159 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">+</sup>
###xml 1232 1233 1176 1177 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="R9">9</xref>
###xml 1282 1283 1223 1224 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">&#8722;</sup>
###xml 1294 1295 1227 1228 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">&#8722;</sup>
###xml 1488 1489 1418 1419 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">&#8722;</sup>
###xml 1554 1555 1481 1482 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">+</sup>
It has been shown that the pre-TCR can promote cell survival by induction of anti-apoptotic agents [9] and that cell death is increase in the adult TCRbeta- DN4 population relative to the adult TCRbeta+ DN4 [7]. The experiments of Falk and others [7] correlated cell surface CD69 expression with absence of icTCRbeta/gammadelta in the DN4 population, and then demonstrated relatively more apoptosis in the CD69+ DN4 population than in the CD69- DN4 cells. Consistent with this study, our analysis confirms directly an increase in apoptosis of TCRbeta-/gammadelta- DN4 cells relative to TCRbeta+ DN4 cells in the adult thymus (Fig. 4c). Our experiments suggest, however, that absence of the pre-TCR complex does not immediately induce apoptosis as we did not observe apoptosis amongst the foetal or recently differentiated adult DN4 cells either by caspase-3 or annexin-V staining (Fig. 4, 6 and data not shown) regardless of the presence or absence of TCRbeta/gammadelta. The increase in apoptosis in the TCRbeta-/gammadelta- DN4 population on successive days after HC treatment is consistent with accumulation of ageing cells and absence of pre-TCR induced pro-survival signals that are upregulated in the TCRbeta+ DN4 population [9]. The proportion but not the number of icTCRbeta-/gammadelta- DN4 cells greatly decreased over time, and was very low in the fully developed adult thymus. Taken together our experiments indicate that the age-dependent decline in the proportion of TCRbeta- DN4 cells is due to pre-TCR induced proliferation of the TCRbeta+ DN4 subset, rather than immediate induction of apoptosis in cells that fail to pass the beta-selection check-point at the DN3 stage.
###end p 35
###begin p 36
###xml 23 24 20 21 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">&#8722;</sup>
###xml 35 36 24 25 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">&#8722;</sup>
###xml 137 138 123 124 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">+</sup>
###xml 237 238 220 221 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">&#8722;</sup>
###xml 249 250 224 225 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">&#8722;</sup>
###xml 1353 1355 1317 1319 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="R26">26</xref>
The fate of the TCRbeta-/gammadelta- DN4 cells is unclear. In the foetal and HC treated adult thymus, there was no enrichment for TCRbeta+ cells in the DN4 subset compared to the DN3 population. It is possible that some wild type TCRbeta-/gammadelta- DN4 cells eventually rearrange and express TCRbeta or TCRgammadelta and continue their maturation, but the foetal Rag1-/- DN4 thymocytes clearly cannot progress beyond the DN4 stage as neither DP nor SP cells are observed in either the foetal or adult Rag1-/- thymi. We showed that the foetal Rag1-/- DN4 population is disadvantaged compared to the DN3 population due to both reduced proliferation and increased apoptosis, explaining the virtual absence of DN4 cells in the adult thymus. In addition, the presence of apoptotic Rag1-/- DN3 and DN4 cells, not detectable in the equivalent wild type populations and the reduced proliferation in the Rag1-/- foetal compared to wild type thymocyte populations seem to reflect differences in the thymic microenvironment of Rag1-/- and wild type thymus. This is consistent with a previous report showing that the reduced proliferation of adult Rag2-/- compared to wild type thymocytes was at least partly due to differences in the thymus environment rather than the absence of cell autonomous, pre-TCR related proliferation signals in the Rag2-/- thymocytes [26].
###end p 36
###begin p 37
In conclusion we have shown that in the foetal thymus the transition from the DN3 to the DN4 stage is frequently independent of pre-TCR signalling. Our data also suggest that pre-TCR signalling induces proliferation and that lack of it does not immediately induce apoptosis.
###end p 37

###xml 752 754 703 705 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="R17">17</xref>
TCRbeta-/gammadelta- and TCRbeta/gammadelta+ DN4 thymocytes were sorted from E16.5 foetal thymi as described above, DNA was extracted using the DNeasy tissue kit (Qiagen) and D to J PCR-Southern hybridisation assay was carried out as previously described [17]. In brief, 24 cycles of PCR amplification for the D to J TCRbeta rearrangements were performed using the following primers: Dbeta2 (5') GTA GGC ACC TGT GGG GAA GAA ACT and Jbeta2.7 (3') TGA GAG CTG TCT CCT ACT ATC GAT T and the amplified products were quantified by Southern blotting using a TCRbeta probe amplified from germline (unrearranged) TCRbeta locus with Dbeta2 and Jbeta2.7 primers, corresponding to the genomic region between the TCRDbeta2 and TCRJbeta2.7 as previously described [17].
###end p 53
