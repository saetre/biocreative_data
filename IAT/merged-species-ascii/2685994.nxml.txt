Does Toxoplasma gondii Infection Affect the Levels of IgE and Cytokines (IL-5, IL-6, IL-10, IL-12, and TNF-alpha)?

###xml 1070 1079 <span type="species:ncbi:5811">T. gondii</span>
In the study performed in a group of patients infected with T. gondii, we evaluated Th2 humoral response (IL-5, IL-6, IL-10) and Th1 cell response (IL-12, TNF-alpha). The study objective was to assess the effect of T. gondii on chosen indices of the immune response. The study involved 52 women infected with T. gondii (aged 18-42 years) prior to antiparasitic treatment. In all the patients, we found IgM (index >0.7) and IgG which exceeded 300 IU/ml. The control group (C) consisted of 40 healthy women aged 18-46 years. In the study group (T) and in the control group (C), the levels of IgE, IL-5, IL-6, IL-10, IL-12, and TNF-alpha were determined. In our study, T. gondii patients had twofold higher levels of IL-5 and IL-6 as compared to healthy subjects, which seems to confirm the presence of an inflammatory state. We found the level of IL-10 to be fivefold higher in the course of toxoplasmosis than in healthy controls. The levels of IL-12 and TNF-alpha were comparable to those observed in healthy controls. The study has revealed that patients infected with T. gondii show increased production of the humoral response cytokines, whereas the generation of the cell response cytokines remains unchanged.

###begin title 4
1. Introduction
###end title 4
###begin p 5
###xml 0 18 0 18 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"> Toxoplasma gondii</italic>
###xml 69 77 69 77 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">Coccidae</italic>
###xml 79 88 79 88 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">T. gondii</italic>
###xml 257 270 257 270 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">Toxoplasmosis</italic>
###xml 631 632 631 632 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="B1">1</xref>
###xml 633 634 633 634 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="B4">4</xref>
###xml 890 891 890 891 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="B5">5</xref>
###xml 1 18 <span type="species:ncbi:5811">Toxoplasma gondii</span>
###xml 79 88 <span type="species:ncbi:5811">T. gondii</span>
###xml 306 312 <span type="species:ncbi:9606">people</span>
###xml 669 674 <span type="species:ncbi:9606">human</span>
 Toxoplasma gondii is a ruthless intracellular parasite belonging to Coccidae. T. gondii occurs in three forms: tachyzoites, bradyzoites (in tissues), and sporozoites. The parasite locates in the brain, heart, lungs, and most frequently in the lymph nodes. Toxoplasmosis is a disease affecting 500 million people worldwide. The seroprevalence varies (from 5% to 90%), depending on geographical location, age, habit of eating raw meat or unwashed fruit and vegetables, and general level of hygiene. The incidence of infections is higher in warmer and humid climate and increases with age. The disease can be congenital or acquired [1-4]. The parasite after invading the human body multiplies inside the cell, causing damage to the reticuloendothelial system. Rapid multiplication of the parasite and formation of the so-called pseudocysts are characteristics of the acute phase of invasion [5]. 
###end p 5
###begin p 6
###xml 467 472 467 468 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">&#945;</italic>
###xml 537 538 533 534 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="B6">6</xref>
###xml 1083 1088 1079 1080 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">&#945;</italic>
###xml 1240 1241 1232 1233 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="B7">7</xref>
###xml 1242 1244 1234 1236 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="B10">10</xref>
In parasitic invasions, an increase is observed in the production of IgE antibodies, especially in helminth infections. This defect results from disturbances in the regulation of antibody production by Th cells, which promotes local inflammatory reaction. Via release of mediators from mast cells IgE participates in the reaction of antibody-dependent cellular cytotoxicity (ADCC). Cytotoxic activity of eosinophils is increased under the influence of cytokines (TNF-alpha and IL-5) released by mast cells, lymphocytes, and macrophages [6]. Th2 lymphocytes synthesize specific cytokines (IL-4, IL-5, IL-6, IL-10, IL-13, and IL-14), which play a major role in the pathogenesis of parasitic diseases. IL-5 is the major cytokine responsible for the increase in the eosinophil population in parasitoses, whereas IL-6 stimulates production of antibodies and exerts a proinflammatory effect by stimulating the generation of acute phase proteins. IL-10 and IL-12 control the type of the immune response. The former inhibits cytokine synthesis and by blocking the production of IL-6 and TNF-alpha causes an advantage of the response occurring with Th2 involvement and B cell activation. However, IL-12 facilitates formation of a Th1 type response [7-10].
###end p 6
###begin p 7
###xml 69 80 69 80 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">T. gondii, </italic>
###xml 185 190 185 186 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">&#945;</italic>
###xml 263 272 259 268 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">T. gondii</italic>
###xml 46 54 <span type="species:ncbi:9606">patients</span>
###xml 69 78 <span type="species:ncbi:5811">T. gondii</span>
###xml 263 272 <span type="species:ncbi:5811">T. gondii</span>
In the current study, performed on a group of patients infected with T. gondii, we evaluated humoral response (IL-5, IL-6, IL-10 secreted by Th2 cells) and cell response (IL-12 and TNF-alpha secreted by Th1 cells). The study objective was to assess the effect of T. gondii on chosen indices of the immune response. 
###end p 7
###begin title 8
2. Materials and Methods
###end title 8
###begin p 9
###xml 42 51 42 51 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">T. gondii</italic>
###xml 467 477 467 477 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">T. gondii,</italic>
###xml 620 629 620 629 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">T. gondii</italic>
###xml 1052 1061 1052 1061 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">T. gondii</italic>
###xml 22 27 <span type="species:ncbi:9606">women</span>
###xml 42 51 <span type="species:ncbi:5811">T. gondii</span>
###xml 115 120 <span type="species:ncbi:9606">women</span>
###xml 335 340 <span type="species:ncbi:9606">women</span>
###xml 383 391 <span type="species:ncbi:9606">patients</span>
###xml 467 476 <span type="species:ncbi:5811">T. gondii</span>
###xml 506 511 <span type="species:ncbi:9606">women</span>
###xml 620 629 <span type="species:ncbi:5811">T. gondii</span>
###xml 947 952 <span type="species:ncbi:9606">women</span>
###xml 1052 1061 <span type="species:ncbi:5811">T. gondii</span>
The study involved 52 women infected with T. gondii (aged 18-42 years) prior to antiparasitic treatment. All these women had swollen cervical and nuchal lymph nodes (toxoplasmic lymphadenopathy), although other diseases were excluded (mononucleosis, cytomegaly, granulomatosis, lymphomas, leukemias, AIDS). Tests were performed in the women who were not able to conceive. In all the patients (group T), the levels of specific IgM and IgG antibodies, directed against T. gondii, were determined. In all the women, IgM was present (index > 0.7) and the level of IgG exceeded 300 IU/ml. The diagnosis of the early phase of T. gondii infection was based on the detection of a significant increase in the levels of specific IgG antibodies of low avidity, with the co-occurrence of IgM antibodies. Assays were performed using ELFA on an immunoserological analyzer VIDAS with a set of reagents (bioMerieux). The control group (C) consisted of 40 healthy women aged 18-46 years, clinically asymptomatic, who underwent prophylactic examinations and had no anti-T. gondii specific antibodies.
###end p 9
###begin p 10
###xml 53 58 53 54 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">&#945;</italic>
The levels of IgE, IL-5, IL-6, IL-10, IL-12, and TNF-alpha were determined in the study group (T) and in the control group (C). 
###end p 10
###begin title 11
2.1. Determination of Total IgE
###end title 11
###begin p 12
###xml 38 40 38 39 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">&#956;</italic>
Total IgE level was determined in 100 mul serum samples by ELFA (bioMerieux, France) on an immunoserological analyzer VIDAS. The sensitivity threshold of the method is 0.5 KIU/l. 
###end p 12
###begin title 13
###xml 85 90 85 86 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">&#945;</italic>
2.2. Determination of the Levels of Interleukins (IL-5, IL-6, IL- 10, IL-12) and TNF-alpha
###end title 13
###begin p 14
###xml 35 40 35 36 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">&#945;</italic>
###xml 116 121 <span type="species:ncbi:9606">human</span>
The levels of interleukins and TNF-alpha were determined in blood serum using ELISA method with a set of Quantikine human (R&D, USA). 
###end p 14
###begin p 15
###xml 215 216 215 216 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">P</italic>
###xml 319 320 319 320 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">t</italic>
The results were subjected to statistical analysis using the program Statistica 8.0. The differences were considered statistically significant when the value of test function was at the level of significance set at P < .05. The Kolgomorov consistency test was used for features consistent with normal distribution, the t-Student test for comparisons between the groups and the Mann-Whitney test for traits inconsistent with this distribution. 
###end p 15
###begin title 16
3. Results
###end title 16
###begin p 17
###xml 27 36 27 36 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">T. gondii</italic>
###xml 234 235 230 231 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">P</italic>
###xml 4 12 <span type="species:ncbi:9606">patients</span>
###xml 27 36 <span type="species:ncbi:5811">T. gondii</span>
 In patients infected with T. gondii the mean IgE level was 74.7 +/- 105.5 IU/l, being twice as high as the mean IgE value in healthy individuals (37.7 +/- 27.6 IU/l). The difference between these values is statistically significant (P < .05).
###end p 17
###begin p 18
###xml 26 35 26 35 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">T. gondii</italic>
###xml 234 235 230 231 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">P</italic>
###xml 26 35 <span type="species:ncbi:5811">T. gondii</span>
The mean level of IL-5 in T. gondii infection was 7.02 +/- 2.9 pg/ml. However, in healthy subjects, the IL-5 concentration was markedly lower (mean 3.5 +/- 1.6 pg/ml). The difference between these values is statistically significant (P < .0001). 
###end p 18
###begin p 19
###xml 26 36 26 36 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">T. gondii-</italic>
###xml 267 268 263 264 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">P</italic>
###xml 26 35 <span type="species:ncbi:5811">T. gondii</span>
###xml 45 53 <span type="species:ncbi:9606">patients</span>
The mean level of IL-6 in T. gondii-infected patients was 5.21 +/- 3.5 pg/ml, being twice as high as the mean value in healthy controls (2.45 +/- 1.4 pg/ml). The difference in the values of IL-6 between the infected and healthy subjects is statistically significant (P < .0001). 
###end p 19
###begin p 20
###xml 80 93 80 93 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">toxoplasmosis</italic>
###xml 258 259 254 255 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">P</italic>
###xml 328 333 324 325 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">&#945;</italic>
###xml 342 351 334 343 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">T. gondii</italic>
###xml 446 447 438 439 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">P</italic>
###xml 342 351 <span type="species:ncbi:5811">T. gondii</span>
###xml 352 360 <span type="species:ncbi:9606">patients</span>
The level of IL-10 was found to be most highly differentiated. In the course of toxoplasmosis its mean level was 4.50 +/- 2.9 pg/ml whereas in healthy controls only 0.76 +/- 0.2 pg/ml. The difference between these values is highly statistically significant (P < .0001). However, a comparison of the mean levels of IL-12 and TNF-alpha between T. gondii patients and healthy subjects did not reveal any statistically significant differences (0.4 < P < .5).
###end p 20
###begin title 21
4. Discussion
###end title 21
###begin p 22
###xml 19 28 19 28 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">T. gondii</italic>
###xml 136 138 136 138 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="B11">11</xref>
###xml 195 204 195 204 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">T. gondii</italic>
###xml 266 279 266 279 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">toxoplasmosis</italic>
###xml 528 530 528 530 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="B12">12</xref>
###xml 609 618 609 618 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">T. gondii</italic>
###xml 877 890 877 890 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">toxoplasmosis</italic>
###xml 949 962 949 962 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">toxoplasmosis</italic>
###xml 964 966 964 966 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="B13">13</xref>
###xml 985 987 985 987 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="B14">14</xref>
###xml 1041 1050 1041 1050 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">T. gondii</italic>
###xml 19 28 <span type="species:ncbi:5811">T. gondii</span>
###xml 87 92 <span type="species:ncbi:9606">women</span>
###xml 195 204 <span type="species:ncbi:5811">T. gondii</span>
###xml 586 594 <span type="species:ncbi:9606">patients</span>
###xml 609 618 <span type="species:ncbi:5811">T. gondii</span>
###xml 1041 1050 <span type="species:ncbi:5811">T. gondii</span>
 The prevalence of T. gondii has been reported to increase with age (>40 years) and in women consuming raw meat, vegetables, and fruit [11]. In the acute phase, directly after invading the body, T. gondii begins to multiply rapidly. In a majority of cases, acquired toxoplasmosis is asymptomatic. In the second week of infection, specific IgM antibodies are present in the blood. IgE antibodies also appear at the same time, slightly preceding specific IgA antibodies, but they are not found 4 months after the infection onset [12]. In our study, the concentration of IgE antibodies in patients infected with T. gondii was statistically significantly higher than in healthy subjects, which was accompanied by an elevated level of IgM antibodies, thus suggesting an acute form of invasion. Increased IgE level correlates with early acute inflammation or with a reactive form of toxoplasmosis. However, negative IgE test result does not exclude acute toxoplasmosis [13]. Dziubek et al. [14] who determined specific antibodies directed against T. gondii (IgM, IgG, IgA) and CD4+ and CD8+ lymphocyte subpopulations found more substantial involvement of CD8+, whereas lower expression of CD4+ lymphocytes might be due to a suppressive action of the parasite.
###end p 22
###begin p 23
###xml 144 153 144 153 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">T. gondii</italic>
###xml 383 385 383 385 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="B15">15</xref>
###xml 412 426 412 426 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">Toxocara canis</italic>
###xml 476 481 476 477 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">&#945;</italic>
###xml 548 550 544 546 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="B16">16</xref>
###xml 578 580 574 576 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="B17">17</xref>
###xml 620 629 616 625 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">T. gondii</italic>
###xml 824 829 820 821 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">&#947;</italic>
###xml 949 951 941 943 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="B18">18</xref>
###xml 1010 1023 1002 1015 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">toxoplasmosis</italic>
###xml 1129 1130 1121 1122 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="B7">7</xref>
###xml 121 129 <span type="species:ncbi:9606">Patients</span>
###xml 144 153 <span type="species:ncbi:5811">T. gondii</span>
###xml 412 426 <span type="species:ncbi:6265">Toxocara canis</span>
###xml 620 629 <span type="species:ncbi:5811">T. gondii</span>
The humoral response involves IL-5 and IL-6 that exert a proinflammatory effect and IL-10 inhibiting the action of IL-6. Patients infected with T. gondii had twofold higher values of IL-5 as compared to healthy subjects. IL-5 prolongs eosinophil survival, stimulates eosinophil degranulation and production of reactive oxygen species, and exerts a chemotactic effect on these cells [15]. It has been proved that Toxocara canis invasion promotes release of IL-5, IL-8, and TNF-alpha, which is accompanied by eosinophilia with an inflammatory state [16]. However, Nickdel et al. [17] have shown that at the early stage of T. gondii infection, coexisting with eosinophilia and increased level of IL-5, pathological changes are prominent in the small intestine, which is accompanied by a simultaneous reduction in IL-12 and IFN-gamma. We also noted an increase in the level of IL-5 and a decrease in the level of IL-12. However, Filisetti and Candolfi [18] revealed an IL-5 induced increase in IL-12 production in toxoplasmosis. A decrease in the production of IL-12 and inhibition of Th1 response have been reported by Lang et al. [7].
###end p 23
###begin p 24
###xml 14 23 14 23 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">T. gondii</italic>
###xml 14 23 <span type="species:ncbi:5811">T. gondii</span>
###xml 24 32 <span type="species:ncbi:9606">patients</span>
In our study, T. gondii patients had a twofold higher level of IL-6 as compared to healthy subjects, which seems to confirm the presence of an inflammatory state. The major function of IL-6 is the involvement in the immune response through the action on lymphocytes B. It is a mediator responsible for the production of acute phase proteins and increased cytotoxic activity of NK cells. IL-6 is an early and sensitive, although nonspecific, marker of inflammatory states.
###end p 24
###begin p 25

###xml 273 282 <span type="species:ncbi:5811">T. gondii</span>
###xml 595 604 <span type="species:ncbi:5811">T. gondii</span>
###xml 672 681 <span type="species:ncbi:5811">T. gondii</span>
###xml 770 779 <span type="species:ncbi:5811">T. gondii</span>
We found the level of IL-10 to be fivefold higher in the course of toxoplasmosis than in healthy controls. Therefore, we could have expected a decrease in IL-6 production, which was however not found. IL-10 plays an essential role in the inflammatory response during acute T. gondii infection, since it inhibits the cellular-type immune response (IL-12, TNF-alpha) and inflammatory response (IL-6) [17, 19]. IL-10 counteracts the harmful effects of the inflammatory response which is based on the increased production of TNF-alpha, IFN-gamma, and NO associated with intestinal multiplication of T. gondii [20]. IL-10 is able to deactivate macrophages, induce IFN-gamma by T. gondii, and facilitate intracellular parasite survival. IL-10 induces immunosuppression during T. gondii invasion, which is beneficial both for the host and the parasite [7].

###xml 149 158 <span type="species:ncbi:5811">T. gondii</span>
###xml 589 598 <span type="species:ncbi:5811">T. gondii</span>
###xml 707 711 <span type="species:ncbi:10090">mice</span>
Also the cellular response, involved among others IL-12 and TNF-alpha, plays an important role in parasitic invasions. In our patients infected with T. gondii, the levels of IL-12 and TNF-alpha were comparable to those observed in healthy controls. IL-12 was found to increase the production of IgG but not to inhibit IgE. IFN-gamma, IL-2, and IL-12 are involved in the protection against parasitic invasions. IL-12 stimulates the production of IFN-gamma and TNF-alpha and activates lymphocyte cytotoxicity [1]. Involvement of IFN-gamma and IL-10 in the immune mechanisms directed against T. gondii has been described by Kang et al. [21]. According to Filisetti and Candolfi [18], secretion of IFN-gamma in mice increases the phagocytic activity of macrophages and cytotoxic activity of CD8+T lymphocytes. Increased production of IFN-gamma is strongly correlated with parasite virulence and enhanced apoptosis.

In our study, in the course of toxoplasmosis the levels of TNF-alpha and IL-12 did not change, whereas the concentration of IL-5 increased. However, as reported by Lang et al. (2007) T. gondii inhibits production of TNF-alpha and IL-12. TNF-alpha is a cytokine of inflammatory and immune response and together with IL-6 can enhance proliferation and differentiation of B lymphocytes [7]. TNF-alpha activates eosinophil cytotoxicity toward protozoa and induces secretion of acute phase proteins via IL-6 production. TNF-alpha and IFN-gamma have antiproliferative properties. In toxoplasmosis, TNF-alpha appears to be indispensable for macrophage activation and inhibition of parasite replication; however, this is possible only in cooperation with IFN-gamma.
