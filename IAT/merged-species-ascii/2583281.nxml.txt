The Sex-Determination Genes fruitless and doublesex Specify a Neural Substrate Required for Courtship Song

###begin p 3
###xml 69 79 69 79 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">Drosophila</italic>

###xml 664 667 664 667 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">dsx</italic>

###xml 927 930 927 930 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">fru</italic>
###xml 935 936 935 936 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">M</sup>
###xml 994 997 994 997 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">dsx</italic>
###xml 1002 1003 1002 1003 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">M</sup>
###xml 1019 1020 1019 1020 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">M</sup>
###xml 1085 1086 1085 1086 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">M</sup>
###xml 1165 1167 1165 1167 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="R14">14</xref>
###xml 1200 1201 1200 1201 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">M</sup>
Courtship song is a critical component of male courtship behavior in Drosophila, making the female more receptive to copulation and communicating species-specific information [1-6]. Sex mosaic studies have shown that the sex of certain regions of the central nervous system (CNS) is critical to song production [7]. Our examination of one of these regions, the mesothoracic ganglion (Msg), revealed the coexpression of two sex-determination genes, fruitless (fru) and doublesex (dsx). Because both genes are involved in creating a sexually dimorphic CNS [8, 9] and are necessary for song production [10-13], we investigated the individual contributions of fru and dsx to the specification of a male CNS and song production. We show a novel requirement for dsx in specifying a sexually dimorphic population of fru-expressing neurons in the Msg. Moreover, by using females constitutively expressing the male-specific isoforms of fru (FruM), we show a critical requirement for the male isoform of dsx (DsxM), alongside FruM, in the specification of courtship song. Therefore, although FruM expression is sufficient for the performance of many male-specific behaviors [14], we have shown that without DsxM, the determination of a male-specific CNS and thus a full complement of male behaviors are not realized.

###begin title 4
Results and Discussion
###end title 4
###begin p 5
###xml 22 45 22 45 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">Drosophila melanogaster</italic>
###xml 321 323 321 323 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="R15">15</xref>
###xml 325 327 325 327 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="R16">16</xref>
###xml 552 553 552 553 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="R7">7</xref>
###xml 555 557 555 557 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="R15">15</xref>
###xml 559 561 559 561 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="R17">17</xref>
###xml 562 564 562 564 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="R21">21</xref>
###xml 610 619 610 619 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">fruitless</italic>
###xml 621 624 621 624 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">fru</italic>
###xml 685 687 685 687 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="R10">10</xref>
###xml 689 691 689 691 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="R12">12</xref>
###xml 692 694 692 694 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="R14">14</xref>
###xml 696 698 696 698 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="R22">22</xref>
###xml 22 45 <span type="species:ncbi:7227">Drosophila melanogaster</span>
Courtship behavior in Drosophila melanogaster consists of a sequence of behaviors performed by males to interest females in copulation. The male orients to the female, follows her, taps her abdomen with his foreleg, sings a species-specific courtship song, licks her genitals, attempts copulation, and finally copulates [15, 16]. Sex mosaic studies have shown that the sex of the central nervous system (CNS) is critical to the performance of these behaviors, suggesting that sex determination in the CNS is required for male sexual behavior in flies [7, 15, 17-21]. In particular, one sex-determination gene, fruitless (fru), is a key regulator of many steps in the courtship ritual [10, 12-14, 22].
###end p 5
###begin p 6
###xml 29 32 29 32 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">fru</italic>
###xml 322 325 322 325 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">fru</italic>
###xml 339 340 339 340 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">M</sup>
###xml 352 354 352 354 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="R10">10</xref>
###xml 356 358 356 358 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="R13">13</xref>
###xml 360 362 360 362 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="R22">22</xref>
###xml 363 365 363 365 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="R25">25</xref>
###xml 434 436 434 436 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="R23">23</xref>
###xml 438 440 438 440 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="R25">25</xref>
###xml 441 443 441 443 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="R28">28</xref>
Transcripts derived from the fru P1 promoter are spliced in females by the sex-specific splice factor Transformer (Tra) in conjunction with the non-sex-specific Transformer-2 (Tra-2), introducing a premature stop codon into female P1 transcripts. In males, a default splice occurs, giving rise to a class of male-specific fru isoforms (FruM proteins) [10, 13, 22-25] that are expressed in the CNS and peripheral nervous system (PNS) [23, 25-28] in regions associated with male-specific behaviors.
###end p 6
###begin p 7
###xml 34 35 34 35 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">M</sup>
###xml 105 107 105 107 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="R14">14</xref>
###xml 203 205 203 205 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="R14">14</xref>
###xml 224 227 224 227 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">fru</italic>
The constitutive expression of FruM isoforms in females triggers many male-specific courtship behaviors [14]. However, these females perform subnormal amounts of courtship and do not attempt copulation [14], suggesting that fru alone cannot specify all male courtship behaviors.
###end p 7

###xml 35 38 35 38 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">dsx</italic>

###xml 473 475 473 475 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="R11">11</xref>
###xml 477 479 477 479 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="R34">34</xref>
###xml 492 495 492 495 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">dsx</italic>
###xml 523 526 523 526 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">fru</italic>
###xml 602 604 602 604 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="R25">25</xref>
###xml 683 686 683 686 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">fru</italic>
###xml 691 694 691 694 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">dsx</italic>
We examined the role of doublesex (dsx), another sex-determination gene, in the specification of male sexual behavior. dsx transcripts also undergo sex-specific splicing by Tra, producing male- and female-specific isoforms: DsxM and DsxF, respectively [29, 30]. dsx is responsible for somatic sexual differentiation [15, 31-33] and aspects of sex-specific development in the CNS [8]. dsx is also expressed in the CNS and is necessary for wild-type courtship song in males [11, 34]. Recently, dsx was shown to act alongside fru in the differentiation of male-specific neurons in the abdominal ganglion [25]; however, few other studies have examined the relative contributions of both fru and dsx in specifying a male-specific CNS and regulating male sexual behavior. Therefore, this study examined the individual contributions of both genes in specifying courtship song.
###end p 8

###xml 3 4 3 4 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">M</sup>
FruM Is Not Sufficient for Courtship Song
###end title 9
###begin p 10
###xml 18 41 18 41 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">Drosophila melanogaster</italic>
###xml 102 103 102 103 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="R4">4</xref>
###xml 105 106 105 106 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="R6">6</xref>
###xml 277 278 277 278 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="R4">4</xref>
###xml 280 281 280 281 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="R6">6</xref>
###xml 392 393 392 393 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="R1">1</xref>
###xml 394 395 394 395 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="R3">3</xref>
###xml 18 41 <span type="species:ncbi:7227">Drosophila melanogaster</span>
Courtship song in Drosophila melanogaster is male-specific and is critical to stimulating the female [4, 6]. It consists of a humming sound called sine song, and a rhythmically patterned pulse song, which together stimulate the female to mate, reducing the time to copulation [4, 6]. Pulse song also communicates species-specific information, allowing females to recognize conspecific males [1-3].
###end p 10

###xml 406 414 402 406 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">&#916;tra</sup>
FruM mutant males lack pulse song [10, 12, 13], and constitutive FruM expression in the CNS of fruM and fruDeltatra females induces the performance of many steps of the male courtship ritual [14], suggesting an important role for FruM in specifying courtship song. To determine the contribution of FruM in the specification of courtship song, we analyzed song production in females of genotype fruM and fruDeltatra.

###xml 396 399 384 387 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">fru</italic>
###xml 399 400 387 388 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">M</sup>
###xml 405 408 393 396 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">fru</italic>

###xml 946 947 926 927 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">M</sup>
###xml 952 955 932 935 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">fru</italic>
###xml 955 963 935 939 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">&#916;tra</sup>
###xml 1118 1119 1094 1095 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">M</sup>
Song analysis was based on 29 fruM and fruDeltatra females because most fruM and fruDeltatra females did not perform sufficient courtship behavior or song for analysis (total n = 61; Table S1 in the Supplemental Data available online). In accordance with [14], the wing-extension indices (WEIs) of these 29 fruM and fruDeltatra females were not significantly different from wild-type and control fruM and fruDeltatra males; however, we found a significant decrease in the song index (SI) (the percentage of time spent singing during wing extension) (p < 0.05) (Table S1). Also, the fruM and fruDeltatra females' pulse song was highly aberrant (Figure 1). The number of pulse trains per minute (PTPM), mean pulses per train (MPPT) and interpulse interval (IPI) were all significantly lower compared to wild-type and control males (Table S1). Most striking, however, was the complete absence of sine song in these females (n = 29). Although the fruM and fruDeltatra females were capable of wild-type wing extension, they spent significantly less time singing during courtship and produced song of poor quality. Thus, FruM expression alone cannot specify wild-type song production.

###begin title 13
###xml 3 4 3 4 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">M</sup>
DsxM Is Required to Specify Courtship Song
###end title 13
###begin p 14
###xml 51 52 51 52 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">M</sup>
###xml 130 131 130 131 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">M</sup>
###xml 153 154 153 154 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">3</sup>
###xml 164 166 164 166 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">23</sup>
###xml 170 171 170 171 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">3</sup>
###xml 181 183 181 183 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">15</sup>
###xml 150 183 150 183 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">fru<sup>3</sup>,In(3R)dsx<sup>23</sup>/fru<sup>3</sup>,Df(3R)dsx<sup>15</sup></italic>
###xml 298 299 298 299 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">M</sup>
###xml 391 392 391 392 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">M</sup>
###xml 437 439 437 439 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="R35">35</xref>
###xml 455 458 455 458 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">fru</italic>
###xml 463 466 463 466 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">dsx</italic>
###xml 539 540 539 540 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">M</sup>
###xml 548 549 548 549 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">M</sup>
###xml 557 568 557 568 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">transformer</italic>
###xml 570 573 570 573 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">tra</italic>
###xml 644 646 644 646 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="R36">36</xref>
###xml 648 650 648 650 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="R37">37</xref>
###xml 756 757 756 757 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">M</sup>
###xml 782 783 782 783 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">M</sup>
To dissect the individual contributions of both FruM and Dsx to the specification of courtship song, we analyzed males lacking FruM and Dsx (genotype fru3,In(3R)dsx23/fru3,Df(3R)dsx15). These double mutants had a courtship index (CI) of 0 toward females (n = 11) and no song. We have shown that FruM expression in females is not sufficient for courtship song. Likewise, the expression of DsxM in females is also not sufficient for song [35]. Thus neither fru nor dsx alone can specify courtship song. In fact, only the presence of both FruM and DsxM, as in transformer (tra) mutant females, renders females capable of wild-type courtship song [36, 37], a finding we confirm. Together, these results demonstrate a previously unrecognized requirement for DsxM, in conjunction with FruM, in specifying courtship song.
###end p 14
###begin title 15
###xml 11 12 11 12 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">M</sup>
Dsx and FruM Colocalize in the CNS
###end title 15
###begin p 16
###xml 213 214 213 214 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="R7">7</xref>
###xml 367 370 367 370 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">fru</italic>
###xml 375 378 375 378 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">dsx</italic>
###xml 481 483 481 483 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="R10">10</xref>
###xml 484 486 484 486 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="R13">13</xref>
###xml 488 490 488 490 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="R23">23</xref>
###xml 492 494 492 494 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="R34">34</xref>
###xml 544 545 544 545 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">M</sup>
###xml 700 702 700 702 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="R25">25</xref>
###xml 736 737 736 737 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">M</sup>
Studies with male-female mosaics have shown that in gynandromorphs with a male head, the ventral thoracic ganglia of the adult CNS (including the mesothoracic ganglion [Msg]) must also be male for courtship song [7]. This suggests that the neural foci of courtship song are located in the ventral thoracic ganglia and that the sex of this region is critical to song. fru and dsx are both expressed in neurons located in this region, and mutations in both genes cause song defects [10-13, 23, 34]. In the abdominal ganglion (Abg) of the CNS, FruM and Dsx were shown to colocalize in a proportion of neurons and play critical roles in the development of male-specific clusters of serotonergic neurons [25]. Therefore, we asked whether FruM and Dsx were also coexpressed in the thoracic ganglia, and whether they act in parallel (if expressed in different neurons) or in concert (if expressed in the same neurons) to determine the neuronal substrate for courtship song in the CNS.
###end p 16
###begin p 17
###xml 30 31 30 31 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">M</sup>
###xml 66 75 66 75 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="fig" rid="F2">Figure 2B</xref>
###xml 175 177 175 177 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="R34">34</xref>
###xml 255 256 255 256 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">M</sup>
###xml 559 561 555 557 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="R34">34</xref>
###xml 627 629 623 625 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="R25">25</xref>
###xml 632 641 628 637 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="supplementary-material" rid="SD1">Figure S1</xref>
###xml 773 774 769 770 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">M</sup>
We determined that Dsx and FruM colocalize in the Msg of the CNS (Figure 2B). Colocalization occurred in a subset of Dsx-expressing neurons (TN1 cluster; nomenclature as per [34]) from the pupal stage onward. The number of neurons coexpressing Dsx and FruM in 2-day-old male pupae was 17.4 +/- 1.7 per hemisegment (mean +/- standard deviation [SD]; n = 10), and was not significantly different in 5-day-old adults (p < 0.05). Colocalization occurred in a further two subsets of Dsx-expressing neurons in the posterior brain, pC1 and pC2 (nomenclature as per [34]), in addition to previously reported colocalization in the Abg [25] (Figure S1). Given the critical importance of the sex of the ventral ganglia (including the Msg) to song production, the colocalization of FruM and Dsx in this region suggests that sexually dimorphic developmental mechanisms might be operating in the Msg, contributing to the sex-specific nature of courtship song production.
###end p 17
###begin title 18
The Neuromuscular Junctions of the Direct Flight Muscles Are Not Sexually Dimorphic
###end title 18
###begin p 19
###xml 157 159 157 159 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="R38">38</xref>
###xml 334 336 334 336 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="R38">38</xref>
###xml 469 471 469 471 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="R39">39</xref>
###xml 583 585 583 585 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="R39">39</xref>
###xml 675 678 675 678 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">fru</italic>
###xml 687 690 687 690 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">dsx</italic>
Electrophysiological studies show that the activity of seven of the direct flight muscles (DFMs) is directly related to the beating of the wing during song [38]. These seven DFMs are the basalar muscles B1-B4, the anterior muscles of the first and third axillaries AX1a and AX3a, and the sternobasalar muscle SB (nomenclature as per [38]). The axonal morphology and cell-body location of the motor neurons innervating six of these DFMs (mnDFMs) has also been reported [39]. The cell bodies of these six mnDFMs lie in the ventral thoracic ganglia, five having cell bodies in the Msg [39]. We therefore investigated whether male-specific song production could be attributed to fru- and/or dsx-regulated sexually dimorphic characteristics of these motor neurons.
###end p 19
###begin p 20
###xml 47 50 47 50 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">fru</italic>
###xml 54 57 54 57 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">dsx</italic>
###xml 79 82 79 82 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">fru</italic>
###xml 82 86 82 86 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">GAL4</sup>
###xml 120 123 120 123 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">fru</italic>
###xml 133 135 133 135 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="R28">28</xref>
###xml 221 223 221 223 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="R39">39</xref>
###xml 230 233 230 233 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">fru</italic>
###xml 233 237 233 237 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">GAL4</sup>
###xml 262 265 262 265 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">fru</italic>
###xml 274 291 274 291 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="fig" rid="F3">Figures 3A and 3B</xref>
###xml 310 313 310 313 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">fru</italic>
###xml 313 317 313 317 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">GAL4</sup>
###xml 434 437 434 437 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">dsx</italic>
###xml 476 479 476 479 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">fru</italic>
First, we asked whether any of the mnDFMs were fru or dsx expressing. By using fruGAL4, a GAL4 driver expressing in all fru neurons [28], we determined that only mnB3/B4 (a single motor neuron innervating both B3 and B4 [39]) was fruGAL4 positive, and thus is a fru neuron (Figures 3A and 3B). This neuron was fruGAL4 positive in both males and females, and the innervation was not obviously sexually dimorphic. However, because some dsx-expressing neurons in the Msg are not fru expressing, we examined the axonal morphology of all mnDFMs to eliminate the possibility of sex-specific DFM innervation.
###end p 20
###begin p 21
###xml 191 204 191 204 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="fig" rid="F3">Figures 3C-3N</xref>
###xml 445 447 445 447 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="R40">40</xref>
###xml 563 566 563 566 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">fru</italic>
###xml 570 573 570 573 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">dsx</italic>
###xml 625 661 625 661 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="supplementary-material" rid="SM">Supplemental Experimental Procedures</xref>
The axonal morphology and expression of common neurotransmitters at the neuromuscular junction (NMJ) of all seven DFMs were examined, and no obvious differences between the sexes were found (Figures 3C-3N). Type I and type II synaptic terminals were present on all mnDFMs, where type I terminals expressed glutamate and type II terminals expressed octopamine, in accordance with previous reports of neurotransmitter expression at the adult NMJ [40]. Moreover, no obvious differences in either axonal morphology or common neurotransmitters were observed in either fru or dsx mutant males (data not shown; genotypes are in the Supplemental Experimental Procedures). Therefore, the sexually dimorphic production of song is not likely to be a result of an obvious dimorphism in the neuronal morphology of the mnDFMs or in the neurotransmitter expression at the NMJs. Where might the critical difference(s) then lie?
###end p 21
###begin title 22
The Mesothoracic Ganglion Is Sexually Dimorphic
###end title 22
###begin p 23
###xml 15 16 15 16 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="R9">9</xref>
###xml 33 34 33 34 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">M</sup>
###xml 56 62 56 62 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">reaper</italic>
###xml 95 97 95 97 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="R41">41</xref>
###xml 99 101 99 101 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="R42">42</xref>
###xml 181 182 181 182 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">M</sup>
###xml 254 255 254 255 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="R8">8</xref>
###xml 373 374 373 374 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">M</sup>
###xml 421 422 421 422 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">M</sup>
###xml 484 485 484 485 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="R8">8</xref>
###xml 487 488 487 488 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="R9">9</xref>
###xml 490 492 490 492 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="R25">25</xref>
Kimura et al. [9] showed that FruM expression prevented reaper-mediated programmed cell death [41, 42] in a cluster of cells, resulting in more neurons in this cluster in males. DsxM, on the other hand, prolongs neuroblast divisions in the Abg of males [8], again resulting in more neurons in males. Thus, sexual dimorphisms might be present in regions in which Dsx and FruM colocalize, as suggested by the ability of FruM and Dsx to generate sexually dimorphic neuronal populations [8, 9, 25]. Given that our investigation found no obvious sex-specific dimorphisms in the mnDFMs, the dimorphism might lie in a population of interneurons. Therefore, the Msg was examined so that it could be determined whether a sexually dimorphic population of neurons was present.
###end p 23
###begin p 24
###xml 12 16 12 16 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">GAL4</sup>
###xml 9 16 9 16 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">fru<sup>GAL4</sup></italic>
###xml 61 63 61 63 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="R28">28</xref>
###xml 97 101 97 101 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">LacZ</italic>
###xml 191 200 188 197 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="fig" rid="F4">Figure 4A</xref>
###xml 407 409 397 399 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="R23">23</xref>
###xml 476 479 466 469 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">fru</italic>
###xml 668 669 658 659 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">M</sup>
###xml 716 719 706 709 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">fru</italic>
###xml 719 723 709 713 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">GAL4</sup>
By using fruGAL4, which expresses in both males and females [28], to drive a GAL4-responsive UAS-LacZ.NZ reporter, we quantified the number of beta-Gal-positive neurons in males and females (Figure 4A). The number of beta-Gal-positive neurons was significantly higher in males, with 136.4 +/- 3.3 cells per hemisegment (n = 10) versus 111.6 +/- 3.1 cells per hemisegment in females. Previously, Lee et al. [23] reported a sexual dimorphism in the number of neurons expressing fru P1 transcripts in the Msg. Together, these results suggest that a sexually dimorphic population of neurons is present in the Msg; therefore, we examined the individual contributions of FruM and Dsx in the creation of this difference in fruGAL4-positive neuron number in the Msg.
###end p 24
###begin title 25
###xml 3 4 3 4 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">M</sup>
FruM Expression Alone Cannot Create a Sexually Dimorphic Msg
###end title 25
###begin p 26
###xml 40 43 40 43 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">fru</italic>
###xml 43 47 43 47 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">GAL4</sup>
###xml 127 128 127 128 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="R7">7</xref>
###xml 146 147 146 147 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">M</sup>
###xml 213 216 213 216 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">dsx</italic>
###xml 221 224 221 224 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">fru</italic>
###xml 299 302 299 302 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">fru</italic>
###xml 302 303 302 303 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">M</sup>
###xml 308 311 308 311 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">fru</italic>
###xml 311 319 311 315 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">&#916;tra</sup>
###xml 341 342 337 338 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">M</sup>
###xml 474 475 470 471 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">M</sup>
###xml 595 604 591 600 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="fig" rid="F4">Figure 4B</xref>
###xml 606 614 602 610 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="supplementary-material" rid="SD1">Table S2</xref>
###xml 650 651 646 647 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">M</sup>
###xml 744 747 740 743 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">fru</italic>
###xml 747 751 743 747 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">GAL4</sup>
###xml 786 790 782 786 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">LacZ</italic>
We found a sexually dimorphic number of fruGAL4-expressing neurons in the Msg, a region of the CNS central to song production [7] and in which FruM and Dsx colocalize. To determine the individual contributions of dsx and fru in the creation of this sexually dimorphic number of neurons, we examined fruM and fruDeltatra females to see if FruM expression alone abolishes the observed difference in neuronal number in the Msg between the sexes. We found that the number of FruM-expressing neurons in the Msg of these females was significantly reduced in comparison to wild-type and control males (Figure 4B; Table S2). Furthermore, this decrease in FruM-expressing neurons was comparable to the difference in neuron number observed in the Msg of fruGAL4 males and females driving the UAS-LacZ.NZ reporter.
###end p 26
###begin p 27
###xml 130 131 130 131 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">M</sup>
###xml 164 165 164 165 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">M</sup>
###xml 225 226 225 226 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">M</sup>
###xml 396 397 396 397 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">M</sup>
###xml 541 542 541 542 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">M</sup>
Our results demonstrate that the difference in neuronal populations of males and females in the Msg lies in a subpopulation of FruM-expressing neurons, and that FruM expression alone cannot eliminate this difference. Thus FruM expression cannot, by itself, dictate the creation of the sexually dimorphic population of neurons in the Msg. We therefore asked whether Dsx, which colocalizes with FruM in the Msg, plays a role in the specification of this sexually dimorphic population of neurons, helping to determine the full complement of FruM neurons.
###end p 27
###begin title 28
###xml 3 4 3 4 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">M</sup>
###xml 34 35 34 35 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">M</sup>
DsxM Is Required for Wild-Type FruM Expression
###end title 28
###begin p 29
###xml 0 3 0 3 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">dsx</italic>
###xml 70 71 70 71 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="R8">8</xref>
###xml 73 75 73 75 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="R25">25</xref>
###xml 99 102 99 102 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">dsx</italic>
###xml 208 209 208 209 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">M</sup>
###xml 243 246 243 246 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">dsx</italic>
###xml 256 259 256 259 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">dsx</italic>
###xml 302 305 302 305 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">dsx</italic>
###xml 346 347 346 347 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">M</sup>
###xml 492 493 492 493 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">M</sup>
###xml 514 523 514 523 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="fig" rid="F4">Figure 4B</xref>
###xml 525 533 525 533 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="supplementary-material" rid="SD1">Table S2</xref>
###xml 544 547 544 547 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">fru</italic>
###xml 547 548 547 548 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">M</sup>
###xml 553 556 553 556 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">fru</italic>
###xml 556 564 556 560 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">&#916;tra</sup>
###xml 617 620 613 616 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">dsx</italic>
###xml 625 626 621 622 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">F</sup>
###xml 664 665 660 661 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">M</sup>
###xml 741 742 737 738 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">M</sup>
###xml 815 816 811 812 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">M</sup>
###xml 824 825 820 821 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">M</sup>
###xml 845 848 841 844 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">tra</italic>
###xml 893 894 889 890 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">M</sup>
###xml 938 947 934 943 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="fig" rid="F4">Figure 4B</xref>
###xml 1022 1023 1018 1019 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">M</sup>
###xml 1067 1068 1063 1064 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">M</sup>
dsx affects the sex-specific development of other regions of the CNS [8, 25]. To determine whether dsx contributes to creating the sex-specific population of neurons in the Msg, we tabulated the number of FruM-expressing neurons in the Msg of dsx null and dsx heterozygote control males. We found that dsx mutant males had significantly fewer FruM-expressing neurons in the Msg than did wild-type and control males, demonstrating that Dsx is indeed required to obtain a full complement of FruM-expressing neurons (Figure 4B; Table S2). Because fruM and fruDeltatra females (who express the female-specific isoform of dsx, DsxF) do not have a full complement of FruM-expressing neurons in the Msg, we have demonstrated a critical role for DsxM in the creation of a sexually dimorphic Msg. In fact, only when both FruM and DsxM are present, as in tra mutant females, can a full complement of FruM-expressing neurons in the Msg be obtained (Figure 4B). Thus, we have demonstrated a previously unrecognized requirement for DsxM in the specification of a population of FruM-expressing neurons in the Msg.
###end p 29
###begin p 30
###xml 3 4 3 4 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">M</sup>
###xml 106 107 106 107 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="R8">8</xref>
###xml 130 131 130 131 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">M</sup>
###xml 167 168 167 168 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">M</sup>
###xml 247 249 247 249 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="R25">25</xref>
###xml 281 282 281 282 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">M</sup>
###xml 547 548 547 548 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">M</sup>
###xml 628 629 628 629 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">M</sup>
###xml 684 686 684 686 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="R25">25</xref>
###xml 694 695 694 695 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="R9">9</xref>
###xml 697 699 697 699 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="R15">15</xref>
DsxM prolongs the division of neuroblasts in the Abg of males, resulting in more neurons in the male Abg [8]. Also in the Abg, DsxM plays a critical role alongside FruM in the differentiation of a male-specific serotonergic population of neurons [25]. Our findings suggest that DsxM operates in a similar manner in the Msg and the posterior brain (E.J.R. and S.F.G., unpublished data) to create sexually dimorphic neuronal numbers. These differences in neuronal populations suggest a common developmental theme in colocalization regions, where DsxM generates a sexually dimorphic population of neurons, which is exploited by FruM to fashion a male-specific behavioral neural network [25] (cf. [9, 15]).
###end p 30
###begin p 31
###xml 73 74 73 74 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">M</sup>
###xml 214 215 214 215 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">M</sup>
###xml 296 297 296 297 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">M</sup>
It is not clear why the absence of a sexually dimorphic population of FruM-expressing neurons in the Msg is associated with striking defects in courtship song because our results suggest that this population of FruM-expressing neurons does not directly innervate the DFMs. We propose that the FruM-expressing neurons form at least part of a male-specific neural network responsible for controlling the production of courtship song.
###end p 31
###begin p 32
###xml 18 19 18 19 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">M</sup>
###xml 97 98 97 98 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">M</sup>
###xml 366 369 366 369 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">fru</italic>
###xml 463 473 463 473 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">Drosophila</italic>
###xml 527 530 527 530 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">fru</italic>
###xml 535 538 535 538 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">dsx</italic>
###xml 637 639 637 639 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="R11">11</xref>
###xml 641 643 641 643 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="R25">25</xref>
###xml 645 647 645 647 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="R34">34</xref>
###xml 649 651 649 651 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="R43">43</xref>
###xml 652 654 652 654 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="R47">47</xref>
Thus, although FruM expression can specify many male-specific behaviors, we show that without DsxM, the determination of a complete male-specific CNS, and therefore a full complement of male behaviors, is not realized. This additional gene function is critical to understanding complex sex-specific phenotypes compared to previous interpretations of function, where fru has been described as the only gene needed for a "genetic switch" to male sexual behavior in Drosophila. Significantly, it adds to the growing evidence that fru and dsx are both necessary for a complete male courtship repertoire, in both neural and nonneural tissues[11, 25, 34, 43-47].
