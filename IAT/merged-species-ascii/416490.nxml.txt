###begin article-title 0
Immunohistochemical localization of integrin alpha V beta 3 and osteopontin suggests that they do not interact during embryo implantation in ruminants
###end article-title 0
###begin title 1
Background
###end title 1
###begin p 2
###xml 253 259 <span type="species:ncbi:9913">bovine</span>
###xml 370 376 <span type="species:ncbi:9913">bovine</span>
###xml 381 386 <span type="species:ncbi:9940">sheep</span>
###xml 524 529 <span type="species:ncbi:9940">sheep</span>
It has been suggested that trophoblast attachment requires co-expression of integrin alpha V beta 3 and its ligand osteopontin at the fetal-maternal interface. Until now the expression patterns of integrin alpha V beta 3 and osteopontin in the pregnant bovine uterus were unknown. The objectives of this study were to localize integrin alpha V beta 3 and osteopontin in bovine and sheep endometrium during the periimplantation period and to compare the distribution patterns using antibodies that had not yet been tested in sheep.
###end p 2
###begin title 3
Methods
###end title 3
###begin p 4
Cell compartments within endometrial tissue sections were scored for immunohistochemical staining intensity and data were analyzed to determine the effects of day of pregnancy or cycle.
###end p 4
###begin title 5
Results
###end title 5
###begin p 6
###xml 12 18 <span type="species:ncbi:9913">bovine</span>
###xml 295 301 <span type="species:ncbi:9913">Bovine</span>
###xml 676 681 <span type="species:ncbi:9940">sheep</span>
###xml 686 692 <span type="species:ncbi:9913">cattle</span>
In pregnant bovine endometrium, integrin alpha V beta 3 was detected in luminal epithelium, stroma, myometrium and smooth muscle. A strong band of immunoreactivity was observed in the subepithelial stroma of intercaruncular regions, but there was reduced reactivity in the caruncles and glands. Bovine trophoblast did not express integrin alpha V beta 3 at any stage of pregnancy. In ovine endometrium a different pattern of staining for integrin alpha V beta 3 was observed. Reactivity was not present in the luminal epithelium or trophoblast. There was strong staining of the deep glands and no reactivity in the superficial glands. Osteopontin distribution was similar for sheep and cattle. For both species, apical staining was present on the luminal epithelium and glands and on embryonic tissues.
###end p 6
###begin title 7
Conclusion
###end title 7
###begin p 8
In ruminants, integrin alpha V beta 3 and osteopontin do not co-localize at the fetal-maternal interface indicating that these proteins could not interact to facilitate embryo attachment as has been proposed in other species.
###end p 8
###begin title 9
Introduction
###end title 9
###begin p 10
###xml 111 112 111 112 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="B1">1</xref>
###xml 113 114 113 114 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="B4">4</xref>
###xml 366 367 366 367 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="B5">5</xref>
###xml 368 369 368 369 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="B6">6</xref>
###xml 661 662 659 660 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="B7">7</xref>
###xml 849 850 847 848 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="B1">1</xref>
###xml 851 852 849 850 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="B8">8</xref>
###xml 402 408 <span type="species:ncbi:9913">cattle</span>
###xml 413 418 <span type="species:ncbi:9940">sheep</span>
Comparative studies of placentation in domestic animals show that implantation is similar among these species [1-4]. However differences in the molecular properties of receptive endometrium are just now being explored. Placentation in ruminants is categorized as non-invasive as chorionic cell migration into maternal tissue is restricted to the luminal epithelium [5,6]. The preimplantation period in cattle and sheep is longer than in rodents, carnivores and primates. This period coincides with secretion of pregnancy supporting proteins from the glandular epithelium and trophoblast production of the pregnancy recognition factor, interferon-tau (IFN-tau) [7]. Unique to ruminants are caruncles. These dense aglandular connective tissue regions are covered by columnar epithelium and their location defines where the finite placentae will form [1,8]. The molecular determinants which prevent villus development in the intercaruncular endometrial tissue, or promote development in the caruncles, are not yet identified.
###end p 10
###begin p 11
###xml 226 227 226 227 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="B9">9</xref>
###xml 228 230 228 230 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="B11">11</xref>
###xml 438 440 438 440 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="B12">12</xref>
###xml 441 443 441 443 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="B17">17</xref>
###xml 525 526 521 522 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">v</sub>
###xml 530 531 523 524 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">3</sub>
###xml 625 627 618 620 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="B18">18</xref>
###xml 628 630 621 623 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="B19">19</xref>
###xml 875 877 868 870 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="B20">20</xref>
###xml 878 880 871 873 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="B21">21</xref>
###xml 995 996 984 985 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">v</sub>
###xml 1000 1002 986 988 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">3 </sub>
###xml 1091 1093 1077 1079 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="B22">22</xref>
Integrins are heterodimeric glycoproteins that facilitate cell-cell and cell-extracellular matrix attachment and are key facilitators of cellular processes involved in tissue remodeling such as cell migration and de-adhesion [9-11]. In many species endometrial expression of integrins and their extracellular matrix (ECM) ligands is correlated with embryo attachment, and uterine differentiation during the receptive phase and pregnancy [12-17]. The most promiscuous integrin in terms of ligand interactions is integrin alphavbeta3, which binds Arg-Gly-Asp (RGD) motifs in fibronectin, osteopontin and laminin, among others [18,19]. The affinity of integrins for their ligands is dependent on cell type, and a change in the activation state that is mediated by phosphorylation of the cytoplasmic domain (inside out signaling), and ligand availability (outside in signaling) [20,21]. Integrin activity is augmented in the presence of growth factors such as the increased binding of integrin alphavbeta3 observed in smooth muscle cells in the presence of insulin-like growth factor I (IGF-1) [22].
###end p 11
###begin p 12
###xml 165 167 165 167 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="B23">23</xref>
###xml 293 295 293 295 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="B24">24</xref>
###xml 527 529 527 529 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="B25">25</xref>
###xml 746 747 742 743 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">v</sub>
###xml 751 753 744 746 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">3 </sub>
###xml 754 756 747 749 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="B25">25</xref>
###xml 757 759 750 752 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="B26">26</xref>
###xml 375 380 <span type="species:ncbi:9940">sheep</span>
Osteopontin was first described as a secreted 60-kDa phosphoprotein associated with bone ECM and as a lymphokine expressed by activated lymphocytes and macrophages [23]. Expression has since been identified on the epithelium of many tissues such as kidney, breast, and the reproductive tract [24]. Osteopontin expression and distribution differs between cycling and pregnant sheep; little secretion occurs in the glands of cycling animals while secretion increases from glandular epithelium during the periimplantation period [25]. As a secreted protein of the ruminant uterus regulated by progesterone, osteopontin has been proposed to support conceptus growth and act as an adhesive between trophoblast and luminal epithelium via integrin alphavbeta3 [25,26].
###end p 12
###begin p 13
###xml 95 96 91 92 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">v</sub>
###xml 100 102 93 95 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">3 </sub>
###xml 230 231 219 220 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">v</sub>
###xml 235 237 221 223 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">3 </sub>
###xml 275 277 261 263 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="B15">15</xref>
###xml 278 280 264 266 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="B16">16</xref>
###xml 281 283 267 269 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="B25">25</xref>
###xml 284 286 270 272 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="B27">27</xref>
###xml 353 354 335 336 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">v</sub>
###xml 358 359 337 338 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">3</sub>
###xml 366 367 341 342 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">v</sub>
###xml 371 372 343 344 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">1</sub>
###xml 379 380 347 348 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">9</sub>
###xml 384 385 349 350 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">1</sub>
###xml 392 393 353 354 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">v</sub>
###xml 397 399 355 357 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">5 </sub>
###xml 408 409 362 363 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">5</sub>
###xml 413 415 364 366 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">1 </sub>
###xml 416 418 367 369 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="B28">28</xref>
###xml 654 656 605 607 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="B16">16</xref>
###xml 657 659 608 610 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="B29">29</xref>
###xml 771 772 718 719 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">v</sub>
###xml 776 778 720 722 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">3 </sub>
###xml 847 848 787 788 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">v</sub>
###xml 852 854 789 791 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">3 </sub>
###xml 1057 1059 994 996 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="B14">14</xref>
###xml 1060 1062 997 999 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="B30">30</xref>
###xml 3 7 <span type="species:ncbi:10090">mice</span>
###xml 9 15 <span type="species:ncbi:9606">humans</span>
###xml 17 24 <span type="species:ncbi:9557">baboons</span>
###xml 29 34 <span type="species:ncbi:9940">sheep</span>
###xml 240 244 <span type="species:ncbi:10090">mice</span>
###xml 424 429 <span type="species:ncbi:9940">sheep</span>
###xml 709 714 <span type="species:ncbi:9940">sheep</span>
###xml 719 725 <span type="species:ncbi:9913">cattle</span>
###xml 805 811 <span type="species:ncbi:9913">cattle</span>
In mice, humans, baboons and sheep, it has been postulated that co-expression of integrin alphavbeta3 and its ligand osteopontin on uterine epithelium is required for trophoblast attachment and/or invasion; blocking integrin alphavbeta3 in mice prevents embryo implantation [15,16,25,27]. Potential osteopontin receptors include CD44 and integrins alphavbeta3, alphavbeta1, alpha9beta1, alphavbeta5 and alpha5beta1 [28]. In sheep, several of these integrin subunits are expressed on the apical surface of uterine epithelium and on conceptus trophoblast, although no change in their expression patterns has been observed between cyclic and pregnant ewes [16,29]. Despite the similarity in placental anatomy of sheep and cattle this widespread distribution pattern of alphavbeta3 is not observed in cycling cattle. In cycling heifers, integrin alphavbeta3 is most strongly expressed in intercaruncular subepithelial stroma, and its expression is temporally regulated with the estrous cycle at least partially under the influence of the reproductive steroids [14,30].
###end p 13
###begin p 14
###xml 45 46 41 42 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">v</sub>
###xml 50 52 43 45 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">3 </sub>
###xml 232 233 221 222 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">v</sub>
###xml 237 239 223 225 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">3 </sub>
###xml 463 464 445 446 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">v</sub>
###xml 468 470 447 449 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">3 </sub>
###xml 117 120 <span type="species:ncbi:9913">cow</span>
###xml 198 203 <span type="species:ncbi:9940">sheep</span>
###xml 287 293 <span type="species:ncbi:9913">cattle</span>
###xml 351 356 <span type="species:ncbi:9940">sheep</span>
###xml 361 365 <span type="species:ncbi:9913">cows</span>
###xml 473 479 <span type="species:ncbi:9913">bovine</span>
###xml 484 489 <span type="species:ncbi:9940">sheep</span>
###xml 638 643 <span type="species:ncbi:9940">sheep</span>
###xml 744 750 <span type="species:ncbi:9913">bovine</span>
###xml 807 813 <span type="species:ncbi:9913">bovine</span>
Until now the distributions of integrin alphavbeta3 and its ligand osteopontin have not been studied in the pregnant cow. The distribution of these proteins has been studied in cycling and pregnant sheep, however, the integrin alphavbeta3 results were unlike what we observed in cycling cattle. This is surprising since histologically placentation in sheep and cows is similar. The objectives of the present study were to localize the expression of integrin alphavbeta3 in bovine and sheep endometrium during the periimplantation period and to compare the distribution patterns using two monoclonal antibodies that had not been tested in sheep. In addition, the expression of osteopontin during the estrous cycle and periimplantation period in bovine and ovine endometrium was examined using an antibody to bovine osteopontin.
###end p 14
###begin title 15
Materials and methods
###end title 15
###begin title 16
Animals
###end title 16
###begin p 17
###xml 293 297 293 295 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">&#174; </sup>
All procedures performed were in accordance with the guidelines of the Canadian Council on Animal Care and were reviewed and approved by the Nova Scotia Agricultural College Animal Care and Use Committee. Sexually mature heifers of mixed beef breeds were synchronized to estrus using Estrumate(R) (500 mug cloprostenol, Schering Canada Inc.; QUE, Canada). Animals were observed for estrous behavior three times a day and had cycle lengths of 19 to 20 days. Uteri were collected at slaughter on days 0 (estrus), 1, 3, 6, 10, 14, 15, 16, 17, 18, 19 and 20 of the estrous cycle, or days 16, 18, 21, 24 or 30 of pregnancy with at least three animals at each day. To confirm estrous cycle stage, blood samples taken at heat and slaughter were assayed for progesterone by radioimmunoassay (RIA) using a Coat-A-Count kit (Diagnostic Products Corporation, Los Angeles CA).
###end p 17
###begin p 18
Mature Rideau Arcott ewes were synchronized to estrus using intravaginal progesterone pessaries for 14 days followed by observation for estrous behavior in the presence of an intact ram. Ewes were randomly assigned to either cyclic or pregnant status, and mating to rams occurred over a 2-day period. Reproductive tracts were collected on days 13 and 17 of the cycle, and on day 13 of pregnancy (n = 5).
###end p 18
###begin p 19
###xml 91 94 <span type="species:ncbi:9913">cow</span>
A single sample of mammary gland parenchyma was excised from a randomly selected lactating cow at a provincial abattoir to serve as a positive control tissue for osteopontin antibody reactivity.
###end p 19
###begin title 20
Tissue collection
###end title 20
###begin p 21
###xml 81 83 81 83 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="B31">31</xref>
###xml 225 227 225 227 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">3 </sup>
Ovarian dating was performed at collection according to the criteria of Ireland [31] and pregnancy was established by the presence of an embryo or trophoblast in the uterine lumen. The ipsilateral horn was dissected into 1 cm3 cross-sectioned blocks and frozen in liquid nitrogen (-196degreesC) at the collection site before transfer to an ultra low temperature freezer (-80degreesC) for storage.
###end p 21
###begin title 22
Antibodies
###end title 22
###begin p 23
###xml 65 66 61 62 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">v</sub>
###xml 70 72 63 65 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">3 </sub>
###xml 217 218 206 207 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">v</sub>
###xml 222 224 208 210 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">3 </sub>
###xml 270 272 256 258 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="B32">32</xref>
###xml 404 405 386 387 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">v</sub>
###xml 409 411 388 390 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">3 </sub>
###xml 518 520 497 499 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="B33">33</xref>
###xml 4 10 <span type="species:ncbi:10090">murine</span>
###xml 45 50 <span type="species:ncbi:9606">human</span>
###xml 262 268 <span type="species:ncbi:9913">cattle</span>
###xml 473 479 <span type="species:ncbi:9913">bovine</span>
###xml 637 648 <span type="species:ncbi:3704">Horseradish</span>
###xml 677 683 <span type="species:ncbi:9793">donkey</span>
###xml 689 695 <span type="species:ncbi:9986">rabbit</span>
###xml 785 791 <span type="species:ncbi:9986">rabbit</span>
###xml 850 855 <span type="species:ncbi:10090">mouse</span>
Two murine monoclonal antibodies specific to human integrin alphavbeta3 were used for immunohistochemical localization. Clone LM609 (Chemicon, Temecula, CA) targeted the functional binding region of the integrin alphavbeta3 complex of several species, including cattle [32], and clone BV4 (Abcam product number ab7167, Novus Biologicals, Littleton, CO) was directed against the full length integrin alphavbeta3 of endothelial cells. Osteopontin was localized using an anti-bovine milk osteopontin polyclonal antibody [33] kindly provided by Dr. G. Killian (Pennsylvania State University, Almiquist Research Centre, University Park, PA). Horseradish peroxidase (HRP) conjugated donkey-anti-rabbit IgG (Pierce, Rockford, IL) was used for detection of osteopontin. Negative controls were rabbit IgG (ICN Biomedical Inc., Aurora, ON, Canada) or purified mouse IgG (Chemicon) and were used at the same concentration as primary antibodies. Optimal dilutions of primary antibodies were empirically determined, and fixation and detection methods were selected based on the conditions that allowed for maximum specific immunostaining.
###end p 23
###begin title 24
Western blotting
###end title 24
###begin p 25
###xml 263 264 263 264 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">3</sub>
###xml 266 267 266 267 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">4</sub>
###xml 276 277 276 277 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">4</sub>
###xml 278 279 278 279 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">2</sub>
###xml 280 281 280 281 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">7</sub>
###xml 1210 1213 1207 1208 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">&#174;</sup>
###xml 27 33 <span type="species:ncbi:9913">bovine</span>
###xml 72 78 <span type="species:ncbi:9913">bovine</span>
###xml 858 864 <span type="species:ncbi:9913">bovine</span>
###xml 880 886 <span type="species:ncbi:9986">rabbit</span>
###xml 1007 1013 <span type="species:ncbi:9793">donkey</span>
###xml 1019 1025 <span type="species:ncbi:9986">rabbit</span>
###xml 1044 1055 <span type="species:ncbi:3704">horseradish</span>
To determine reactivity of bovine anti-milk osteopontin antibodies with bovine and ovine endometrium, 0.3 g samples of endometrial and mammary gland tissues were thawed and homogenized in 2 ml extraction buffer (50 mM Tris, pH 8.0, 300 mM NaCl, 20 mM NaF, 1 mM Na3VO4, 1 mM Na4P2O7, 1 mM EDTA, 1 mM EGTA, 10% v/v glycerol, 1% v/v NP-40, 0.5 mM PMSF and 10 mug/mL aprotinin). Concentrations of protein were determined using a Bradford protein assay (Bio-Rad Laboratories, Hercules, CA) with BSA as the standard. Following denaturation in Laemmli buffer, proteins (200 mug) were separated using 10% SDS-PAGE and blotted to PVDF membrane. Blots were blocked in PBS containing 0.05% (v/v) Tween-20 (PBS-T) and 2% (w/v) BSA for 60 min at RT. Blots were washed 3 times for 5 min each in PBS-T, then incubated for 2 h in PBS-T containing 1% BSA and 2.5 mug/ml anti-bovine osteopontin or rabbit IgG (negative control). Blots were washed 3 times for 5 min each, then incubated for 1 h in PBS-T containing 1% BSA and donkey anti-rabbit IgG conjugated to horseradish peroxidase. Following 3 washes for 5 min each in PBS-T, and one wash in PBS, immunoreactive proteins were detected using a chemiluminescent substrate (ECL(R), Amersham Biosciences Inc., Baie d'Urfe, Quebec, Canada).
###end p 25
###begin title 26
Immunohistochemistry
###end title 26
###begin p 27
###xml 193 197 192 194 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">&#174; </sup>
###xml 570 571 555 556 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">v</sub>
###xml 575 577 557 559 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">3 </sub>
###xml 630 631 607 608 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">v</sub>
###xml 635 637 609 611 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">3 </sub>
###xml 910 911 880 881 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">v</sub>
###xml 915 917 882 884 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">3 </sub>
###xml 953 957 920 922 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">&#174; </sup>
###xml 1204 1205 1165 1166 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">v</sub>
###xml 1209 1210 1167 1168 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">3</sub>
###xml 1414 1418 1372 1374 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">&#174; </sup>
Immunoperoxidase staining for integrins was performed on 5 to 8 mum cryostat cross-sections of endometrium. Serial sections were mounted on 3-aminopropyl-triethoxysilane- coated-Superfrost Plus(R) slides (Fisher Scientific, Whitby, ON, Canada), then fixed in acetone for 10 min and air dried overnight. Cut sections were stored at -80degreesC until use. Sections were blocked with 2% w/v BSA in PBS (pH 7.2 to 7.4) for 30 min. Primary antibodies were diluted in 2% w/v BSA in PBS and applied at concentrations of 10 mug/ml for anti-osteopontin, 0.6 mug/ml for anti-alphavbeta3 clone LM609 (Chemicon), and 0.2 mug/ml for anti-alphavbeta3 clone BV4 (Novus Biologicals). All primary antibodies were allowed to bind for 2 h, then the slides were washed in PBS 3 times for 3 min each, and secondary antibody was applied and allowed to react for 45 min followed by washing in PBS. For amplification of integrin alphavbeta3 signal, the Vectastain Elite ABC kit(R) (Vector laboratories, Burlingame, CA) was used. To prevent non-specific staining, avidin (reagent A) was used at half the recommended concentration. Immunostaining was visualized using the chromogen metal enhanced diamino benzidine (integrin alphavbeta3; Pierce) or 3-amino-9-ethylcarbazole (osteopontin; Sigma, Saint Louis, MO). Slides were counterstained with Mayers Hematoxylin (Electron Microscopy Sciences, Fort Washington, PA) and coated with Aquaperm(R) mounting media (Fisher). Slides were viewed on a Leica DMLB microscope (Leica Microsystems, Wetzlar, Germany) and photographed on Ektachrome 160T tungsten film (Eastman Kodak, Rochester, NY). Slides were scanned and images assembled in Adobe Photoshop (Adobe Systems Incorporated, San Jose, CA). All experiments were replicated at least three times for each animal on different days.
###end p 27
###begin title 28
Statistical analysis
###end title 28
###begin p 29
###xml 882 884 873 875 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="B34">34</xref>
###xml 964 966 955 957 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="B34">34</xref>
Cell compartments within sections were scored by two independent observers on a six point scale (0-negative, 1-very weak, 2-weak but clearly positive, 3-intermediate, 4- high, and 5- very high) for staining intensity. Data were checked for normalcy using the univariate procedure in SAStrade mark (Statistical Analysis Software Version 8, SAS Institute Inc. Cary NC) and log transformed if transformation corrected to a normal distribution. Normal data were analyzed using a general linear models procedure to examine the effect of day on staining intensity, and the Duncan's multiple range test was used to compare population means. Nonparametric statistical comparisons between day of cycle, day of pregnancy or cycle stage (stage1-metestrus, stage 2-early diestrus; stage 3-late diestrus; stage 4-proestrus and estrus) were made by the Kruskal-Wallis test of population medians [34]. When significant effects were observed, Mann-Whitney's two sample rank test [34] was used to test the equality of population medians. The selected alpha level was 0.05.

At all stages examined integrin alphaVbeta3 was detected in luminal epithelium, endometrial stroma, myometrium and arteriolar smooth muscle (Fig. 1). There was no difference in staining intensity or the distribution pattern in tissue sections treated with antibody to integrin alphavbeta3 from either Chemicon or Novus Biologicals. ###begin title 47
Discussion
###end title 47
###begin p 48
###xml 67 68 63 64 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">V</sub>
###xml 72 74 65 67 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">3 </sub>
###xml 313 315 306 308 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="B14">14</xref>
###xml 341 342 330 331 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">V</sub>
###xml 346 348 332 334 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">3 </sub>
###xml 619 620 601 602 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">V</sub>
###xml 624 626 603 605 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">3 </sub>
###xml 954 955 933 934 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="B6">6</xref>
###xml 1132 1133 1111 1112 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="B6">6</xref>
###xml 1245 1246 1220 1221 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">V</sub>
###xml 1250 1252 1222 1224 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">3 </sub>
###xml 12 18 <span type="species:ncbi:9913">bovine</span>
###xml 305 311 <span type="species:ncbi:9913">cattle</span>
###xml 321 325 <span type="species:ncbi:9913">cows</span>
In pregnant bovine endometrium reactivity scores for integrin alphaVbeta3 were always highest in the stroma underlying the luminal epithelium of intercaruncular tissues, and weak or no staining was detected in the caruncles or stroma underlying the glands. This concurs with our previous study of cycling cattle [14]. In cows, integrin alphaVbeta3 may be involved in constraining trophoblast and endometrium in the intercaruncular regions, since the growth of both tissues to form the chorionic villi and intervening maternal septae occurs only in the caruncles. We hypothesize that subepithelial stromal integrin alphaVbeta3 is involved in regulating the behavior of the overlying epithelium and adjacent trophoblast. Such a signal from stroma underlying the uterine epithelium is logical, since the epithelium itself is modified rapidly after attachment by trophoblast binucleate cell migration and fusion with maternal epithelium to form giant cells [6]. This migration is maximal about day 24, then subsides to the extent that trinucleate, rather than giant cells, form and there is some regeneration of the luminal epithelium [6]. The observation in the current study that this is preceeded by increased expression of stromal integrin alphaVbeta3 provides support for a role of this integrin in constraining invasion.
###end p 48
###begin p 49
###xml 14 15 10 11 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">V</sub>
###xml 19 21 12 14 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">3 </sub>
###xml 151 152 144 145 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="table" rid="T1">1</xref>
###xml 202 209 195 202 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">et al. </italic>
###xml 210 212 203 205 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="B16">16</xref>
###xml 716 717 709 710 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="B8">8</xref>
###xml 718 720 711 713 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="B35">35</xref>
###xml 793 794 782 783 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">V</sub>
###xml 798 800 784 786 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">3 </sub>
###xml 903 904 885 886 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">V</sub>
###xml 908 910 887 889 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">3 </sub>
###xml 164 169 <span type="species:ncbi:9940">sheep</span>
###xml 239 244 <span type="species:ncbi:9940">sheep</span>
###xml 249 255 <span type="species:ncbi:9913">cattle</span>
###xml 359 364 <span type="species:ncbi:9940">sheep</span>
###xml 382 388 <span type="species:ncbi:9913">cattle</span>
###xml 393 398 <span type="species:ncbi:9940">sheep</span>
###xml 508 514 <span type="species:ncbi:9913">cattle</span>
###xml 807 810 <span type="species:ncbi:9913">cow</span>
###xml 913 918 <span type="species:ncbi:9940">sheep</span>
Integrin alphaVbeta3 reactivity was not concentrated in the basal lamina region of the subepithelial stroma in sections from ewes in this study (Table 1) or in the sheep endometrium examined by Johnson et al. [16]. This difference between sheep and cattle is interesting and may be attributable to the slightly more invasive attachment process that occurs in sheep in comparison to cattle. In sheep there is more extensive degeneration of luminal epithelium from migrant trophoblast binucleate cells than in cattle, that results in a syncytium that persists throughout pregnancy. In addition, cytoplasmic processes penetrate the basal lamina of ovine fetomaternal hybrid epithelium to contact the underlying stroma [8,35]. Although speculative, it is possible that subepithelial integrin alphaVbeta3 in the cow somehow acts to restrict trophoblast penetration whereas the relative lack of integrin alphaVbeta3 in sheep subepithelial stroma permits a higher degree of invasion.

###xml 754 756 740 742 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="B38">38</xref>
###xml 999 1000 981 982 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">v</sub>
###xml 1004 1006 983 985 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">3 </sub>
###xml 1037 1044 1016 1023 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">et al. </italic>
###xml 1045 1047 1024 1026 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="B37">37</xref>
###xml 1291 1293 1270 1272 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="B39">39</xref>
###xml 1342 1343 1317 1318 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">v</sub>
###xml 1347 1349 1319 1321 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">3 </sub>
###xml 1396 1398 1368 1370 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="B22">22</xref>
###xml 1482 1484 1454 1456 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="B40">40</xref>
###xml 1485 1487 1457 1459 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="B43">43</xref>
###xml 1550 1552 1522 1524 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="B44">44</xref>
###xml 1617 1618 1585 1586 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">v</sub>
###xml 1622 1624 1587 1589 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">3 </sub>
###xml 3 9 <span type="species:ncbi:9913">cattle</span>
###xml 540 544 <span type="species:ncbi:9913">cows</span>
###xml 939 943 <span type="species:ncbi:9913">cows</span>
###xml 1069 1073 <span type="species:ncbi:9913">cows</span>
###xml 1224 1229 <span type="species:ncbi:9940">sheep</span>
###xml 1544 1548 <span type="species:ncbi:9913">cows</span>
###xml 1712 1717 <span type="species:ncbi:9940">sheep</span>
###xml 1722 1726 <span type="species:ncbi:9913">cows</span>
In cattle, the transient disappearance of subepithelial integrin alphaVbeta3 on day 16 of the cycle, but not day 16 of pregnancy, suggests a possible role for this integrin in the onset of luteolysis. Our more recent studies have shown that this temporary downregulation may be mediated by estrogen [30], a known stimulator of the luteolytic mechanism [36]. Expression of IGF-1, like integrin alphavbeta3, is high at estrus and throughout the cycle is restricted to the subepithelial stroma [37]. Furthermore, in tissue from day 16 cycling cows expression of IGF-1 mRNA in this region reached a cycle low which preceded the proestrous increase beginning on day 17 [37]. Similar patterns of IGF-1 protein expression were observed by Ohtani and coworkers [38] in cyclic heifers, although the downregulation was observed on day 14-15, rather than day 16-17, consistent with the shorter estrous cycle generally observed in heifers compared to cows. Again, similar to what was observed for integrin alphavbeta3 in the current study, Robinson et al. [37] found that pregnant cows were more likely to express stromal IGF-1 mRNA on day 16 than nonpregnant animals, although the results were not conclusive. IGF-1 mRNA expression in sheep was evident in caruncular as well as intercaruncular stroma [39]. In other cell systems IGF-1 and integrin alphavbeta3 are known to have cooperative signaling roles [22]. Both proteins have been linked to prostaglandin production via cyclooxygenase 2 [40-43], which has been localized to the luminal epithelium of cows [44]. Differences in expression patterns of IGF-1 and integrin alphavbeta3 may mark a subtle difference in the mechanisms regulating prostaglandin release between sheep and cows.
###end p 52
###begin p 53
###xml 28 29 24 25 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">V</sub>
###xml 33 35 26 28 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">3 </sub>
###xml 372 373 365 366 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="table" rid="T1">1</xref>
###xml 405 406 394 395 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">V</sub>
###xml 410 412 396 398 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">3 </sub>
###xml 551 553 537 539 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="B16">16</xref>
###xml 746 747 728 729 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">V</sub>
###xml 751 752 730 731 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">3</sub>
###xml 846 848 821 823 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">V </sub>
###xml 856 857 828 829 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">3</sub>
###xml 875 877 847 849 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="B16">16</xref>
###xml 1071 1072 1043 1044 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="B9">9</xref>
###xml 1073 1075 1045 1047 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="B10">10</xref>
###xml 1153 1154 1121 1122 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">V</sub>
###xml 1158 1160 1123 1125 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">1 </sub>
###xml 1168 1169 1129 1130 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">V</sub>
###xml 1173 1174 1131 1132 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">5</sub>
###xml 51 57 <span type="species:ncbi:9913">bovine</span>
###xml 484 488 <span type="species:ncbi:9913">cows</span>
In this study integrin alphaVbeta3 was absent from bovine trophoblast, but was expressed at moderate intensity in the glandular epithelium, and in the luminal epithelium until it was modified by binucleate cell migration. In ovine endometrium, glandular epithelium exhibited a strong pericellular expression but no reactivity was observed in the luminal epithelium (Table 1). The pattern of integrin alphaVbeta3 expression we observed in ovine endometrium was not only different from cows, but also from what has previously been reported for the ewe [16]. This difference in localization patterns could be attributable to the antibodies used. In this study we used two antibodies that targeted different epitopes of the integrin heterodimer alphaVbeta3., whereas the earlier study used antibodies targeting the individual integrin subunits, alphaV and beta3. Johnson et al. [16] reported antibody reactivity for these subunits on the apical surfaces of uterine epithelium and trophoblast. Monomeric integrin subunit protein is not normally expressed at the cell surface [9,10], so it is possible that other integrin heterodimers, such as integrins alphaVbeta1 or alphaVbeta5, were being detected.
###end p 53
###begin p 54
###xml 226 227 222 223 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">V</sub>
###xml 231 232 224 225 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">3</sub>
###xml 306 308 299 301 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="B27">27</xref>
###xml 309 311 302 304 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="B45">45</xref>
###xml 312 314 305 307 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="B46">46</xref>
###xml 331 332 320 321 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">V</sub>
###xml 336 338 322 324 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">3 </sub>
###xml 441 443 427 429 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="B47">47</xref>
###xml 444 446 430 432 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="B48">48</xref>
###xml 565 567 551 553 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="B15">15</xref>
###xml 604 605 586 587 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">V</sub>
###xml 609 611 588 590 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">3 </sub>
###xml 783 784 758 759 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">V</sub>
###xml 788 789 760 761 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">1</sub>
###xml 796 797 764 765 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">V</sub>
###xml 801 802 766 767 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">5</sub>
###xml 809 810 770 771 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">4</sub>
###xml 814 815 772 773 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">1</sub>
###xml 822 823 776 777 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">8</sub>
###xml 827 829 778 780 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">1 </sub>
###xml 838 839 785 786 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">9</sub>
###xml 843 844 787 788 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">1</sub>
###xml 885 887 829 831 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="B28">28</xref>
###xml 888 890 832 834 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="B29">29</xref>
###xml 947 949 888 890 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">1 </sub>
###xml 960 962 901 903 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="B17">17</xref>
###xml 963 965 904 906 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="B49">49</xref>
###xml 1011 1013 949 951 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">1 </sub>
###xml 1115 1117 1049 1051 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">4 </sub>
###xml 1156 1158 1090 1092 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="B50">50</xref>
###xml 1205 1206 1135 1136 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">4</sub>
###xml 1210 1212 1137 1139 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">1 </sub>
###xml 101 106 <span type="species:ncbi:9940">sheep</span>
###xml 111 117 <span type="species:ncbi:9913">cattle</span>
###xml 540 545 <span type="species:ncbi:9606">human</span>
###xml 701 706 <span type="species:ncbi:9940">sheep</span>
###xml 710 716 <span type="species:ncbi:9913">cattle</span>
###xml 893 899 <span type="species:ncbi:9913">Bovine</span>
###xml 1258 1264 <span type="species:ncbi:9913">bovine</span>
Moderate but consistent osteopontin expression was observed in uterine epithelium and trophoblast of sheep and cattle. It has been proposed that osteopontin or another matrix molecule could act as a "bridge" for integrin alphaVbeta3, connecting trophoblast and uterine epithelium during embryo attachment [27,45,46]. Integrin alphaVbeta3 is thought to be the functionally most important receptor for osteopontin in bone and vascular tissue [47,48], and experiments with the Ishikawa endometrial cell line suggest that this is also true for human endometrial cells [15]. Since in this study integrin alphaVbeta3 was not detected on trophoblast or the apical surface of the luminal epithelium of either sheep or cattle, another integrin may be anchoring the bridge. The integrins alphaVbeta1, alphaVbeta5, alpha4beta1, alpha8beta1 and alpha9beta1, as well as CD44, can bind osteopontin [28,29]. Bovine trophoblast and uterine epithelium express beta1 integrins [17,49], but the alpha subunits associated with beta1 in these tissues have not been fully characterized. We have not been able to detect the integrin alpha4 subunit at the fetomaternal interface [50], therefore it is unlikely that integrin alpha4beta1 is the interaction partner for osteopontin in bovine uterine epithelium.
###end p 54
###begin p 55
###xml 152 153 152 153 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="table" rid="T1">1</xref>
###xml 178 180 178 180 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="B13">13</xref>
###xml 181 183 181 183 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="B25">25</xref>
###xml 184 186 184 186 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="B51">51</xref>
###xml 526 528 526 528 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="B25">25</xref>
###xml 817 819 817 819 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="B52">52</xref>
###xml 820 822 820 822 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="B53">53</xref>
###xml 971 973 971 973 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="B26">26</xref>
###xml 1191 1193 1188 1190 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="B15">15</xref>
###xml 1194 1196 1191 1193 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="B47">47</xref>
###xml 1197 1199 1194 1196 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="B54">54</xref>
###xml 66 72 <span type="species:ncbi:9913">bovine</span>
###xml 159 164 <span type="species:ncbi:9606">human</span>
###xml 503 508 <span type="species:ncbi:9940">sheep</span>
###xml 545 550 <span type="species:ncbi:9606">human</span>
###xml 555 561 <span type="species:ncbi:10090">murine</span>
###xml 825 830 <span type="species:ncbi:9940">Sheep</span>
The pattern of expression of osteopontin was generally similar in bovine endometrium to what has been observed in other species, including ovine (Table 1) and human endometrium [13,25,51]. However, unlike what has been observed in those species, the scores for osteopontin reactivity in luminal epithelium decreased during the luteal phase in cyclic heifers, and strong apical reactivity was not observed on trophoblast, luminal or glandular epithelial cells during implantation as has been observed in sheep [current study, [25]]. Although the human and murine promoters for osteopontin possess progesterone response elements, the role of progesterone is complex in endometrium. The downregulation we observed occurred when progesterone levels are high but expression of progesterone receptors in epithelium is low [52,53]. Sheep infused with progesterone showed increased osteopontin mRNA in the glands, however no progesterone receptors were expressed by these cells [26]. These experiments suggest that progesterone may act in concert with local modulators of osteopontin expression, which include hepatocyte growth factor, transforming growth factor beta 1 and epidermal growth factor [15,47,54], perhaps via a stromal mediated paracrine mechanism.
###end p 55
###begin p 56
###xml 100 102 100 102 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="B55">55</xref>
###xml 372 373 368 369 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">v</sub>
###xml 377 379 370 372 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">3 </sub>
###xml 418 420 411 413 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="B56">56</xref>
###xml 451 452 440 441 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">v</sub>
###xml 456 458 442 444 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">3 </sub>
###xml 765 766 751 752 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="B6">6</xref>
###xml 825 827 811 813 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="B56">56</xref>
###xml 922 923 904 905 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">v</sub>
###xml 927 929 906 908 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">3 </sub>
###xml 1167 1168 1142 1143 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">v</sub>
###xml 1172 1174 1144 1146 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">3 </sub>
###xml 1354 1356 1326 1328 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="B17">17</xref>
###xml 3 8 <span type="species:ncbi:9606">human</span>
###xml 568 571 <span type="species:ncbi:9913">cow</span>
###xml 1265 1270 <span type="species:ncbi:9606">human</span>
In human placenta, the chorionic villus cytotrophoblasts but not the overlying syncytiotrophoblast [55] produce osteopontin. It has been proposed that differentiated syncytiotrophoblasts control a paracrine communication loop with underlying cytotrophoblasts by releasing progesterone to stimulate secretion of osteopontin, which in turn binds and activates integrin alphavbeta3 on the adjacent syncytial trophoblast [56]. Engagement of integrin alphavbeta3 may then signal increased adhesion, or other intracellular events that promote normal tissue function. In the cow, there is no syncytial trophoblast per se but there are syncytial plaques and trinucleate cells formed by migration and fusion of trophoblast binucleate cells with maternal luminal epithelium [6]. These are exposed to progesterone from the trophoblast [56] and showed reactivity to osteopontin antibodies in the current study. However, integrin alphavbeta3 was not expressed on trophoblast and expression decreased in the fetomaternal hybrid epithelium as it formed from day 24 to 30. On the other hand the underlying stroma of the hybrid epithelium showed increased expression of integrin alphavbeta3 through this period. Whether this is related to a paracrine loop similar to what occurs in human placenta and/or a response to extracellular matrix changes occurring at this time [17] remains to be determined.
###end p 56
###begin title 57
Conclusions
###end title 57
###begin p 58
###xml 14 15 10 11 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">v</sub>
###xml 19 21 12 14 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">3 </sub>
###xml 277 278 266 267 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">v</sub>
###xml 282 284 268 270 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">3 </sub>
###xml 638 639 620 621 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">v</sub>
###xml 643 645 622 624 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">3 </sub>
###xml 59 65 <span type="species:ncbi:9913">bovine</span>
###xml 226 229 <span type="species:ncbi:9913">cow</span>
###xml 299 304 <span type="species:ncbi:9940">sheep</span>
###xml 351 355 <span type="species:ncbi:9913">cows</span>
###xml 513 518 <span type="species:ncbi:9940">sheep</span>
###xml 542 546 <span type="species:ncbi:9913">cows</span>
Integrin alphavbeta3 in the subepithelial stromal cells of bovine endometrium may have a role in the epithelial-stromal signaling events that regulate remodeling of pregnant epithelium and trophoblast during attachment in the cow. The endometrial distribution of integrin alphavbeta3 we observed in sheep endometrium differed from the distribution in cows. Apparently at the molecular level, the signaling mechanisms between the epithelium and stroma that lead to formation of the synepitheliochorial placenta of sheep are not the same as in cows. At the fetomaternal interface in both species, the distribution patterns of integrin alphavbeta3 and osteopontin did not overlap. This suggests that, unlike in primates and rodents, in ruminants these proteins could not act together to facilitate embryo attachment.
