Identification of the Saccharomyces cerevisiae RNA:pseudouridine synthase responsible for formation of Psi2819 in 21S mitochondrial ribosomal RNA

###begin p 2
###xml 77 101 75 99 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">Saccharomyces cerevisiae</italic>

###xml 1523 1527 1493 1497 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">2819</sub>
###xml 71 76 <span type="species:ncbi:4932">yeast</span>

###xml 507 519 <span type="species:ncbi:4932">S.cerevisiae</span>
###xml 944 949 <span type="species:ncbi:4932">yeast</span>
So far, four RNA:pseudouridine (Psi)-synthases have been identified in yeast Saccharomyces cerevisiae. Together, they act on cytoplasmic and mitochondrial tRNAs, U2 snRNA and rRNAs from cytoplasmic ribosomes. However, RNA:Psi-synthases responsible for several U-->Psi conversions in tRNAs and UsnRNAs remained to be identified. Based on conserved amino-acid motifs in already characterised RNA:Psi-synthases, four additional open reading frames (ORFs) encoding putative RNA:Psi-synthases were identified in S.cerevisiae. Upon disruption of one of them, the YLR165c ORF, we found that the unique Psi residue normally present in the fully matured mitochondrial rRNAs (Psi2819 in 21S rRNA) was missing, while Psi residues at all the tested pseudouridylation sites in cytoplasmic and mitochondrial tRNAs and in nuclear UsnRNAs were retained. The selective U-->Psi conversion at position 2819 in mitochondrial 21S rRNA was restored when the deleted yeast strain was transformed by a plasmid expressing the wild-type YLR165c ORF. Complementation was lost after point mutation (D71-->A) in the postulated active site of the YLR165c-encoded protein, indicating the direct role of the YLR165c protein in Psi2819 synthesis in mitochondrial 21S rRNA. Hence, for nomenclature homogeneity the YLR165c ORF was renamed PUS5 and the corresponding RNA:Psi-synthase Pus5p. As already noticed for other mitochondrial RNA modification enzymes, no canonical mitochondrial targeting signal was identified in Pus5p. Our results also show that Psi2819 in mitochondrial 21S rRNA is not essential for cell viability.
###end p 2
###begin title 3
INTRODUCTION
###end title 3
###begin p 4
###xml 254 255 250 251 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="gkd325c1">1</xref>
###xml 256 257 252 253 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="gkd325c3">3</xref>
###xml 576 577 568 569 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="gkd325c4">4</xref>
###xml 578 579 570 571 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="gkd325c7">7</xref>
Pseudouridine (Psi) is the most abundant and universally found modified nucleoside in RNA. Indeed, numerous Psi residues are present at specific positions in rRNAs and tRNAs from Archaea, Bacteria and Eukarya and in UsnRNAs from Eukarya (for reviews see 1-3). They are all formed post-transcriptionally by a family of enzymes designated as RNA:Psi-synthases. The detailed mechanism of this reaction however is still unknown, yet it is thought that the carboxyl group of a conserved aspartate residue present in all RNA:Psi-synthases identified so far plays an essential role (4-7, see below).
###end p 4

###xml 83 99 <span type="species:ncbi:562">Escherichia coli</span>
###xml 146 152 <span type="species:ncbi:562">E.coli</span>
###xml 403 409 <span type="species:ncbi:562">E.coli</span>

Present knowledge of RNA:Psi-synthases is largely based on their identification in Escherichia coli. Two tRNA:Psi-synthases were characterised in E.coli and other bacteria: PSU-I (recently renamed TruA) is specific for the formation of Psi at positions 38, 39 and/or 40 in several tRNAs (8,9), while TruB catalyses the formation of the conserved Psi residue at position 55 (10). Among the characterised E.coli rRNA:Psi-synthases, RluC and RluD catalyse the formation of Psi residues at six distinct sites in E.coli 23S rRNA (5,11,12), whereas RsuA and RluE catalyse each a unique U-->Psi conversion (6,13,14). In addition, RluA displays a dual-substrate specificity since it is implicated in the formation of both Psi746 in 23S rRNAs and Psi32 in tRNAs (15,16).
###end p 5
###begin p 6
###xml 171 173 169 171 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="gkd325c17">17</xref>
###xml 248 249 246 247 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="fig" rid="gkd325f1">1</xref>
###xml 269 271 267 269 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="gkd325c17">17</xref>

###xml 373 375 371 373 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="gkd325c17">17</xref>
###xml 501 502 499 500 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="gkd325c4">4</xref>
###xml 503 504 501 502 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="gkd325c7">7</xref>
###xml 725 726 721 722 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="gkd325c6">6</xref>
###xml 294 299 <span type="species:ncbi:42554">tetra</span>
Analysis of the sequence similarity between already characterised RNA:Psi-synthases showed that these enzymes belong to four distinct families: TruA, TruB, RsuA and RluA (17). The TruB, RsuA and RluA families share two short conserved motifs (Fig. 1, motifs I and II) (17), while only the GRTD tetra-amino acid sequence found in motif II is conserved in the TruA family (6,17). Site-directed mutagenesis of the aspartate residue (D) in motif II showed that this amino acid is essential for catalysis (4-7). Extensive searches in DNA databases identified more than 100 genes coding for putative Psi-synthases in 47 different organisms covering altogether the three domains of living organisms (Archaea, Bacteria and Eukarya) (6).
###end p 6
###begin p 7
###xml 77 101 75 99 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">Saccharomyces cerevisiae</italic>
###xml 110 111 108 109 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="gkd325c6">6</xref>

###xml 1064 1066 1052 1054 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="gkd325c25">25</xref>
###xml 1207 1209 1195 1197 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="gkd325c26">26</xref>
###xml 1210 1212 1198 1200 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="gkd325c27">27</xref>
###xml 77 101 <span type="species:ncbi:4932">Saccharomyces cerevisiae</span>
###xml 696 702 <span type="species:ncbi:562">E.coli</span>
###xml 981 986 <span type="species:ncbi:4932">yeast</span>
Such analysis revealed nine genes encoding putative RNA:Psi-synthases in the Saccharomyces cerevisiae genome (6). Five of them correspond to the already characterised RNA:Psi-synthases (Pus1p, Pus2p, Pus3p, Pus4p and Cbf5p) (18-22). Pus1p, Pus2p and Pus3p are members of the TruA family, whereas Pus4p and Cbf5p belong to the TruB family. The targets of the Pus1p, Pus3p, Pus4p and Cbf5p enzymes were identified. Pus3p catalyses Psi formation at positions 38 and 39 in the anticodon loop of cytoplasmic and mitochondrial tRNAs (22), while Pus1p is implicated in Psi formation at eight different positions in cytoplasmic tRNAs (18,20) and also at position 44 in U2 snRNA (23). Pus4p, like TruB in E.coli, is responsible for the site-specific formation of the highly conserved Psi residue at position 55 in both cytoplasmic and mitochondrial tRNAs (19). Finally, Cbf5p, a protein present in the H/ACA snoRNPs (21,24), catalyses the formation of most, if not all, Psi residues in the yeast 18S and 25S rRNAs at the precursor level (35S pre-rRNA) in the nucleolus (21,25). Selection of the pseudouridylation sites in rRNAs is mediated through direct base pairing between the snoRNAs and the nucleolar pre-rRNA (26,27).
###end p 7
###begin p 8
###xml 93 95 93 95 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="gkd325c28">28</xref>

Previous examination of 34 cytoplasmic and 17 mitochondrial tRNAs so far sequenced in yeast (28) revealed that Psi can be found at 15 distinct positions in cytoplasmic tRNAs and at eight positions in mitochondrial tRNAs (for review see 1). Altogether, the Pus1p, Pus3p and Pus4p RNA:Psi-synthases mentioned above are responsible for Psi formation at 11 out of 15 positions in cytoplasmic tRNAs and 5 out of 8 positions in mitochondrial tRNAs (18-20,22). Only the enzymes catalysing the formation of Psi residues at positions 1, 13, 31 and 32 in cytoplasmic tRNAs and at positions 31, 32 and 72 in mitochondrial tRNAs remain to be identified. Moreover, while yeast Pus1p is capable of forming Psi35 in tRNATyrin vitro, the disruption of the corresponding PUS1 gene does not affect the formation of Psi35in vivo, attesting for the existence of another enzyme acting on U35 with overlapping specificity with Pus1p (20).
###end p 8
###begin p 9
###xml 54 66 52 64 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">S.cerevisiae</italic>
###xml 68 70 66 68 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="gkd325c23">23</xref>
###xml 141 143 137 139 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">44</sub>
###xml 180 182 176 178 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="gkd325c23">23</xref>
###xml 54 66 <span type="species:ncbi:4932">S.cerevisiae</span>
Six Psi residues have been identified in UsnRNAs from S.cerevisiae (23). So far, only the enzyme responsible for the formation of residue Psi44 in U2 snRNA was identified (Pus1p) (23). The enzymes responsible for Psi formation at the five other sites in UsnRNAs have still to be identified.
###end p 9
###begin p 10
###xml 13 25 13 25 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">S.cerevisiae</italic>
###xml 108 110 106 108 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="gkd325c29">29</xref>
###xml 13 25 <span type="species:ncbi:4932">S.cerevisiae</span>
Finally, the S.cerevisiae mitochondrial 21S LSU rRNA was found to contain one Psi residue at position 2819 (29) and the corresponding Psi-synthase had not been identified.
###end p 10
###begin p 11
###xml 58 70 58 70 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">S.cerevisiae</italic>
###xml 408 409 404 405 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="fig" rid="gkd325f1">1</xref>
###xml 772 784 766 778 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">S.cerevisiae</italic>
###xml 58 70 <span type="species:ncbi:4932">S.cerevisiae</span>
###xml 772 784 <span type="species:ncbi:4932">S.cerevisiae</span>
In this work, we focused our attention on one of the four S.cerevisiae genes encoding putative RNA:Psi-synthases with unknown substrate, the YLR165c open reading frame (ORF). The encoded protein displays the two characteristic motifs I and II common to proteins of the three distinct RNA:Psi-synthases families TruA, RsuA and RluA, and also an additional motif III common to the RsuA and RluA families (Fig. 1). To identify the substrate of the YLR165c ORF protein product, the effect of the deletion of this ORF on the formation of Psi residues at positions 13, 31 and 32 in cytoplasmic tRNAs, 31 and 32 in mitochondrial tRNAs, 5 and 6 in U1 snRNA, 35 and 42 in U2 snRNA, 99 in U5 snRNA and 2819 in mitochondrial 21S rRNA was tested. The results obtained reveal that the S.cerevisiae YLR165c ORF encodes the mitochondrial rRNA:Psi-synthase.

The YLR165c ORF (accession number in TREMBL, Q06244) was disrupted in the S.cerevisiae strains BMA64 (MAT a/alpha ura 3-1 ade 2-1 leu 2-3, 112 his3-11, 15 trp1D can 1-100) and BMA64-1A (MAT a ura 3-1 ade 2-1 leu 2-3, 112 his3-11, 15 trp1D can 1-100), by replacement of the entire coding region with the TRP1 auxotrophic marker. Psi residues analysis in UsnRNAs and mitochondrial 21S LSU rRNA
###end title 17
###begin p 18
###xml 93 105 87 99 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">S.cerevisiae</italic>
###xml 155 157 149 151 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="gkd325c33">33</xref>

Psi residues in the UsnRNAs and mitochondrial 21S LSU rRNA of the DeltaYLR165c and wild-type S.cerevisiae strains were identified by the CMCT-RT approach (33), with the modifications described previously (23). In brief, total RNA fractions were treated by CMCT, followed by alkaline treatment to remove the CMC group from U. The remaining bulky CMC-Psi were identified by reverse transcription using various oligonucleotides complementary to snRNAs or 21S rRNA. The oligonucleotides used for Psi identification in U1, U2 and U5 snRNAs were previously described (23). The oligonucleotide complementary to nt 2846-2866 of the mitochondrial 21S LSU rRNA was used to detect the formation of a Psi residue at position 2819 in this RNA.

###begin title 26
RESULTS AND DISCUSSION
###end title 26
###begin title 27
Disruption of the YLR165c ORF does not affect tRNA or UsnRNA pseudouridylation
###end title 27
###begin p 28
###xml 196 199 196 199 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">TRP</italic>
###xml 678 686 664 672 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">in vitro</italic>
###xml 706 709 692 695 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">Asp</sup>
###xml 780 782 764 766 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">35</sub>
###xml 881 883 857 859 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">32</sup>
###xml 911 914 887 890 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">Tyr</sup>

###xml 1173 1175 1145 1147 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">35</sub>
###xml 1258 1260 1228 1230 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">13</sub>
###xml 1265 1267 1233 1235 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">32</sub>
###xml 1274 1276 1240 1242 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">35</sub>
###xml 1363 1364 1329 1330 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="table" rid="gkd325tb1">1</xref>
###xml 249 254 <span type="species:ncbi:4932">yeast</span>
###xml 696 701 <span type="species:ncbi:4932">yeast</span>
###xml 856 861 <span type="species:ncbi:4932">yeast</span>
###xml 930 940 <span type="species:ncbi:3702">A.thaliana</span>
###xml 1002 1007 <span type="species:ncbi:4932">yeast</span>
###xml 1037 1047 <span type="species:ncbi:3702">A.thaliana</span>
###xml 1137 1142 <span type="species:ncbi:4932">yeast</span>
To identify the RNA substrate(s) of the protein encoded by the YLR165c ORF, we performed a one-step gene replacement of this ORF by the gene encoding n-(5'-phosphoribosyl)-anthranilate isomerase (TRP1 auxotrophic marker). The resulting DeltaYLR165c yeast cells did not show any growth phenotype when plated on rich or minimal media and grown at 20, 30 or 37degreesC. The cell-free extract and total RNA fraction obtained from the strain with the DeltaYLR165c ORF were used to determine the consequences of the gene deletion on the pseudouridylation pattern of tRNAs, UsnRNAs and mitochondrial rRNA. The activity towards positions 13 and 32 in cytoplasmic tRNAs was tested on an in vitro produced yeast tRNAAsp as described in Materials and Methods. To test for a putative tRNA:Psi35-synthase activity the action of extracts from wild-type and DeltaYLR165c yeast cells on an [alpha-32P]ATP radiolabelled pre-tRNATyr from the plant A.thaliana was compared. Indeed, previous data showed that the purified yeast Pus1p enzyme does not modify A.thaliana tRNATyr at position 35 in vitro, whereas the putative tRNA:Psi35-synthase present in the yeast cell extract does catalyse Psi35 formation. As evidenced by 2D-TLC of the tRNA RNase T2 digest, the presence of Psi13, Psi32 or Psi35 in the various tRNA substrates was not affected by deletion of the YLR165c ORF (Table 1).
###end p 28
###begin p 29
###xml 316 318 312 314 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">31</sub>
###xml 326 328 320 322 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">32</sub>
###xml 354 357 348 351 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">Met</sup>
###xml 366 369 360 363 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">Ser</sup>
###xml 385 387 377 379 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">31</sub>
###xml 411 414 403 406 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">Met</sup>
###xml 75 80 <span type="species:ncbi:4932">yeast</span>
Influence of the disruption of the YLR165c ORF on the pseudouridylation of yeast cytoplasmic tRNA at position 31 and mitochondrial tRNAs at positions 31 and 32 was tested with the CMCT-RT approach as described in Materials and Methods. As demonstrated by identification of the alkaline resistant CMC-Psi adducts, Psi31 and Psi32 in the mitochondrial tRNAMet and tRNASer, as well as Psi31 in the cytoplasmic tRNAMet, were retained upon gene disruption (data not shown).
###end p 29
###begin p 30
###xml 221 222 217 218 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">5</sub>
###xml 230 231 224 225 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">6</sub>
###xml 248 250 240 242 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">35</sub>
###xml 258 260 248 250 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">42</sub>
###xml 280 282 268 270 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">99</sub>
###xml 461 462 445 446 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="fig" rid="gkd325f2">2</xref>
###xml 639 646 621 628 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">in vivo</italic>
Hence, altogether these results strongly suggested that the protein encoded by the YLR165c ORF is not a tRNA:Psi-synthase. To investigate its possible involvement in UsnRNAs pseudouridylation, the presence of residues Psi5 and Psi6 in U1 snRNA, Psi35 and Psi42 in U2 snRNA and Psi99 in U5 snRNA was tested in the DeltaYLR165c haploid strain using the CMCT modification approach and appropriate oligonucleotides for primer extension analysis. As shown in Figure 2, all the Psi residues present in UsnRNAs were conserved upon deletion of the YLR165c ORF, indicating that the corresponding protein is not required for modification of UsnRNAs in vivo.
###end p 30
###begin title 31
###xml 34 38 32 36 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">2819</sub>
YLR165c ORF is responsible for Psi2819 formation in mitochondrial 21S rRNA
###end title 31
###begin p 32
###xml 91 103 91 103 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">S.cerevisiae</italic>
###xml 191 193 189 191 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="gkd325c33">33</xref>
###xml 340 344 332 336 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">2819</sub>
###xml 448 449 440 441 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="fig" rid="gkd325f3">3</xref>
###xml 91 103 <span type="species:ncbi:4932">S.cerevisiae</span>
###xml 303 308 <span type="species:ncbi:4932">yeast</span>
Finally, we tested the possibility that the protein encoded by the YLR165c ORF acts on the S.cerevisiae mitochondrial LSU rRNA that is known to contain a single Psi residue at position 2819 (33). Modification by CMCT was done on total RNA fractions from the DeltaYLR165c haploid strain and the isogenic yeast strain, and the presence of Psi2819 in the mitochondrial LSU 21S rRNA was tested as above by primer extension analysis. As shown in Figure 3, the results obtained with the DeltaYLR165c strain differed from those obtained with the wild-type strain by the absence of the Psi residue at position 2819 in the mitochondrial 21S rRNA.
###end p 32
###begin p 33
###xml 69 73 67 71 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">2819</sub>
###xml 245 246 239 240 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="fig" rid="gkd325f3">3</xref>
###xml 592 593 580 581 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="fig" rid="gkd325f1">1</xref>
###xml 606 613 594 601 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">in vivo</italic>
###xml 699 700 687 688 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="fig" rid="gkd325f3">3</xref>
###xml 1015 1019 999 1003 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">PUS5</italic>
###xml 361 366 <span type="species:ncbi:4932">yeast</span>
###xml 969 974 <span type="species:ncbi:4932">yeast</span>
In order to verify that the YLR165c protein is indeed involved in Psi2819 formation, the DeltaYLR165c strain was complemented by the p413TEF-YLR165c plasmid, containing the wild-type ORF under the control of the TEF promoter. As shown in Figure 3 (lanes 7 and 8), Psi formation at position 2819 in the mitochondrial LSU 21S rRNA was restored in the transformed yeast strain. To confirm the direct implication of the YLR165c-encoded protein in U-->Psi conversion at position 2819, we substituted the aspartate residue (D71) by an alanine in the GRTD sequence of the YLR165c ORF motif II (Fig. 1) and tested in vivo the complementation capacity of the mutated protein. The results presented in Figure 3 (lanes 9 and 10) show that the point mutation abrogated the complementation capacity of the cloned gene. Altogether, these results confirmed a mitochondrial rRNA:Psi-synthase activity of the YLR165c-encoded protein. For homogeneity of RNA:Psi-synthase nomenclature in yeast we propose to rename the YLR165c ORF to PUS5 and the corresponding protein to Pus5p.
###end p 33
###begin title 34
Mitochondrial targeting of YLR165c-encoded protein
###end title 34
###begin p 35
###xml 427 429 425 427 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="gkd325c41">41</xref>
###xml 430 432 428 430 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="gkd325c42">42</xref>
###xml 508 510 506 508 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="gkd325c39">39</xref>
###xml 775 777 773 775 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="gkd325c40">40</xref>
Since mitochondrial 21S rRNA is synthesised and maturated exclusively in mitochondria, the corresponding rRNA:Psi-synthase has to enter into mitochondrial compartments by a mechanism which involves some kind of addressing signal. So far, only a few mitochondrial rRNA and tRNA modifying enzymes were characterised; all of them are encoded by nuclear DNA, synthesised by cytoplasmic ribosomes and then targeted to mitochondria (41,42). The analysis of the YLR165c protein using the PSORT prediction software (39), which takes into account only the first 20 amino acids, did not reveal any plausible mitochondrial leader peptide. Another approach for determination of possible subcellular localisation is based on the global amino acid composition of proteins (NNPLS software, 40). Using this prediction system the protein, encoded by the YLR165c ORF, can be classified as mitochondrial enzyme. Thus, despite the absence of pronounced N-terminal leader peptide, the YLR165c encoded protein is targeted into mitochondria.
###end p 35
###begin p 36
###xml 126 131 <span type="species:ncbi:4932">yeast</span>
The YLR165c encoded protein is likely dedicated only to mitochondrial rRNA modification since the residue at position 2945 in yeast cytoplasmic 26S rRNA (counterpart of position 2819 in the mitochondrial 21S rRNA) is not converted into a Psi residue. In addition, even if a modification had to occur at this position, it would likely depend upon the Cbf5p/snoRNA-guided system.
###end p 36

###xml 716 718 712 714 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="gkd325c29">29</xref>

###xml 1247 1249 1235 1237 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="gkd325c11">11</xref>

###xml 128 140 <span type="species:ncbi:4932">S.cerevisiae</span>
###xml 373 385 <span type="species:ncbi:4932">S.cerevisiae</span>
###xml 535 540 <span type="species:ncbi:4932">yeast</span>
###xml 646 651 <span type="species:ncbi:9606">human</span>
###xml 656 668 <span type="species:ncbi:10090">Mus musculus</span>
###xml 708 714 <span type="species:ncbi:562">E.coli</span>
###xml 786 803 <span type="species:ncbi:1423">Bacillus subtilis</span>
###xml 890 912 <span type="species:ncbi:2242">Halobacterium halobium</span>
###xml 1062 1068 <span type="species:ncbi:562">E.coli</span>
###xml 1142 1147 <span type="species:ncbi:4932">yeast</span>
Several previous studies revealed a low content of post-transcriptional modifications in mitochondrial rRNAs. The mitochondrial S.cerevisiae LSU rRNA contains only three modified nucleosides: two 2'-O-methylated residues (Gm at position 2270 and Um at position 2791) and one Psi residue at position 2819 (43-45, reviewed in 46). The two 2'-O-methylation sites found in the S.cerevisiae mitochondrial 21S rRNA are strongly conserved in cytoplasmic rRNAs from Eukarya and Bacteria (47-49). In contrast, the unique Psi residue present in yeast mitochondrial 21S rRNA is conserved only in mitochondrial LSU rRNAs from some other species (for example human and Mus musculus) and in Gram-negative bacteria such as E.coli (29,33). It is absent in the LSU rRNAs from the Gram-positive bacteria Bacillus subtilis (29), in 26S rRNA of eukaryotic cytoplasmic ribosomes (45,49) and also in the archaea Halobacterium halobium (29). This is in agreement with our observation that the presence of Psi2819 is not essential for growth on fermentable carbon source. Similarly, in E.coli, inactivation of RluC that forms residue Psi2580 (the counterpart of the yeast mitochondrial Psi2819 residue) and two other Psi residues in 23S rRNA does not affect growth rate (11,12).
###end p 38
###begin p 39
###xml 137 141 135 139 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">2819</sub>
###xml 149 161 147 159 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">S.cerevisiae</italic>
###xml 332 336 328 332 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">2819</sub>
###xml 396 402 392 398 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">et al.</italic>
###xml 402 404 398 400 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="gkd325c50">50</xref>
###xml 412 413 408 409 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="fig" rid="gkd325f4">4</xref>
###xml 471 473 467 469 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="gkd325c47">47</xref>
###xml 474 476 470 472 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="gkd325c48">48</xref>
###xml 477 479 473 475 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="gkd325c50">50</xref>
###xml 676 678 672 674 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="gkd325c51">51</xref>
###xml 746 758 742 754 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">S.cerevisiae</italic>
###xml 762 766 758 762 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">2791</sub>
###xml 882 883 878 879 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="fig" rid="gkd325f4">4</xref>
###xml 895 899 891 895 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">2791</sub>
###xml 951 953 947 949 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="gkd325c47">47</xref>
###xml 954 956 950 952 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="gkd325c52">52</xref>
###xml 1049 1051 1045 1047 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="gkd325c52">52</xref>
###xml 149 161 <span type="species:ncbi:4932">S.cerevisiae</span>
###xml 746 758 <span type="species:ncbi:4932">S.cerevisiae</span>
Nevertheless, because of its location in a 23S rRNA region considered to be located at the ribosome peptidyl transfer centre, residue Psi2819 in the S.cerevisiae mitochondrial 21S rRNA may modulate the efficiency or the fidelity of the translation machinery. Indeed, the single-stranded sequences flanking helix 90 that contains Psi2819 (helices are numbered according to nomenclature of Leffers et al.50) (Fig. 4), are the most highly conserved regions in the LSU rRNA (47,48,50). Several mutations in these single-stranded sequences confer resistance of bacterial or mitochondrial ribosomes to various antibiotics affecting the peptidyl transferase activity (for review see 51). The second modified nucleotide found in 21S mitocondrial rRNA of S.cerevisiae (Um2791) is also located in the vicinity of the peptidyl transferase centre. Note that the terminal loop of helix 90 (Fig. 4) bearing Um2791 was shown to crosslink with puromycin derivatives (47,52) and to base-pair to the CCA terminal sequence of the tRNA located at the ribosomal A site (52).
###end p 39
###begin p 40
###xml 69 74 <span type="species:ncbi:4932">yeast</span>
###xml 157 162 <span type="species:ncbi:4932">yeast</span>
In conclusion, this study demonstrated that one of the four putative yeast RNA:Psi-synthases (Pus5p) of the RluA-family is implicated in the modification of yeast mitochondrial rRNA. The functional characterisation and the identification of RNA targets for the three other putative RNA:Psi-synthases are in progress in our laboratory.
