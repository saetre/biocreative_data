###begin article-title 0
Adoption of PERILIPIN as a unifying nomenclature for the mammalian PAT-family of intracellular lipid storage droplet proteins
###end article-title 0
###begin p 1
###xml 826 835 826 835 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">PERILIPIN</italic>
###xml 837 841 837 841 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">PLIN</italic>
###xml 474 479 <span type="species:ncbi:10090">Mouse</span>
###xml 516 521 <span type="species:ncbi:9606">Human</span>
The PAT family of proteins has been identified in eukaryotic species as diverse as vertebrates, insects, and amebazoa. These proteins share a highly conserved sequence organization and avidity for the surfaces of intracellular, neutral lipid storage droplets. The current nomenclature of the various members lacks consistency and precision, deriving more from historic context than from recognition of evolutionary relationship and shared function. In consultation with the Mouse Genomic Nomenclature Committee, the Human Genome Organization Genomic Nomenclature Committee, and conferees at the 2007 FASEB Conference on Lipid Droplets: Metabolic Consequences of the Storage of Neutral Lipids, we have established a unifying nomenclature for the gene and protein family members. Each gene member will incorporate the root term PERILIPIN (PLIN), the founding gene of the PAT family, with the different genes/proteins numbered sequentially.
###end p 1
###begin p 2
###xml 193 194 193 194 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="bib1">1</xref>
###xml 287 288 287 288 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="bib2">2</xref>
PAT derives from names of three proteins, PERILIPIN, ADRP, and TIP47, with each having highly related N-terminal sequences and common affinity for intracellular neutral lipid storage droplets (1). Of these, PERILIPIN comes closest to incorporating a biological connection into its name (2). The PERILIPIN appellation is, also, without alternative.
###end p 2
###begin p 3
###xml 117 118 117 118 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="bib3">3</xref>
###xml 243 244 243 244 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="bib2">2</xref>
###xml 246 247 246 247 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="bib4">4</xref>
###xml 335 336 335 336 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="bib5">5</xref>
###xml 363 364 363 364 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="bib6">6</xref>
###xml 453 454 453 454 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="bib7">7</xref>
###xml 455 456 455 456 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="bib9">9</xref>
PERILIPIN was originally identified as the most highly phosphorylated protein in lipolytically-activated adipocytes (3) and was subsequently shown to localize specifically to the surfaces of intracellular neutral lipid storage droplets [LSDs (2, 4)]. Although perilipin mRNA and protein expression is largely restricted to adipocytes (5) and steroidogenic cells (6), when ectopically expressed, it is exclusively found on LSDs, regardless of cell type (7-9). Indeed, the name "perilipin" derives from piœµrhoiota lambdaiotapiomicronsigma, "surrounding lipid".
###end p 3
###begin p 4
###xml 6 8 6 8 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="bib10">10</xref>
###xml 91 93 91 93 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="bib11">11</xref>
###xml 105 109 105 109 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">ADRP</italic>
###xml 184 186 184 186 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="bib10">10</xref>
###xml 188 190 188 190 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="bib11">11</xref>
###xml 289 291 289 291 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="bib11">11</xref>
###xml 293 295 293 295 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="bib12">12</xref>
###xml 382 384 382 384 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="bib11">11</xref>
###xml 386 388 386 388 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="bib13">13</xref>
###xml 507 509 507 509 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="bib14">14</xref>
###xml 396 401 <span type="species:ncbi:9606">human</span>
###xml 473 478 <span type="species:ncbi:9606">human</span>
ADRP (10), adipocyte differentiation-related protein, is now appreciated to be a misnomer (11). Although ADRP mRNA is upregulated during the differentiation of cultured preadipocytes (10, 11), ADRP protein is rapidly degraded during differentiation and is undetected in mature adipocytes (11, 12). Although additional studies showed that ADRP is expressed in most other cell types (11, 13), when human ADRP was later found to associate tightly with lipid droplet surfaces, human ADRP was named adipophilin (14). Unfortunately, the term "adipophilin" inaccurately implies a specific association with adipose tissue. Further confusion of the acronym "ADRP" with autosomal dominant retinitis pigmentosa (adRP) led to another often- used designation, ADFP.
###end p 4
###begin p 5
###xml 7 9 7 9 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="bib15">15</xref>
###xml 38 40 38 40 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="bib16">16</xref>
###xml 486 488 486 488 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="bib15">15</xref>
###xml 631 633 631 633 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="bib16">16</xref>
###xml 817 819 817 819 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="bib17">17</xref>
###xml 820 822 820 822 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="bib19">19</xref>
###xml 162 167 <span type="species:ncbi:4932">yeast</span>
TIP47 (15), also referred to as PP17 (16), was identified by two groups in separate functional studies. TIP47, tail-interacting protein of 47 kDa, was found in a yeast two-hybrid screen for proteins that interacted with the C-terminal tail of the mannose 6-phosphate receptor (M6PR), hence the alternative terminology, M6PR binding protein 1 (MP6PRBP1). Functional studies suggested linkage as a cargo sorting protein for M6PR trafficking between endosomes and the trans-Golgi network (15). Other workers interested in defining and characterizing placental tissue markers identified the same protein as Placental Protein 17, PP17 (16). This group also characterized several TIP47/PP17 splice variants. The close sequence similarity of ADRP and TIP47 prompted a reexamination of the subcellular distribution of TIP47 (17-19). Like perilipin and ADRP, TIP47 is now a recognized member of the PAT protein family that binds to LSDs.
###end p 5
###begin p 6
###xml 134 136 134 136 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="bib20">20</xref>
###xml 138 140 138 140 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="bib21">21</xref>
###xml 169 170 169 170 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="bib1">1</xref>
###xml 172 174 172 174 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="bib22">22</xref>
###xml 175 177 175 177 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="bib24">24</xref>
Based upon sequence similarities and functional associations with LSDs, two additional PAT proteins are documented in mammals, S3-12 (20, 21) and PAT1/LSDP5/OXPAT/MLDP (1, 22-24).
###end p 6
###begin p 7
###xml 142 149 142 149 <bold xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">Table 1</bold>
###xml 142 149 142 149 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="table" rid="tbl1"><bold>Table 1</bold></xref>
###xml 260 265 260 265 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">Lspd5</italic>
###xml 270 275 270 275 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">S3-12</italic>
###xml 233 239 <span type="species:ncbi:10090">murine</span>
The genes for the five PAT proteins share a common underlying structural organization and are acknowledged to define a novel gene family (see Table 1). Moreover, the genes for TIP47, LSDP5, and S3-12 reside within a 200 kb region of murine chromosome 17, with Lspd5 and S3-12 separated by <2kb.
###end p 7
###begin p 8
A unified nomenclature for the mammalian perilipin-related, PAT-family of intracellular, lipid storage droplet proteins
###end p 8
###begin title 9
NOMENCLATURE
###end title 9
###begin p 10
###xml 533 540 533 540 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="table" rid="tbl1">Table 1</xref>
###xml 331 336 <span type="species:ncbi:10090">Mouse</span>
###xml 401 406 <span type="species:ncbi:9606">Human</span>
Researchers within the lipid droplet field have debated nomenclature confusion regarding the multiple names of the various PAT proteins. Formal reexamination of PAT nomenclature was initiated at the FASEB Summer Research Conference-Lipid Droplets: Metabolic Consequences of the Storage of Neutral Lipids, with encouragement by the Mouse Genomic Nomenclature Committee (MGNC) and consultation with the Human Genome Organization Genomic Nomenclature Committee (HGNC). The nomenclature recommendations were agreed upon without dissent (Table 1).
###end p 10
###begin p 11
###xml 302 304 302 304 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="bib25">25</xref>
###xml 326 335 326 335 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">PERILIPIN</italic>
###xml 421 425 421 425 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">Plin</italic>
###xml 430 434 430 434 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">PLIN</italic>
###xml 444 450 <span type="species:ncbi:10090">murine</span>
###xml 455 460 <span type="species:ncbi:9606">human</span>
Nomenclature based upon variations of the most obvious terms, PAT, LSD, etc., suffer from alternative and prior usage. For example, LSD also designates Lysine Specific Demethylase; these LSD complexes regulate histone methylation and dynamic aspects of chromatin structure and transcriptional control (25). We, thus, selected PERILIPIN as the founding root term based on its precision and elegance, with the gene symbols Plin and PLIN, for the murine and human genomes, respectively. The MGNC and HGNC have approved this nomenclature.
###end p 11
###begin p 12
###xml 14 23 14 23 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">Plin/PLIN</italic>
###xml 94 99 94 99 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">PLIN1</italic>
###xml 104 113 104 113 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">PERILIPIN</italic>
###xml 115 120 115 120 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">PLIN2</italic>
###xml 125 129 125 129 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">ADFP</italic>
###xml 131 136 131 136 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">PLIN3</italic>
###xml 141 145 141 145 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">TIP4</italic>
###xml 148 153 148 153 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">PLIN4</italic>
###xml 158 163 158 163 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">S3-12</italic>
###xml 169 174 169 174 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">PLIN5</italic>
###xml 179 184 179 184 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">LSDP5</italic>
###xml 224 229 224 229 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">PLIN2</italic>
###xml 230 234 230 234 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">ADFP</italic>
###xml 366 370 366 370 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">PP17</italic>
###xml 372 376 372 376 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">PAT1</italic>
###xml 378 383 378 383 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">OXPAT</italic>
###xml 385 389 385 389 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">MLDP</italic>
###xml 395 398 395 398 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">LSD</italic>
Following the Plin/PLIN gene symbol, each family member is numbered sequentially in the order PLIN1 for PERILIPIN, PLIN2 for ADFP, PLIN3 for TIP47, PLIN4 for S3-12, and PLIN5 for LSDP5. Both new and old terminologies (e.g., PLIN2/ADFP) may be of use in the short-term, but we strongly discourage continuous reference to additional alternatives, such as adipophilin, PP17, PAT1, OXPAT, MLDP, and LSD.
###end p 12
###begin p 13
###xml 16 21 <span type="species:ncbi:9606">human</span>
###xml 47 52 <span type="species:ncbi:9606">human</span>
###xml 101 106 <span type="species:ncbi:10090">mouse</span>
###xml 303 308 <span type="species:ncbi:9606">human</span>
###xml 313 318 <span type="species:ncbi:10090">mouse</span>
As standard for human and rodent nomenclature, human gene symbols are fully capitalized, whereas for mouse, only the first letter is upper case. For both systems, gene symbols are italicized, whereas full-length gene names are nonitalics, lower case. Protein symbols are nonitalics, upper case for both human and mouse.
###end p 13
###begin title 14
SPLICE VARIANTS
###end title 14
###begin p 15
###xml 11 16 11 16 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">Plin1</italic>
###xml 74 78 74 78 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">Plin</italic>
###xml 92 93 92 93 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="bib1">1</xref>
###xml 391 392 391 392 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="bib5">5</xref>
###xml 394 395 394 395 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="bib6">6</xref>
###xml 500 506 500 506 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">Plin1a</italic>
###xml 508 514 508 514 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">Plin1b</italic>
###xml 516 522 516 522 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">Plin1c</italic>
###xml 528 534 528 534 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">Plin1d</italic>
###xml 620 641 620 641 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">Plin1a_v1, Plin1a_v2,</italic>
###xml 4 10 <span type="species:ncbi:10090">murine</span>
The murine Plin1 gene organization is the most fully characterized of the Plin gene family (1). There are four splice variant transcripts. As well, closely situated alternative 5'-transcriptional start sites have been described. The mRNA splice variants are predicted to encode four distinct proteins, previously termed perilipin A, B, C, and D; three of these proteins have been confirmed (5, 6). Lower case letters will now denote alternative protein forms PLIN1a, PLIN1b, PLIN1c, and PLIN1d, with Plin1a, Plin1b, Plin1c, and Plin1d as their respective mRNAs. Alternative 5'-starts would be noted as the mRNA variants Plin1a_v1, Plin1a_v2, etc. Similar nomenclature will follow for the other members.
###end p 15
###begin title 16
EVOLUTIONARY RELATIONSHIPS AMONG PLIN GENE FAMILY MEMBERS
###end title 16
###begin p 17
###xml 195 208 195 208 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">Dictyostelium</italic>
###xml 210 211 210 211 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="bib1">1</xref>
###xml 213 215 213 215 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="bib19">19</xref>
###xml 334 336 334 336 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="bib19">19</xref>
###xml 496 501 496 501 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">Plin1</italic>
###xml 510 515 510 515 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">Plin2</italic>
###xml 516 520 516 520 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">Adfp</italic>
###xml 526 543 526 543 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">Drosophila LSDP-1</italic>
###xml 605 609 605 609 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">PLIN</italic>
###xml 623 624 623 624 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="bib1">1</xref>
###xml 759 761 759 761 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="bib26">26</xref>
###xml 762 764 762 764 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="bib28">28</xref>
###xml 489 495 <span type="species:ncbi:10090">murine</span>
###xml 503 509 <span type="species:ncbi:10090">murine</span>
###xml 526 536 <span type="species:ncbi:7227">Drosophila</span>
Sequence similarity argues strongly for orthologs of PLIN1, PLIN2 (ADFP), and PLIN3 (TIP47) in Osteichthyes and Amphibia. Multiple PLIN family members are present in Insecta, and one is found in Dictyostelium (1, 19). These nonvertebrate proteins clearly associate with lipid storage droplets, even when expressed in mammalian cells (19), but their current nomenclatures derive from an LSD protein root (e.g., LSD or LSDP). Nonetheless, the common exon/intron gene organizations among the murine Plin1, murine Plin2/Adfp, and Drosophila LSDP-1 genes indicate an ancient evolutionary origin for the entire PLIN gene family (1). In addition, the insect proteins exhibit lipolytic regulation of LSDs that are comparable to that of mammalian PLIN family members (26-28).
###end p 17
###begin p 18
###xml 4 8 4 8 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">PLIN</italic>
###xml 219 223 219 223 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">PLIN</italic>
###xml 285 303 285 303 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">Dictyostelium PLIN</italic>
###xml 311 315 311 315 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">LSD1</italic>
###xml 316 321 316 321 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">DdlSD</italic>
###xml 323 335 323 335 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">DDB_G0279791</italic>
###xml 370 374 370 374 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">Plin</italic>
###xml 482 487 482 487 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">PLIN1</italic>
The PLIN-based nomenclature is sufficiently flexible and unifying to allow inclusion of all members of this evolutionarily diverse gene family and we encourage all genome annotating organizations to consider the use of PLIN in their current and future nomenclatures. Hence, the single Dictyostelium PLIN member LSD1/DdlSD (DDB_G0279791) would now be cross-referenced as Plin. As with other orthologous genes, particularly in distantly related species, identical nomenclature (e.g., PLIN1) would not imply a common function.
###end p 18
###begin title 19
OTHER LIPID STORAGE DROPLET BINDING PROTEINS
###end title 19
###begin p 20
###xml 78 79 78 79 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="bib1">1</xref>
###xml 127 129 127 129 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="bib29">29</xref>
###xml 437 439 413 415 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="bib29">29</xref>
###xml 795 797 767 769 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="bib30">30</xref>
###xml 799 801 771 773 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="bib31">31</xref>
###xml 984 986 956 958 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="bib32">32</xref>
###xml 987 989 959 961 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="bib35">35</xref>
###xml 875 892 <span type="species:ncbi:11103">hepatitis C virus</span>
The defining characteristics of PLIN proteins include a conserved PAT-domain (1) and an 11-mer repeating helical organization (29). Although, the extreme N-terminal approximately100 amino acids are the most conserved in PLIN1, 2, 3, and 5 proteins, similarity extends through approximately250 amino acids. PLIN4/S3-12 is somewhat distinct, with a highly expanded (>75n) 11-mer repeat region. Although structurally similar 11-mer motifs (29) are found in other lipid-associated proteins (e.g., apolipoproteins and alpha-synuclein), their sequences are unrelated to the PLIN family and are not classified as PAT domains. Although the lipid droplet-binding protein CIDEC/FSP27 (and related family members) shares some limited sequence similarity to PLIN1, it has an unrelated genomic organization (30, 31). Additional lipid storage droplet proteins, such as the plant oleosins, hepatitis C virus core protein, caveolins, and METTL7A/B, which possess other domains for lipid interaction (32-35), are also not members of the PLIN family. Thus, the PLIN nomenclature should not be applied to proteins within a functional category defined by constitutive or transient localization to lipid droplets, but reserved for proteins with an evolutionary relationship that yields conservation of the primary amino acid sequence. We also recognize that PLIN-family member functions may not be solely restricted to their lipid-binding character.
###end p 20
###begin title 21
CONCLUSION
###end title 21
###begin p 22
###xml 101 110 101 110 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">PERILIPIN</italic>
In summary, we recommend adoption of PLIN nomenclature for lipid droplet binding proteins within the PERILIPIN gene family. This nomenclature will reduce confusion over the multiplicity of names for the individual members of the family.
