Mcp7, a meiosis-specific coiled-coil protein of fission yeast, associates with Meu13 and is required for meiotic recombination

###xml 336 360 <span type="species:ncbi:4932">Saccharomyces cerevisiae</span>

We previously showed that Meu13 of Schizosaccharomyces pombe functions in homologous pairing and recombination at meiosis I. Here we show that a meiosis-specific gene encodes a coiled-coil protein that complexes with Meu13 during meiosis in vivo. This gene denoted as mcp7+ (after meiotic coiled-coil protein) is an ortholog of Mnd1 of Saccharomyces cerevisiae. Mcp7 proteins are detected on meiotic chromatin. The phenotypes of mcp7Delta cells are similar to those of meu13Delta cells as they show reduced recombination rates and spore viability and produce spores with abnormal morphology. However, a delay in initiation of meiosis I chromosome segregation of mcp7Delta cells is not so conspicuous as meu13Delta cells, and no meiotic delay is observed in mcp7Deltameu13Delta cells. Mcp7 and Meu13 proteins depend on each other differently; Mcp7 becomes more stable in meu13Delta cells, whereas Meu13 becomes less stable in mcp7Delta cells. Genetic analysis shows that Mcp7 acts in the downstream of Dmc1, homologs of Escherichia coli RecA protein, for both recombination and subsequent sporulation. Taken together, we conclude that Mcp7 associates with Meu13 and together they play a key role in meiotic recombination.

###begin title 3
INTRODUCTION
###end title 3
###begin p 4
###xml 318 319 318 319 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="gkh654c1">1</xref>
###xml 320 321 320 321 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="gkh654c3">3</xref>

###xml 781 783 781 783 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="gkh654c10">10</xref>
###xml 431 455 <span type="species:ncbi:4932">Saccharomyces cerevisiae</span>
###xml 460 485 <span type="species:ncbi:4896">Schizosaccharomyces pombe</span>
###xml 582 594 <span type="species:ncbi:4932">S.cerevisiae</span>
###xml 621 641 <span type="species:ncbi:3702">Arabidopsis thaliana</span>
###xml 646 658 <span type="species:ncbi:10090">Mus musculus</span>
Meiosis is a special type of cell division that produces haploid gametes from diploid parental cells. In many eukaryotes, meiosis-specific events that are important for the generation of genetic diversity, such as synaptonemal complex (SC) formation, homologous pairing and recombination, occur at meiotic prophase I (1-3). Meiotic recombination is initiated by double-strand breaks (DSBs) in the genomic DNA that are catalyzed in Saccharomyces cerevisiae and Schizosaccharomyces pombe by the topoisomerase-like proteins Spo11 (4) and Rec12 (5-7), respectively. Mutants of spo11 in S.cerevisiae and the spo11 homologs in Arabidopsis thaliana and Mus musculus that are incapable of initiating homologous recombination show defects in homologous pairing and/or synapsis formation (8-10).
###end p 4
###begin p 5
###xml 96 108 96 108 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">S.cerevisiae</italic>
###xml 264 266 264 266 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="gkh654c11">11</xref>
###xml 297 304 297 304 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">S.pombe</italic>
###xml 446 448 446 448 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="gkh654c12">12</xref>
###xml 628 631 628 631 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">rad</italic>
###xml 631 632 631 632 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">+</sup>
###xml 640 642 640 642 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="gkh654c12">12</xref>
###xml 744 756 744 756 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">S.cerevisiae</italic>
###xml 820 822 820 822 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="gkh654c13">13</xref>
###xml 1083 1085 1083 1085 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="gkh654c14">14</xref>
###xml 96 108 <span type="species:ncbi:4932">S.cerevisiae</span>
###xml 297 304 <span type="species:ncbi:4896">S.pombe</span>
###xml 744 756 <span type="species:ncbi:4932">S.cerevisiae</span>
Since homologous recombination is critical for the generation of viable gametes, a mechanism in S.cerevisiae called the pachytene checkpoint is known to prevent meiotic progression at the pachytene stage if abnormal recombination and/or chromosome synapsis occur (11). In contrast, a mechanism in S.pombe called the meiotic recombination checkpoint delays initiation of meiosis I chromosome segregation but does not arrest at meiotic prophase I (12). Notably, this delay is tightly linked to the prolonged inactivation of Cdc2 due to the phosphorylation of its tyrosine 15 residues, an event that is dependent on the checkpoint rad+ genes (12). The proteins involved in the DNA damage checkpoint also participate in the pachytene checkpoint in S.cerevisiae and the event depends on the formation and processing of DSBs (13). This mechanism also seems to be conserved in mammals since the Atm, Atr, Rad1 and Chk1 proteins that are involved in the damage checkpoint localize to the meiotic chromosomal cores and are thought to play important roles in recombination and meiotic arrest (14).
###end p 5
###begin p 6
###xml 3 15 3 15 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">S.cerevisiae</italic>
###xml 188 194 188 194 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">E.coli</italic>
###xml 201 203 201 203 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="gkh654c15">15</xref>

###xml 582 604 582 604 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">Caenorhabditis elegans</italic>
###xml 711 716 711 716 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">spo11</italic>
###xml 748 750 748 750 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="gkh654c18">18</xref>
###xml 751 753 751 753 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="gkh654c19">19</xref>
###xml 3 15 <span type="species:ncbi:4932">S.cerevisiae</span>
###xml 188 194 <span type="species:ncbi:562">E.coli</span>
###xml 223 227 <span type="species:ncbi:10090">mice</span>
###xml 273 285 <span type="species:ncbi:4932">S.cerevisiae</span>
###xml 554 577 <span type="species:ncbi:7227">Drosophila melanogaster</span>
###xml 582 604 <span type="species:ncbi:6239">Caenorhabditis elegans</span>
In S.cerevisiae, Hop2 plays a crucial role in the proper alignment of homologous chromosomes during meiotic prophase and functions in the same pathway with Rad51 and Dmc1, two homologs of E.coli RecA (15,16). Hop2 knockout mice display similar phenotypes to hop2 mutant of S.cerevisiae such that spermatocytes arrest at the stage of pachytene-like chromosome condensation and the meiotic DSBs fail to be repaired (17). Thus, the Hop2 functions seem to be conserved in mammals. It should be remembered, however, that a Hop2 ortholog has not been found in Drosophila melanogaster and Caenorhabditis elegans and that recombination itself is not required for either homologous pairing or synapsis in mutants of the spo11 homologues in these organisms (18,19). Thus, the mechanisms regulating meiotic pairing and recombination in these species seem to differ from those in many other organisms.
###end p 6

###xml 1087 1091 1087 1091 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">mnd1</italic>
###xml 1196 1198 1196 1198 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="gkh654c23">23</xref>

###xml 1360 1362 1360 1362 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="gkh654c24">24</xref>
###xml 1407 1411 1407 1411 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">mcp7</italic>
###xml 1411 1412 1411 1412 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">+</sup>
###xml 0 25 <span type="species:ncbi:4896">Schizosaccharomyces pombe</span>
###xml 819 826 <span type="species:ncbi:4896">S.pombe</span>
###xml 1032 1044 <span type="species:ncbi:4932">S.cerevisiae</span>
Schizosaccharomyces pombe is a useful model organism for the study of meiotic recombination, checkpoint and chromosome pairing because it lacks SC formation and harbors only three chromosomes. This makes it possible to study the regulatory mechanisms of these events independent of the SC formation event. With this in mind, we have isolated many meiosis-specific genes (20) and found that one of these, meu13+, which encodes an ortholog of Hop2, plays an important role in homologous pairing (21). In this paper, we report the identification of an association partner of Meu13, and the functional analysis of this protein. This identification was initially made on the basis that Meu13 harbors a coiled-coil motif that is required for protein-protein interaction (22). We searched the genome database for unidentified S.pombe genes that harbor coiled-coil motifs and found one that has a meiosis-specific expression pattern. This gene is referred to as mcp7+ (after meiotic coiled-coil protein) that encodes an ortholog of Mnd1 of S.cerevisiae (23), an association partner of Hop2. The mnd1-null mutant arrests in meiotic prophase with most DSBs unrepaired and homolog pairing severely reduced (23). Mnd1 is suggested to be involved in strand invasion since mnd1-1 mutant cells initiated recombination but did not form heteroduplex DNA or Holliday junctions (24). We report here the functional analysis of mcp7+.

For PCR, we synthesized the following four oligonucleotides and used them as primers: mcp7-5F (5'-CACCGGGGTACCTACTTTATCACCTAAAAATAAAAATGTGTCAACATGTG-3'), mcp7-5R (5'-AGTTCCCTCGAGTGTTTAAAAATTTATTAATTAGTTTGAACAAATATGG-3'), mcp7-3F (5'-TTAAAAACTGCAGACTTGACATGCTTCCTTAATTCAAAGAGTTGTTTTCC-3'), mcp7-3R (5'-CGGGTCGAGCTCGTTTCCAAAGAGAAAGTTCAATATAGCTGTCATAGTTG-3'). To prepare the mcp7+-gfp construct, we performed PCR and obtained a DNA fragment carrying the open reading frame (ORF) region and the 3' downstream region of the mcp7+ gene. ###begin title 18
Immunoprecipitation of Mcp7-3HA and Meu13-GFP
###end title 18
###begin p 19
###xml 472 478 <span type="species:ncbi:9986">rabbit</span>
###xml 529 535 <span type="species:ncbi:9986">rabbit</span>
###xml 1090 1093 <span type="species:ncbi:10116">rat</span>
###xml 1143 1146 <span type="species:ncbi:10116">rat</span>
We added 10 mul protein G-Sepharose beads to 200 mul of the whole cell extract (WCE) and incubated the mixture on a rotating wheel for 1 h at 4degreesC. The beads were pelleted by centrifugation at 12 000 r.p.m. for 20 s at 4degreesC and the supernatant was transferred to a fresh 1.5 ml micro-centrifuge tube on ice. This step removes the proteins in the WCE that interact non-specifically with the beads. We then added primary antibodies specific for HA or GFP, namely, rabbit polyclonal anti-HA (MBL Inc., Japan) antibody and rabbit polyclonal anti-GFP antibody (MBL Inc., Japan) at 1 : 50 and 1 : 200 dilutions, respectively to the supernatant and incubated the mixture on a rotating wheel overnight at 4degreesC. Thereafter, 10 mul of protein G-Sepharose beads were added to the mixture, followed by incubation on a rotating wheel for 1 h at 4degreesC. We pelleted the beads by centrifugation at 5000 r.p.m. for 20 s at 4degreesC, discarded the supernatant, and washed them three times with 1 ml of ice-cold Buffer I. The samples were then subjected to western blot analysis using the rat monoclonal anti-HA antibody 3F10 (Roche) and the rat monoclonal anti-GFP antibody JFP-J5 (Mitsubishi-kagaku Institute of Life Sciences, Tokyo) at 1:1 000 dilutions.

RESULTS
###end title 20
###begin title 21
Mcp7 is a meiosis-specific coiled-coil protein that is conserved in a variety of species
###end title 21
###begin p 22
###xml 54 61 54 61 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">S.pombe</italic>
###xml 347 353 347 353 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">CD16-1</italic>
###xml 355 356 355 356 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">h</italic>
###xml 356 357 356 357 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">+</sup>
###xml 358 359 358 359 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">h</italic>
###xml 359 360 359 360 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">&#8722;</sup>
###xml 366 372 366 372 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">CD16-5</italic>
###xml 375 376 375 376 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">&#8722;</sup>
###xml 374 376 374 376 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">h<sup>&#8722;</sup></italic>
###xml 377 378 377 378 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">h</italic>
###xml 378 379 378 379 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">&#8722;</sup>
###xml 497 501 497 501 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">mcp7</italic>
###xml 501 502 501 502 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">+</sup>
###xml 673 674 673 674 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="fig" rid="gkh654f1">1</xref>
###xml 54 61 <span type="species:ncbi:4896">S.pombe</span>
To identify a meiosis-specific coiled-coil protein of S.pombe that may interact with Meu13, we first searched the genome database for unidentified genes that harbor coiled-coil motifs (), and found 59 genes in this way. We then obtained DNA fragments from each of these genes and used them as probes in northern blot analysis of RNA obtained from CD16-1 (h+/h-) and CD16-5 (h-/h-) cells harvested at various times after the induction of meiosis by nitrogen starvation. This analysis revealed that mcp7+ displays meiosis-specific expression that peaks at the horsetail phase (6 h after induction), which is when homologous chromosome pairing and recombination occur (Figure 1A).
###end p 22
###begin p 23
###xml 152 156 152 156 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">mcp7</italic>
###xml 156 157 156 157 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">+</sup>
###xml 166 174 166 174 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">pat1-114</italic>
###xml 185 189 185 189 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">mcp7</italic>
###xml 189 190 189 190 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">+</sup>
###xml 191 194 191 194 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">3ha</italic>
###xml 291 303 291 303 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">pat1-114mcp7</italic>
###xml 303 304 303 304 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">+</sup>
###xml 305 308 305 308 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">3ha</italic>
###xml 517 529 517 529 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">pat1-114mcp7</italic>
###xml 529 530 529 530 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">+</sup>
###xml 531 534 531 534 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">3ha</italic>
###xml 565 573 565 573 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">pat1-114</italic>
###xml 600 601 600 601 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="fig" rid="gkh654f1">1</xref>
###xml 723 735 723 735 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">S.cerevisiae</italic>
###xml 809 811 809 811 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="gkh654c15">15</xref>
###xml 812 814 812 814 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="gkh654c21">21</xref>
###xml 723 735 <span type="species:ncbi:4932">S.cerevisiae</span>
The production of the Mcp7 protein during meiosis was then assessed by western blot analysis. To attain a synchronized meiotic progression, we replaced mcp7+ gene of pat1-114 cell with mcp7+-3ha fusion gene that can express Mcp7 protein tagged with three copies of the HA epitope. Then, the pat1-114mcp7+-3ha diploid cells were induced to enter synchronized meiosis and their lysates were subjected to western blot analysis using an anti-HA antibody. We confirmed that the meiotic progression and spore morphology of pat1-114mcp7+-3ha diploid cells were similar to pat1-114 cells. As shown in Figure 1B, the timing of appearance of Mcp7-3HA protein during meiosis is the same with that of Meu13, an ortholog of the protein S.cerevisiae Hop2, which is required for proper homologous pairing and recombination (15,21). However, the timings of peaks and disappearance of Mcp7 protein are about 30 min delay as compared to those of Meu13.
###end p 23
###begin p 24
###xml 142 143 142 143 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="fig" rid="gkh654f1">1</xref>
###xml 221 233 221 233 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">S.cerevisiae</italic>
###xml 242 253 242 253 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">Arabidopsis</italic>
###xml 279 280 279 280 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="fig" rid="gkh654f1">1</xref>
###xml 413 415 413 415 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="gkh654c29">29</xref>
###xml 529 530 529 530 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="fig" rid="gkh654f1">1</xref>
###xml 221 233 <span type="species:ncbi:4932">S.cerevisiae</span>
###xml 235 239 <span type="species:ncbi:4932">Mnd1</span>
###xml 255 259 <span type="species:ncbi:10090">mice</span>
###xml 264 270 <span type="species:ncbi:9606">humans</span>
Mcp7 consists of 210 amino acids and harbors a coiled-coil motif (amino acids 75-134) and a nuclear localization signal (11-14; KRRR) (Figure 1C). Using the BLAST algorithm (), we identified putative orthologs of Mcp7 in S.cerevisiae (Mnd1), Arabidopsis, mice and humans (Figure 1D). All of these orthologs bear a coiled-coil motif in the middle. The phylogenetic tree constructed by the neighbor-joining method (29) indicates that the amino acid sequences of these five Mcp7 orthologs are closely related to one another (Figure 1E). Thus, Mcp7 may play a conserved role in different species.
###end p 24
###begin title 25
Mcp7 forms a complex with Meu13 and localizes in the nucleus
###end title 25
###begin p 26
###xml 136 143 136 143 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">in vivo</italic>
###xml 401 402 401 402 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="fig" rid="gkh654f2">2</xref>
###xml 457 464 457 464 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">in vivo</italic>
###xml 856 857 856 857 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="fig" rid="gkh654f1">1</xref>
Since both Mcp7 and Meu13 harbor a coiled-coil motif, we first investigated by immunoprecipitation analysis whether they form a complex in vivo. To do this, lysates of cells expressing Mcp7-3HA and Meu13-GFP were subjected to immunoprecipitation with an anti-GFP antibody followed by western blotting and probing with an anti-HA antibody. This revealed a band of the expected size of Mcp7-3HA (Figure 2A). This suggests that Mcp7 forms a complex with Meu13 in vivo. The blot also reveals the presence of another, smaller band that is apparently recognized by the anti-HA antibody. This band did not disappear when the lysates were treated with phosphatase (data not shown), which suggests that it is not a phosphorylated form of Mcp7. Instead, this band disappeared when the cell lysates were boiled just after dissolving them in the HB buffer (see Figure 1B and Materials and Methods). Thus, it may be a degradation product because the cell lysates were kept intact overnight for immunoprecipitation, during which the lysates were exposed to proteases before they were boiled for electrophoresis.
###end p 26
###begin p 27
###xml 237 241 237 241 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">mcp7</italic>
###xml 242 245 242 245 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">gfp</italic>
###xml 393 394 393 394 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="fig" rid="gkh654f2">2</xref>
###xml 430 432 430 432 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="gkh654c21">21</xref>
###xml 629 633 629 633 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">mcp7</italic>
###xml 633 634 633 634 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">+</sup>
###xml 635 638 635 638 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">gfp</italic>
###xml 866 867 866 867 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="fig" rid="gkh654f2">2</xref>
To examine the subcellular localization of Mcp7, we constructed a Mcp7-GFP-expressing strain, induced it to undergo meiosis and observed the GFP signal under a microscope. We considered the Mcp7-GFP fusion protein functional because the mcp7-gfp strain showed normal meiotic progression and spore morphology. The Mcp7-GFP protein began to appear in the nucleus at the horsetail period (Figure 2B). This suggests that, like Meu13 (21), Mcp7 localizes in the nucleus during meiosis. To determine whether this nuclear localization is due to the attachment of Mcp7 to the chromosomes, we performed a chromatin spread experiment with mcp7+-gfp cells that were collected 6 h after meiotic induction. The GFP signal was observed on the chromatin in most of the chromatin spreads counted (more than 50), which suggests that Mcp7 is tightly attached to the chromatin (Figure 2C). The result suggests that Mcp7 may be involved in the events on chromatin during meiosis, such as pairing of homologous chromosomes, DNA recombination and/or formation of linear element.
###end p 27
###begin p 28
###xml 70 74 70 74 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">mcp7</italic>
###xml 95 99 91 95 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">mcp7</italic>
###xml 154 158 146 150 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">pat1</italic>
###xml 182 183 174 175 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="fig" rid="gkh654f2">2</xref>
###xml 312 316 304 308 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">pat1</italic>
###xml 332 337 324 329 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">meu13</italic>
###xml 445 450 433 438 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">meu13</italic>
###xml 471 472 455 456 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="fig" rid="gkh654f2">2</xref>
###xml 675 680 659 664 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">meu13</italic>
###xml 690 694 670 674 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">mcp7</italic>
When we confirmed by western blot analysis that Meu13 is expressed in mcp7Delta, we found that mcp7Delta cells contain less Meu13 protein compared to the pat1 control strain (Figure 2D-i). This suggests as one of the possibilities that Meu13 is less stable when Mcp7 is absent. On the other hand, as compared to pat1 control cells, meu13Delta cells contain similar amounts of Mcp7-3HA whose expression is prolonged due to the delayed meiosis of meu13Delta strain (Figure 2D-ii). The result may also suggest that Mcp7 becomes more stable when Meu13 is absent. This difference in dependence between Mcp7 and Meu13 proteins may explain some of the phenotypic difference between meu13Delta and mcp7Delta cells (see Discussion).
###end p 28
###begin title 29
###xml 4 8 4 8 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">mcp7</italic>

To determine whether, like Meu13 (21), Mcp7 plays a role in meiotic recombination, we measured the rates of intergenic and intragenic recombinations in the mcp7Delta strain. For comparison, we also measured them in the meu13Delta strain and in a null mutant of a checkpoint rad+ gene, rad17Delta. The mcp7Deltameu13Delta and mcp7Deltarad17Delta double null mutants were also assessed.
###end p 30

We first investigated the crossover recombination of zygotic meiosis by tetrad analysis, which allowed us to measure the genetic distance between leu1 and his2 (Figure 3A insets). When the mcp7Delta strain was crossed, the genetic distance between leu1 and his2 was only 5% of the distance obtained when the wild-type strain was crossed. In the mcp7Deltarad17Delta strains, spore formation was too poor to analyze statistically (only two four viable spores were detected among 126 tetrads tested).

To further characterize the meiotic recombination competency of the mcp7Delta strain, we examined the allelic intragenic recombination between two different mutant alleles of ade6 (M26 and 469). Next, to investigate the DNA distributions in the asci, we stained DNAs with Hoechst33342 and observed them under a microscope. As shown in Figure 4B, approximately30% of the asci of the mcp7Delta, meu13Delta and mcp7Deltameu13Delta mutants contained abnormal number of nuclei or fragmented DNA, most of them inside and the others outside the ascospores, which indicates that nuclear segregation occurred abnormally in these mutants. However, only 12% of the rad17Delta asci showed similar abnormalities, but this value increased up to 62% in the mcp7Deltarad17Delta double mutant. This is similar to what has been observed for the meu13Deltarad17Delta double mutant (12). As shown in Figure 4C, the spore viability of rad17Delta was similar to that of the wild-type cells, whereas the mcp7Delta, meu13Delta and mcp7Deltameu13Delta mutants displayed slightly reduced spore viability. On the other hand, the mcp7Deltarad17 mutant showed significantly reduced spore viability compared with the corresponding single mutants.
###end p 35
###begin p 36
###xml 92 93 92 93 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="fig" rid="gkh654f4">4</xref>
###xml 151 152 151 152 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="fig" rid="gkh654f4">4</xref>

###xml 376 380 368 372 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">mcp7</italic>
###xml 386 391 374 379 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">meu13</italic>
###xml 531 535 515 519 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">mcp7</italic>
###xml 540 545 520 525 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">rad17</italic>
###xml 613 614 589 590 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="fig" rid="gkh654f4">4</xref>
These results indicate a close correlation between aberrant spore numbers per ascus (Figure 4A) and percentages of unequally segregated nuclei (Figure 4B), except for the mcp7Deltarad17Delta double mutant where there is good spore formation but many unequally-segregated nuclei. This seems to imply that Rad17 activity delays meiosis to allow some repair of fragmented DNA in mcp7Delta/meu13Delta backgrounds, but at the cost of defective spore formation. In contrast, spores form mostly normally but DNA is highly fragmented in a mcp7Deltarad17Delta double mutant, hence yielding the low spore viability (Figure 4C).

###begin title 37
###xml 0 4 0 4 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">mcp7</italic>
mcp7Delta cells show a delay in initiation of meiosis I chromosome segregation
###end title 37
###begin p 38
###xml 26 31 26 31 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">meu13</italic>
###xml 146 151 142 147 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">rad17</italic>
###xml 151 152 147 148 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">+</sup>

###xml 178 180 174 176 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="gkh654c12">12</xref>
###xml 204 208 200 204 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">mcp7</italic>
###xml 323 331 315 323 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">pat1-114</italic>
###xml 443 445 435 437 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="gkh654c30">30</xref>
###xml 473 477 465 469 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">pat1</italic>
###xml 479 487 471 479 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">pat1mcp7</italic>
###xml 494 503 482 491 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">pat1meu13</italic>
###xml 513 521 497 505 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">pat1mcp7</italic>
###xml 526 531 506 511 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">meu13</italic>
###xml 561 562 537 538 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">1</sub>
###xml 648 649 624 625 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="fig" rid="gkh654f5">5</xref>
###xml 1016 1020 968 972 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">pat1</italic>
We previously showed that meu13Delta cells exhibit a delay in initiation of meiosis I chromosome segregation, and that this delay is dependent on rad17+, a checkpoint rad+ gene (12). To determine whether mcp7Delta cells also show similar defects, we monitored their meiotic progression. To synchronize meiosis, we used the pat1-114 mutant, which enters meiosis in a highly synchronous manner when it is shifted to its restrictive temperature (30). Thus, homozygous diploid pat1, pat1mcp7Delta, pat1meu13Delta and pat1mcp7Deltameu13Delta cells were arrested at G1 stage by nitrogen starvation and then shifted to the restrictive temperature (Figure 5A). Meiotic recombination occurs at the horsetail stage, which is when the nucleus moves backwards and forwards several times. The horsetail stage started at similar time points in all the strains tested (approximately2 h after temperature shift, peaking at approximately3 h; data not shown). Thus, the absence of Mcp7 or Meu13 does not appear to affect the entry of pat1 cells into meiotic prophase.
###end p 38
###begin p 39
###xml 7 17 7 17 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">pat1 meu13</italic>
###xml 161 165 157 161 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">pat1</italic>
###xml 379 380 375 376 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="fig" rid="gkh654f5">5</xref>
###xml 435 443 431 439 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">pat1mcp7</italic>
###xml 471 475 463 467 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">pat1</italic>
###xml 567 575 559 567 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">pat1mcp7</italic>
###xml 580 585 568 573 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">meu13</italic>
###xml 626 630 610 614 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">pat1</italic>
###xml 679 684 663 668 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">meu13</italic>
###xml 728 732 708 712 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">mcp7</italic>
In the pat1 meu13Delta double mutant, appearance of binucleates due to meiosis I chromosome segregation peaked at 5.25 h. This represents a delay as compared to pat1 peaking at 4.75 h. To show this delay more clearly, number of cells that harbor one, two or more than three nuclei at 4.5 h or 5.25 h after temperature shift to induce meiosis were displayed by bar graphs (Figure 5B). In contrast, the profile of meiotic progression of pat1mcp7Delta mutant was similar to pat1 cells, showing only a slight delay. Notably, the peak (4.75 h) of binucleate appearance of pat1mcp7Deltameu13Delta cells was almost equal to those of pat1 cells. This indicates that the meiotic delay of meu13Delta mutation was suppressed by additional mcp7Delta mutation and that the checkpoint is not triggered in the absence of both Mcp7 and Meu13 (see Discussion). These meiotic progression patterns are reliable because we confirmed them by performing more than three independent experiments and averaging the results.
###end p 39
###begin p 40
###xml 59 68 59 68 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">pat1rad17</italic>
###xml 78 86 74 82 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">pat1mcp7</italic>

###xml 331 334 315 318 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">rad</italic>
###xml 334 335 318 319 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">+</sup>
###xml 360 362 344 346 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="gkh654c12">12</xref>
###xml 424 429 408 413 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">rec12</italic>
###xml 439 443 419 423 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">mcp7</italic>
###xml 448 453 424 429 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">rec12</italic>
###xml 545 546 517 518 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="fig" rid="gkh654f5">5</xref>
###xml 639 643 611 615 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">mcp7</italic>
We further examined the profiles of meiotic progression in pat1rad17Delta and pat1mcp7Delta rad17Delta mutants, and found that they are similar to pat1 cells (Figure 5B). This suggests that the slight meiotic delay of the pat1mcp7Delta cells is partly due to defective recombination or DSB repair, which is monitored by checkpoint rad+ proteins such as Rad17 (12). We also investigated the profile of meiotic progression in rec12Delta and mcp7Deltarec12Delta mutants and found that no meiotic delay is observed in these two mutant cells (Figure 5B). The result indicates that the delay in initiation of meiosis I chromosome segregation of mcp7Delta cells is Rec12-dependent, namely, it is dependent on meiotic DSB formation. The result suggests that Mcp7 functions after meiotic DSB formation.
###end p 40
###begin title 41
###xml 11 15 11 15 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">mcp7</italic>
###xml 33 42 29 38 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">pat1meu13</italic>
Additional mcp7Delta mutation in pat1meu13Delta cells abolished the delay in phosphorylation of the tyrosine 15 residue of Cdc2

We previously showed that the phosphorylated form of Cdc2 increases during the pre-meiotic S phase of pat1 cells and then decreases during meiotic division (12). Moreover, we revealed that the delay pat1meu13Delta cells show in initiation of meiosis I chromosome segregation is tightly linked to a delay in the dephosphorylation of the tyrosine 15 residue of Cdc2 (12). To determine whether the suppression of meiotic delay by additional mcp7Delta mutation in pat1meu13Delta cells is linked to Cdc2 dephosphorylation, we performed time-course western blot analysis using an anti-phospho-Cdc2 antibody. As shown in Figure 6, the timing of tyrosine 15 dephosphorylation of Cdc2 in the pat1mcp7Deltameu13Delta triple mutant cells was almost similar to the pat1 control. The delay in tyrosine 15 dephosphorylations of the pat1meu13Delta cells was pronounced as we reported previously (12), whereas only a slight delay is observed in pat1mcp7Delta cells. The results are consistent with the onset timing of meiosis I chromosome segregation in these mutant cells (Figure 5A and B). These results indicate that the suppression of meiotic delay in pat1meu13Delta cells by additional mcp7Delta mutation is also tightly linked to the tyrosine 15 dephosphorylation of Cdc2.

###begin title 43

###end title 43
###begin p 44
###xml 78 80 78 80 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="gkh654c12">12</xref>

###xml 413 414 397 398 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="fig" rid="gkh654f7">7</xref>
###xml 422 426 406 410 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">mcp7</italic>

###xml 522 526 498 502 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">dmc1</italic>
###xml 655 667 627 639 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">S.cerevisiae</italic>

We previously reported that disruption of Dmc1 function caused meiotic delay (12). To know if mcp7+ shows any genetic interactions with dmc1+, we first compared the recombination rates between mcp7Delta and dmc1Delta mutants. As shown in Figure 7A, the rates of crossing over were severely reduced in mcp7Delta, and dmc1Delta cells. Gene conversion rates were also reduced similarly in these mutant cells (Figure 7B). The mcp7Deltadmc1Delta double mutant shows similar levels of crossing over and gene conversion rates to dmc1Delta mutant. These results suggest that Mcp7 and Dmc1 play key roles in meiotic recombination in the same pathway. Similarly in S.cerevisiae, Mnd1 is supposed to operate downstream of Dmc1 binding to DNA (24).
###end p 44
###begin p 45
###xml 23 27 23 27 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">mcp7</italic>

###xml 459 460 435 436 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="fig" rid="gkh654f7">7</xref>
###xml 491 495 467 471 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">mcp7</italic>
###xml 500 504 472 476 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">dmc1</italic>
###xml 555 559 523 527 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">dmc1</italic>
###xml 608 612 572 576 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">dmc1</italic>
###xml 855 859 815 819 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">dmc1</italic>
###xml 891 895 851 855 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">hop2</italic>
###xml 915 927 875 887 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">S.cerevisiae</italic>

The spore viability of mcp7Delta mutant was slightly lowered as compared to wild-type and dmc1Delta mutants (Figure 7C). On the other hand, the spore viability of mcp7Deltadmc1Delta double mutant was a wild-type level. We also observed DNAs in the asci by staining them with Hoechst33342 and found that the percentages of the asci harboring unequally distributed DNA contents increased to 30.3 and 15.5% in mcp7Delta and dmc1Delta cells, respectively (Figure 7D). ###begin title 46
DISCUSSION
###end title 46
###begin title 47
Mcp7 and Meu13 show similar properties and cooperate during meiosis
###end title 47
###begin p 48
###xml 766 767 766 767 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="fig" rid="gkh654f1">1</xref>
###xml 850 851 850 851 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="fig" rid="gkh654f2">2</xref>
###xml 857 858 857 858 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="fig" rid="gkh654f2">2</xref>
###xml 982 983 982 983 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="fig" rid="gkh654f3">3</xref>
###xml 988 989 988 989 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="fig" rid="gkh654f4">4</xref>
###xml 1051 1052 1051 1052 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="fig" rid="gkh654f4">4</xref>
###xml 1104 1109 1104 1109 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">rad17</italic>
###xml 1109 1110 1109 1110 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">+</sup>
###xml 1125 1128 1125 1128 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">rad</italic>
###xml 1128 1129 1128 1129 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">+</sup>
###xml 478 484 <span type="species:ncbi:9606">humans</span>
###xml 486 490 <span type="species:ncbi:10090">mice</span>
###xml 511 516 <span type="species:ncbi:4932">yeast</span>
In the present study, we show that Mcp7 associates with Meu13 to play a role in meiotic recombination and generation of normal spores, probably due to appropriate chromosome pairing. We demonstrate here that these two proteins share the following similar properties. First, their sizes are similar (210 and 216 amino acids), and they both harbor a centrally located coiled-coil motif. Second, they are both evolutionarily conserved among species as their orthologs are found in humans, mice, plants and budding yeast, although the nematode and the fly are exceptions to this, as is discussed above. Third, they are specifically expressed during meiosis at similar time points, namely, at the pre-S phase through to meiosis II, peaking at the horsetail phase (Figure 1A). Fourth, they both localize to the nucleus, in particular, on chromatin (Figure 2B and 2C). Fifth, the phenotypes of the null mutants are similar, showing reduced recombination rates and spore viability (Figures 3 and 4C). They also produce spores with abnormal morphology (Figure 4). Sixth, some of these properties are dependent on rad17+, a checkpoint rad+ gene.
###end p 48
###begin p 49
###xml 22 34 22 34 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">S.cerevisiae</italic>
###xml 36 38 36 38 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="gkh654c23">23</xref>
###xml 277 278 277 278 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="fig" rid="gkh654f2">2</xref>
###xml 335 342 335 342 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">in vivo</italic>
###xml 532 534 532 534 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="gkh654c21">21</xref>
###xml 5 9 <span type="species:ncbi:4932">Mnd1</span>
###xml 22 34 <span type="species:ncbi:4932">S.cerevisiae</span>
Like Mnd1 and Hop2 of S.cerevisiae (23), we assume that Mcp7 and Meu13 cooperate during meiosis through interaction with each other. Evidence supporting this is that when they are both expressed within a cell, antibodies specific for one co-immunoprecipitate the other (Figure 2A). This suggests that these proteins can form a complex in vivo. The result indicates that Mcp7 and Meu13 form a complex during meiosis and as components of the Meu13 protein complex, they may cooperatively participate in homologous chromosome pairing (21).
###end p 49
###begin title 50
Mcp7 and Meu13 distinctly cooperate during meiosis
###end title 50
###begin p 51
###xml 342 346 342 346 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">mcp7</italic>
###xml 366 371 362 367 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">meu13</italic>
###xml 391 392 383 384 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="fig" rid="gkh654f5">5</xref>
###xml 500 501 492 493 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="fig" rid="gkh654f1">1</xref>
###xml 539 544 531 536 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">meu13</italic>
###xml 594 598 582 586 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">mcp7</italic>
###xml 633 634 617 618 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="fig" rid="gkh654f2">2</xref>
On the other hand, Mcp7 and Meu13 differ in the following properties. First, Mcp7 and Meu13 show no homology in their amino acid sequences, and Mcp7 alone harbors a nuclear localization signal. Second, their meiotic progression patterns differ slightly, namely, the delay in initiation of meiosis I chromosome segregation is less apparent in mcp7Delta cells than in meu13Delta cells (Figure 5A). Third, the timing of disappearance during meiosis of Mcp7 protein is 30 min after that of Meu13 (Figure 1B). Fourth, Mcp7 protein is stable in meu13Delta cells, whereas Meu13 protein is unstable in mcp7Delta cells during meiosis (Figure 2D).
###end p 51
###begin p 52
###xml 19 23 19 23 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">mcp7</italic>
###xml 28 33 24 29 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">meu13</italic>
###xml 324 328 316 320 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">mcp7</italic>
###xml 338 343 326 331 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">meu13</italic>
###xml 459 461 443 445 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="gkh654c12">12</xref>

###xml 1567 1571 1519 1523 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">mcp7</italic>
###xml 1576 1581 1524 1529 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">meu13</italic>
###xml 1685 1692 1629 1636 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">S.pombe</italic>
###xml 1685 1692 <span type="species:ncbi:4896">S.pombe</span>
It is notable that mcp7Deltameu13Delta double mutants exhibit no delay, not only in initiation of meiosis I chromosome segregation but also in dephosphorylating the tyrosine 15 residue of Cdc2. This phenotype can be explained as follows although it is speculative with the present results: delays in meiotic progressions in mcp7Delta and meu13Delta cells are due to the meiotic checkpoint that may monitor aberrant pairing between non-homologous chromosomes (12,15,21,23). Mcp7 and Meu13 cooperate to form proper pairing between homologous chromosomes in wild-type cells. However, in the absence of association partners in mcp7Delta or meu13Delta cells, Meu13 proteins alone or Mcp7 proteins alone are considered to promote aberrant chromosome pairing in proportion to their amounts. Since the amount of Meu13 proteins in mcp7Delta cells is much less than that of Mcp7 proteins in meu13Delta cells (Figure 2D), a larger amount of aberrantly paired chromosome is expected to exist in meu13Delta cells than mcp7Delta cells. These aberrantly paired chromosomes may trigger the meiotic checkpoint machinery to delay meiotic progression before they are repaired. The extent of meiotic delay may be in proportion to the aberrantly paired chromosome to be repaired, which explains why the delay of meu13Delta cells is larger than mcp7Delta cells (Figure 5). The amount of aberrantly paired chromosomes, however, may be reduced because of inefficient chromosome pairing itself in the absence of both Mcp7 and Meu13 proteins, and thus no triggering of checkpoint may occur in mcp7Deltameu13Delta double mutant cells, explaining the absence of meiotic delay. Examination of these assumptions in S.pombe will require further technical developments.

###begin title 53
Evolutionary conservation of Mcp7/Meu13 function
###end title 53
###begin p 54

###xml 84 88 <span type="species:ncbi:4932">Mnd1</span>
###xml 355 359 <span type="species:ncbi:4932">Mnd1</span>
Mcp7 is an ortholog of the Mnd1 protein of S.cerevisiae (23,24). Mcp7 is similar to Mnd1 in that both proteins are meiosis-specific chromosomal proteins that harbor a coiled-coil motif and that are required for meiotic recombination. Moreover, they need at least one association partner to fulfill their functions, namely, Mcp7 interacts with Meu13 while Mnd1 interacts with Hop2 (15).

Orthologs of Mcp7 and Meu13 have been identified in S.cerevisiae, Arabidopsis, mice and humans as described above. Mnd1 and Hop2 depend on each other for their nuclear localization and it has been reported that they also form a stable complex (23). In S.cerevisiae, when SC formation is abnormal due to the defect in homologous chromosome pairing or recombination, meiosis arrests at the pachytene stage (23). In contrast, in S.pombe, meiosis is not arrested even in such abnormal cases. However, recombination-defective mutants of S.pombe, such as the meu13Delta strain, will delay their initiations of meiosis I chromosome segregation, and this delay is imposed by the monitoring system that is called the 'meiotic recombination checkpoint' (12). Notably, orthologs of Mcp7 and Meu13 have not been identified in C.elegans and D.melanogaster. SC formation also differs in these organisms in that Spo11 is not required for synapsis in C.elegans (31) and D.melanogaster (32) but is required for synapsis in S.cerevisiae (33), mice (9,10) and Arabidopsis (8). Thus, the mechanism regulating synapsis in C.elegans and D.melanogaster may have evolved differently. It may be that orthologs of Mcp7 and Meu13 are required only in organisms that have selected homologous recombination as an essential event in chromosome synapsis.

###xml 464 466 464 466 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="gkh654c16">16</xref>
###xml 175 187 <span type="species:ncbi:4932">S.cerevisiae</span>
###xml 194 198 <span type="species:ncbi:4932">Mnd1</span>
###xml 348 352 <span type="species:ncbi:4932">Mnd1</span>
Our result suggests that Mcp7 forms a complex with Meu13 that plays a key role in chromosome pairing (21) and seems to act after the formation of meiotic DSBs (Figure 5D). In S.cerevisiae, Hop2/Mnd1 complex promotes meiotic chromosome pairing and DSB repair (23). We also showed that Mcp7 functions in the downstream of Dmc1 (Figure 7). Similarly, Mnd1 operates downstream of Dmc1 binding to DNA (24), and Hop2 functions in the downstream of Dmc1 for sporulation (16). Thus, function of Mcp7/Meu13 complex seems to be conserved among these species.
