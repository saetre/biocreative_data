The Toll-->NFkappaB Signaling Pathway Mediates the Neuropathological Effects of the Human Alzheimer's Abeta42 Polypeptide in Drosophila
###end article-title 0
###begin p 1

###begin p 2
###xml 526 536 505 515 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">Drosophila</italic>
###xml 736 740 708 712 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">Toll</italic>

###xml 589 594 <span type="species:ncbi:9606">human</span>
###xml 1738 1746 <span type="species:ncbi:9606">patients</span>
Alzheimer's (AD) is a progressive neurodegenerative disease that afflicts a significant fraction of older individuals. Although a proteolytic product of the Amyloid precursor protein, the Alphabeta42 polypeptide, has been directly implicated in the disease, the genes and biological pathways that are deployed during the process of Alphabeta42 induced neurodegeneration are not well understood and remain controversial. To identify genes and pathways that mediated Alphabeta42 induced neurodegeneration we took advantage of a Drosophila model for AD disease in which ectopically expressed human Alphabeta42 polypeptide induces cell death and tissue degeneration in the compound eye. One of the genes identified in our genetic screen is Toll (Tl). It encodes the receptor for the highly conserved Tl-->NFkB innate immunity/inflammatory pathway and is a fly homolog of the mammalian Interleukin-1 (Ilk-1) receptor. We found that Tl loss-of-function mutations dominantly suppress the neuropathological effects of the Alphabeta42 polypeptide while gain-of-function mutations that increase receptor activity dominantly enhance them. Furthermore, we present evidence demonstrating that Tl and key downstream components of the innate immunity/inflammatory pathway play a central role in mediating the neuropathological activities of Alphabeta42. We show that the deleterious effects of Alphabeta42 can be suppressed by genetic manipulations of the Tl-->NFkB pathway that downregulate signal transduction. Conversely, manipulations that upregulate signal transduction exacerbate the deleterious effects of Abeta42. Since postmortem studies have shown that the Ilk-1-->NFkB innate immunity pathway is substantially upregulated in the brains of AD patients, the demonstration that the Tl-->NFkB signaling actively promotes the process of Alphabeta42 induced cell death and tissue degeneration in flies points to possible therapeutic targets and strategies.
###end p 2
###begin title 3
Introduction
###end title 3
###begin p 4
###xml 209 212 209 212 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0003966-FaberLangendoen1">[1]</xref>
###xml 213 216 213 216 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0003966-Brouwers1">[5]</xref>
###xml 345 348 342 345 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0003966-Cruts1">[3]</xref>
###xml 349 352 346 349 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0003966-Kang1">[6]</xref>
###xml 683 686 674 677 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0003966-Goate1">[7]</xref>
###xml 687 691 678 682 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0003966-Watson1">[10]</xref>
###xml 947 951 935 939 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0003966-Sherrington1">[11]</xref>
###xml 952 956 940 944 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0003966-Rogaev1">[14]</xref>
###xml 518 526 <span type="species:ncbi:9606">patients</span>
Although the neurodegenerative Alzheimer's disease (AD) is usually associated with the aging process, mutations in a number of different genes have been correlated with a familial, early onset of the dementia [1]-[5]. The first AD-related mutations identified were in the amyloid beta precursor protein (APP), a type I integral membrane protein [3]-[6]. The APP protein is a precursor for a 42 residue proteolytic cleavage product, Abeta42, that accumulates in large aggregates or plaques in the brains of Alzheimer's patients. Mutations in the APP protein which are associated with early onset of AD increase the production of Abeta42 polypeptide or its tendency to form aggregates [7]-[10]. The connection between APP and, in particular, the Abeta42 polypeptide and AD was further strengthened by the finding that mutations in presenlin, a protease involved in the processing of the APP protein, are also associated with familial early onset AD [11]-[14]. These mutations are thought to increase the amount of Abeta42 polypeptide produced relative to another non-toxic APP cleavage product, the Abeta40 polypeptide.
###end p 4
###begin p 5
###xml 398 402 392 396 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0003966-Carter1">[15]</xref>
###xml 403 407 397 401 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0003966-Imahori1">[19]</xref>
###xml 959 963 947 951 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0003966-Catalano1">[20]</xref>
###xml 964 968 952 956 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0003966-Cleary1">[22]</xref>
While these observations have implicated Abeta42 in AD, how this polypeptide induces neurodegeneration remains uncertain. The brains of individuals with AD are characterized by large plaques of aggregated Abeta42 protein, neurofibrillary tangles composed of hyperphosphorylated Tau protein, and the loss of a significant fraction of the neurons in the hippocampus, prefrontal and entorhinal cortex [15]-[19]. Initially it was thought that the aggregated Abeta42 containing amyloid plaques induced the neurofibrillary tangles and neurodegeneration. However, the connection between large amyloid plaques and AD is unlikely to be direct as the correlation between the extent of plaque formation and either the severity of dementia or the loss of neurons is poor. Instead, recent studies have suggested that the large plaques may actually be relatively inert and that smaller oligomers of the Abeta42 polypeptide correspond to the primary neurotoxic agent for AD [20]-[22].
###end p 5
###begin p 6
###xml 360 370 357 367 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">Drosophila</italic>
###xml 370 374 367 371 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0003966-Crowther1">[23]</xref>
###xml 375 379 372 376 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0003966-Iijima1">[26]</xref>
###xml 933 943 924 934 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">Drosophila</italic>
###xml 1228 1232 1207 1211 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0003966-Iijima1">[26]</xref>
###xml 350 359 <span type="species:ncbi:7227">fruit fly</span>
###xml 403 408 <span type="species:ncbi:9606">human</span>
###xml 954 959 <span type="species:ncbi:9606">human</span>
###xml 1098 1103 <span type="species:ncbi:9606">human</span>
If the Abeta42 polypeptide is a causative agent for AD, it is important to understand what biological pathways are targeted by the polypeptide and how these pathways are deployed to produce the neuropathological phenotypes associated with the disease. With this aim in mind several laboratories have independently developed Alzheimer's models in the fruit fly Drosophila[23]-[26]. Ectopic expression of human Abeta42 polypeptide in the CNS of the fly was found to cause a range of phenotypes including a progressive decline in locomotor function, age-dependent learning defects, progressive neurodegeneration and loss of neurons and a significant reduction in lifespan. The expression of this protein was accompanied by the formation of diffuse amyloid deposits that seemed to be composed primarily of Abeta42 oligomers in younger animals, while in older animals small AD-like plaques. Further supporting the potential value of this Drosophila model for human AD, the deleterious effects of the Abeta polypeptide are specific to the Abeta42 isoform. Thus, unlike Abeta42, ectopic expression of the human Abeta40 polypeptide did not disrupt locomotor function or alter the lifespan, nor did it appear to induce neurodegeneration [26].
###end p 6
###begin p 7
###xml 173 177 167 171 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0003966-Finelli1">[25]</xref>
###xml 179 183 173 177 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0003966-Iijima1">[26]</xref>
###xml 1428 1432 1413 1417 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0003966-Cao1">[27]</xref>
###xml 37 42 <span type="species:ncbi:9606">human</span>
The neuropathological effects of the human Abeta42 polypeptide are not limited to the CNS. When Abeta42 is expressed in the eye it causes cell death and tissue degeneration [25], [26]. This disrupts the stereotypic and repeating morphology of the lens and gives a readily visible rough eye phenotype that worsens with age. Within a single transgenic line the severity of the rough eye phenotype is quite similar amongst flies of the same age; however, the phenotype depends upon the level of expression of the transgene insert and some inserts have only mild effects on eye development while others have moderate or even severe effects. Since the rough eye phenotype is sensitive to changes in Abeta42 activity it is well suited for identifying genes that modify the pathological effects of the Abeta42 polypeptide. This phenotypic assay has been used to screen a collection of nearly 2,000 EP transposon strains for insertions that alter the Abeta42-induced rough eye phenotype. The EP transposon has a GAL4 activated promoter and, depending upon the site and orientation of the insertion, it will upregulate gene activity, have no effect, or downregulate gene activity. Altogether 23 lines that modified the rough eye phenotype were recovered in the EP screen. The interacting EP insertions were found to be located in genes involved in secretion, cholesterol homeostatis, the innate immune pathway and chromatin organization [27].
###end p 7

###xml 1031 1035 1022 1026 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0003966-Anderson1">[32]</xref>
###xml 1037 1041 1028 1032 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0003966-Hashimoto1">[33]</xref>
###xml 1161 1165 1152 1156 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0003966-Morisato1">[34]</xref>
###xml 1346 1350 1333 1337 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0003966-Steward1">[35]</xref>
###xml 1351 1355 1338 1342 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0003966-DeLotto1">[36]</xref>
###xml 1442 1444 1429 1431 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">Tl</italic>
###xml 1592 1596 1573 1577 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0003966-Brennan1">[28]</xref>
###xml 1597 1601 1578 1582 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0003966-Hoffman1">[31]</xref>
###xml 1603 1607 1584 1588 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0003966-Meng1">[38]</xref>
###xml 1608 1612 1589 1593 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0003966-Rutschmann1">[40]</xref>
As a complementary approach for elucidating the genes and pathways that mediate the neurodegenerative effects of the Abeta42 polypeptide, we have screened for second site loss-of-function mutations that dominantly enhance or suppress the degeneration of the eye induced by the Abeta42 polypeptide. To identify genes important for Abeta42 neuropathology we first screened the Bloomington stock center 2nd and 3rd chromosome deficiency kit for interacting deletions. We then pinpointed the locus responsible for modifying the rough eye phenotype by testing mutations in genes that are included in the deficiency. One of the suppressing deficiencies uncovered the Drosophila Toll (Tl) gene. Toll encodes a transmembrane receptor which has a leucine rich extracellular domain and intracellular signaling domain that is closely related to the mammalian Interleukin-1 receptor [28]-[31]. The Tl gene was first identified in maternal effect screens because of its role in establishing the dorsal-ventral (D-V) polarity axis of the embryo [32], [33]. In D-V polarity, binding of Spatzle ligand to the Toll extracellular domain activates a cytoplasmic signaling cascade [34]. This cascade promotes the nuclear translocation of two NFkappaB-like transcription factors, Dorsal and Dif, which function to specify ventral cell fate in blastoderm stage embryo [35]-[36]. Subsequent work demonstrated that in addition to its role in embryonic polarity, the Tl receptor-->NFkappaB signal transduction pathway is deployed, just as it is in mammals, in the innate immunity and inflammatory pathways of the fly [28]-[31], [38]-[40].
###end p 8
###begin p 9
###xml 46 48 46 48 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">Tl</italic>
###xml 175 180 <span type="species:ncbi:9606">human</span>
###xml 304 310 <span type="species:ncbi:9606">humans</span>
In the studies reported here we show that the Tl-->NFkappaB innate immunity-inflammatory pathway plays a central role in orchestrating the neuropathological activities of the human Abeta42 polypeptide in flies. Our findings support emerging models for AD and other forms of neurodegenerative diseases in humans in which the inflammatory response acts as a critical catalyst in promoting the process of neurodegeneration.
###end p 9

###begin title 11
Reducing Tl activity ameliorates the neuropathological effects of the Abeta42 polypeptide on eye development

###xml 129 133 126 130 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0003966-Iijima1">[26]</xref>

###xml 562 564 550 552 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">nd</sup>
###xml 570 572 558 560 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">rd</sup>
Ectopic expression of Abeta42 in the developing eye using the Glass Multimer Reporter, pGMR, induces a rough eye phenotype [25], [26]. This phenotype can be used to screen for interacting genes that mediate the neurodegenerative effects of the Abeta42 polypetide on the Drosophila eye. Since we wished to identify mutations in genes that can either ameliorate or potentiate the effects of the Abeta42 polypeptide, we selected a pGMR:Abeta42 insertion that has a moderate rough eye phenotype (Fig. 1A). We used this insert to screen the Fly Center collection of 2nd and 3rd chromosome deficiencies (which is thought to account for approximately60% of fly genes) for deletions that dominantly (Df/+) suppress or enhance the Abeta42 eye phenotype. Of the 130 deficiencies tested, we identified 14 strong or moderate suppressors and 9 strong or moderate enhancers.

To identify the suppressor we next tested mutations in genes that are known to be located in this chromosomal interval. One of the genes that is included in the 97D and is deleted in all of the suppressing deficiencies is Tl. The first Tl mutation we tested, Tlr4, is an ethylmethanesulfonate (EMS) induced allele. It is a hypomorphic mutation that has two amino acid substitutions in the extracellular domain of the receptor [46]. ###begin title 20
Excess Tl activity enhances the neuropathological effects of Abeta42
###end title 20
###begin p 21
###xml 67 69 67 69 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">Tl</italic>
###xml 294 298 291 295 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0003966-Hoffmann1">[45]</xref>
###xml 299 303 296 300 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0003966-Golde1">[49]</xref>
###xml 640 644 637 641 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0003966-Gahtan1">[47]</xref>
###xml 645 649 642 646 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0003966-WyssCoray2">[52]</xref>
###xml 774 776 771 773 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">Tl</italic>
###xml 419 427 <span type="species:ncbi:9606">patients</span>
The finding that several independent loss-of-function mutations in Tl dominantly suppress the rough eye phenotype induced by ectopic Abeta42 is intriguing as connections between AD and canonical mammalian innate immunity/inflammatory pathways have been extensively documented in the literature [45]-[49]. However, while these studies clearly demonstrate that the inflammatory pathways are activated in the brains of AD patients, it is not clear from this correlation whether the deployment of these pathways actively promotes the process of neurodegeneration or whether they are functioning instead to retard the progression of the disease [47]-[52]. Thus, it would be important to determine whether there is actually a causal relationship between the activation of the fly Tl-->NFkappaB innate immunity/inflammatory pathway and the disruptive effects of Abeta42 on eye development.
###end p 21
###begin p 22
###xml 11 13 11 13 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">Tl</italic>
###xml 127 129 118 120 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">Tl</italic>
###xml 273 275 261 263 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">Tl</italic>
###xml 381 383 369 371 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">Tl</italic>
###xml 849 851 831 833 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">Tl</italic>
###xml 862 863 844 845 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">3</sup>
###xml 860 863 842 845 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">Tl<sup>3</sup></italic>
###xml 1014 1018 996 1000 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0003966-Hoffman1">[31]</xref>
###xml 1020 1024 1002 1006 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0003966-Hoffmann1">[45]</xref>
###xml 1038 1045 1020 1027 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="fig" rid="pone-0003966-g001">Fig. 1F</xref>
###xml 1051 1058 1033 1040 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="table" rid="pone-0003966-t001">Table 1</xref>
If the fly Tl-->NFkappaB signaling pathway functions to actively promote the neuropathological effects of Abeta42, then excess Tl activity would be expected to exacerbate the Abeta42 induced rough eye phenotype. Consistent with this prediction, the transposon insertion in Tl identified in the EP screen strongly enhances the rough eye phenotype. Since the transposon upregultates Tl mRNA by nearly 5 fold, this would be expected to substantially increase the amount of Tl protein. On the other hand, while elevated levels of the receptor should potentiate signal transduction, we cannot exclude the possibility that Abeta42 or some side product induces neurodegeneration by some type of interaction with Tl that does not involve an activation of the receptor. To test this possibility we combined the pGMR-Abeta42 transgene with a gain-of-function Tl allele, Tl3. This allele has an amino acid substitution in the extracellular domain that leads to constitutive signaling by the Tl receptor independent of ligand [31], [45]. As shown in Fig. 1F (and Table 1) excess Tl signaling enhances the neuropathological effects of the Abeta42 polypeptide.
###end p 22
###begin title 23
Neuropathological effects of Abeta42 are mediated by the Tl signaling pathway
###end title 23
###begin p 24
###xml 39 41 39 41 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">Tl</italic>
###xml 98 100 98 100 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">Tl</italic>
###xml 406 408 394 396 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">Tl</italic>
###xml 513 515 495 497 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">Tl</italic>
The effects of reducing and increasing Tl activity would be consistent with the idea that the fly Tl-->NFkappaB signaling pathway plays an instrumental role in promoting Abeta42 induced cell death and tissue degeneration in the eye. This hypothesis makes two strong predictions. The first is that the severity of the Abeta42 rough eye phenotype will be sensitive to the activity of other components of the Tl-->NFkappaB signaling pathway. The second is that genetic manipulations which lead to an upregulation of Tl-->NFkappaB signal transduction will tend to exacerbate the rough eye phenotype, while manipulations that downregulate signal transduction will tend ameliorate the phenotype.
###end p 24
###begin p 25
###xml 12 18 12 18 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="fig" rid="pone-0003966-g002">Fig. 2</xref>
###xml 158 162 158 162 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0003966-Brennan1">[28]</xref>
###xml 163 167 163 167 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0003966-Hoffman1">[31]</xref>
###xml 169 173 169 173 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0003966-Hashimoto1">[33]</xref>
As shown in Fig. 2, a circulating peptide, Spatzle, which is activated by proteolysis binds to the Tl receptor and turns on the cytoplasmic signaling cascade [28]-[31], [33]. Signal transduction depends upon an adaptor protein Tube (Tub) and the IRAK-like kinase Pelle (Pll). The target for the Pll kinase is the fly IkappaB homolog Cactus (Cact). In the absence of signaling, the Cact protein binds to the NFkappaB family transcription factors Dl and Dif and retains them in the cytoplasm. Phosphorylation of Cact by Pll is thought to target Cact for degradation, and this releases Dl and Dif for nuclear translocation and regulation of their target genes.

The disruptions in eye development induced by the Abeta42 polypetide are dose sensitive and can be greatly enhanced when there are two copies of the pGMR-Abeta42 transgene. Unlike the hemizygotes (see Figs 1 & 3), the eyes of homozygous flies are smaller in size and instead of a regular repeating hexagonal array of ommatidia, the surviving ommatidia are highly disorganized and have a blistered and very glossy morphology (see Abeta/Abeta eyes in Fig. 4). We reasoned that if the Tl-->dl pathway is a key player in Abeta42 induced neurodegeneration, then it would be possible to ameliorate even this much more severe disruption in eye development by reducing dl activity. This is the case. In fact, we found that reducing the dose of the dl gene from 2 to 1 wild type copy strongly suppresses the neuropathological effects of the Abeta42 polypeptide. This was observed not only for the moderate suppressor, dl4, but also for both of the weaker suppressors dl1 and dl8 (Fig. 4). In all three cases, the eye of the pGMR-Abeta42/pGMR-Abeta42; dl/+ fly is nearly wild type in size. Moreover, instead of the very rough and glossy eye morphology evident in the Abeta42 homozygotes, the ommatidia of the dl/+ flies are arranged in a considerably more regular and repeating hexagonal array across most of the eye. Suppression is not, however, complete and the eye morphology is not like wild type. For example, there are many speckles scattered across the dl1/+ eye shown in Fig. 3A (see arrows) which correspond to ommatidia that have dead or dying cells. Similarly, though the ommatidia in the dl-/+ flies shown in Fig. 3B & C have a much more regular organization than their dl+ sibs, the surface of the eye still appears rough and glossy.

###begin title 44
Abeta42 upregulates Cact expression in fly heads
###end title 44
###begin p 45
###xml 153 157 145 149 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0003966-Sun1">[54]</xref>
###xml 210 212 202 204 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">Tl</italic>
###xml 317 321 305 309 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0003966-Boutros1">[55]</xref>
###xml 323 327 311 315 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0003966-DeGregorio1">[56]</xref>
###xml 397 399 382 384 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">Tl</italic>
###xml 937 944 906 913 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="fig" rid="pone-0003966-g005">Fig. 5A</xref>
In vertebrates one of the genes that is upregulated by the inflammatory response is IkappaB which is responsible for retaining NFkappaB in the cytoplasm [54]. This is also true in flies where activation of the Tl pathway following microbial infection is found to induce the expression of the fly IkappaB homolog Cact [55], [56]. Our results would argue that ectopic Abeta42 must also activate the Tl-->NFkappaB innate immune response pathway. If this supposition is correct, then the expression of the fly IkappaB homolog Cact should be upregulated by Abeta42. To test this prediction we compared Cact protein accumulation in head extracts prepared from pooled samples of either 10 wild type flies or 10 transgenic flies that carry two copies of the pGMR-Abeta42 transgene. In wild type flies, only low levels of Cact protein are detected in head extracts. However, as evident from inspection of the two independent experiments shown in Fig. 5A, there is substantial increase in the levels of Cact protein in the heads of pGMR-Abeta42 flies compared to the wild type controls.
###end p 45
###begin title 46
Cactus protein expression is induced by the Abeta42 polypeptide.
###end title 46
###begin p 47
Panel A. Extracts were prepared from the heads of 10 wild type flies (WT) or 10 flies that are homozygous for the pGMR-Abeta42 transgene (Abeta). After gel electrophoresis and blotting, the blots were probed with Cactus and Snf (Sans fille-the fly U1A/U2B'' snRNP protein) antibodies. The upper panel is Cactus, while the lower panel is the Snf loading control. Two independent experiments are shown. Quantitation indicates that the level of Cactus protein is about 3.5-5 fold higher in the heads of flies carrying the transgene. Panel B. Extracts were prepared from heads of single wild type or pGMR-Abeta42 transgenic flies. After gel electrophoresis and blotting the blots were probed with Cactus (and Snf: not shown) antibodies. Quantitation indicates that the level of Cactus protein in heads of individual transgenic flies was between 3-8 fold higher than in wild type.
###end p 47
###begin p 48
###xml 361 368 352 359 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="fig" rid="pone-0003966-g005">Fig. 5B</xref>
###xml 829 836 811 818 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="fig" rid="pone-0003966-g005">Fig. 5B</xref>
###xml 944 951 926 933 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="fig" rid="pone-0003966-g005">Fig. 5B</xref>
We wondered whether the induction of the inflammatory response by ectopic Abeta42 is stochastic and is upregulated in only subset of the flies, or whether most flies exhibit a similar reaction to the Abeta42 polypeptide. To investigate this question, we examined Cact expression in heads from individual wild type and Abeta42 transgene flies. The experiment in Fig. 5B shows Cact protein accumulation in heads from three wild flies and from six flies carrying two copies of pGMR-Abeta42 transgene. Cact protein levels are elevated compared to wild type in heads from five of the six transgenic flies. Altogether we examined Cact expression in heads from 22 wild type flies and 18 pGMR-Abeta42/pGMR-Abeta42 flies. In all of the wild type flies we observed only low levels of Cact protein comparable to the three examples shown in Fig. 5B. By contrast, Cact protein levels were elevated to an extent similar to that seen in the examples shown in Fig. 5B in 17 of the 18 transgenic flies. In other experiments we tested whether another marker for the induction of the inflammatory response, Dorsal, was also upregulated in the heads of pGMR-Abeta42/pGMR-Abeta42 flies. Elevated levels of Dl were observed in the heads of the 5 flies that were tested (data not shown).
###end p 48
###begin p 49
While these findings indicate that the inflammatory response is consistently activated by ectopic Abeta42, it seemed possible that the efficiency or extent of induction would depend upon the dose of the Abeta42 protein. To address this question we examined Cact expression in the heads of transgenic flies that were hemizygous rather than homozygous for the pGMR-Abeta42 transgene. As might be expected if induction of the inflammatory response depends upon the dose of the Abeta42 protein, we found that only 13 of the 18 head extracts examined had higher levels of Cact protein than the wild type controls. We also noted that the level of Cact protein in the pGMR-Abeta42 hemizygotes did not appear to be as high as in the flies homozygous for the pGMR-Abeta42 transgene (not shown).

Tl signaling pathway and life span
###end title 50
###begin p 51
###xml 97 106 94 103 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">elav-GAL4</italic>

###xml 310 312 304 306 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">Tl</italic>

Since the deleterious effects of ectopic Abeta42 in the eye are dependent upon activation of the Tl pathway, we wondered whether this was also true in the CNS. To address this question we asked whether modulating the activity of the Tl signaling pathway alters the life span defects observed in Abeta42 flies.

Discussion

###begin p 56
###xml 262 266 262 266 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0003966-Schneider1">[46]</xref>
###xml 267 271 267 271 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0003966-McGeer1">[51]</xref>
###xml 648 652 648 652 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0003966-McGeer2">[57]</xref>
###xml 653 657 653 657 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0003966-McGeer4">[59]</xref>
###xml 860 864 857 861 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0003966-McGeer4">[59]</xref>
###xml 865 869 862 866 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0003966-Walter1">[62]</xref>
###xml 1385 1389 1369 1373 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0003966-McGeer4">[59]</xref>
###xml 1391 1395 1375 1379 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0003966-Walter1">[62]</xref>
###xml 1396 1399 1380 1383 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0003966-He1">[4]</xref>
###xml 1676 1680 1660 1664 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0003966-WyssCoray1">[48]</xref>
###xml 1682 1686 1666 1670 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0003966-Golde1">[49]</xref>
###xml 1688 1692 1672 1676 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0003966-WyssCoray2">[52]</xref>
###xml 1926 1930 1910 1914 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0003966-WyssCoray2">[52]</xref>
###xml 155 163 <span type="species:ncbi:9606">patients</span>
###xml 977 982 <span type="species:ncbi:9606">human</span>
###xml 1212 1217 <span type="species:ncbi:10090">mouse</span>
While it has been known for more than two decades that there is a global upregulation of the inflammatory and innate immunity pathways in the brains of AD patients, it has not been clear what role, if any, the inflammatory response plays in AD neurodegeneration [46]-[51]. Over the years a number of findings have raised the possibility that the inflammatory response plays an active role in promoting neurodegeneration. One of the first indications that it could contribute to disease progression came from epidemiological studies indicating that prolonged treatment with anti-inflammatory drugs is correlated with a reduced risk of developing AD [57]-[59]. Experiments using either mammalian tissue culture cell or whole organism AD models have also pointed to a connection between Abeta induced neurodegeneration and activation of the inflammatory response [59]-[62]. These studies have shown that expression of the cytokines IL-1, IL-6 and TNF-alpha are upregulated by the human Abeta peptide and that induction of these cytokines both by Abeta and by other mechanisms can have deleterious effects on neuronal cell viability and cognition. Moreover, downregulation of the innate immune response pathway in a mouse AD model by treatment with anti-inflammatory drugs or disruption of the tumor necrosis factor death receptor gene was found to ameliorate the effects of ectopic Abeta [59], [62]-[4]. On the other hand, not all findings have been consistent with the notion that the inflammatory response/innate immunity pathways promote neurodegeneration. In fact, a number of reports have suggested instead that these pathways play a beneficial rather than deleterious role [48], [49], [52]. Thus, it is not clear at this point whether inflammation is a causative agent in the process of neurodegeneration, is an essentially benign response to AD, or is actually beneficial and acts to retard the progression of the disease [52].
###end p 56
###begin p 57
###xml 68 70 68 70 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">Tl</italic>
###xml 371 381 359 369 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">Drosophila</italic>
###xml 450 452 438 440 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">Tl</italic>
###xml 178 183 <span type="species:ncbi:9606">human</span>
The findings reported here strongly support the hypothesis that the Tl-->NFkappaB innate immunity pathway plays a critical role in mediating the neuropathological effects of the human Abeta42 polypeptide. We identified this pathway in an unbiased genetic screen for mutations that either promote or suppress the neurpathological effects of the Abeta42 polypeptide on the Drosophila eye. One of the suppressors recovered in our genetic screen was the Tl receptor, which is a key component of the fly innate immunity pathway.
###end p 57
###begin p 58
###xml 133 135 130 132 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">Tl</italic>
###xml 289 291 277 279 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">Tl</italic>
###xml 336 340 314 318 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">cact</italic>
###xml 656 660 624 628 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0003966-Gahtan1">[47]</xref>
###xml 661 665 629 633 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0003966-Golde1">[49]</xref>
###xml 667 671 635 639 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0003966-McGeer4">[59]</xref>
###xml 672 676 640 644 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0003966-Lim1">[63]</xref>
###xml 702 706 670 674 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">cact</italic>
###xml 826 830 788 792 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">cact</italic>
###xml 624 632 <span type="species:ncbi:9606">patients</span>
###xml 640 645 <span type="species:ncbi:10090">mouse</span>
Several lines of evidence demonstrate a direct connection between the neuropathological effects of Abeta42 and the activation of the Tl-->NFkappaB signaling cascade. First, the Abeta42 polypeptide induces the accumulation of one of the well known downstream transcriptional targets of the Tl-->NFkappaB pathway, the fly IkappaB homolog cact. We also found that the levels of the fly NFkappaB protein Dl are upregulated in pGMR-Abeta42 transgenic flies as well. Thus, the response of flies to the neurotoxic effects of the Abeta42 polypeptide appears to mimic the upregulation of the inflammatory pathways evident in both AD patients and in mouse AD models [47]-[49], [59]-[63]. Moreover, the extent of cact induction depends upon the dose of the Abeta42 polypeptide. In flies carrying two copies of the pGMR-Abeta42 transgene cact accumulation is substantially elevated in virtually every fly. By contrast, in flies carrying only a single pGMR-Abeta42 transgene there is typically a smaller increase in the level of the Cact protein, and in a subset of the transgenic flies little change in Cact accumulation is evident.
###end p 58
###begin p 59
###xml 184 186 181 183 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">Tl</italic>
###xml 278 280 272 274 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">Tl</italic>
###xml 452 454 443 445 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">Tl</italic>
###xml 501 505 492 496 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0003966-Cao1">[27]</xref>
###xml 511 513 502 504 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">Tl</italic>
###xml 652 654 640 642 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">Tl</italic>
Second, as would be predicted if activation of the inflammatory response mediates the neuropathological effects of the Abeta42 polypeptide, we found that loss-of-function mutations in Tl dominantly suppress neurodegeneration of the eye induced by ectopic Abeta42. Conversely, a Tl gain-of-function allele that signals constitutively independent of ligand exacerbates the degenerative effects of Abeta42. In this context, it is interesting to note that Tl was identified in the screen of EP insertions [27]. The Tl insertion was found to be a strong enhancer of Abeta42 induced degeneration of the eye and it substantially upregulates the expression of Tl mRNA. Thus, it is possible to enhance the sensitivity of the fly to the pathological effects of Abeta42 not only by increasing the activity of the Tl receptor, but also by increasing the amount of the receptor.
###end p 59
###begin p 60
###xml 86 88 83 85 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">Tl</italic>
###xml 123 125 114 116 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">dl</italic>
###xml 228 230 216 218 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">dl</italic>
###xml 347 349 332 334 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">dl</italic>
###xml 673 675 652 654 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">dl</italic>
###xml 772 774 751 753 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">dl</italic>
###xml 921 923 897 899 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">dl</italic>
###xml 1096 1098 1069 1071 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">dl</italic>
###xml 1213 1215 1186 1188 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">dl</italic>
Third, Abeta42 induced neurodegeneration is mediated by the downstream target for the Tl-->NFkappaB signaling cascade, the dl transcription factor. This is most clearly demonstrated in pGMR-Abeta42 flies that are homozygous for dl mutations. When there is only a single copy of the transgene, suppression of the Abeta42 rough eye phenotype by the dl mutation is nearly complete and the eyes resemble wild type. Degeneration of the eye is dependent on the dose of the Abeta42 polypeptide and is much more severe when there are two copies of the pGMR-Abeta42 transgene. However, even in this case strong suppression is observed when the transgenic flies are homozygous for a dl loss of function mutation. Moreover, this is not due to genetic background as three independent dl alleles substantially reduce the much more extreme neuropathological effects produced by two copies of the pGMR-Abeta42 transgene. The effects of dl are not limited to homozygous mutant flies; we also found that the pGMR-Abeta42 induced disruptions in eye development are suppressed when the flies are heterozygous for a dl mutation. In this case, suppression is less complete than that observed in flies that are homozygous for the same dl mutation.
###end p 60

###xml 132 134 126 128 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">dl</italic>
###xml 157 160 151 154 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">dif</italic>
###xml 361 365 352 356 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0003966-Cao1">[27]</xref>
###xml 367 371 358 362 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0003966-Lehming1">[65]</xref>
###xml 418 420 409 411 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">Tl</italic>
###xml 547 549 529 531 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">Tl</italic>

###xml 689 693 671 675 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">cact</italic>
###xml 717 722 699 704 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">myd88</italic>
###xml 735 739 717 721 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">cact</italic>
###xml 744 749 726 731 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">myd88</italic>
###xml 812 814 794 796 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">Tl</italic>
###xml 947 950 923 926 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">spz</italic>
###xml 1050 1052 1026 1028 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">Tl</italic>
Fourth, like Tl and dl, mutations in three other components of the Tl-->NFkappaB signaling cascade, tub, pll and the partner of the dl transcription factor, dif also dominantly suppress the degenerative effects of Abeta42 on eye development. In addition, one of the chromatin modifiers recovered in the EP screen, Dsp1, functions as a Dl dependent co-repressor [27], [65]. It should be noted that not all genes in the Tl-->NFkappaB signaling cascade show dominant genetic interactions with pGMR-Abeta42. We tested three different mutations in the Tl ligand spz; however, no alterations in the rough eye phenotype were observed. Similarly no effects were observed with a mutation in either cact or the adaptor protein myd88. Since both cact and myd88 are thought to be canonical cell autonomous components of the Tl-->NFkappaB signaling pathway a plausible explanation is that these genes are not haploinsufficient in the eye assay. In the case of spz we cannot exclude the possibility that some other effector molecule mediates the activation of the Tl-->NFkappaB pathway.

###begin p 62
###xml 47 49 47 49 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">Tl</italic>
###xml 269 271 257 259 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">Tl</italic>
###xml 332 334 320 322 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">r4</sup>
###xml 330 334 318 322 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">Tl<sup>r4</sup></italic>
###xml 371 382 359 367 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">UAS-A&#946;42</italic>
###xml 383 392 368 377 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">elav-GAL4</italic>
###xml 490 492 475 477 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">Tl</italic>
###xml 530 533 515 518 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">tub</italic>
###xml 538 541 523 526 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">pll</italic>
###xml 744 746 729 731 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">Tl</italic>
###xml 851 862 830 838 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">UAS-A&#946;42</italic>
###xml 863 872 839 848 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">elav-GAL4</italic>
###xml 892 894 868 870 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">Tl</italic>
###xml 998 1001 974 977 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">tub</italic>
###xml 1006 1009 982 985 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">pll</italic>
###xml 1135 1137 1108 1110 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">dl</italic>
###xml 1258 1261 1231 1234 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">tub</italic>
###xml 1265 1268 1238 1241 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">pll</italic>
###xml 2075 2077 2036 2038 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">Tl</italic>
While our results clearly demonstrate that the Tl-->NFkappaB pathway plays a key role in facilitating the degenerative effects of the Abeta42 polypeptide on the eye, the evidence that it also mediates the Abeta42-dependent reduction in life span is less clear cut. The Tl allele that is the strongest suppressor in the eye assay, Tlr4, appears to extend the life span of UAS-Abeta42/elav-GAL4 flies by about a quarter. However, much more modest effects, if any, were observed for two other Tl alleles, as well as for mutations in tub and pll. Several factors could explain why these other mutations didn't greatly extend the life span. For one, it is possible that the mutations we tested do not sufficiently reduce the overall activity of the Tl-->NFkappaB signaling pathway as heterozygotes to have much of an impact on the average life span of the UAS-Abeta42/elav-GAL4 flies. Both of the Tl alleles are hypomorphs and neither was a strong suppressor in the eye assay. This is also true for the tub and pll mutations. Given the very strong suppression of the rough eye phenotype observed pGMR- Abeta42 flies that are homozygous for dl mutations, it is possible that we would observe a significant suppression of the life span defects in mutants that lack tub or pll altogether. Another difference is that the life span assay is likely to be a much more indirect measure of the degenerative effects of the Abeta42 polypeptide. While the deleterious effects of the Abeta42 polypeptide on individual cells in each ommatidia can be observed directly, life span depends upon a complex combination of genetic background, environmental conditions and chance circumstances and the neurodegeneration induced by Abeta42 is just one element among many that determine mortality rates. Additionally, there may be specific neuronal circuits whose activity must be maintained above a critical threshold in order to prolong survival. In this case, even if the overall neurodegenerative effects of the Abeta42 polypeptide in the CNS are greatly ameliorated by reducing the activity of the Tl-->NFkappaB pathway, this might not be sufficient to substantially increase life span.
###end p 62
###begin p 63
###xml 54 56 54 56 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">Tl</italic>
###xml 351 353 342 344 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">Tl</italic>
###xml 654 656 639 641 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">Tl</italic>
It is also possible that reducing the activity of the Tl-->NFkappaB signaling pathway by two-fold (in animals heterozygous for a null allele) will not be sufficient in itself to strongly suppress the deleterious effects of the Abeta42 polypeptide on life span. One reason why this might be the case is that neurodegenerative effects of activating the Tl-->NFkappaB signaling pathway in the CNS likely depend upon triggering other, cell autonomous pathways or processes after a prolonged or chronic inflammatory response. In this case, it might be necessary to simultaneously alter the activity of one or more of these interacting pathways in addition to Tl-->NFkappaB.
###end p 63
###begin p 64
###xml 206 215 203 212 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">Df(3)H99,</italic>

###xml 2075 2079 2057 2061 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0003966-Sorrentino1">[70]</xref>

###xml 2160 2163 2139 2142 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">tau</italic>
###xml 2234 2237 2213 2216 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">tau</italic>
###xml 2431 2433 2407 2409 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">Tl</italic>
###xml 2634 2636 2601 2603 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">Tl</italic>
###xml 428 433 <span type="species:ncbi:9606">human</span>
###xml 785 793 <span type="species:ncbi:9606">patients</span>
###xml 1988 1996 <span type="species:ncbi:9606">patients</span>
Some potential candidates for the participating pathways were uncovered in our genetic screen for genes that modulate the Abeta42 induced rough eye phenotype. For example, we found that a small deficiency, Df(3)H99, which removes three pro-apoptotic genes [66], reaper, grim and hid, is a strong suppressor of the rough eye phenotype. This would suggest that the cell death pathway promotes the neurodegenerative effects of the human Abeta42 polypeptide in the Drosophila eye. Consistent with an induction of apoptosis, we found that a mutation in croquemort, which is required for the phagocytosis of apoptotic cells in Drosophila[67], dominantly enhances the rough eye phenotype. A role for apoptosis in the degenerative process would also be consistent with studies on brains of AD patients which have shown that markers for apoptosis are elevated. In this regard it is interesting to note that c-Jun N-terminal kinase (JNK) signaling cascade, which is thought to promote apoptosis, is upregulated in flies by both the Tl-->NFkappaB and IMD innate immunity pathways [56]. Moreover, a number of observations support a possible role for the JNK pathway in Abeta42 induced neurodegeneration in the fly as well. First, one of the chromatin modifiers identified in the EP screen was the histone deacetylase Rpd3 (Hdac1)[27]. Rpd3 histone deacetylase activity is required to downregulate the JNK pathway and it functions in complexes with Sin3A and Sap130 [68]. EP insertions that disrupt the function of Rpd3, Sin3A and Sap130 were found to enhance the rough eye phenotype. Second, we found that two mutations in pucker (puc), which encodes a tyrosine phosphatase that antagonizes the JNK kinase enhance the rough eye phenotype. It has also recently been suggested that activation of the apoptosis cascade, by JNK, promotes the cleavage and hyperphosphorylation of Tau [69]. Tau is a microtubule-associated protein which accumulates in large aggregates in the neurofibrillary tangles of AD patients [70]. The Tau protein can also have neurotoxic effects independent of Abeta42 [70], [71]. The Abeta42 induced rough eye phenotype seems to be connected to the fly tau gene as well as since we found that it is enhanced by two independent tau mutations. Further studies will be required to elucidate the role of these and other genes in modulating the neuropathological effects of Abeta42 and their connection to the functioning of the Tl-->NFkappaB pathway. It will also be important to determine if it is possible to more substantially ameliorate the life span defects of Abeta42 flies by simultaneously manipulating the activity of the Tl-->NFkappaB pathway and one of these other pathways.

GMR-Abeta42/Ubx virgin females were used for crosses to males from different deficiencies and candidates strains. Crosses were kept at 22degreesC, F1 flies were collected and aged for three weeks before being scored for eye phonotype. On average, 50-100 flies that are trans-heterozygote for a deletion or candidate mutant and one copy of GMR-Abeta42 transgene were scored simultaneously and were compared to their similarly aged siblings who only had one copy of the GMR-Abeta42 transgene. The suppression/enhancement of the rough eye phenotype was characterized into weak, moderate or strong categories as indicated in the tables.
