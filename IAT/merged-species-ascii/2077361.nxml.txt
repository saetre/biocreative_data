Mrc1 protects uncapped budding yeast telomeres from exonuclease EXO1

###begin title 2
Abstract
###end title 2
###begin p 3
###xml 301 308 301 308 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">cdc13-1</italic>
###xml 313 323 313 319 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">yku70&#916;</italic>
###xml 397 401 393 397 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">MRC1</italic>
###xml 405 409 401 405 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">mrc1</italic>
###xml 409 411 405 407 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">AQ</italic>
###xml 409 411 405 407 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><italic>AQ</italic></sup>
###xml 497 514 493 506 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">cdc13-1 mrc1&#916;</italic>
###xml 652 659 644 651 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">cdc13-1</italic>
###xml 805 809 797 801 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">EXO1</italic>
###xml 929 946 921 934 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">cdc13-1 mrc1&#916;</italic>
Mrc1 (Mediator of Replication Checkpoint 1) is a component of the DNA replication fork machinery and is necessary for checkpoint activation after replication stress. In this study, we addressed the role of Mrc1 at uncapped telomeres. Our experiments show that Mrc1 contributes to the vitality of both cdc13-1 and yku70Delta telomere capping mutants. Cells with telomere capping defects containing MRC1 or mrc1AQ, a checkpoint defective allele, exhibit similar growth, suggesting growth defects of cdc13-1 mrc1Delta are not due to checkpoint defects. This is in accordance with Mrc1-independent Rad53 activation after telomere uncapping. Poor growth of cdc13-1 mutants in the absence of Mrc1 is a result of enhanced single stranded DNA accumulation at uncapped telomeres. Consistent with this, deletion of EXO1, encoding a nuclease that contributes to single stranded DNA accumulation after telomere uncapping, improves growth of cdc13-1 mrc1Delta strains and decreases ssDNA production. Our observations show that Mrc1, a core component of the replication fork, plays an important role in telomere capping, protecting from nucleases and checkpoint pathways.
###end p 3
###begin title 4
Keywords
###end title 4
###begin title 5
Introduction
###end title 5
###begin p 6
###xml 577 582 577 582 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" rid="bib1 bib2 bib3 bib4" ref-type="bibr">[1&#8211;4]</xref>
Telomeres are specialized DNA-protein complexes at the end of eukaryotic chromosomes. Proper telomere structure is essential for chromosome integrity and genome stability because telomeres protect natural chromosome ends from degradation and end-to-end fusion and because they ensure complete genome replication. Telomeres differ from Double Strand Breaks (DSBs) in that they normally fail to activate DNA repair or DNA damage checkpoint pathways. If that was the case, then they would undergo recombination and chromosomal fusions and dicentric chromosomes would be generated [1-4].
###end p 6
###begin p 7
###xml 382 385 382 385 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" rid="bib5" ref-type="bibr">[5]</xref>
###xml 504 510 504 510 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" rid="bib6 bib7 bib8 bib9 bib10 bib11" ref-type="bibr">[6&#8211;11]</xref>
###xml 259 264 <span type="species:ncbi:4932">yeast</span>
###xml 399 404 <span type="species:ncbi:4932">yeast</span>
Many proteins associate with telomeric DNA. These proteins ensure that telomeres behave differently from DSB ends and help maintain chromosomal stability. Some telomeric proteins bind specifically to dsDNA and others show higher affinity to ssDNA. In budding yeast, there are numerous proteins with affinity for telomeric dsDNA, such as Rap1, Sir2, Sir3, Sir4, Rif1 and Yku70/Yku80 [5]. The budding yeast telomeric ssDNA ends are thought to be protected by three essential proteins, Cdc13, Stn1 and Ten1 [6-11].
###end p 7
###begin p 8
###xml 103 110 103 110 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" rid="bib12 bib13 bib14" ref-type="bibr">[12&#8211;14]</xref>
###xml 285 289 285 289 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" rid="bib15" ref-type="bibr">[15]</xref>
###xml 552 556 552 556 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" rid="bib16" ref-type="bibr">[16]</xref>
###xml 681 685 681 685 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" rid="bib17" ref-type="bibr">[17]</xref>
###xml 731 740 731 736 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">mrc1&#916;</italic>
###xml 903 912 899 904 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">mrc1&#916;</italic>
###xml 981 985 973 977 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" rid="bib17" ref-type="bibr">[17]</xref>
If telomeres become uncapped, they activate a DNA damage response pathway leading to cell cycle arrest [12-14]. Moreover, recently it has been suggested that telomeres trigger a transient DNA damage response in each S phase in order to complete DNA replication and cap chromosome ends [15]. DNA damage response pathways are complex networks which include - among other components - mediators. Mediators facilitate the transmission of the DNA damage signal from sensors to downstream effectors; activation of the latter affecting cell cycle progression [16]. Mrc1 (Mediator of Replication Checkpoint 1) appears to take the mediator role in Rad53 activation under replication stress [17]. However, parallel pathways exist because in mrc1Delta mutants Rad53 activation occurs through Rad9 (another mediator protein), presumably because the accumulation of "DNA damage" rather than "replication defects" in mrc1Delta mutants leads to activation of Rad9 and thereby activation of Rad53 [17].
###end p 8
###begin p 9
###xml 83 92 83 88 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">mrc1&#916;</italic>
###xml 122 126 118 122 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" rid="bib18" ref-type="bibr">[18]</xref>
###xml 293 300 289 296 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" rid="bib19 bib20 bib21" ref-type="bibr">[19&#8211;21]</xref>
###xml 496 503 492 499 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" rid="bib20 bib21 bib22" ref-type="bibr">[20&#8211;22]</xref>
###xml 691 695 687 691 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" rid="bib22" ref-type="bibr">[22]</xref>
###xml 821 825 817 821 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">mrc1</italic>
###xml 825 827 821 823 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">AQ</italic>
###xml 825 827 821 823 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><italic>AQ</italic></sup>
###xml 930 940 926 936 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" rid="bib18 bib20 bib21" ref-type="bibr">[18,20,21]</xref>
###xml 942 946 938 942 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">mrc1</italic>
###xml 946 948 942 944 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">AQ</italic>
###xml 946 948 942 944 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><italic>AQ</italic></sup>
###xml 1101 1105 1097 1101 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" rid="bib18" ref-type="bibr">[18]</xref>
Mrc1 also appears to be directly involved in DNA replication and, because of this, mrc1Delta cells display a slow S phase [18]. Mrc1 is an active component of the replication machinery, loaded onto DNA shortly after replication initiates, and moving with other components of replication forks [19-21]. In the presence of hydroxyurea, a type of replication stress, Mrc1 appears to form a stable replication-pausing complex preventing the uncoupling of the replication machinery from DNA synthesis [20-22]. According to this model, Mrc1 mediates activation of Rad53 under conditions of replication stress so that subsequent DNA repair events occur and cell replication resumes normal function [22]. However, recent experiments suggest that the role of Mrc1 at stalled replication forks is more than activating Rad53, since mrc1AQ cells, defective in Rad53 activation, are not defective in replication fork initiation or progression [18,20,21]. mrc1AQ is a mutant allele in which SQ/TQ residues have been substituted with AQ, resulting in its inability to mediate phosphorylation and activation of Rad53 [18].
###end p 9
###begin p 10
###xml 140 157 140 153 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">cdc13-1 mrc1&#916;</italic>
###xml 217 221 213 217 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" rid="bib17" ref-type="bibr">[17]</xref>
###xml 492 496 488 492 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" rid="bib23" ref-type="bibr">[23]</xref>
###xml 520 524 516 520 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">tlc1</italic>
###xml 530 534 522 526 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">mrc1</italic>
###xml 685 692 673 680 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">cdc13-1</italic>
###xml 786 790 774 778 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" rid="bib23" ref-type="bibr">[23]</xref>
Although Mrc1 is involved in the DNA replication checkpoint, it has been shown that it is not required for the DNA damage checkpoint, since cdc13-1 mrc1Delta double mutants arrest in G2 at non-permissive temperatures [17]. It has been reported that activation of Rad53 in response to telomere shortening still occurs in the absence of Rad9 and that Mrc1 is responsible for this activation in telomerase-deficient cells, in which telomeres continually shorten until they activate a checkpoint [23]. Surprisingly, though, tlc1Delta mrc1Delta double mutants arrest cell division, suggesting that Mrc1 is not required for cell cycle arrest in telomerase negative cells. In contrast, after cdc13-1 induced telomere uncapping, Rad53 activation is entirely Rad9-dependent and Mrc1-independent [23].
###end p 10
###begin p 11
###xml 93 100 93 100 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">cdc13-1</italic>
###xml 105 115 105 111 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">yku70&#916;</italic>
Here we investigated the role of Mrc1 at uncapped telomeres, using the temperature sensitive cdc13-1 and yku70Delta mutations to uncap telomeres. Our experiments indicate that Mrc1 protects telomeres from the DNA damage response and that the role of Mrc1 in DNA replication forks, rather than in checkpoint activation, is important for protection of telomeres.
###end p 11

MRC1 was disrupted in two different ways. Firstly, the MRC1 ORF was substituted with KanMX6, with a PCR based method [27]. Primers 5'-tcgttattcgcttttgaacttatcaccaaatattttagtgCGGATCCCCGGGTTAATTAA-3' (#878) and 5'-ctggagttcaatcaacttcttcggaaaagataaaaaaccaGAATTCGAGCTCGTTTAAAC-3' (#881), which contain homology to upstream and downstream sequences of MRC1 (bases in lowercase), were used to amplify a 1559 bp KanMX6 sequence (pFA6a-kanMX6; [27]). Spot tests
###end title 16
###begin p 17
###xml 490 491 477 478 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">7</sup>
###xml 805 809 791 795 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">MRC1</italic>
###xml 812 816 798 802 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">mrc1</italic>
###xml 816 818 802 804 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">AQ</italic>
###xml 816 818 802 804 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><italic>AQ</italic></sup>
Single colonies were inoculated into 2 ml YEPDextrose (YEPD) and incubated overnight, with aeration, at 23 degreesC. The following day, 200 mul of each culture was inoculated into 2 ml of fresh YEPD and returned to 23 degreesC. Cells were grown for three more hours, and cell numbers were determined in a haemocytometer. The cells were then centrifuged (13,000 rpm for 10 s in a microcentrifuge), washed twice with sterile water and resuspended in water to a final concentration of 1.5 x 107 cells/ml. A five-fold dilution series of each of the cultures was prepared using sterile water in a 96 well plate and 3-5 mul spotted onto plates using a 48-prong replica plating device. Plates were incubated at various temperatures for 2-3 days before being photographed. For spot tests with strains containing pMRC1, pmrc1AQ or pRS416 the steps were as described above, but strains were grown on selective medium (-URA). All strains shown as if on a single agar plate were grown on the same plate, although in some cases their positions were moved using Adobe Photoshop and Adobe Illustrator CS. Unless otherwise stated, at least two different strains of the same genotype were spot tested and representative examples are shown.

###begin title 18
###xml 0 5 <span type="species:ncbi:4932">Yeast</span>
Yeast transformation
###end title 18
###begin p 19
###xml 101 105 101 105 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" rid="bib28" ref-type="bibr">[28]</xref>

Cell cycle position determination
###end title 28
###begin p 29
###xml 672 679 671 678 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">cdc13-1</italic>

###begin title 30
Results
###end title 30
###begin title 31
###xml 36 43 36 43 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">cdc13-1</italic>
###xml 48 58 48 54 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">yku70&#916;</italic>
Mrc1 contributes to the vitality of cdc13-1 and yku70Delta mutants
###end title 31
###begin p 32
###xml 160 167 160 167 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">cdc13-1</italic>
###xml 344 354 344 354 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" rid="bib12 bib37 bib38" ref-type="bibr">[12,37,38]</xref>
###xml 475 482 475 482 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">cdc13-1</italic>
###xml 523 530 523 530 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" rid="bib39 bib40 bib41" ref-type="bibr">[39&#8211;41]</xref>
###xml 834 841 834 841 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">cdc13-1</italic>
###xml 850 854 850 854 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" rid="bib42" ref-type="bibr">[42]</xref>
###xml 989 995 989 995 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" rid="fig1" ref-type="fig">Fig. 1</xref>
###xml 1020 1024 1020 1024 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">MRC1</italic>
###xml 1060 1067 1060 1067 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">cdc13-1</italic>
###xml 1168 1186 1162 1176 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">cdc13-1 mre11&#916;</italic>
###xml 1191 1209 1181 1195 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">cdc13-1 rad50&#916;</italic>
###xml 1250 1267 1236 1249 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">cdc13-1 mrc1&#916;</italic>
###xml 1289 1309 1265 1285 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" rid="app1" ref-type="sec">Supplementary Fig. 1</xref>
###xml 1413 1420 1389 1396 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">cdc13-1</italic>
Since Mrc1 plays a role in the checkpoint response to stalled replication, we wondered if it also plays a role at uncapped telomeres. The temperature sensitive cdc13-1 mutation causes a defect in Cdc13, a telomere binding protein, and cells containing this mutation accumulate large amounts of ssDNA at telomeres at non-permissive temperatures [12,37,38]. Interestingly, deletion of checkpoint proteins, like Chk1, Mec1, Mec3, Rad9, Rad17, Rad24 and Rad53 improves growth of cdc13-1 strains at semi-permissive temperatures [39-41]. This is presumably because checkpoint pathways inhibit cell division by responding to low levels of ssDNA that accumulates at telomeres at semi-permissive temperatures. Deletion of other checkpoint proteins, like the MRX complex, which appears to play a role in telomere capping, worsens the growth of cdc13-1 strains [42]. Therefore, we wanted to investigate whether Mrc1 plays a role at uncapped telomeres and, if so, whether it behaved like Rad9 or MRX. Fig. 1A shows that deletion of MRC1 dramatically reduces the growth of cdc13-1 mutant strains at 25 degreesC. The effect of Mrc1 is not as profound as that of the MRX complex, as cdc13-1 mre11Delta and cdc13-1 rad50Delta display more severe growth defects than cdc13-1 mrc1Delta even at 23 degreesC (Supplementary Fig. 1). Thus, Mrc1, like MRX, but unlike the majority of checkpoint proteins, contributes to the vitality of cdc13-1 strains.
###end p 32
###begin p 33
###xml 66 83 66 79 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">cdc13-1 mrc1&#916;</italic>
###xml 101 111 97 103 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">yku70&#916;</italic>
###xml 255 259 247 251 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" rid="bib43" ref-type="bibr">[43]</xref>
###xml 274 279 266 271 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">YKU70</italic>
###xml 419 423 405 409 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" rid="bib36" ref-type="bibr">[36]</xref>
###xml 425 431 411 417 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" rid="fig1" ref-type="fig">Fig. 1</xref>
###xml 463 467 449 453 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">MRC1</italic>
###xml 505 515 491 497 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">yku70&#916;</italic>
###xml 605 615 575 581 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">yku70&#916;</italic>
We next wanted to investigate whether analogous growth defects of cdc13-1 mrc1Delta strains occur in yku70Delta strains. Yku70 is a telomere capping protein which is also involved in dsDNA damage repair and in Non-Homologous End Joining (reviewed in Ref. [43]). Deletion of YKU70 results in a temperature sensitive phenotype at 37 degreesC, due to telomere uncapping, which activates a Chk1-dependent cell cycle arrest [36]. Fig. 1B demonstrates that deletion of MRC1 results in a severe growth defect of yku70Delta mutant strains at 35 degreesC and 36 degreesC. Thus, Mrc1 contributes to the vitality of yku70Delta strains. We conclude that Mrc1 contributes to the vitality of two cell types defective in telomere capping.
###end p 33
###begin p 34
###xml 101 111 101 111 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" rid="bib19 bib20 bib21 bib44" ref-type="bibr">[19&#8211;21,44]</xref>
###xml 210 217 210 217 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">cdc13-1</italic>
###xml 279 283 279 283 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">TOF1</italic>
###xml 287 291 287 291 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">CSM3</italic>
###xml 328 335 328 335 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">cdc13-1</italic>
###xml 357 361 357 361 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">MRC1</italic>
###xml 388 394 388 394 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" rid="fig1" ref-type="fig">Fig. 1</xref>
###xml 458 462 458 462 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">MRC1</italic>
Mrc1, Tof1 and Csm3 are three proteins that play similar, although distinct roles in DNA replication [19-21,44]. Therefore, we wished to address whether Tof1 and Csm3, like Mrc1, contributed to the vitality of cdc13-1 mutants. Spot test analysis showed that although deletion of TOF1 or CSM3 also confers some growth defects on cdc13-1 mutants, deletion of MRC1 has a stronger phenotype (Fig. 1C). Therefore, we decided to focus on understanding the role of MRC1 at uncapped telomeres.
###end p 34
###begin title 35
###xml 18 35 18 31 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">cdc13-1 mrc1&#916;</italic>
Growth defects of cdc13-1 mrc1Delta cells are not due to checkpoint defects
###end title 35
###begin p 36
###xml 228 235 228 235 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" rid="bib17 bib18 bib19 bib20 bib21" ref-type="bibr">[17&#8211;21]</xref>
###xml 324 341 324 337 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">cdc13-1 mrc1&#916;</italic>
###xml 11 16 <span type="species:ncbi:4932">yeast</span>
In budding yeast, two independent roles have been previously reported for Mrc1. One role implicates Mrc1 as a mediator of checkpoint activation under replication stress and the other role is as part of the replication machinery [17-21]. Therefore, we investigated whether the heightened temperature sensitivity phenotype of cdc13-1 mrc1Delta mutant strains is a result of a replication defect, a checkpoint defect or both.
###end p 36
###begin p 37

###xml 272 274 258 260 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">AQ</italic>
###xml 272 274 258 260 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><italic>AQ</italic></sup>

Fig. 2 shows that at 26.2 degreesC, complementation of mrc1Delta cdc13-1 mutant strains, with either pMRC1 or pmrc1AQ allele improves growth compared to the empty vector control (compare rows 1-3). Thus, we conclude that the checkpoint role of Mrc1 is not important for the vitality of cdc13-1 strains.
###end p 37
###begin p 38
###xml 56 73 56 69 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">cdc13-1 mrc1&#916;</italic>

###begin p 40
###xml 0 4 0 4 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">EXO1</italic>
###xml 89 96 89 96 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">cdc13-1</italic>

###xml 622 642 584 596 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">mrc1&#916; yku70&#916;</italic>

EXO1 encodes a nuclease known to contribute to ssDNA production at uncapped telomeres of cdc13-1 and yku70Delta strains [36,39]. If Exo1-dependent ssDNA production at uncapped telomeres is responsible for the poor growth of cdc13-1 mrc1Delta and yku70Delta mrc1Delta mutants, then removing Exo1 should suppress their poor growth. Fig. 3A demonstrates that at 25 degreesC, cdc13-1 mrc1Delta exo1Delta triple mutants exhibit better growth than cdc13-1 mrc1Delta strains, showing that Exo1 contributes to the growth defects observed in cdc13-1 mrc1Delta strains. Importantly, deleting EXO1 also reverses the growth defect of mrc1Delta yku70Delta mutants (Fig. 3B). These data suggest that Mrc1 protects uncapped telomeres from Exo1.

To understand if checkpoint pathways are activated in mrc1Delta strains after telomere uncapping, we wanted to combine cdc13-1 mrc1Delta and yku70Delta mrc1Delta strains with checkpoint mutations. A genetic screen has revealed that mrc1Delta is synthetically lethal with rad9Delta, rad17Delta or rad24Delta checkpoint mutations [24]. When cdc13-1 mutants grow at non-permissive temperatures Rad53 and Chk1 dependent parallel pathways are activated [36,45,46]. To test the role of Mrc1 in checkpoint control in a single cell cycle we combined mrc1Delta with cdc13-1 cdc15-2 and bar1 mutations. Over many years we and others have used these mutations to determine the effects of checkpoint proteins in responding to telomere uncapping [13,39,40,45]. Bar1 encodes a protease that degrades the mating pheromone alpha-factor. Cells bearing the bar1 mutation can efficiently arrest in G1 phase of the cell cycle with low levels of alpha-factor. Cdc15 is necessary for mitotic exit. At 36 degreesC, cdc13-1 cdc15-2 bar1 control strains, released from alpha factor arrest, accumulate at medial nuclear division due to cdc13-1-dependent telomere uncapping. However, if cells have escaped the G2/M checkpoint, like cdc13-1 rad9Delta cdc15-2 bar1 strains, they arrest at late nuclear division due to cdc15-2 and they are unable to proceed to the next cycle. The cdc15-2 dependent cell cycle arrest helps in two ways, it ensures that DNA damage checkpoint defects are easily quantified because cells with checkpoint defects accumulate at a later stage of the cell cycle and that DNA damage caused by cdc13-1 is not amplified during new rounds of DNA replication.

###xml 424 441 414 427 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">cdc13-1 rad9&#916;</italic>
###xml 451 457 437 443 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" rid="fig5" ref-type="fig">Fig. 5</xref>
###xml 489 493 475 479 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" rid="bib23" ref-type="bibr">[23]</xref>
###xml 538 545 524 531 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">cdc13-1</italic>
In response to both replication stress and DNA damage, activation of the checkpoint machinery induces phosphorylation and activation of Rad53 kinase. Therefore, we addressed the role of Mrc1 in Rad53 phosphorylation after telomere uncapping. cdc13-1 strains were exposed to 36 degreesC for 2 h and Rad53 phosphorylation was measured by Western blot. Rad53 phosphorylation is observed in cdc13-1 mrc1Delta strains but not in cdc13-1 rad9Delta mutants (Fig. 5F), confirming a previous study [23]. We conclude that activation of Rad53 after cdc13-1 dependent uncapping at non-permissive temperatures is Rad9-dependent but Mrc1-independent.
###end p 51
###begin title 52
Mrc1 contributes to telomere length regulation
###end title 52
###begin p 53
###xml 112 118 112 118 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" rid="fig6" ref-type="fig">Fig. 6</xref>
###xml 244 253 244 249 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">mrc1&#916;</italic>
###xml 286 296 282 288 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">rad50&#916;</italic>
###xml 300 310 292 298 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">yku70&#916;</italic>
###xml 350 367 338 351 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">cdc13-1 mrc1&#916;</italic>
###xml 391 409 375 389 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">cdc13-1 rad50&#916;</italic>
###xml 417 437 397 417 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" rid="app1" ref-type="sec">Supplementary Fig. 1</xref>
###xml 550 557 530 537 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" rid="bib49 bib50 bib51 bib52" ref-type="bibr">[49&#8211;52]</xref>
If Mrc1 plays a protective role at telomeres, this predicts that strains lacking Mrc1 may have short telomeres. Fig. 6A shows that absence of Mrc1 results in shorter telomeres, compared to the wild type. However, the telomere length defects of mrc1Delta mutants are not as severe as in rad50Delta or yku70Delta mutants, and this may help explain why cdc13-1 mrc1Delta cells grow better than cdc13-1 rad50Delta cells (Supplementary Fig. 1). Our experiment is consistent with replication proteins having an important role in telomere length regulation [49-52].
###end p 53
###begin title 54
Mrc1 protects telomeres from extended ssDNA accumulation
###end title 54
###begin p 55
###xml 211 215 211 215 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">PDA1</italic>
###xml 300 307 300 307 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">cdc13-1</italic>
###xml 317 323 317 323 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" rid="fig6" ref-type="fig">Fig. 6</xref>
###xml 327 334 327 334 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">cdc13-1</italic>
###xml 368 374 368 374 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" rid="fig5" ref-type="fig">Fig. 5</xref>
###xml 515 532 515 528 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">cdc13-1 mrc1&#916;</italic>
###xml 547 564 543 556 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">cdc13-1 rad9&#916;</italic>
###xml 605 609 597 601 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">PDA1</italic>
###xml 657 664 649 656 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">cdc13-1</italic>
###xml 674 680 666 672 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" rid="fig6" ref-type="fig">Fig. 6</xref>
###xml 683 687 675 679 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" rid="bib39" ref-type="bibr">[39]</xref>
###xml 731 738 723 730 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">cdc13-1</italic>
###xml 902 919 894 907 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">cdc13-1 mrc1&#916;</italic>
###xml 924 944 912 924 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">yku70&#916; mrc1&#916;</italic>
###xml 993 997 973 977 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" rid="bib53" ref-type="bibr">[53]</xref>
###xml 1043 1070 1023 1042 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">cdc13-1 mrc1&#916; exo1&#916;</italic>
###xml 1096 1113 1068 1081 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">cdc13-1 mrc1&#916;</italic>
###xml 1296 1313 1264 1277 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">cdc13-1 mrc1&#916;</italic>
###xml 1318 1338 1282 1294 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">yku70&#916; mrc1&#916;</italic>
###xml 1348 1354 1304 1310 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" rid="fig3" ref-type="fig">Fig. 3</xref>
All our data suggest an important role of Mrc1 in telomere capping but no role in cell cycle arrest. To directly test the role of Mrc1 in telomere capping, we measured ssDNA accumulation on the 3' TG strand, at PDA1, a single copy locus approximately 30 kb away from the right end of chromosome V in cdc13-1 strains (Fig. 6B). cdc13-1 strains were synchronised, as in Fig. 5C and D in order to follow the effects of Mrc1 on ssDNA accumulation at non-permissive temperatures within a single cell cycle. We find that cdc13-1 mrc1Delta mutants, like cdc13-1 rad9Delta strains, accumulate more 3' TG ssDNA at PDA1, 30 kb from the uncapped telomere, compared to cdc13-1 strains (Fig. 6C) [39]. This shows that Mrc1, like Rad9, protects cdc13-1 mutants from ssDNA production. Consistent with our conclusion, increased ssDNA accumulation, closer to the telomere, in telomere repeats, was recently reported in cdc13-1 mrc1Delta and yku70Delta mrc1Delta mutants using both in gel and dot blot analyses [53]. Importantly, ssDNA production is reduced in cdc13-1 mrc1Delta exo1Delta strains in comparison to cdc13-1 mrc1Delta strains showing that Mrc1 protects uncapped telomeres from Exo1-dependent nuclease action. This ssDNA data is consistent with our finding that Exo1 contributes to the poor growth of cdc13-1 mrc1Delta and yku70Delta mrc1Delta mutants (Fig. 3). Taken together, we conclude that Mrc1 inhibits accumulation of Exo1-dependent ssDNA accumulation after telomere uncapping and, by this criterion, contributes to telomere capping.
###end p 55
###begin title 56
Discussion
###end title 56
###begin p 57
###xml 282 289 282 289 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">cdc13-1</italic>
###xml 330 337 330 337 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" rid="bib39 bib40" ref-type="bibr">[39,40]</xref>
###xml 460 473 460 473 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" rid="bib36 bib42 bib54 bib55" ref-type="bibr">[36,42,54,55]</xref>
###xml 751 758 751 758 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">cdc13-1</italic>
###xml 762 772 762 768 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">yku70&#916;</italic>
###xml 825 829 821 825 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" rid="bib53" ref-type="bibr">[53]</xref>
###xml 894 911 890 903 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">cdc13-1 mrc1&#916;</italic>
###xml 916 936 908 920 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">yku70&#916; mrc1&#916;</italic>
###xml 974 991 958 971 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">cdc13-1 mrc1&#916;</italic>
###xml 1044 1048 1024 1028 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">EXO1</italic>
###xml 77 82 <span type="species:ncbi:4932">yeast</span>
Our experiments demonstrate that Mrc1 contributes to the vitality of budding yeast cells with uncapped telomeres. Therefore, Mrc1 behaves differently from many other known checkpoint proteins such as Chk1, Mec1, Rad9, Rad17, Rad24 or Rad53, deletion of which improves the growth of cdc13-1 mutants at semi-permissive temperatures [39,40]. The effect of Mrc1 is more similar to that of the MRX complex, another checkpoint complex with roles in telomere capping [36,42,54,55]. It seems that it is the role of Mrc1 at the replication fork that contributes to the vitality of telomere capping mutants, rather than its role in checkpoint activation. Our findings are in accordance with recent work that demonstrated a protective role of Mrc1 in cells with cdc13-1 or yku70Delta uncapped telomeres or in telomerase deficient cells [53]. Additionally, our work demonstrates that the growth defects of cdc13-1 mrc1Delta and yku70Delta mrc1Delta mutants and enhanced ssDNA levels of cdc13-1 mrc1Delta strains are suppressed when the nuclease encoded by EXO1 is deleted. Therefore, Mrc1 protects uncapped telomeres from Exo1.
###end p 57
###begin p 58
###xml 141 151 141 151 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" rid="bib17 bib18 bib22" ref-type="bibr">[17,18,22]</xref>
###xml 631 635 631 635 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" rid="bib22" ref-type="bibr">[22]</xref>
Mrc1 is recruited to the replication machinery as DNA replication initiates and is required for normal rates of replication fork progression [17,18,22]. The biochemical role of Mrc1 in replication fork progression is unclear which makes it difficult to know its precise role in telomere capping. Mrc1 is also part of a replication-pausing complex formed when replication is arrested by the S phase poison hydroxyurea (HU), and required for replication fork restart after HU. However, this restart role for Mrc1 is not universal, since Mrc1 plays no role in replication restart after cells are treated with the alkylating agent MMS [22].
###end p 58
###begin p 59
###xml 173 183 173 183 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" rid="bib15 bib56 bib57" ref-type="bibr">[15,56,57]</xref>
###xml 443 452 443 448 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">mrc1&#916;</italic>
###xml 54 59 <span type="species:ncbi:4932">yeast</span>
###xml 61 74 <span type="species:ncbi:4896">fission yeast</span>
###xml 79 84 <span type="species:ncbi:9606">human</span>
It is interesting that there is evidence from budding yeast, fission yeast and human cells that telomeric sequences contain DNA regions that slow or stall replication forks [15,56,57]. From this it seems plausible that telomeric DNA may be more dependent on proteins like Mrc1, which contribute to fork stability and restart, than other chromosomal regions. That is because the replication fork struggles to reach the end of the chromosome in mrc1Delta mutants where a telomere capping defect is observed.
###end p 59
###begin p 60
###xml 167 177 167 177 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">cdc17/pol1</italic>
###xml 182 192 182 192 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">cdc44/rfc1</italic>
###xml 264 268 264 268 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" rid="bib49" ref-type="bibr">[49]</xref>
###xml 302 306 302 306 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">mrc1</italic>
###xml 345 358 341 354 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">S. cerevisiae</italic>
###xml 360 370 356 366 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">cdc17/pol1</italic>
###xml 533 540 525 532 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" rid="bib50 bib51" ref-type="bibr">[50,51]</xref>
###xml 662 669 650 657 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" rid="bib10 bib52" ref-type="bibr">[10,52]</xref>
###xml 1019 1026 1007 1014 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" rid="bib36 bib39" ref-type="bibr">[36,39]</xref>
###xml 1071 1075 1059 1063 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" rid="bib58" ref-type="bibr">[58]</xref>
###xml 28 33 <span type="species:ncbi:4932">yeast</span>
###xml 296 301 <span type="species:ncbi:4932">yeast</span>
###xml 345 358 <span type="species:ncbi:4932">S. cerevisiae</span>
###xml 733 738 <span type="species:ncbi:4932">yeast</span>
Numerous studies on budding yeast mutants with DNA replication defects have demonstrated interactions between DNA replication and telomere structure. For example both cdc17/pol1 and cdc44/rfc1 (large subunit of replication factor C) mutants affect telomere length [49]. Here we show that budding yeast mrc1Delta mutants have short telomeres. In S. cerevisiae, cdc17/pol1 mutants, encoding temperature sensitive DNA polymerase alpha, exhibit very long telomeres, high levels of telomeric ssDNA and elevated recombination at telomeres [50,51]. Interestingly, the B subunit of DNA polymerase alpha physically interacts with Stn1, which in turn interacts with Cdc13 [10,52]. This shows there is a very direct interaction between budding yeast telomere capping proteins and the replication fork machinery, and suggests that telomere capping is intimately linked with DNA replication. In this regard it is, perhaps, relevant that the 5'-3' exonuclease Exo1 is involved in generating single stranded DNA at uncapped telomeres [36,39] and in processing stalled replication forks [58] and highlights the similarities between uncapped telomeres and stalled replication forks.
###end p 60
###begin p 61
###xml 0 7 0 7 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">cdc13-1</italic>
###xml 248 255 248 255 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">cdc13-1</italic>
###xml 404 408 404 408 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" rid="bib59" ref-type="bibr">[59]</xref>
cdc13-1 cells maintain a functional telomere cap (low levels of telomeric ssDNA), when released from G1 arrest into the S phase poison hydroxyurea. HU stalls replication forks and stops late origins of replication from firing. However, if the same cdc13-1 cells are permitted to complete DNA replication, by removing the S phase poison HU, telomere uncapping occurs and high levels of ssDNA are observed [59]. Therefore, Cdc13-dependent telomere capping may depend on a coordinated interaction between the chromosome end, Cdc13/Stn1/Ten1 and the DNA replication fork. Further studies examining the interactions between telomeric DNA, the telomere cap and the replication fork will be necessary to better understand these interactions.
