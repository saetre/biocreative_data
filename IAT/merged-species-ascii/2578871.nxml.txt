Novel Mutations in BBS5 Highlight the Importance of this Gene in non-Caucasian Bardet-Biedl Syndrome Patients
###end article-title 0
###begin p 1
To the Editor,
###end p 1
###begin p 2
###xml 209 228 209 228 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="R2">Beales et al., 1999</xref>

###xml 383 404 383 404 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="R14">Stoetzel et al., 2007</xref>

###xml 641 645 641 645 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">BBS1</italic>

###xml 755 777 755 777 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="R12">Stoetzel et al., 2006a</xref>
###xml 889 908 889 908 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="R1">Ansley et al., 2003</xref>
###xml 910 929 910 929 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="R4">Chiang et al., 2004</xref>

###xml 1054 1058 1054 1058 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">BBS5</italic>
###xml 1070 1085 1070 1085 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="R7">Li et al., 2004</xref>

###xml 723 731 <span type="species:ncbi:9606">patients</span>
###xml 879 887 <span type="species:ncbi:9606">patients</span>
Bardet-Biedl syndrome (BBS) is a rare genetically heterogeneous disorder presenting with retinal dystrophy, postaxial polydactyly, obesity, renal malformations, learning disabilities, and male hypogenitalism [Beales et al., 1999]. To date, 12 genes have proven to be implicated in the disease, accounting for the mutational load in 70-90 % of the patients [Blacque and Leroux, 2006; Stoetzel et al., 2007]. BBS is inherited as an autosomal recessive disease though in some instances, digenic triallelic inheritance has been suggested [Katsanis et al., 2001]. Although mutations in many different BBS genes have been described in Caucasians, BBS1 and BBS10 are the two major genes accounting for greater than 50% of the BBS patients [Mykytyn et al., 2002; Stoetzel et al., 2006a]. Mutational findings in the remaining known genes have predominately been reported in non-Caucasian patients [Ansley et al., 2003; Chiang et al., 2004; Stoetzel et al., 2006b]. BBS5 is a minor contributor to BBS as only 2% of families from various ethnic backgrounds harbour BBS5 mutations [Li et al., 2004].
###end p 2
###begin p 3
###xml 453 463 453 463 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="R5">Haim, 2002</xref>
###xml 696 715 696 715 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="R2">Beales et al., 1999</xref>
###xml 878 897 878 897 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="R10">Rotimi et al., 2001</xref>
###xml 20 28 <span type="species:ncbi:9606">patients</span>
###xml 176 179 <span type="species:ncbi:9606">boy</span>
###xml 222 229 <span type="species:ncbi:9606">patient</span>
###xml 251 256 <span type="species:ncbi:9606">child</span>
###xml 329 337 <span type="species:ncbi:9606">patients</span>
###xml 731 739 <span type="species:ncbi:9606">patients</span>
###xml 991 999 <span type="species:ncbi:9606">patients</span>
We studied five BBS patients from two nonconsanguineous families residing in Denmark: A Somali family (Family 1) with five siblings of whom four were affected, and an affected boy from Sri Lanka (Family 2). The Sri Lankan patient was a single adopted child and no further information of his biological parents was available. The patients were identified from the files of the Retinitis Pigmentosa Registry at the National Eye Clinic, Hellerup, Denmark [Haim, 2002]. Diagnosis was based on an ERG-verified panretinal photoreceptor dystrophy in association with three or more systemic manifestations; i.e. postaxial polydactyly, obesity, cognitive impairment, renal signs, and male hypogenitalism [Beales et al., 1999]. DNA from the patients was collected for mutation analysis. The control group consisted of 43 East Africans (Kenya) and 58 West Africans (Nigeria) for family 1 [Rotimi et al., 2001], and 54 Indian individuals for family 2. Appropriate informed consent was obtained from the patients and their families.
###end p 3
###begin p 4
###xml 39 43 39 43 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">BBS1</italic>
###xml 45 49 45 49 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">BBS2</italic>
###xml 51 55 51 55 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">BBS4</italic>
###xml 57 61 57 61 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">MKKS</italic>
###xml 67 72 67 72 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">BBS10</italic>
###xml 393 397 393 397 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">BBS5</italic>
###xml 490 494 490 494 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">BBS5</italic>
As part of a larger study screening of BBS1, BBS2, BBS4, MKKS, and BBS10 was done by DHPLC (denaturing high performance liquid chromatography, Varian Inc., Palo Alto, CA, USA) followed by DNA sequencing of aberrant products on an ABI3100 automated capillary sequencer using Big Dye Terminator v.3.1 (Applied Biosystems, Foster City, CA, USA) (authors' unpublished data, manuscript submitted). BBS5 mutational analysis was likewise performed by DHPLC. Primer sequences and PCR protocols for BBS5 are available upon request.
###end p 4
###begin p 5
###xml 366 371 <span type="species:ncbi:9606">Human</span>
Genotyping of the two families was done separately. For Family 1 we used the Affymetrix GeneChip Mapping 10K 2.0 Array to search for regions of shared genotypes among the affected siblings. Sample processing for this part of the study was carried out at the Microarray Facility in Tubingen, Germany. For Family 2, SNP genotyping was performed on Affymetrix GeneChip Human Mapping 50K Hind 240 SNP microarrays (Affymetrix, Santa Clara, CA, USA). We allowed SNPs that could not be scored to be included in the regions of interest. An average call rate > 95% was obtained.
###end p 5
###begin p 6
###xml 13 17 13 17 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">BBS1</italic>
###xml 19 23 19 23 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">BBS2</italic>
###xml 25 29 25 29 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">BBS4</italic>
###xml 31 35 31 35 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">MKKS</italic>
###xml 41 46 41 46 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">BBS10</italic>
###xml 549 553 549 553 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">BBS5</italic>
###xml 622 626 622 626 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">BBS5</italic>
###xml 964 969 964 969 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="fig" rid="F1">Fig 1</xref>
###xml 103 111 <span type="species:ncbi:9606">patients</span>
Screening of BBS1, BBS2, BBS4, MKKS, and BBS10 by DHPLC did not reveal causative mutations in the five patients. We therefore performed SNP genotyping in order to identify other BBS loci. Since we had no information of consanguinity in Family 1, we searched for regions of shared genotypes covering more than 5Mb among the four affected sibs and different from the unaffected sib. We identified three major regions: Two regions at 6q15 (23 Mb) and 12q32 (34 Mb) did not contain any known BBS gene while a region spanning 11 Mb at 2q31 contained the BBS5 locus (Table I - online supplementary only). Mutational analysis of BBS5 revealed a homozygous nucleotide change, c.214G>A (p.Gly72Ser) in exon 4, identified in all four affected siblings in Family 1 but not in the unaffected sib. Both parents were carriers. The mutation was absent in 202 ethnically matched control chromosomes. Sequence alignment showed the change to be localized within a conserved region (Fig 1).
###end p 6
###begin p 7
###xml 192 198 192 198 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">TRIM32</italic>
###xml 200 205 200 205 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">BBS11</italic>
###xml 264 268 264 268 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">BBS5</italic>
###xml 324 330 324 330 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">TRIM32</italic>
###xml 435 439 435 439 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">BBS5</italic>
###xml 604 609 604 609 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="fig" rid="F1">Fig 1</xref>
###xml 22 29 <span type="species:ncbi:9606">patient</span>
###xml 386 393 <span type="species:ncbi:9606">patient</span>
###xml 729 737 <span type="species:ncbi:9606">patients</span>
SNP genotyping of the patient in Family 2 lead to the identification of nine homozygous regions including two regions containing a known BBS gene. One locus spanning 19.6 Mb at 1p32 contained TRIM32 (BBS11) while the other locus spanning 16.6 Mb at 2p31 contained BBS5 (Table I - online supplementary). Sequence analysis of TRIM32 did not reveal any causative nucleotide changes in the patient. However, direct screening of the entire BBS5 gene identified a novel single base pair change, c.547A>G, in exon 7 in the homozygous state predicted to result in a non-conserved amino acid change, p.Thr183Ala (Fig 1). The mutation was absent in 108 ethnically matched control chromosomes. Furthermore, it was not detected among 60 BBS patients primarily of Northern European origin (authors' unpublished data, manuscript submitted). No family members were available for testing.
###end p 7
###begin p 8
###xml 50 54 50 54 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">BBS5</italic>
###xml 215 224 215 224 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">In silico</italic>
###xml 375 379 375 379 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">BBS5</italic>
###xml 446 451 446 451 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="fig" rid="F2">Fig 2</xref>
###xml 594 598 594 598 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">BBS5</italic>
###xml 684 699 684 699 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="R7">Li et al., 2004</xref>
###xml 1099 1103 1099 1103 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">BBS5</italic>
###xml 1126 1130 1126 1130 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">BBS5</italic>
###xml 166 174 <span type="species:ncbi:9606">patients</span>
###xml 1104 1112 <span type="species:ncbi:9606">patients</span>
We report here on two novel missense mutations in BBS5. Both mutations are localized within conserved regions of the gene, are present in the homozygous state in the patients, and are absent in control chromosomes. In silico analysis predicts the mutations to affect protein function and in Family 1 the mutation segregates with the phenotype. Most of the known mutations in BBS5 are localized within either of two putative domains, called DM16 (Fig 2); as for the mutations reported here, p.Gly72Ser is localized in the first domain while p.Thr183Ala is localized in the second DM16 domain of BBS5. DM16 is a domain of unknown function and evolutionary conserved among many species [Li et al., 2004]. The fact that the mutations reported here are located in the DM16 domains supports their pathogenecity. We cannot rule out though, the existence of another mutation in linkage disequilibrium with these missense mutations. However, all coding regions plus 20 base pairs in the flanking regions were sequenced. Our findings represent the first missense mutations detected in the homozygous state in BBS5 patients. Thus, these BBS5 missense mutations might be useful for functional studies.
###end p 8
###begin p 9
###xml 100 104 100 104 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">BBS5</italic>
###xml 139 154 139 154 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="R7">Li et al., 2004</xref>
###xml 156 178 156 178 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="R9">Nishimura et al., 2005</xref>
###xml 180 199 180 199 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="R11">Smaoui et al., 2006</xref>
###xml 492 507 492 507 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="R7">Li et al., 2004</xref>
###xml 509 528 509 528 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="R11">Smaoui et al., 2006</xref>
###xml 406 414 <span type="species:ncbi:9606">patients</span>
###xml 464 471 <span type="species:ncbi:9606">patient</span>
Only five other point mutations, two indels and one large deletion have been previously reported in BBS5 (Table I - online supplementary) [Li et al., 2004; Nishimura et al., 2005; Smaoui et al., 2006]. Most of these were detected in the homozygous state with the exception of the two previously reported missense variants (p.Asn184Ser and p.Arg207His). The previously published mutations are identified in patients from Africa and the Middle East, and in a single patient from New Foundland [Li et al., 2004; Smaoui et al., 2006].
###end p 9
###begin p 10
###xml 57 61 57 61 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">BBS5</italic>
###xml 239 243 239 243 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">BBS5</italic>
###xml 321 325 321 325 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">BBS5</italic>
###xml 141 149 <span type="species:ncbi:9606">patients</span>
###xml 184 192 <span type="species:ncbi:9606">patients</span>
In conclusion, we report two novel missense mutations in BBS5. Several pieces of information support that the mutations are pathogenic. Both patients are non-European. Screening of 60 patients from Northern Europe revealed no mutations in BBS5. These data might have implications for the mutational screening strategy of BBS5.
###end p 10
