Arabidopsis WRKY2 transcription factor mediates seed germination and postgermination arrest of development by abscisic acid

###begin title 2
Background
###end title 2
###begin p 3
Plant WRKY DNA-binding transcription factors are key regulators in certain developmental programs. A number of studies have suggested that WRKY genes may mediate seed germination and postgermination growth. However, it is unclear whether WRKY genes mediate ABA-dependent seed germination and postgermination growth arrest.
###end p 3
###begin title 4
Results
###end title 4
###begin p 5
###xml 34 52 34 52 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">Arabidopsis WRKY2 </italic>
###xml 227 233 227 233 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">WRKY2 </italic>
###xml 333 339 333 339 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">wrky2 </italic>
###xml 392 397 392 397 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">ABI5 </italic>
###xml 401 405 401 405 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">ABI3</italic>
###xml 448 452 448 452 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">Em1 </italic>
###xml 456 459 456 459 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">Em6</italic>
###xml 461 467 461 467 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">wrky2 </italic>
###xml 529 536 529 536 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">miR159 </italic>
###xml 557 563 557 563 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">MYB33 </italic>
###xml 567 573 567 573 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">MYB101</italic>
###xml 587 593 587 593 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">WRKY2 </italic>
###xml 655 661 655 661 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">abi5-1</italic>
###xml 663 669 663 669 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">abi3-1</italic>
###xml 671 678 671 678 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">aba2-3 </italic>
###xml 682 689 682 689 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">aba3-1 </italic>
###xml 724 730 724 730 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">WRKY2 </italic>
###xml 804 808 804 808 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">ABI5</italic>
###xml 810 814 810 814 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">ABI3</italic>
###xml 816 821 816 821 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">ABA2 </italic>
###xml 825 829 825 829 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">ABA3</italic>
To determine directly the role of Arabidopsis WRKY2 transcription factor during ABA-dependent seed germination and postgermination growth arrest, we isolated T-DNA insertion mutants. Two independent T-DNA insertion mutants for WRKY2 were hypersensitive to ABA responses only during seed germination and postgermination early growth. wrky2 mutants displayed delayed or decreased expression of ABI5 and ABI3, but increased or prolonged expression of Em1 and Em6. wrky2 mutants and wild type showed similar levels of expression for miR159 and its target genes MYB33 and MYB101. Analysis of WRKY2 expression level in ABA-insensitive and ABA-deficient mutants abi5-1, abi3-1, aba2-3 and aba3-1 further indicated that ABA-induced WRKY2 accumulation during germination and postgermination early growth requires ABI5, ABI3, ABA2 and ABA3.
###end p 5
###begin title 6
Conclusion
###end title 6
###begin p 7
###xml 28 34 28 34 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">wrky2 </italic>
###xml 158 162 158 162 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">ABI5</italic>
###xml 164 169 164 169 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">ABI3 </italic>
###xml 173 177 173 177 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">ABI5</italic>
###xml 186 190 186 190 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">Em1 </italic>
###xml 194 198 194 198 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">Em6 </italic>
###xml 214 219 214 219 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">WRKY2</italic>
###xml 262 269 262 269 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">miR159 </italic>
###xml 290 296 290 296 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">MYB33 </italic>
###xml 300 306 300 306 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">MYB101</italic>
###xml 308 312 308 312 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">ABI5</italic>
###xml 314 318 314 318 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">ABI3</italic>
###xml 320 325 320 325 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">ABA2 </italic>
###xml 329 334 329 334 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">ABA3 </italic>
###xml 381 387 381 387 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">WRKY2 </italic>
###xml 430 436 430 436 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">WRKY2 </italic>
ABA hypersensitivity of the wrky2 mutants during seed germination and postgermination early seedling establishment is attributable to elevated mRNA levels of ABI5, ABI3 and ABI5-induced Em1 and Em6 in the mutants. WRKY2-mediated ABA responses are independent of miR159 and its target genes MYB33 and MYB101. ABI5, ABI3, ABA2 and ABA3 are important regulators of the transcripts of WRKY2 by ABA treatment. Our results suggest that WRKY2 transcription factor mediates seed germination and postgermination developmental arrest by ABA.

###begin p 9
###xml 176 177 176 177 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="B1">1</xref>
###xml 178 179 178 179 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="B4">4</xref>

###xml 1194 1199 1194 1199 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">ABI5 </italic>

###xml 1514 1518 1514 1518 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">ABI5</italic>
###xml 1520 1525 1520 1525 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">ABI3 </italic>
###xml 1595 1597 1595 1597 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="B15">15</xref>
###xml 1609 1614 1609 1614 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">ABI3 </italic>
###xml 1631 1640 1631 1640 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">ABI5 and </italic>
###xml 1657 1662 1657 1662 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">ABI5 </italic>
###xml 1679 1681 1679 1681 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="B15">15</xref>
###xml 1834 1838 1834 1838 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">ABI3</italic>
###xml 1840 1845 1840 1845 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">ABI5 </italic>
###xml 1915 1920 1915 1920 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">ABI5 </italic>
###xml 2002 2006 2002 2006 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">Em1 </italic>
###xml 2010 2014 2010 2014 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">Em6 </italic>
###xml 2043 2045 2043 2045 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="B15">15</xref>
###xml 2046 2048 2046 2048 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="B16">16</xref>
###xml 2082 2089 2082 2089 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">miR159 </italic>
###xml 2111 2116 2111 2116 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">ABI3 </italic>
###xml 2151 2155 2151 2155 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">ABI5</italic>
###xml 2157 2163 2157 2163 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">MYB33 </italic>
###xml 2167 2173 2167 2173 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">MYB101</italic>
###xml 2179 2186 2179 2186 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">miR159 </italic>
###xml 2288 2295 2288 2295 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">miR159 </italic>
###xml 2307 2309 2307 2309 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="B17">17</xref>
Abscisic acid (ABA) is a phytohormone regulating plant responses to a variety of environmental stress, particularly water deprivation, notably by regulating stomatal aperture [1-4]. It also plays an essential role in mediating the initiation and maintenance of seed dormancy [5]. Late in seed maturation, the embryo develops and enters a dormant state that is triggered by an increase in the ABA concentration. This leads to the cessation of cell division and activation of genes encoding seed storage proteins and proteins required to establish desiccation tolerance [6]. Exposure of seeds to ABA during germination leads to rapid but reversible arrest in development. ABA-mediated postgermination arrest allows germinating seedlings to survive early water stress [5]. Based on ABA inhibition of seed germination, mutants with altered ABA sensitivity have been identified. These screens have led to the identification of several ABA-insensitive genes [7-13]. The transcription factors ABI3 and ABI5 are known to be important regulators of ABA-dependent growth arrest during germination [14,15]. ABI5, an ABA-insensitive gene, encodes a basic leucine zipper transcription factor. Expression of ABI5 defines a narrow developmental checkpoint following germination, during which Arabidopsis plants sense the water status in the environment. ABI5 is a rate-limiting factor conferring ABA-mediated postgermination developmental growth arrest [5].ABI3 is also reactivated by ABA during a short development window. Like ABI5, ABI3 is also required for the ABA-dependent postgermination growth arrest [15]. However, ABI3 acts upstream of ABI5 and is essential for ABI5 gene expression [15]. In arrested, germinated embryos, ABA can activate de novo late embryogenesis programs to confer osmotic tolerance. During a short development window, ABI3, ABI5 and late embryogenesis genes are reactivated by ABA. ABA can activate ABI5 occupancy on the promoter of several late embryogenesis-abundant genes, including Em1 and Em6 and induce their expression [15,16]. On the other hand, ABA-induced miR159 accumulation requires ABI3 but is only partially dependent on ABI5. MYB33 and MYB101, two miR159 targets, are positive regulators of ABA responses during germination and are subject to ABA-dependent miR159 regulation [17].
###end p 9
###begin p 10
###xml 84 105 84 105 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">Arabidopsis thaliana </italic>
###xml 106 108 106 108 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="B18">18</xref>
###xml 109 111 109 111 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="B20">20</xref>
###xml 361 363 361 363 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="B21">21</xref>
###xml 500 512 500 512 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">Arabidopsis </italic>
###xml 513 515 513 515 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="B20">20</xref>
###xml 84 104 <span type="species:ncbi:3702">Arabidopsis thaliana</span>
The family of plant-specific WRKY transcription factors contains over 70 members in Arabidopsis thaliana [18-20]. WRKY proteins typically contain one or two domains composed of about 60 amino acids with the conserved amino acid sequence WRKYGQK, together with a novel zinc-finger motif. WRKY domain shows a high binding affinity to the TTGACC/T W-box sequence [21]. Based on the number of WRKY domains and the pattern of the zinc-finger motif, WRKY proteins can be divided into 3 different groups in Arabidopsis [20].
###end p 10
###begin p 11
###xml 249 251 249 251 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="B20">20</xref>
###xml 252 254 252 254 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="B22">22</xref>
###xml 255 257 255 257 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="B25">25</xref>
###xml 329 331 329 331 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="B26">26</xref>
###xml 493 495 493 495 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="B27">27</xref>
###xml 516 518 516 518 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="B28">28</xref>
###xml 556 558 556 558 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="B29">29</xref>
###xml 559 561 559 561 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="B31">31</xref>
A growing body of studies has shown that WRKY genes are involved in regulating plant responses to biotic stresses. A majority of reported studies on WRKY genes address their involvement in disease responses and salicylic acid (SA)-mediated defense [20,22-25]. In addition, WRKY genes are involved in plant responses to wounding [26]. Although most WRKY proteins studied thus far have been implicated in regulating biotic stress responses, some WRKY genes regulate plant responses to freezing [27], oxidative stress [28], drought, salinity, cold, and heat [29-31].
###end p 11
###begin p 12
###xml 172 174 172 174 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="B32">32</xref>
###xml 185 187 185 187 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="B33">33</xref>
###xml 209 211 209 211 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="B34">34</xref>
###xml 259 261 259 261 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="B35">35</xref>
###xml 275 277 275 277 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="B36">36</xref>
###xml 316 318 316 318 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="B32">32</xref>
###xml 319 321 319 321 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="B37">37</xref>
###xml 340 342 340 342 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="B38">38</xref>
###xml 343 345 343 345 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="B40">40</xref>
There is also increasing evidence indicating that WRKY proteins are key regulators in certain developmental programs. Some WRKY genes regulate biosynthesis of anthocyanin [32], starch [33], and sesquiterpene [34]. Other WRKY genes may regulate embryogenesis [35], seed size [36], seed coat and trichome development [32,37], and senescence [38-40].
###end p 12
###begin p 13
###xml 203 214 203 210 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">&#945;-Amy2 </italic>
###xml 224 226 220 222 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="B41">41</xref>

###xml 721 728 717 724 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">abi3-1 </italic>
###xml 732 739 728 735 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">abi5-1 </italic>

###xml 877 881 873 877 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">Em1 </italic>
###xml 885 888 881 884 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">Em6</italic>
###xml 903 907 899 903 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">ABI5</italic>
###xml 909 913 905 909 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">ABI3</italic>
###xml 915 920 911 916 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">ABA2 </italic>
###xml 924 929 920 925 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">ABA3 </italic>
###xml 983 988 979 984 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">WRKY2</italic>
###xml 126 129 <span type="species:ncbi:4498|species:ncbi:50455">oat</span>
###xml 231 237 <span type="species:ncbi:112509|species:ncbi:4513">barley</span>
###xml 267 271 <span type="species:ncbi:4530">rice</span>
###xml 273 285 <span type="species:ncbi:4530">Oryza sativa</span>
A number of studies have suggested that WRKY genes may mediate seed germination and postgermination growth. For example, wild oat WRKY proteins (ABF1 and ABF2) bind to the box2/W-box of the GA-regulated alpha-Amy2 promoter [41]. A barley WRKY gene, HvWRKY38, and its rice (Oryza sativa) ortholog, OsWRKY71 act as a transcriptional repressor of gibberellin-responsive genes in aleurone cells [42]. However, it is unclear whether WRKY genes mediate ABA-dependent seed germination and postgermination growth arrest. In this study, we report that wrky2-1 and wrky2-2 mutants are hypersensitive to ABA during germination and postgermination early growth. We have analyzed genetic interactions between the wrky2 mutant and the abi3-1 and abi5-1 mutants and found that ABA hypersensitivity of the wrky2 mutants is attributable to increased mRNA levels for ABI5, ABI3 and ABI5-induced Em1 and Em6. Furthermore, ABI5, ABI3, ABA2 and ABA3 are important regulators of ABA-induced expression of WRKY2.

###begin title 14
Results
###end title 14
###begin title 15
###xml 0 6 0 6 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">WRKY2 </italic>
WRKY2 T-DNA insertion mutants are hypersensitive to ABA during seed germination and postgermination early growth
###end title 15
###begin p 16
###xml 256 273 256 273 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">Arabidopsis WRKY </italic>
###xml 410 411 410 411 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="fig" rid="F1">1</xref>
###xml 413 419 413 419 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">WRKY2 </italic>
###xml 443 451 443 451 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">wrky2-1 </italic>
###xml 469 477 469 477 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">wrky2-2 </italic>
###xml 507 513 507 513 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">WRKY2 </italic>
To analyze the molecular events in ABA-regulated germination and seedling growth, we sought to identify WRKY transcription factors associated with these growth and developmental stages. For this purpose, we screened T-DNA insertion mutants for a number of Arabidopsis WRKY genes for altered sensitivity to ABA based on their germination rates and seedling growth in MS media containing ABA. As shown in Figure 1, WRKY2 T-DNA insertion mutants wrky2-1 (Salk_020399) and wrky2-2 (Sail_739_F05) accumulated no WRKY2 transcript of expected size and exhibited increased sensitivity to ABA during seed germination and seedling growth. The two mutants display no other obvious phenotypes in morphology, growth, development or seed size (data not shown).

The transcription factors ABI3 and ABI5 are known to be important regulators of ABA-dependent growth arrest during germination [14,15]. ABI3 and ABI5 are reactivated by ABA during a short development window. Given the ABA hypersensitivity of wrky2 mutants, we studied the effect of ABA on the early development of wrky2 mutants. Application of 5 muM ABA [5] within 48 h post-stratification maintained the germinated embryos of wrky2 mutants in an arrested state for several days, but did not prevent germination, whereas a significant percentage of wild-type germinated embryos escaped growth arrest and turned green (Figure 5A and 5B). ABA applied outside the 48-h time frame failed to arrest growth and prevent greening (Figure 5C). If the seeds were transferred to ABA-containing media immediately or one day after stratification, we observed that the wrky2 mutants were more sensitive to ABA responses than the wild type. However, if the seeds were transferred to ABA-containing media 2 days post-stratification, there was no significant difference between the wild type and wrky2 mutants. These results indicated that wrky2 mutants were hypersensitive to ABA responses for a short development window.

###xml 877 882 877 882 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">ABA2 </italic>
###xml 886 890 886 890 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">ABA3</italic>
###xml 928 932 928 932 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">ABI5</italic>
###xml 934 938 934 938 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">ABI3</italic>
###xml 940 945 940 945 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">ABA2 </italic>
###xml 949 954 949 954 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">ABA3 </italic>
###xml 994 1000 994 1000 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">WRKY2 </italic>
Without ABA, the level of WRKY2 in the wild type (Ler) was 1.2-fold higher than that in abi3-1 mutant. In the presence of ABA, the WRKY2 transcript level in wild type was 3.5-fold higher than that in abi3-1 mutant (Figure 7). These results indicate ABI3 maybe is a positive regulator of ABA-induced WRKY2. We also examined the expression level of WRKY2 in ABA-deficient aba2-3 and aba3-1 mutants [43,44]. As shown in Figure 7, with ABA, the expression level of WRKY2 in the wild type was 7.5 time higher than that without ABA, but the expression levels of WRKY2 in aba2-3 and aba3-1 mutants were only 1.6 and 0.38 times of those without ABA, respectively. On the other hands, without ABA, the expression levels of WRKY2 in aba2-3 and aba3-1 mutants were 1.2 and 2.3 times of that in the wild type. These observations show elevated expression of WRKY2 by ABA treatment requires ABA2 and ABA3. These results strongly suggest that ABI5, ABI3, ABA2 and ABA3 are important regulators of ABA-induced WRKY2 expression.
###end p 37
###begin title 38

It is known that ABA-induced miR159 accumulation requires ABI3 but is only partially dependent on ABI5. Furthermore, MYB33 and MYB101, which are miR159 targets, are positive regulators of ABA responses during germination and are subject to ABA-dependent miR159 regulation [17]. Figure 8A indicates that there was no significant difference in the level of mature miR159 between wrky2 mutants and the wild type. As shown in Figure 8B and Table 1, there was no difference in levels of transcripts of MYB33 and MYB101 either. These results indicate that WRKY2-mediated ABA signaling pathway is independent of miR159 and its target genes (MYB33 and MYB101) during seed germination and postgermination growth arrest.

###begin title 41
Discussion
###end title 41
###begin p 42
###xml 138 140 138 140 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="B45">45</xref>
###xml 184 192 184 192 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">wrky2-1 </italic>
###xml 196 204 196 204 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">wrky2-2 </italic>
The ability of exogenous ABA to arrest postgermination development has been used extensively to identify genes involved in ABA signaling [45]. In the present study, we discovered that wrky2-1 and wrky2-2 mutants were more sensitive than wild type to ABA responses during seed germination and postgermination early growth.
###end p 42
###begin p 43
###xml 4 10 4 10 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">wrky2 </italic>
###xml 212 217 212 217 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">ABI3 </italic>
###xml 221 226 221 226 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">ABI5 </italic>
###xml 381 383 381 383 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="B14">14</xref>

###xml 389 394 389 394 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">ABI5 </italic>
###xml 466 471 466 471 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">abi5 </italic>

###xml 595 596 595 596 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="B5">5</xref>
###xml 599 604 599 604 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">ABI3 </italic>
###xml 621 626 621 626 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">ABI5 </italic>
###xml 706 708 706 708 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="B15">15</xref>
###xml 723 728 723 728 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">ABI5 </italic>
###xml 758 762 758 762 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">Em1 </italic>
###xml 766 770 766 770 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">Em6 </italic>
###xml 829 831 829 831 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="B15">15</xref>
###xml 832 834 832 834 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="B16">16</xref>
###xml 850 855 850 855 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">et al</italic>
###xml 955 956 954 955 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="B5">5</xref>
###xml 1063 1068 1062 1067 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">ABI5 </italic>
###xml 1230 1235 1229 1234 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">ABI5 </italic>
###xml 1287 1288 1286 1287 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="B5">5</xref>
###xml 1355 1361 1354 1360 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">wrky2 </italic>
###xml 1418 1422 1417 1421 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">ABI5</italic>
###xml 1475 1481 1474 1480 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">wrky2 </italic>
###xml 1537 1542 1536 1541 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">ABI5 </italic>
###xml 1546 1550 1545 1549 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">ABI5</italic>
###xml 1651 1657 1650 1656 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">wrky2 </italic>
###xml 1691 1695 1690 1694 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">ABI5</italic>
###xml 1697 1702 1696 1701 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">ABI3 </italic>
###xml 1706 1710 1705 1709 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">ABI5</italic>
###xml 1719 1723 1718 1722 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">Em1 </italic>
###xml 1727 1731 1726 1730 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">Em6 </italic>
###xml 1758 1759 1757 1758 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="fig" rid="F6">6</xref>
###xml 1813 1819 1812 1818 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">wrky2 </italic>
###xml 1949 1950 1948 1949 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="fig" rid="F3">3</xref>
###xml 1955 1956 1954 1955 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="fig" rid="F4">4</xref>
The wrky2 mutants are hypersensitive to ABA responses only within a short development window during seed germination and early seedling growth. During seed germination and early growth, the transcription factors ABI3 and ABI5 are known to be important regulators of ABA-dependent growth arrest, and their expression defines a narrow developmental checkpoint following germination [14,15]. ABI5 is a key player in ABA-triggered postgermination growth arrest, and the abi5 mutant seeds are insensitive to growth arrest by ABA, whereas seeds of ABI5-overexpressing lines are hypersensitive to ABA [5]. ABI3 acts upstream of ABI5 and is an important regulator of germination and postgermination growth by ABA [15]. ABA-induced ABI5 also occupies the promoter of Em1 and Em6 and activates these two late embryogenesis-abundant genes [15,16]. Lopez-Molina et al. (2001) have shown that application of 5 muM ABA within 60 h post-stratification in the wild type [5] (Ws) does not prevent germination, but arrest the germinated embryos for several days, during which both ABI5 transcripts and ABI5 proteins are detected at high levels. When applied outside the 60-h time frame, however, ABA fails to arrest growth and prevent greening and ABI5 transcripts and proteins are present at low levels [5]. Therefore, it is possible that the ABA-induced growth-arrest of wrky2 mutants might be associated with the expression level of ABI5. To test this hypothesis, we compared wild type and wrky2 mutants for analyzed ABA-regulated expression levels of ABI5 and ABI5-related important regulators during seed germination and postgermination development. We found that wrky2 mutants had higher mRNA levels of ABI5, ABI3 and ABI5-induced Em1 and Em6 than the wild type (Figure 6). The higher expression levels of these genes in the wrky2 mutants may be partly responsible for the enhanced growth arrest relative to that in the wild type in the presence of ABA (Figure 3 and 4).
###end p 43
###begin p 44
###xml 20 27 20 27 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">miR159 </italic>
###xml 49 54 49 54 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">ABI3 </italic>
###xml 89 94 89 94 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">ABI5 </italic>
###xml 95 97 95 97 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="B17">17</xref>
###xml 100 106 100 106 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">MYB33 </italic>
###xml 110 116 110 116 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">MYB101</italic>
###xml 128 135 128 135 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">miR159 </italic>
###xml 205 207 205 207 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="B17">17</xref>

###xml 379 385 379 385 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">wrky2 </italic>
###xml 419 420 419 420 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="fig" rid="F8">8</xref>
###xml 472 478 472 478 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">WRKY2 </italic>
###xml 539 546 539 546 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">miR159 </italic>
###xml 568 574 568 574 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">MYB33 </italic>
###xml 578 584 578 584 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">MYB101</italic>
With ABA treatment, miR159 accumulation requires ABI3 but is only partially dependent on ABI5 [17]. MYB33 and MYB101, which are miR159 targets, are positive regulators of ABA responses during germination [17]. Therefore we examined whether WRKY2 affected the mRNA levels of these genes. We found that the expression levels of these genes were not significantly different between wrky2 mutants and the wild type (Figure 8). These observations indicate that the response of WRKY2 to ABA during germination and early growth is independent of miR159 and its target genes (MYB33 and MYB101).
###end p 44
###begin p 45
###xml 18 22 18 22 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">ABI5</italic>
###xml 24 28 24 28 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">ABI3</italic>
###xml 30 35 30 35 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">ABA2 </italic>

###xml 149 155 149 155 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">WRKY2 </italic>
###xml 211 218 211 218 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">abi5-1 </italic>

We also show that ABI5, ABI3, ABA2 and ABA3 are important positive regulators of ABA-induced WRKY2 accumulation (Figure 7). The expression levels of WRKY2 were elevated more drastically in the wild type than in abi5-1 and abi3-1 mutants after ABA treatment (Figure 7). This result indicates that these two genes are positive regulators of ABA-induced WRKY2 accumulation.

###xml 230 233 <span type="species:ncbi:4498|species:ncbi:50455">oat</span>

###xml 450 454 <span type="species:ncbi:4530">rice</span>

Several studies have shown that ABI5 binds to the ABA-responsive DNA elements (ABREs) with an ACGT core in the promoter of Em1 and Em6, and activates their expression [15,16]. On the other hand, other reports have shown that wild oat WRKY proteins (ABF1 and ABF2) bind to the box2/W-box of the GA-regulated alpha-Amy2 promoter [41], and GaWRKY1 highly activated the CAD1-A promoter by binding to W-box [34], and a barley WRKY gene, HvWRKY38, and its rice (Oryza sativa) ortholog, OsWRKY71 act as a transcriptional repressor of gibberellin-responsive genes in aleurone cells [42]. The promoter zone of WRKY2 has an ABA-responsive DNA element (CACGTGGC) containing an ACGT core, and the promoter zone of ABI5 has 6 W-box, whereas the promoter of ABI3 has 2 W-box. This raises the possibility that WRKY2, ABI3 and ABI5 are mutually regulated.

###xml 602 606 602 606 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">ABI5</italic>
###xml 615 619 615 619 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">Em1 </italic>
###xml 623 627 623 627 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">Em6 </italic>
###xml 643 648 643 648 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">WRKY2</italic>
###xml 691 698 691 698 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">miR159 </italic>
###xml 719 725 719 725 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">MYB33 </italic>
###xml 729 735 729 735 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">MYB101</italic>
###xml 749 755 749 755 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">WRKY2 </italic>
###xml 829 833 829 833 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">ABI5</italic>
###xml 835 839 835 839 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">ABI3</italic>
###xml 841 846 841 846 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">ABA2 </italic>
###xml 850 854 850 854 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">ABA3</italic>
###xml 924 930 924 930 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">WRKY2 </italic>
###xml 973 979 973 979 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">WRKY2 </italic>
Transcription factors ABI5 is an important regulator of postgermination developmental arrest mediated by ABA. Postgermination proteolytic degradation of the essential ABI5 transcription factor is interrupted by perception of an increase in ABA concentration, leading to ABI5 accumulation and reactivation of embryonic genes. Here we report that wrky2-1 and wrky2-2 mutants are more sensitive to ABA responses than the wild type during seed germination and postgermination early seedling establishment. ABA hypersensitivity of the wrky2 mutants is attributable to elevated mRNA levels of ABI5, ABI3 and ABI5-induced Em1 and Em6 in the mutants. WRKY2-mediated ABA responses are independent of miR159 and its target genes MYB33 and MYB101. ABA-induced WRKY2 accumulation during germination and postgermination early growth requires ABI5, ABI3, ABA2 and ABA3, suggesting that they are important regulators of the transcripts of WRKY2 by ABA treatment. Our results suggest that WRKY2 transcription factor mediates seed germination and postgermination developmental arrest by ABA.

###begin title 51
Plant material and growth conditions
###end title 51
###begin p 52
###xml 4 25 4 25 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">Arabidopsis thaliana </italic>
###xml 148 169 148 169 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">Arabidopsis thaliana </italic>
###xml 699 705 681 687 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">abi5-1</italic>
###xml 707 713 689 695 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">abi3-1</italic>
###xml 715 722 697 704 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">aba2-3 </italic>
###xml 726 733 708 715 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">aba3-1 </italic>
###xml 4 24 <span type="species:ncbi:3702">Arabidopsis thaliana</span>
###xml 148 168 <span type="species:ncbi:3702">Arabidopsis thaliana</span>
The Arabidopsis thaliana ecotypes Columbia, Wassilewskija and Landsberg erecta were used throughout this study. Seeds of the different genotypes of Arabidopsis thaliana were harvested from plants of the same age and stored for 5 weeks in the dark at 4degreesC. Seeds were surface-sterilized with 10% bleach and washed three times with sterile water. Sterile seeds were suspended in 0.1% agarose and plated on MS medium plus 1% sucrose. ABA (mixed isomers, Sigma) was added to the medium where indicated. Plates were routinely kept for 3 days in the dark at 4degreesC to break dormancy (stratification) and transferred thereafter to a tissue culture room under constant light at 22degreesC. Seeds of abi5-1, abi3-1, aba2-3 and aba3-1 were obtained from the Arabidopsis Biological Resource Center (ABRC) (Alonso et al., 2003).

###begin title 53
###xml 22 28 22 28 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">wrky2 </italic>
Identification of the wrky2 T-DNA Insertion Mutants
###end title 53
###begin p 54
###xml 4 12 4 12 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">wrky2-1 </italic>
###xml 171 177 171 177 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">WRKY2 </italic>
###xml 189 197 189 197 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">wrky2-2 </italic>
###xml 368 376 368 376 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">wrky2-1 </italic>
The wrky2-1 mutant (Salk_020399), obtained from the Arabidopsis Biological Resource Center (ABRC) (Alonso et al., 2003), contains a T-DNA insertion in the promoter of the WRKY2 gene, while wrky2-2 mutant (Sail_739_F05) is a gift of Dr. Zhixiang Chen (Department of Botany and Plant Pathology, Purdue University, West Lafayette, Indiana, USA). Homozygous plants of the wrky2-1 mutant were identified by two PCRs. In the first PCR, a pair of gene-specific primers designed to anneal outside of the T-DNA insertion were used, which in case of homozygosity does not produce a band of the predicted size (negative selection): forward primer 5'-ATCGTCATCATCTTCACCATTT-3' and reverse primer 5'-AACTGAAATCCTCAGTTCCGT-3'. In the subsequent PCR, the T-DNA border primer (5'-AAACGTCCGCAATGTGTTAT-3') in combination with forward primer in the first PCR. To confirm the nature and location of the T-DNA insertion, the PCR products were sequenced. To remove additional T-DNA loci or mutations from the mutants, backcrosses to wild-type plants were performed and plants homozygous for the T-DNA insertion were again identified.
###end p 54
###begin title 55
Quantitative Real-Time PCR
###end title 55
###begin p 56
###xml 41 47 41 47 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">wrky2 </italic>
###xml 141 147 140 146 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">abi5-1</italic>
###xml 149 155 148 154 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">abi3-1</italic>
###xml 157 163 156 162 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">aba2-3</italic>
###xml 165 172 164 171 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">aba3-1 </italic>
###xml 988 993 976 981 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">ACT2 </italic>
###xml 1087 1091 1075 1079 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">ACT2</italic>
###xml 1093 1098 1081 1086 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">WRKY2</italic>
###xml 1100 1104 1088 1092 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">ABI5</italic>
###xml 1106 1110 1094 1098 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">ABI3</italic>
###xml 1112 1116 1100 1104 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">Em1 </italic>
###xml 1120 1124 1108 1112 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">Em6 </italic>
###xml 1144 1145 1132 1133 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="table" rid="T2">2</xref>
###xml 1172 1178 1160 1166 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">MYB33 </italic>
###xml 1182 1189 1170 1177 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">MYB101 </italic>
###xml 1215 1220 1203 1208 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">et al</italic>
###xml 1230 1232 1218 1220 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="B53">53</xref>
###xml 1351 1352 1337 1338 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">2</sub>
###xml 1617 1622 1595 1600 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">ACT2 </italic>
We germinated seeds of the wild type and wrky2 mutants on MS medium with or without 1.5 muM ABA for 4 or 7 days, and germinated the seeds of abi5-1, abi3-1, aba2-3, aba3-1 mutants and the wild type (Col, Ws and Ler) on MS medium with or without 1.5 muM ABA for 4 days. Harvest samples were froze immediately in liquid nitrogen, and stored at 80degreesC. RNA was extracted from these samples using An RNeasy Plant Mini kit (QIAgen, Valencia, CA), and DNA was removed via an on-column DNase treatment. For real-time PCR, the First Strand cDNA Synthesis kit (Roche, Diagnostics, Mannheim, Germany) was used to make cDNA from 1 mug of RNA in a 20 muL reaction volume. Each cDNA sample was diluted 1:20 in water, and 2 muL of this dilution was used as template for qPCR. Half-reactions (10 muL each) were performed with the Lightcycler FastStart DNA Master SYBR Green I kit (Roche, Mannheim, Germany) on a Roche LightCycler real-time PCR machine, according to the manufacturer's instructions. ACT2 (AT3G18780) was used as a control in qPCR. Gene-specific primers for detecting transcripts of ACT2, WRKY2, ABI5, ABI3, Em1 and Em6 are listed in Table 2. Gene-specific primers of MYB33 and MYB101 are as described by Allen et al. (2007) [53]. The qPCR reactions (10 muL each) for these genes contained the following: 1 muL SYBR Green I reaction mix, 3 mM MgCl2, 0.5 muM forward and reverse primers and 2 muL cDNA. The annealing temperature was 52degreesC in all cases. A no-template control was routinely included to confirm the absence of DNA or RNA contamination. The mean value of four replicates was normalized using the ACT2 gene as the control. Standard curves were generated using linearized plasmid DNA for each gene of interest. A second set of experiments was conducted on an independent set of tissue as a control.

###begin title 59
Northern blotting
###end title 59
###begin p 60
###xml 205 207 204 206 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">+ </sup>
###xml 354 361 353 360 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">miR159 </italic>
###xml 482 487 481 486 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">WRKY2</italic>
###xml 620 622 614 616 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">32</sup>

Total RNA was isolated using the TRIZOL reagent (BRL Life Technologies, Rockville, MD). 20 mug RNA was separated by electrophoresis on denaturing 17% polyacrylamide gels, and electroblotted onto a Hybond-N+ membrane. The membrane was UV cross-linked and hybridized with PerfectHyb plus hybridization buffer (Sigma). DNA oligonucleotides complementary to miR159 were end-labeled using T4 polynucleotide kinase (Roche Applied Science, Penzberg, Germany). For RNA gel blot analysis of WRKY2, 20 mug total RNA was separated on 1.5% agarose-formaldehyde gels and blotted to nylon membranes. Blots were hybridized with [alpha-32P]dATP labeled gene-specific probes. Hybridization was performed in PerfectHyb plus hybridization buffer (Sigma) overnight at 68degreesC. The membrane was then washed for 10 minutes twice with 2x SSC (1x SSC is 0.15 M NaCl and 0.015 M sodium citrate) and 1% SDS and for 10 minutes with 0.1x SSC and 1% SDS at 68degreesC. Transcripts for WRKY2 were detected with about 1 kb before stop codon of WRKY2 cDNA as probe.
