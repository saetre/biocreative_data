###begin article-title 0
Interactions between Casein Kinase Iepsilon (CKIepsilon) and Two Substrates from Disparate Signaling Pathways Reveal Mechanisms for Substrate-Kinase Specificity
###end article-title 0
###begin p 1
Conceived and designed the experiments: CLD EZN DRG DK. Performed the experiments: CLD EZN. Analyzed the data: CLD EZN DK. Contributed reagents/materials/analysis tools: EZN DRG DK. Wrote the paper: CLD DK.
###end p 1
###begin title 2
Background
###end title 2
###begin p 3
Members of the Casein Kinase I (CKI) family of serine/threonine kinases regulate diverse biological pathways. The seven mammalian CKI isoforms contain a highly conserved kinase domain and divergent amino- and carboxy-termini. Although they share a preferred target recognition sequence and have overlapping expression patterns, individual isoforms often have specific substrates. In an effort to determine how substrates recognize differences between CKI isoforms, we have examined the interaction between CKIepsilon and two substrates from different signaling pathways.
###end p 3
###begin title 4
Methodology/Principal Findings
###end title 4
###begin p 5
CKIepsilon, but not CKIalpha, binds to and phosphorylates two proteins: Period, a transcriptional regulator of the circadian rhythms pathway, and Disheveled, an activator of the planar cell polarity pathway. We use GST-pull-down assays data to show that two key residues in CKIalpha's kinase domain prevent Disheveled and Period from binding. We also show that the unique C-terminus of CKIepsilon does not determine Dishevelled's and Period's preference for CKIepsilon nor is it essential for binding, but instead plays an auxillary role in stabilizing the interactions of CKIepsilon with its substrates. We demonstrate that autophosphorylation of CKIepsilon's C-terminal tail prevents substrate binding, and use mass spectrometry and chemical crosslinking to reveal how a phosphorylation-dependent interaction between the C-terminal tail and the kinase domain prevents substrate phosphorylation and binding.
###end p 5
###begin title 6
Conclusions/Significance
###end title 6
###begin p 7
The biochemical interactions between CKIepsilon and Disheveled, Period, and its own C-terminus lead to models that explain CKIepsilon's specificity and regulation.
###end p 7

Introduction

###xml 732 735 681 684 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0004766-Green1">[9]</xref>
###xml 781 785 726 730 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0004766-Davidson1">[10]</xref>
###xml 959 962 890 893 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0004766-Green1">[9]</xref>
###xml 964 968 895 899 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0004766-Trangle1">[11]</xref>
###xml 969 973 900 904 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0004766-Lein1">[14]</xref>
###xml 1127 1131 1058 1062 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0004766-Davidson1">[10]</xref>
###xml 1133 1137 1064 1068 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0004766-Yin1">[15]</xref>
###xml 1138 1142 1069 1073 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0004766-Cong1">[18]</xref>
CKI family members have been implicated in a wide range of signaling activities including involvement in the Wnt, planar cell polarity (PCP) and circadian rhythms pathways [1]-[3]. In mammals, the CKI family of serine/threonine kinases consists of seven distinct genes (alpha, beta, gamma1, gamma2, gamma3, delta, epsilon) that code for enzymes with highly conserved kinase domains and divergent amino- (N-) and carboxy- (C-) termini. CKIepsilon in particular has a long (approximately100 residue) C-terminus that undergoes inhibitory autophosphorylation at conserved serine and threonine residues [4]-[7]. Subcellular localization of different isoforms in some cases is determined by splice variants (in the case of CKIalpha) [8], [9] or fatty-acylation (in the case of CKIgamma) [10], but Western and Northern blotting experiments, and in situ hybridization studies indicate that the alpha, delta, and epsilon isoforms have generally overlapping expression [9], [11]-[14]. It is therefore interesting that despite high sequence similarity and common expression patterns, CKI family members have different targets in the cell [10], [15]-[18].
###end p 9
###begin p 10
###xml 101 105 101 105 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0004766-Klein1">[19]</xref>
###xml 107 111 107 111 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0004766-Matsubayashi1">[20]</xref>
###xml 112 116 112 116 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0004766-Strutt1">[23]</xref>
###xml 204 208 204 208 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0004766-Cong1">[18]</xref>
###xml 209 213 209 213 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0004766-Zeng1">[30]</xref>
###xml 423 427 420 424 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0004766-Bilic1">[26]</xref>
###xml 428 432 425 429 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0004766-Zeng2">[31]</xref>
###xml 512 515 509 512 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0004766-Price1">[2]</xref>
###xml 517 521 514 518 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0004766-Klein1">[19]</xref>
###xml 523 527 520 524 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0004766-Penton1">[21]</xref>
###xml 528 532 525 529 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0004766-Seifert1">[33]</xref>
###xml 899 903 864 868 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0004766-Klein1">[19]</xref>
###xml 905 909 870 874 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0004766-Strutt1">[23]</xref>
###xml 911 915 876 880 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0004766-Bryja1">[27]</xref>
Dishevelled (Dsh) is a 100 kDa protein that is required for canonical Wnt and Frizzled/PCP signaling [19], [20]-[23]. Hyperphosphorylation is accepted as an indicator of Dsh's activation in both pathways [18]-[30]. During canonical Wnt signaling, phosphorylated Dsh induces the translocation of Wnt responsive signalosomes to the cell membrane, as an early step in stabilizing the transcriptional coactivator, beta-catenin [26]-[31]. Dsh's phosphorylation also indicates its activation in Frizzled/PCP signaling [2], [19], [21]-[33]. Although CKIalpha, CKIgamma, and CKIepsilon have all been implicated in Wnt and/or Frizzled/PCP signaling, only CKIepsilon has been shown to bind to and phosphorylate Dsh, in vivo. Despite the similarity between the kinase domains of CKIepsilon and the other CKI isoforms, CKIepsilon is the kinase required for the full activation of Dsh and its downstream effects [19], [23], [27].
###end p 10
###begin p 11
###xml 227 242 227 242 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0004766-Virshup1">[reviewed in 3]</xref>
###xml 244 248 244 248 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0004766-Mackey1">[34]</xref>
###xml 250 254 250 254 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0004766-Hardin1">[35]</xref>
###xml 414 418 408 412 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0004766-Miyazaki1">[36]</xref>
###xml 419 423 413 417 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0004766-Vielhaber1">[40]</xref>
###xml 542 546 536 540 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0004766-Bae1">[41]</xref>
###xml 548 552 542 546 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0004766-Lee1">[42]</xref>
###xml 659 663 647 651 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0004766-Takano2">[39]</xref>
###xml 665 669 653 657 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0004766-Loudon1">[43]</xref>
###xml 670 674 658 662 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0004766-Xu1">[45]</xref>
###xml 844 848 822 826 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0004766-Lein1">[14]</xref>
###xml 181 187 <span type="species:ncbi:9606">humans</span>
###xml 457 461 <span type="species:ncbi:10090">mice</span>
###xml 810 814 <span type="species:ncbi:10090">mice</span>
Physiological outputs (hormonal, metabolic and cellular) that are entrained to the 24 hour cycle of the earth's rotation have been described in organisms from cyanobacteria through humans, and are based on molecular clockwork, [reviewed in 3], [34], [35]. The cyclic expression and localization of the transcriptional regulator, Period (Per), is in large part regulated by phosphorylation by CKIepsilon in mammals [36]-[40]. There are three Per isoforms in mice (mPer1, mPer2, mPer3) that display different expression and regulation patterns [41], [42]. Mutations in CKIepsilon disrupt the circadian pathway through aberrant or reduced phosphorylation of Per [39], [43]-[45]. Both CKIepsilon and CKIalpha isoforms are found in cells that exhibit circadian cycling, including the master circadian oscillator in mice, the suprachiasmatic nucleus [14]. Why one CKI isoform is active in the pathway while the other is not has not been investigated.
###end p 11
###begin p 12
###xml 44 48 38 42 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0004766-Klein1">[19]</xref>
###xml 50 54 44 48 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0004766-Shirogane1">[37]</xref>
###xml 56 60 50 54 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0004766-Hino1">[46]</xref>
###xml 61 65 55 59 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0004766-Gao1">[48]</xref>
Per and Dsh bind specifically to CKIepsilon [19], [37], [46]-[48]. To determine how CKIepsilon and CKIalpha differ with respect to binding by substrates, we performed a series of in vitro experiments to probe the direct interaction between CKIepsilon and two substrates from disparate signaling pathways. We show that, surprisingly, features C-terminal to the kinase domain of CKIepsilon are not responsible for the difference in binding of the substrate proteins to the two CKI kinases. Instead, two residues on the kinase domain of CKIepsilon determine Dsh's and Per's affinity for CKIepsilon, whereas the C-terminus of CKIepsilon stabilizes interactions between CKIepsilon and Dsh and Per. Finally, we show that autophosphorylation of CKIepsilon's C-terminus inhibits substrate binding. Using chemical cross-linking and tandem mass spectrometry, we demonstrate that autophosphoryation of the C-terminal tail changes its interaction with the kinase domain, revealing why the autophosphorylated tail inhibits substrate phosphorylation and binding.
###end p 12

Results
###end title 13
###begin title 14
CKIepsilon, but not CKIalpha, binds to xDsh and mPer1 in vitro
###end title 14
###begin p 15
###xml 100 104 90 94 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0004766-Strutt1">[23]</xref>
###xml 106 110 96 100 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0004766-Vielhaber2">[47]</xref>

###xml 500 504 474 478 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0004766-Longenecker1">[50]</xref>
###xml 506 510 480 484 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0004766-Ha1">[51]</xref>
###xml 704 711 678 685 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">Xenopus</italic>
###xml 757 764 719 726 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="sec" rid="s4">Methods</xref>
###xml 822 829 784 791 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">Xenopus</italic>
###xml 1201 1203 1140 1142 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">35</sup>
###xml 1214 1221 1153 1160 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">Xenopus</italic>
###xml 1311 1317 1240 1246 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="fig" rid="pone-0004766-g001">Fig. 1</xref>
###xml 1372 1376 1301 1305 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0004766-Klein1">[19]</xref>
###xml 1378 1382 1307 1311 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0004766-Shirogane1">[37]</xref>
###xml 1384 1388 1313 1317 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0004766-Hino1">[46]</xref>
###xml 1389 1393 1318 1322 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0004766-Gao1">[48]</xref>
###xml 369 376 <span type="species:ncbi:110766|species:ncbi:562">E. coli</span>
###xml 1237 1242 <span type="species:ncbi:10090">mouse</span>
Per1 and Dsh bind to CKIepsilon in vivo, but they do not interact with the related isoform CKIalpha [23], [47]-[49]. In order to determine if these substrates bind to the two kinases with the corresponding affinities in vitro, we used a Glutathione S-Transferase (GST) pull-down system to follow protein-protein interactions. Due to protein instability and toxicity in E. coli, prior in vitro studies of CKIepsilon have used a C-terminally truncated form of the protein called CKIepsilonDeltaC [48], [50], [51], which eliminates the last 98 residues of the protein. Since we wanted to include all of the protein in our analysis, we developed an expression system for large quantities of pure full-length Xenopus CKIepsilon fused to GST (CKIepsilon-GST; see Methods). Using a similar strategy we also expressed full-length Xenopus CKIalpha fused to GST (CKIalpha-GST). Both kinases were catalytically active against beta-catenin, indicating that they were both well folded (data not shown). Because CKIepsilon autophosphorylation regulates substrate binding (see below), we used dephosphorylated CKIepsilon for our binding studies except where otherwise noted. Using GST-pull-down assays we found that 35S-labelled Xenopus Dsh (xDsh) and mouse Per1 (mPer1) bound specifically to CKIepsilon compared to CKIalpha (Fig. 1), recapitulating previously published in vivo results [19], [37], [46]-[48]. This provided us with a useful assay system to examine CKIepsilon specificity for these substrates.

###begin title 18
CKIepsilon does not require its C-terminus to interact with substrate proteins
###end title 18
###begin p 19
###xml 84 90 74 80 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="fig" rid="pone-0004766-g002">Fig. 2</xref>
###xml 387 393 355 361 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="fig" rid="pone-0004766-g002">Fig. 2</xref>
###xml 396 405 364 373 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="fig" rid="pone-0004766-g003">Figure 3A</xref>
CKIepsilon and CKIalpha are 75% identical, and 89% similar in their kinase domains (Fig. 2). This high degree of conservation between the kinases suggested that full binding by mPer1 and xDsh to CKIepsilon might depend on the long C-terminal tail, which is unique to CKIepsilon. We first examined binding of mPer1 and xDsh to CKIepsilonDeltaC, which truncates the kinase at residue 319 (Fig. 2). Figure 3A shows that xDsh and mPer bound to CKIepsilon and CKIepsilonDeltaC to the same extent, demonstrating that CKIepsilon does not require the autophosphorylation region of the C-terminus to bind to substrate proteins.

Because truncation of CKIepsilon at residue 319 did not diminish xDsh or mPer1's affinity for the kinase, another part of the protein must determine CKIepsilon's interaction with substrate proteins. CKIepsilonDeltaC retains 22 residues that are not conserved between CKIepsilon and CKIalpha (Fig. 2) and these could contribute to the substrate-kinase interaction. ###begin title 25
Residues N275 and R295 are responsible for enhanced substrate binding to CKIepsilon
###end title 25
###begin p 26

###xml 358 362 338 342 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0004766-Gao1">[48]</xref>
###xml 364 368 344 348 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0004766-Zhang1">[52]</xref>
###xml 396 405 376 385 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="fig" rid="pone-0004766-g004">Figure 4A</xref>
###xml 667 674 619 626 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="fig" rid="pone-0004766-g004">Fig. 4A</xref>
To identify residues within the CKIepsilon kinase domain that could confer specificity, we examined the structure of CKIdelta. CKIdelta is considered a very close relative of CKIepsilon since they share 82% identity over the entire protein and 96% identity in their kinase domains, and since their cellular activities are indistinguishable [11], [13], [24], [48], [52]. Residues shown in cyan in Figure 4A are conserved between CKIepsilon and CKIdelta but are not conserved in CKIalpha. Two residues that are conserved in CKIepsilon and CKIdelta but not CKIalpha, and have solvent exposed side-chains, Asparagine 275 (N275) and Arginine 279 (R279), are shown in red (Fig. 4A). In CKIalpha, the corresponding residues have chemical properties that differ substantially from the CKIepsilon residues (Asp-->Ile and Arg-->Thr), and therefore these residues could contribute specificity to CKIepsilon-substrate interactions.

We initially changed N275 and R279 to alanines in the context of the conventional CKIepsilonDeltaC truncation (CKIepsilonDeltaC N275A/R279A, Fig. 2). These mutations significantly disrupted the binding of xDsh to CKIepsilon, although they had no effect on mPer1 binding (Fig. 4B, lane 4, and Fig. 4C). In conclusion, N275 and R279 are required for strong binding between CKIepsilon and xDsh, whereas the corresponding chemically distinct residues in CKIalpha prevent binding of mPer1 to CKIepsilon.

The C-terminus of CKIepsilon modulates the effect of mutations in the kinase domain
###end title 30
###begin p 31
###xml 185 191 173 179 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="fig" rid="pone-0004766-g002">Fig. 2</xref>
###xml 367 373 339 345 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="fig" rid="pone-0004766-g004">Fig. 4</xref>
###xml 377 383 349 355 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="fig" rid="pone-0004766-g005">Fig. 5</xref>
###xml 477 483 443 449 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="fig" rid="pone-0004766-g003">Fig. 3</xref>
In order to determine what role the C-terminus might play in substrate recognition, we introduced the N275I and R279T substitutions into full-length CKIepsilon (CKIepsilon N275I/R279T, Fig. 2). xDsh's binding to CKIepsilon N275I/R279T is marginally enhanced compared to its binding to CKIepsilonDeltaC N275I/R279T, while mPer1 binding is completely restored (compare Fig. 4 to Fig. 5). We conclude that the C-terminus is not necessary for CKIepsilon to bind to substrates (see Fig. 3), but that it stabilizes otherwise weakened interactions between xDsh or mPer1 and CKIepsilon.

###begin title 37
Conversion of CKIalpha to an xDsh-binding protein
###end title 37
###begin p 38
###xml 369 375 323 329 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="fig" rid="pone-0004766-g002">Fig. 2</xref>
We hypothesized that CKIalpha's residues I283 and T287, which correspond to CKIepsilon's N275 and R279, were responsible for preventing interactions with CKIalpha-->epsilon. We therefore engineered CKIalpha-->epsilon I283N/T287R, which is nearly identical to CKIalpha-->epsilon but contains two point mutations that could potentially enhance binding by xDsh and mPer1 (Fig. 2). Impressively, conversion of CKIalpha residues I283 and T287 to the CKIepsilon identity enabled xDsh to fully interact with the kinase. In contrast, mPer1 did not bind CKIalpha-->epsilon I283N/T287R. This data clearly demonstrates that xDsh depends on the CKIepsilon residues N275 and R279 for strong binding to CKIepsilon, and reveals that mPer1 requires an additional CKIepsilon-like environment elsewhere on the kinase in order to bind CKIalpha.

###begin title 39
Phosphorylation of the C-terminus of CKIepsilon inhibits the binding of target proteins
###end title 39
###begin p 40
###xml 158 171 152 165 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="fig" rid="pone-0004766-g006">Fig. 6A and B</xref>
###xml 298 301 286 289 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0004766-Gietzen1">[6]</xref>
###xml 303 306 291 294 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0004766-Rivers1">[7]</xref>
###xml 308 312 296 300 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0004766-Cegielska1">[53]</xref>
###xml 314 318 302 306 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0004766-Cegielska2">[54]</xref>
###xml 511 517 493 499 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="fig" rid="pone-0004766-g007">Fig. 7</xref>
Our data shows that the C-terminus of CKIepsilon stabilizes some substrate interactions, specifically in the absence of motifs that are required for binding (Fig. 6A and B). However, autophosphorylation of the C-terminus has also been shown to inhibit CKIepsilon's activity towards protein targets [6], [7], [53], [54]. We hypothesized that this effect might be partially mediated by a change in affinity for substrates. When CKIepsilon is allowed to fully autophosphorylate, neither mPer1 nor xDsh bind to it (Fig. 7). In addition, hyperphosphorylated CKIepsilon no longer binds to mAxin, a protein that is able to bind to both CKIalpha and CKIepsilon. Hyperphosphorylation of CKIepsilon's C-terminus thus inhibits the binding of substrate proteins, and may act as a regulatory mechanism to control phosphorylation of targets.

###begin title 43
Autophosphorylation of the C-terminus of CKIepsilon stabilizes its interaction with the kinase domain
###end title 43
###begin p 44
Despite numerous attempts, we were unable to crystallize full-length autophosphorylated CKIepsilon, raising the possibility that the phosphorylated C-terminus does not adopt a single conformation, which is necessary to obtain a crystal. It was possible, however, that the autophosphorylated C-terminus of CKIepsilon adopts a stable set of conformations that blocks the binding site for substrates. As an alternative approach to crystallography, we performed a set of crosslinking and mass spectrometry experiments to establish how autophosphorylation of CKIepsilon's C-terminus interferes with substrate binding.
###end p 44
###begin p 45
###xml 706 713 682 689 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="fig" rid="pone-0004766-g008">Fig. 8A</xref>
###xml 866 873 836 843 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="fig" rid="pone-0004766-g008">Fig. 8A</xref>
We first looked for evidence of a conformational change by following differences in electrophoretic mobility of purified, hyperphosphorylated CKIepsilon upon the addition of 1-Ethyl-3-[3-dimethylaminopropyl]carbodiimide Hydrochloride (EDC), a zero-length crosslinker. When two or more proteins are crosslinked by EDC, the expected product is a band of approximately their additive molecular weights. In the case of CKIepsilon, no high molecular weight bands appear, demonstrating that CKIepsilon does not form dimers or higher-order oligomers in solution. Instead, the single band corresponding to CKIepsilon broadened considerably, and showed faster mobility after EDC treatment as seen by PAGE analysis (Fig. 8A, lane 4). Interestingly, unphosphorylated CKIepsilon did not show a change in mobility after crosslinking and it continued to migrate as a single band (Fig. 8A, lane 2). The broad band that appears after crosslinking suggests that the autophosphorylated CKIepsilon occupies multiple conformations.

###begin title 51
Discussion
###end title 51
###begin p 52
###xml 279 283 253 257 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0004766-Strutt1">[23]</xref>
###xml 285 289 259 263 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0004766-Vielhaber2">[47]</xref>
###xml 290 294 264 268 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0004766-Okamura1">[49]</xref>
CKIepsilon and CKIalpha are closely related kinases with very divergent C-termini. We show here that recombinant CKIepsilon and CKIalpha interact in vitro with two known CKIepsilon substrates similarly to the previously published in vivo interactions between endogenous proteins [23], [47]-[49]. Using recombinant proteins, we demonstrate that CKIepsilon's C-terminus is not essential for binding to xDsh and mPer1, and show that two residues in the conserved kinase domain, N275 and R279, provide a critical interface for binding by these substrates. Additionally, we show that the C-terminus of CKIepsilon can positively regulate substrates by stabilizing contacts with the kinase domain when dephosphorylated, whereas it can negatively regulate substrate-kinase interactions when autophosphorylated. Finally, we show that a conformational change occurs in the C-terminus of CKIepsilon such that the C-terminus probably binds near the active site when the kinase is autophosphorylated.
###end p 52
###begin title 53
Selectivity of CKIepsilon binding for Dishevelled
###end title 53
###begin p 54
###xml 98 105 88 95 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="fig" rid="pone-0004766-g009">Fig. 9A</xref>
###xml 221 228 207 214 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="fig" rid="pone-0004766-g009">Fig. 9B</xref>
###xml 470 476 456 462 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="fig" rid="pone-0004766-g004">Fig. 4</xref>
###xml 835 841 809 815 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="fig" rid="pone-0004766-g005">Fig. 5</xref>
###xml 1034 1041 998 1005 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="fig" rid="pone-0004766-g009">Fig. 9C</xref>
The binding of xDsh to CKIepsilon and not CKIalpha primarily requires the residues N275 and R279 (Fig. 9A). Mutation of these residues to either alanines or the CKIalpha identities, isoleucine and threonine respectively (Fig. 9B), prevented binding. Thus, the identity of these residues is essential, suggesting that Dsh directly contacts these residues. We also noted that the extent to which Dsh was phosphorylated (visualized by a change in electrophoretic mobility, Fig. 4) was different depending on the mutations that were made. The identities of the residues at positions 275 and 279 in CKIepsilon may therefore also be important for correctly positioning Dsh for full phosphorylation. The C-terminal tail of CKIepsilon provided some enhancement of xDsh binding and increased the level of phosphorylation by the mutant protein (Fig. 5), but it was not sufficient for binding. Strikingly, we saw full binding of xDsh to CKIalpha simply by changing the I283 and T287 to the CKIepsilon identity and adding on the C-terminal tail (Fig. 9C). In the absence of the C-terminal tail, Dsh did not bind to the mutated CKIalpha (data not shown), which strengthens our hypothesis that the C-terminus of CKIepsilon does contact Dsh, but is not required in for interactions with wild-type CKIepsilon. These results allow us to fully explain the selectivity of CKIepsilon for Dishevelled.

(D) Binding of the autophosphorylated tail to the backside prevents the binding of substrates. Phosphorylated sites detected by mass spectrometry are shown on the tail. (E) View of the front side of the kinase. Left, CKIepsilon's C-terminus is labile when it is not phosphorylated, and CKIepsilon is able to bind to partners. Right, upon incubation with ATP, CKIepsilon autophosphorylates and the C-terminus binds tightly to the back side of the kinase domain. This positions the peptide PEDLDRERREHDREER next to the active site and the phosphate recognition groove. The X's show identified crosslinks between the peptide and the kinase domain.
###end p 56
###begin title 57
Selectivity of CKIepsilon binding for Period
###end title 57
###begin p 58
###xml 445 452 411 418 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="fig" rid="pone-0004766-g009">Fig. 9B</xref>
The binding of mPer1 to CKIepsilon is more complex than for Dishevelled. Changing residues N275 and R279 to alanine in the context of CKIepsilonDeltaC did not disrupt mPer1 binding, whereas mutation of these residues to the CKIalpha identity in CKIepsilonDeltaC eliminated all binding. This result indicates that the CKIalpha residues at this position prevent binding by mPer1, but that the residues may not be directly involved in its binding (Fig. 9B). This is supported by the observation that changing these residues in CKIalpha to the CKIepsilon identity (as well as adding the C-terminal tail) did not rescue mPer1 binding even though it completely rescued xDsh binding.
###end p 58
###begin p 59
The C-terminal tail does have an important contribution to mPer1 binding since full-length CKIepsilon N275I/R279T could fully bind mPer1 whereas CKIepsilonDeltaC N275I/R279T could not bind mPer1. Thus the C-terminal tail can override the repulsive effect of N275I and R279T. However, since the tail did not rescue CKIalpha-->epsilon I283N/T287R, it alone is not sufficient for binding mPer1 to CKIalpha. These results reveal that another site (or sites) on CKI must also contribute to the difference in binding between CKIepsilon and CKIalpha.
###end p 59
###begin p 60
###xml 133 137 133 137 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0004766-Okamura1">[49]</xref>
###xml 273 277 263 267 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0004766-Okamura1">[49]</xref>
###xml 457 461 437 441 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0004766-Shirogane1">[37]</xref>
###xml 463 467 443 447 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0004766-Okamura1">[49]</xref>
###xml 750 757 714 721 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="fig" rid="pone-0004766-g009">Fig. 9A</xref>
One CKI-binding motif, F-X-X-X-F (where F is phenylalanine and X can be any amino acid residue), is present in mPer1 but not in xDsh [49]. Studies of the NFAT family of proteins suggest that the F-X-X-X-F motif enables stable interactions with both CKIalpha and CKIepsilon [49]. Mutation of the phenylalanine residues to alanines abolishes binding by NFAT to CKIalpha and CKIepsilon. mPer1 shows the same loss of binding when its F-X-X-X-F motif is mutated [37], [49]. Since the F-X-X-X-F motif binds both CKIalpha and CKIepsilon, it contributes to the binding affinity of mPer1 to CK1 but it is not involved in the selectivity. Thus, we conclude that another residue (or residues) on CKIepsilon besides N275 and R279 contributes to the selectivity (Fig. 9A). This additional residue (or residues) could be amino acids that in CKIepsilon positively enhance the binding of mPer1, or residue (or residues) that in CKIalpha repel mPer1. Nonetheless, our results demonstrate that the lack of the tail and changes in two residues in CKIalpha are sufficient to explain the lack of binding of CKIalpha to mPer1.
###end p 60
###begin title 61
The role and structure of the C-terminal tail
###end title 61
###begin p 62
###xml 154 158 148 152 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0004766-Liu1">[61]</xref>
###xml 159 163 153 157 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0004766-Swiatek2">[63]</xref>
###xml 246 250 234 238 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0004766-Swiatek2">[63]</xref>
The C-terminus of CKIepsilon has been shown in previous reports to be catalytically autoinhibitory and important for regulation within signaling pathways [61]-[63]. Wnt signaling lowers the level of autophosphorylation on CKIepsilon's C-terminus [63] though only a subset of the sites are phosphorylated in vivo, judging by polyacrylamide gel and isoelectric focusing data. Our data show that phosphorylation of the C-terminus blocks interactions between CKIepsilon and binding partners, and it is therefore likely that some portion of the lowered phosphorylation activity seen in vivo is due to less efficient substrate binding.
###end p 62
###begin p 63
###xml 113 116 107 110 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0004766-Graves1">[4]</xref>
###xml 118 122 112 116 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0004766-Longenecker1">[50]</xref>
###xml 124 128 118 122 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0004766-Xu2">[57]</xref>
###xml 301 304 295 298 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0004766-Gross1">[1]</xref>
###xml 306 309 300 303 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0004766-Graves1">[4]</xref>
###xml 311 315 305 309 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0004766-Longenecker1">[50]</xref>
###xml 317 321 311 315 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0004766-Flotow1">[55]</xref>
###xml 322 326 316 320 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0004766-Marin1">[59]</xref>
###xml 328 332 322 326 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0004766-Knippschild1">[64]</xref>
###xml 763 770 757 764 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="fig" rid="pone-0004766-g008">Fig. 8B</xref>
###xml 775 777 769 771 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="fig" rid="pone-0004766-g009">9E</xref>
###xml 880 884 866 870 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0004766-Longenecker1">[50]</xref>
###xml 886 890 872 876 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0004766-Longenecker2">[58]</xref>
The C-terminus of CKIepsilon has been proposed to act as a pseudo-substrate for the kinase during autoinhibition [4], [50], [57]. The positively charged groove in front of the active site has been postulated to bind phosphopeptides that helps determine the S(p)-X-X-S specificity of the kinase family [1], [4], [50], [55]-[59], [64]. Thus the phosphates in the tail could occupy that site and prevent substrates from binding. Our data suggest the alternative view that the phosphorylation of the tail causes the unphosphorylated but highly charged peptide, PEDLDRERREHDREER to sit across the front of the kinase's active site when the C-terminal tail is autophosphorylated, thus preventing substrate access to the active site and/or phosphate recognition groove (Fig. 8B and 9E). Intriguingly, two previous structures of CKIdeltaDeltaC failed to show any density for this peptide [50], [58], indicating that phosphorylation of the C-terminal tail is necessary to lock this peptide into place.
###end p 63
###begin p 64
###xml 28 37 28 37 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="fig" rid="pone-0004766-g009">Figure 9D</xref>
###xml 177 184 177 184 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="fig" rid="pone-0004766-g009">Fig. 9D</xref>
###xml 250 257 250 257 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="fig" rid="pone-0004766-g008">Fig. 8B</xref>
###xml 664 668 648 652 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0004766-Longenecker1">[50]</xref>
###xml 670 674 654 658 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0004766-Longenecker2">[58]</xref>
###xml 828 835 812 819 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="fig" rid="pone-0004766-g009">Fig. 9E</xref>
In the model illustrated in Figure 9D, after PEDLDRERREHDREER is directed through the phosphate recognition groove, the rest of the C-terminus is led to the back of the kinase (Fig. 9D). The back face is very basic as shown in an electrostatic view (Fig. 8B), providing many sites for the phosphates in the tail to interact. The heterogeneity of the cross-linked phosphorylated CKIepsilon and the lack of identification of prominent cross-linked tail peptides suggests that the interaction of the tail with the back side is highly variable, which also explains our inability to crystallize full-length CKIepsilon (the same was also found with full-length CKIdelta [50], [58]). Once at the back side of the kinase domain, some phosphorylated residues on the C-terminus are buried in the basic face to stabilize the conformation (Fig. 9E). The long C-terminus could then fold against the back side of the kinase domain to occlude the binding sites for Dsh, Per and Axin. Thus, we suggest that phosphorylation of the tail has two roles, to bring the proximal C-terminus in front of the active site to prevent substrate phosphorylation and to prevent substrate binding using the distal part of the tail.
###end p 64
###begin p 65
Casein Kinase family members have many targets in the cell. The results of this study designate two amino acid residues that determine the ability of two different CKI isoforms to select and bind their target proteins. Furthermore, our work on auto- and dephosphorylated CKIepsilon introduces a means to regulate CKIepsilon's in the presence of its targets. These two biochemical mechanisms could help maintain the strict spatial and temporal control of CKIepsilon and CKIalpha that is necessary in both the circadian rhythms and Wnt pathways.
###end p 65

###end title 73
###begin p 74
###xml 43 47 43 47 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0004766-Sampietro1">[65]</xref>

Pull-down experiments were as performed in [65] with the following differences. ###begin title 75
Crosslinking and mass spectrometry
###end title 75
###begin p 76
###xml 59 60 53 54 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">2</sub>
###xml 1007 1011 961 965 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0004766-Singh1">[67]</xref>

Crosslinked peptides were identified by using the open modification search tool POPITAM [67]. Post-translational modifications that corresponded to peptides were searched to identify intramolecular crosslinks.
