###begin article-title 0
Differential expression of transforming growth factor-beta isoforms in bullous keratopathy corneas
###end article-title 0
###begin title 1
Purpose
###end title 1
###begin p 2
The aim of this study was to investigate transcriptional activities of genes encoding transforming growth factor (TGF)-beta isoforms in bullous keratopathy corneas.
###end p 2
###begin title 3
Methods
###end title 3
###begin p 4
###xml 183 192 183 189 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">TGF-&#946;1</italic>
###xml 194 203 191 197 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">TGF-&#946;2</italic>
###xml 209 218 203 209 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">TGF-&#946;3</italic>
###xml 32 40 <span type="species:ncbi:9606">patients</span>
The study group consisted of 45 patients with bullous keratopathy (22 females and 23 males). The control group included 45 corneal donors (21 females and 24 males). Quantification of TGF-beta1, TGF-beta2, and TGF-beta3 mRNAs was performed by real-time quantitative reverse transcription PCR (QRT-PCR).
###end p 4
###begin title 5
Results
###end title 5
###begin p 6
###xml 0 9 0 6 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">TGF-&#946;1</italic>
###xml 11 20 8 14 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">TGF-&#946;2</italic>
###xml 26 35 20 26 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">TGF-&#946;3</italic>
###xml 180 189 171 177 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">TGF-&#946;3</italic>
###xml 254 263 242 248 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">TGF-&#946;1</italic>
###xml 268 277 253 259 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">TGF-&#946;2</italic>
TGF-beta1, TGF-beta2, and TGF-beta3 mRNAs were detected in both normal and pseudophakic bullous keratopathy (PBK) corneas. We found significantly lower transcriptional activity of TGF-beta3 mRNA in bullous keratopathy corneas compared to normal tissues. TGF-beta1 and TGF-beta2 expressions were at the same level in both PBK and healthy corneas.
###end p 6
###begin title 7
Conclusions
###end title 7
###begin p 8
###xml 18 27 18 24 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">TGF-&#946;3</italic>
Downregulation of TGF-beta3 gene expression may play a significant role in molecular changes observed in bullous keratopathy.
###end p 8
###begin title 9
Introduction
###end title 9
###begin p 10
###xml 324 325 324 325 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="r1">1</xref>
###xml 326 327 326 327 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="r3">3</xref>
###xml 473 474 473 474 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="r1">1</xref>
###xml 475 476 475 476 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="r4">4</xref>
###xml 477 478 477 478 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="r5">5</xref>
###xml 552 553 552 553 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="r3">3</xref>
Pseudophakic bullous keratopathy (PBK) is a complication of cataract surgery with intraocular lens placement and is an indication for corneal transplantation. Clinical hallmarks of this disease are chronic corneal edema due to corneal endothelial cell dysfunction, subepithelial bullae (blisters), and loss of transparency [1-3]. This disease is also characterized by extensive fibrosis with abnormal deposition of extracellular matrix proteins, tenascin-C, and fibrillin [1,4,5]. Moreover, PBK is often accompanied by scarring and neovascularization [3].
###end p 10
###begin p 11
###xml 79 80 79 80 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="r6">6</xref>
###xml 81 82 81 82 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="r7">7</xref>
###xml 224 225 215 216 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="r8">8</xref>
###xml 226 227 217 218 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="r9">9</xref>
###xml 296 297 278 279 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="r9">9</xref>
###xml 298 300 280 282 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="r10">10</xref>
###xml 458 460 437 439 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="r11">11</xref>
###xml 461 463 440 442 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="r13">13</xref>
###xml 570 572 546 548 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="r13">13</xref>
###xml 573 575 549 551 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="r15">15</xref>
###xml 621 623 597 599 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="r16">16</xref>
###xml 624 626 600 602 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="r18">18</xref>
###xml 288 294 <span type="species:ncbi:9606">humans</span>
###xml 554 559 <span type="species:ncbi:9606">human</span>
Various cytokines and growth factors are strongly involved in these processes [6,7]. One of the most important mediators is the family of transforming growth factors beta (TGF-beta), composed of five isoforms (TGF-beta1-5) [8,9]. Among them, only TGF-beta1, beta2, and beta3 are found in humans [9,10]. The TGF-beta family of cytokines regulates such fundamental aspects of cellular function as cell growth, differentiation, inflammation, and wound healing [11-13]. In addition, there is substantial evidence suggesting participation of TGF-beta in many human diseases [13-15], including fibrotic pathologies of the eye [16-18].
###end p 11
###begin p 12
###xml 268 270 265 267 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="r19">19</xref>
###xml 271 273 268 270 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="r21">21</xref>
###xml 407 409 395 397 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="r22">22</xref>
###xml 525 527 504 506 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="r23">23</xref>
In vitro TGF-beta isoforms have a similar effect on biologic tissues; however, in vivo they are generally characterized by varied degrees of expression and different functions. Their biologic activity depends on quantitative relationships between individual isoforms [19-21]. TGF-beta1 and TGF-beta2 isoforms have been reported to play a profibrotic role, whereas TGF-beta3 possesses antifibrotic activity [22]. Embryonic wounds with a high level of TGF-beta3 and low levels of TGF-beta1 and TGF-beta2 heal with no scarring [23]. During scar-forming in adults, however, TGF-beta1 and TGF-beta2 expression is significantly higher than TGF-beta3 expression during wound healing. Such relationships during development of bullous keratopathy as a result of cornea injury after cataract surgery remain unclear.
###end p 12
###begin p 13
###xml 133 138 <span type="species:ncbi:9606">human</span>
Therefore, the present study focuses on transcriptional activities of genes encoding TGF-beta1, TGF-beta2, and TGF-beta3 isoforms in human corneas with bullous keratopathy. Quantitative relationships between mRNA levels of these three isoforms were also assessed.
###end p 13
###begin title 14
Methods
###end title 14
###begin title 15
Tissues
###end title 15
###begin p 16
###xml 7 12 <span type="species:ncbi:9606">human</span>
Normal human corneas used as controls were taken within 12 h after death from 45 donors (21 females and 24 males; mean age 53.4 years; range 42-65 years). Inclusion criteria for becoming a corneal tissue donor were determined by the Eye Bank Association of America (EBAA).
###end p 16
###begin p 17
###xml 4 11 <span type="species:ncbi:9606">patient</span>
###xml 688 696 <span type="species:ncbi:9606">patients</span>
The patient group involved 45 individuals (22 females and 23 males; mean age 56.1 years; range 45-65 years) with a clinical diagnosis of PBK, treated in the Department of Ophthalmology, Medical University of Silesia, St. Barbara Hospital, Katowice, Poland. The PBK diagnosis was based on the presence of chronic corneal stromal and epithelial edema, painful epithelial bullae with recurrent erosions as well as signs and symptoms of chronic ocular irritation. Exclusion criteria were as follows: the absence of inflammation and degeneration of anterior and posterior segment of eyeball, corneal neovascularization, diabetic retinopathy, pseudoexfoliation syndrome (PEX) and glaucoma. All patients were subjected to cataract surgery in the past; the difference in time between cataract surgery and corneal transplantation averaged 32.4 months. PBK corneas were obtained within 12 h of penetrating keratoplasty.
###end p 17
###begin p 18
###xml 575 583 <span type="species:ncbi:9606">patients</span>
Surgical anesthesia was as follows: Fentanyl (2 mg), Midazolam (2 mg), Athropine (0,01 mg/kg body mass), Thiopental (4-5 mg/kg body mass), Vecuronium (0,1 mg/kg body mass). Because only the central corneal buttons (7.5 mm diameter) were available for PBK corneas, normal corneas were trephined, and only the central portions were used. Tissue specimens were stored in EUSOL C (Alchimia, Padova, Italy) at -70 degreesC for 24 h until RNA extraction. The research was approved by the Bioethics Committee of Medical University of Silesia, Katowice, Poland (NN-6501-146/06). All patients were informed about the research and signed an informed consent form.
###end p 18
###begin title 19
RNA extraction from tissue specimens
###end title 19
###begin p 20
Total RNA was extracted from the specimens using a commercially available kit (Total RNA Prep Plus Kit; A&A Biotechnology, Gdansk, Poland) based on acid guanidinium-thiocyanate phenol-chloroform method by Chomczynski and Sacchi, according to the manufacturer's instructions. RNA extracts were treated with DNase I (MBI Fermentas, Vilnius, Lithuania). The quality of extracts was checked electrophoretically using an 0.8% agarose (Sigma-Aldrich, Munich, Germany) gel stained with ethidium bromide (Sigma-Aldrich). Results were analyzed and recorded using the gel documentation system 1D Bas-Sys (Biotech-Fisher, Perth, Australia). Total RNA concentration was determined by spectrophotometric measurement using the Gene Quant II RNA/DNA Calculator (Pharmacia Biotech, Cambridge, UK).
###end p 20

###xml 1055 1059 991 995 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">ACTB</italic>
###xml 1073 1076 1009 1010 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">&#174;</sup>
###xml 1224 1228 1158 1162 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">ACTB</italic>
###xml 1262 1264 1196 1198 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="r27">27</xref>
###xml 1470 1474 1404 1408 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">ACTB</italic>
Transcriptional activities of TGF-beta1, TGF-beta2, TGF-beta3, and glyceraldehyde-3-phosphate dehydrogenase (GAPDH) genes were evaluated using real time quantitative reverse transcription (QRT)-PCR and SYBR Green I chemistry (QuantiTect(R) SYBR(R) Green RT-PCR kit; QIAGEN, Valencia, CA). Analysis was performed using an Opticontrade mark DNA Engine Continuous Fluorescence Detector (MJ Research, Watertown, MA). All samples were tested in triplicate. GAPDH was included to monitor the QRT-PCR efficiency. Oligonucleotide primers specific for TGF-beta1, TGF-beta2, TGF-beta3, and GAPDH genes were described previously by Strzalka et al. [24,25] and Ercolani et al. [26], respectively (Table 1). The thermal profile for one-step RT-PCR was as follows: reverse transcription at 50 degreesC for 30 min, denaturation at 95 degreesC for 15 min, 50 cycles consisting of temperatures 94 degreesC for 15 s, 60 degreesC for 30 s, and 72 degreesC for 30 s. To detect the expression profile of each investigated gene, commercially available standards of beta-actin (ACTB) cDNA (TaqMan(R) DNA Template Reagent kit; PE Applied Biosystems, Inc., Foster, CA) were used at five different concentrations (ranging from 400 to 8,000 copies of ACTB cDNA), as recommended by Bustin [27]. Amplification plots for each standard template were used to determine the cycle threshold values (Ct). A standard curve was generated by plotting the Ct values against the log of the known amount of the ACTB cDNA copy number. The obtained results of the mRNA copy number were recalculated per 1 mug of total RNA. Each run was completed using melting curve analysis to confirm specificity of the amplification and absence of the primer dimers. The RT-PCR products were also separated in 6% polyacrylamide gels (PAA) and visualized with silver salts.

###begin title 25
Statistical analyses
###end title 25
###begin p 26
###xml 352 360 352 357 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">TGF-&#946;</italic>
###xml 526 527 523 524 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">U</italic>
Statistical analyses were performed using Statistica 8.0 software (StatSoft, Tulsa, OK), with a significance level set at p<0.05. Values are expressed as median (Me), minimum, and maximum. The Kruskal-Wallis one-way analysis of variance test and post hoc multiple test based on the average ranks were applied to assess differences in the expression of TGF-beta isoforms in normal and pathological tissues. Comparison of transcriptional activity of examined genes between normal and PBK corneas was made using the Mann-Whitney U test.

In the present study, transcriptional activity of TGF-beta isoforms in both normal and bullous keratopathy human corneas was determined using real-time QRT-PCR. In the first step of the study, specificity of the RT-PCR assay for the target genes was confirmed experimentally on the basis of the amplimers' melting temperatures. For each RT-PCR product, a single peak at the expected temperature was observed: TGF-beta1 85.4 degreesC; TGF-beta2 80.0 degreesC; TGF-beta3 80.6 degreesC; GAPDH 80.1 degreesC (data not shown). Gel electrophoresis also revealed the presence of a single product of the predicted length (Figure 1).

###xml 1058 1067 982 988 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">TGF-&#946;1</italic>
###xml 1068 1077 989 995 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">TGF-&#946;3</italic>
###xml 1114 1123 1032 1038 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">TGF-&#946;3</italic>
###xml 1190 1191 1105 1106 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">U</italic>
###xml 1236 1245 1151 1160 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="fig" rid="f2">Figure 2C</xref>
###xml 1289 1298 1204 1210 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">TGF-&#946;1</italic>
###xml 1314 1323 1226 1232 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">TGF-&#946;2</italic>
###xml 104 109 <span type="species:ncbi:9606">human</span>
In the next step, levels of TGF-beta1, TGF-beta2, and TGF-beta3 mRNAs in normal and bullous keratopathy human corneas were assessed and the quantitative relations among the mRNA of these three isoforms were then evaluated (Figure 2A,B). TGF-beta1, TGF-beta2, and TGF-beta3 isoforms were detected in all tested samples obtained from normal corneas (TGF-beta1 Me=4,693.0 copies/mug RNA; TGF-beta2 Me=719.0 copies/mug RNA; TGF-beta3 Me=3,844.7 copies/mug RNA) and bullous keratopathy corneas (TGF-beta1 Me=5,553.0 copies/mug RNA; TGF-beta2 Me=738.9 copies/mug RNA; TGF-beta3 Me=2,176.5 copies/mug RNA). Comparable analysis of all TGF-beta mRNA copies/mug of total RNA revealed the following relationships in healthy cornea: TGF-beta1>TGF-beta2 (p=0.0164, post hoc test); TGF-beta3>TGF-beta2 (p<0.001, post hoc test); TGF-beta1=TGF-beta3 (not significant [NS], post hoc test). Pathologically changed cornea relationships were similar to that observed in normal cornea: TGF-beta1>TGF-beta2 (p<0.001, post hoc test); TGF-beta3>TGF-beta2 (p=0.0221, post hoc test); TGF-beta1=TGF-beta3 (NS, post hoc test). In PBK corneas TGF-beta3 mRNA expression was found to be significantly lower (Mann-Whitney U test, p=0.0107) compared to normal tissues (Figure 2C). However, transcriptional activity of the TGF-beta1 (p=0.0585) and TGF-beta2 (p=0.5540) genes in both healthy and PBK corneas was at the same level.
###end p 30

###begin title 32
Discussion
###end title 32
###begin p 33
###xml 12 21 12 18 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">TGF-&#946;1</italic>
###xml 23 32 20 26 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">TGF-&#946;2</italic>
###xml 38 47 32 38 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">TGF-&#946;3</italic>
###xml 93 95 84 86 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="r10">10</xref>
###xml 96 98 87 89 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="r13">13</xref>
###xml 99 101 90 92 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="r28">28</xref>
###xml 299 307 290 295 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">TGF-&#946;</italic>
###xml 349 351 337 339 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="r10">10</xref>
###xml 352 354 340 342 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="r21">21</xref>
###xml 355 357 343 345 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="r24">24</xref>
###xml 358 360 346 348 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="r25">25</xref>
###xml 500 501 479 480 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="r1">1</xref>
###xml 502 503 481 482 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="r3">3</xref>
###xml 504 505 483 484 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="r5">5</xref>
The role of TGF-beta1, TGF-beta2, and TGF-beta3 in the cornea is relatively well understood [10,13,28]. However, quantitative relationships between mRNA expressions of different isoforms in the course of some corneal pathologies are still unclear. In previously published reports mRNA expression of TGF-beta was evaluated mostly in healthy tissues [10,21,24,25], and only a few authors have analyzed the expression profile of TGF-beta1, TGF-beta2, and TGF-beta3 in the course of bullous keratopathy [1,3,5].
###end p 33
###begin p 34
###xml 96 104 96 101 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">TGF-&#946;</italic>
###xml 315 317 311 313 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="r29">29</xref>
###xml 345 353 341 346 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">TGF-&#946;</italic>
###xml 518 520 511 513 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="r10">10</xref>
###xml 521 523 514 516 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="r30">30</xref>
###xml 552 554 545 547 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="r13">13</xref>
###xml 555 557 548 550 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="r28">28</xref>
###xml 117 122 <span type="species:ncbi:9606">human</span>
###xml 528 531 <span type="species:ncbi:10116">rat</span>
In the present study real-time RT-PCR was used to examine the mRNA expression of genes encoding TGF-beta isoforms in human normal and pathologically changed cornea. Transcriptional activity was measured on the basis of the mRNA copy number per 1 mug of total RNA, following the recommendations of Tricarico et al. [29]. Transcripts of all three TGF-beta isoforms were detected in PBK corneas and in healthy ones, which is in agreement with other published results when the examined material constituted cell cultures [10,30] or rat corneal epithelium [13,28].
###end p 34
###begin p 35
###xml 11 13 11 13 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="r21">21</xref>
###xml 29 38 29 35 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">TGF-&#946;1</italic>
###xml 194 202 191 196 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">TGF-&#946;</italic>
###xml 297 306 291 297 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">TGF-&#946;1</italic>
###xml 311 320 302 308 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">TGF-&#946;3</italic>
###xml 463 465 451 453 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="r31">31</xref>
###xml 485 487 473 475 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="r32">32</xref>
###xml 514 523 502 508 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">TGF-&#946;1</italic>
###xml 700 709 685 691 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">TGF-&#946;1</italic>
###xml 733 735 715 717 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="r12">12</xref>
###xml 880 889 862 868 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">TGF-&#946;2</italic>
###xml 894 903 873 879 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">TGF-&#946;3</italic>
###xml 223 228 <span type="species:ncbi:9606">human</span>
###xml 357 362 <span type="species:ncbi:9606">human</span>
###xml 561 567 <span type="species:ncbi:9913">bovine</span>
###xml 634 639 <span type="species:ncbi:9606">human</span>
Li et al. [21] reported that TGF-beta1 transcriptional activity was the highest in all tested parts of the anatomy of the eye. However, they studied the expression of genes encoding isoforms of TGF-beta only in the healthy human cornea. This remains partly consistent with current results showing TGF-beta1 and TGF-beta3 as the predominant isoforms both in human healthy cornea and in affected cornea. Similar results were also demonstrated by Carrington et al. [31] and Tseng et al. [32]. Carrington et al. found TGF-beta1 to be the predominant isoform in the bovine cornea during wound healing. Tseng et al. postulated that healthy human cornea is characterized by high transcriptional activity of TGF-beta1. However, Tuli et al. [12], based on investigations using animal models, revealed that damage of the corneal surface leads to an increase in expression of genes encoding TGF-beta2 and TGF-beta3.
###end p 35
###begin p 36
###xml 104 112 104 109 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">TGF-&#946;</italic>
###xml 179 181 176 178 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="r33">33</xref>
###xml 211 220 208 214 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">TGF-&#946;2</italic>
###xml 368 369 362 363 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="r2">2</xref>
###xml 491 500 485 491 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">TGF-&#946;1</italic>
###xml 505 514 496 502 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">TGF-&#946;2</italic>
###xml 659 668 647 653 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">TGF-&#946;1</italic>
###xml 673 682 658 664 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">TGF-&#946;2</italic>
###xml 692 700 <span type="species:ncbi:9606">patients</span>
Of importance here is that only a fraction of previous studies shows quantitative relationships between TGF-beta isoforms in the course of bullous keratopathy. Saghizadeh et al. [33] evaluated expression of the TGF-beta2 isoform both at the mRNA and protein levels in PBK and normal cornea; however statistically significant differences were not found. Kenney et al. [2] performed similar studies but revealed a significant increase in transcriptional activity of genes encoding isoforms of TGF-beta1 and TGF-beta2 in the course of bullous keratopathy. Their report contradicts our findings, which demonstrated that the differences in mRNA expression of both TGF-beta1 and TGF-beta2 genes in patients with bullous keratopathy compared to the control group were not statistically significant.
###end p 36
###begin p 37
###xml 130 139 127 133 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">TGF-&#946;3</italic>
###xml 221 230 215 221 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">TGF-&#946;3</italic>
###xml 333 335 324 326 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="r34">34</xref>
###xml 407 416 398 404 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">TGF-&#946;3</italic>
###xml 538 540 526 528 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="r35">35</xref>
###xml 826 835 814 820 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">TGF-&#946;3</italic>
###xml 857 866 842 848 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">TGF-&#946;3</italic>
###xml 999 1001 981 983 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="r31">31</xref>
###xml 1345 1347 1321 1323 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="r22">22</xref>
###xml 1096 1104 <span type="species:ncbi:9606">patients</span>
###xml 1190 1198 <span type="species:ncbi:9606">patients</span>
Interestingly, transcriptional activity of TGF-beta3 was reduced in PBK compared to the control group. Data are lacking regarding TGF-beta3 expression in bullous keratopathy. Downregulation of transcriptional activity of TGF-beta3 in the present study may have been caused by the loss of keratinocytes observed in the course of PBK [34]. On the other hand, molecular mechanisms leading to a decrease in the TGF-beta3 mRNA level cannot be ruled out. After cataract surgery epithelial cells undergo epithelial-mesenchymal transition (EMT) [35]. In this process not only the morphology but also the transcriptional program of the epithelial cells is altered. After epithelial-mesenchymal transition cells become capable of expressing components of the extracellular matrix and probably other molecules, which can lead to reduced TGF-beta3 gene expression. The TGF-beta3 isoform is a potential therapeutic agent of corneal repair, especially as it has no harmful effect on corneal re-epithelialization [31]. Thus, early application of TGF-beta3 during or shortly after cataract surgery would prevent patients from such complications as PBK. The question remains about whether such treatment in patients with bullous kerathopathy could restore normal corneal morphology, taking into account the role of TGF-beta3 in tissue remodeling after wounding [22].
###end p 37
###begin p 38
###xml 155 164 155 161 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">TGF-&#946;3</italic>
###xml 255 264 252 258 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">TGF-&#946;3</italic>
Summarizing the results of the present study, all three isoforms were found to be differentially expressed in the course of bullous kerathopathy, but only TGF-beta3 was changed compared to normal cornea. Obtained data suggest that decreased expression of TGF-beta3 may play a significant role in molecular changes observed in bullous keratopathy.
