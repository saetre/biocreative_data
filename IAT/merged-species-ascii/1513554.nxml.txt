The contractile vacuole in Ca2+-regulation in Dictyostelium: its essential function for cAMP-induced Ca2+-influx

###begin title 2
Background
###end title 2
###begin p 3
###xml 15 17 15 17 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">2+</sup>
###xml 28 42 28 42 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">Dictyostelium </italic>
###xml 92 94 92 94 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">2+</sup>
###xml 329 331 329 331 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">2+</sup>
cAMP-induced Ca2+-influx in Dictyostelium is controlled by at least two non-mitochondrial Ca2+-stores: acidic stores and the endoplasmic reticulum (ER). The acidic stores may comprise the contractile vacuole network (CV), the endosomal compartment and acidocalcisomes. Here the role of CV in respect to function as a potential Ca2+-store was investigated.
###end p 3
###begin title 4
Results
###end title 4
###begin p 5
###xml 180 182 180 182 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">2+</sup>
###xml 197 200 197 200 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">2+ </sup>
###xml 333 335 333 335 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">2+</sup>
###xml 352 354 352 354 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">2+</sup>
###xml 496 498 496 498 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">2+</sup>
###xml 576 578 576 578 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">2+</sup>
###xml 610 611 610 611 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">-</sup>
###xml 649 651 649 651 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">2+</sup>
###xml 735 737 735 737 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">2+</sup>
Dajumin-GFP labeled contractile vacuoles were purified 7-fold by anti-GFP-antibodies in a magnetic field. The purified CV were shown for the first time to accumulate and release Ca2+. Release of Ca2+ was elicited by arachidonic acid or the calmodulin antagonist W7, the latter due to inhibition of the pump. The characteristics of Ca2+-transport and Ca2+-release of CV were compared to similarly purified vesicles of the ER labeled by calnexin-GFP. Since the CV proved to be a highly efficient Ca2+-compartment we wanted to know whether or not it takes part in cAMP-induced Ca2+-influx. We made use of the LvsA--mutant expected to display reduced Ca2+-transport due to loss of calmodulin. We found a severe reduction of cAMP-induced Ca2+-influx into whole cells.
###end p 5
###begin title 6
Conclusion
###end title 6
###begin p 7
###xml 28 42 28 42 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">Dictyostelium </italic>
###xml 80 82 80 82 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">2+</sup>
###xml 125 127 125 127 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">2+</sup>
The contractile vacuoles in Dictyostelium represent a highly efficient acidic Ca2+-store that is required for cAMP-induced Ca2+-influx.
###end p 7
###begin title 8
Background
###end title 8
###begin p 9
###xml 40 54 40 54 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">Dictyostelium </italic>
###xml 217 218 217 218 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="B1">1</xref>
###xml 219 220 219 220 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="B2">2</xref>
###xml 273 275 273 275 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">2+</sup>
###xml 318 320 318 320 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">2+</sup>
###xml 348 349 348 349 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="B3">3</xref>
###xml 419 421 419 421 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">2+</sup>
###xml 433 434 433 434 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="B4">4</xref>
###xml 530 532 530 532 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">2+</sup>
###xml 541 542 541 542 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="B5">5</xref>
###xml 605 607 605 607 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">2+</sup>
The contractile vacuole (CV) network of Dictyostelium consists of tubes and bladders. It transiently fuses with the plasma membrane to expel water and ions and thereby serves as an efficient osmoregulatory organelle [1,2]. The CV-system is also assumed to be involved in Ca2+-transport since it contains a PMCA-type Ca2+-ATPase (PAT1), calmodulin [3] and a vacuolar proton pump that establishes a proton gradient for Ca2+-transport [4]. PAT1 is localized to the CV and the plasma membrane and is upregulated under conditions of Ca2+-stress [5]. Downregulation of PAT1 by antisense RNA reduced vesicular Ca2+-uptake.
###end p 9
###begin p 10
###xml 47 49 47 49 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">2+</sup>
###xml 93 95 93 95 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">2+</sup>
###xml 148 150 148 150 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">2+</sup>
###xml 167 168 167 168 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">3</sub>
###xml 217 218 217 218 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="B6">6</xref>
###xml 219 221 219 221 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="B11">11</xref>
###xml 281 282 281 282 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">+</sup>
###xml 310 312 310 312 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">2+</sup>
###xml 323 337 323 337 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">Dictyostelium </italic>
###xml 392 394 392 394 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="B12">12</xref>
###xml 395 397 395 397 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="B13">13</xref>
###xml 486 489 486 489 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">2+ </sup>
We are interested in the characterization of Ca2+-stores that are involved in cAMP-induced Ca2+-influx. Previously, it has been shown that acidic Ca2+-stores and an IP3-sensitive store participate in this regulation [6-11]. Acidic means that the stores are equipped with a V-type H+-ATPase. Acidic vesicular Ca2+-stores in Dictyostelium comprise the CV-system, endosomes and acidocalcisomes [12,13]. In the present study we focus on the contribution of the CV-system to intracellular Ca2+ regulation.
###end p 10
###begin p 11
###xml 134 136 134 136 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="B14">14</xref>
###xml 259 261 259 261 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="B15">15</xref>
###xml 383 385 383 385 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">2+</sup>
###xml 399 401 399 401 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">2+</sup>
###xml 559 563 559 563 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">lvsA</italic>
###xml 606 608 606 608 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="B16">16</xref>
###xml 879 881 879 881 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="B17">17</xref>
###xml 910 912 910 912 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">2+</sup>
###xml 981 983 981 983 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">2+</sup>
###xml 1095 1097 1095 1097 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">2+</sup>
It has been shown previously that GFP-tagged dajumin labels the entire CV whereas the endosomal compartments are devoid of the label [14]. By contrast, drainin, a peripheral membrane protein involved in discharge of the bladder, is found only on the bladder [15]. We used dajumin-GFP expressing cells to obtain a fraction enriched in CV membranes and used this fraction to measure Ca2+-transport. Ca2+-transport activity increased with enhanced purity of the CV. We also employed a LvsA minus strain which lacks the gene for the protein large volume sphereA (lvsA). Besides its involvement in cytokinesis [16] it is known that the LvsA-protein is localized to the CV. This association with the CV occurs only during the discharge phase of the vacuole. In the lvsA mutant calmodulin was lost from the CV-membranes and the CV became disorganized, unable to discharge its contents [17]. We found that vesicular Ca2+-transport in the lvsA-mutant was diminished and that cAMP-induced Ca2+-influx was drastically reduced, indicating that functional CV are absolutely required for the cAMP-dependent Ca2+-influx.
###end p 11
###begin title 12
Results
###end title 12
###begin title 13
Distribution of CV in vesicular fractions
###end title 13
###begin p 14

###xml 1061 1063 1061 1063 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="B18">18</xref>
###xml 1068 1070 1068 1070 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">2+</sup>
###xml 1122 1123 1122 1123 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">0</sub>
###xml 1258 1259 1256 1257 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">0</sub>
###xml 1277 1279 1273 1275 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">1 </sub>
###xml 1304 1305 1300 1301 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">2</sub>
###xml 1498 1501 1494 1497 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">2+ </sup>
###xml 1525 1527 1521 1523 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">2+</sup>
We used differentiated cells 4 to 5 hrs after starvation for preparation of Ca2+-transporting vesicles because cAMP-induced Ca2+-influx is present at that time and endosomal content is low (see below). Cells labeled with dajumin-GFP as a marker for the CV-system or with calnexin-GFP cells as a marker for the endoplasmic reticulum (ER) are shown in Figure 1. Dajumin-GFP allows to visualize the dynamics of the bladder by formation of irregular ventricles and ducts (A). The ER is prominently labeled in the perinuclear region and close to the plasma membrane (B). The cells were lysed by passage through nuclepore filters. Vesicular fractions were separated by differential centrifugation and assayed for Ca2+-transporting activity. The dajumin-GFP label was detected in vesicular fractions with the majority being present in the fast sedimenting fraction P0 (Table 1). By contrast, most of the ER occurred in P1, whereas the lightest fraction P2 contained only a quarter of both organelles (Table 1). Plasma membranes, as shown previously, sedimented in P1 [18]. Ca2+-transport activity was strikingly concentrated in P0. The presence of endosomes was measured with RITC-dextran. In two independent experiments 38 +/- 6% of the label was associated with P0, 62 +/- 6% with P1 and none was present in P2. However, the amount of endosomes of 4 hour starved cells was low and barely detectable. This result is expected since the cells develop in the absence of nutrients. If endosomes accumulate Ca2+ their contribution to Ca2+-transport was therefore considered to be insignificant under the present experimental conditions.
###end p 14

###begin title 17
Purification of CV with magnetic antibody-beads
###end title 17
###begin p 18
###xml 7 9 7 9 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">0 </sub>
###xml 52 54 52 54 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">0 </sub>
###xml 312 313 312 313 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="table" rid="T2">2</xref>
###xml 374 375 374 375 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">0</sub>
###xml 399 401 399 401 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">2+</sup>
###xml 429 430 429 430 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">0</sub>
###xml 456 458 456 458 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">2+</sup>
Since P0 was the richest source of CV we incubated P0 isolated from dajumin-GFP labeled cells with anti-GFP-magnetic beads and loaded the mixture onto a column. During loading and washing the beads were retained on the column by applying a magnetic field. Elution was performed in its absence. As shown in Table 2 almost all of the dajumin-label was present in the eluate (P0E) as well as most of Ca2+-uptake. The flow through (P0F) still contained some Ca2+-transport activity the percentage of which, however, exceeded only slightly the remaining dajumin-GFP-label.

###begin p 20
###xml 74 76 74 76 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">0 </sub>
###xml 272 273 269 270 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">0</sub>
###xml 346 347 340 341 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">0</sub>
The selectivity of the GFP-antibody beads for GFP was tested by assaying P0 from unlabeled Ax2-cells, the parent strain of dajumin-GFP expressing cells, for binding to the anti-GFP-beads. In three independent experiments 54 +/- 31 mug (n = 6) protein was associated with P0E in the controls whereas 192 +/- 60 mug (n = 6) protein was present in P0E from dajumin-GFP labeled cells. This result shows that the GFP-antibody specifically targets the GFP-tagged protein and that 28% of unspecific binding does occur.
###end p 20
###begin p 21
###xml 36 38 36 38 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">0 </sub>
###xml 220 221 220 221 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="table" rid="T2">2</xref>
###xml 352 354 352 354 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="B19">19</xref>
###xml 511 513 511 513 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">2+</sup>
###xml 562 564 562 564 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">2+</sup>
###xml 577 578 577 578 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">0</sub>
###xml 721 722 721 722 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">0</sub>
###xml 832 834 832 834 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">2+</sup>
In order to avoid contamination of P0 and subsequent fractions with plasma membranes due to possible association of plasma membranes and CV by cortical actin filaments we preincubated the cells (all experiments of Table 2) with latrunculin B. Latrunculin B, a unique marine toxin, inhibits actin polymerization and disrupts microfilament organization [19]. In the presence of latrunculin B the cells rounded up and were completely immotile before they were pressed through the filters. The characteristics of Ca2+-transport, its inhibition by drugs as well as Ca2+-release in P0E were in the same range as in preparations performed without latrunculin B treatment. Latrunculin B treatment reduced the protein content of P0E by a factor of 1.26 but there was no statistically significant difference in the specific activity of the Ca2+-ATPase.
###end p 21
###begin p 22
###xml 79 80 79 80 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">+</sup>
###xml 181 182 181 182 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">0</sub>

A specific marker for the CV as compared to the plasma membrane is the V-type H+-ATPase. Antibodies to the 37/42 kDa subunit revealed that the proton pump was markedly enriched in P0E (Fig. 2). A mitochondrial contribution to Ca2+-fluxes was prevented by inclusion of mitochondrial inhibitors in all vesicular Ca2+-transport measurements.

###begin title 24
Purification of ER with magnetic antibody-beads
###end title 24
###begin p 25
###xml 7 9 7 9 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">1 </sub>
###xml 45 46 45 46 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="table" rid="T1">1</xref>

###xml 284 285 284 285 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">1</sub>
###xml 309 311 309 311 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">2+</sup>

Since P1 was the richest source of ER (Table 1) we incubated P1 isolated from calnexin-GFP labeled cells with anti-GFP-magnetic beads and separated ER-vesicles from other vesicles by a magnetic field. As shown in Table 2 almost all of the calnexin-GFP-label was found in the eluate (P1E) as well as most of Ca2+-uptake activity. The flow through (P1F) also contained some Ca2+-transport activity.
###end p 25
###begin p 26
###xml 21 22 21 22 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="table" rid="T2">2</xref>
###xml 236 238 236 238 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">2+</sup>
###xml 302 316 302 316 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">Dictyostelium </italic>
###xml 336 338 336 338 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">2+</sup>
The results of Table 2 thus show that by using the anti-GFP-antibody beads a 6-7-fold enrichment of either CV or ER can be obtained. Furthermore, both fractions enriched either in CV or ER vesicles, displayed a similar enhancement of Ca2+-transport. This result demonstrates that vesicles of the CV of Dictyostelium are functional in Ca2+-homeostasis.

###begin title 34
###xml 37 39 37 39 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">2+</sup>
Is the CV involved in cAMP-induced Ca2+-influx?
###end title 34
###begin p 35
###xml 37 39 37 39 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">2+</sup>

Given our results that the CV is a Ca2+-store that transports Ca2+ most efficiently at low Ca2+-concentrations we wanted to know whether the store is also involved in receptor-mediated Ca2+-flux. Although it was previously shown that NBD-Cl and CMA inhibited the cAMP mediated Ca2+-influx [7,22] these results only proved that acidic stores as a whole were involved. We made use of the LvsA--mutant whose CV-function is abolished with respect to fusion of CV with the plasma membrane and is disturbed by a loss of calmodulin. Previously, we have shown that the calmodulin antagonist W7 inhibits Ca2+-transport of acidic Ca2+-stores [24]. A localized loss of calmodulin confined to the CV would have the same consequences. Therefore, it was interesting to know whether or not Ca2+-influx was also disturbed in the mutant. As shown in Table 4 there was no receptor-mediated Ca2+-influx in the LvsA minus strain at physiological cAMP-concentrations (0.1-10 muM) in contrast to the parent strain DH1. Only higher concentrations of cAMP yielded Ca2+-influx that was reduced as compared to DH1 (Table 4).

###begin title 40
Discussion
###end title 40
###begin p 41
###xml 67 80 67 80 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">Dictyostelium</italic>
###xml 128 130 128 130 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">2+</sup>
###xml 215 217 215 217 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">2+</sup>
###xml 241 243 241 243 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">2+</sup>
###xml 315 317 315 317 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">2+</sup>
###xml 482 484 482 484 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">2+</sup>
###xml 546 548 546 548 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">2+</sup>
###xml 581 583 581 583 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">2+</sup>
###xml 630 631 630 631 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">0</sub>
###xml 682 684 682 684 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">2+</sup>
###xml 741 744 741 744 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">2+ </sup>
###xml 775 778 775 778 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">2+ </sup>
###xml 920 922 920 922 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">2+</sup>
###xml 931 933 931 933 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="B23">23</xref>
###xml 934 936 934 936 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="B24">24</xref>
###xml 954 956 954 956 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">2+</sup>
###xml 1010 1011 1010 1011 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">+</sup>
###xml 1075 1076 1075 1076 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">0</sub>
###xml 1171 1173 1171 1173 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">2+</sup>
###xml 1309 1311 1309 1311 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">2+</sup>
###xml 1338 1340 1338 1340 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">2+</sup>
In this study we have shown that one of the acidic compartments of Dictyostelium, the CV-system, is required for cAMP-induced Ca2+-influx in intact cells and that isolated CV-vesicles mediate an essential part of Ca2+-transport as well as Ca2+-release in response to AA. Previously, it was only known that acidic Ca2+-stores were involved. The use of dajumin-GFP-labeled CV allowed a 7-fold enrichment of CV by a specific method utilizing antibodies directed against the GFP-tag. Ca2+-transport was enhanced 5- to 6-fold. This result is due to Ca2+-transport activity of another Ca2+-store that was retained in the flow through (P0F). Enriched CV displayed a potent and efficient Ca2+-transport that resulted in a low basal extravesicular Ca2+ concentration. Moreover, the Ca2+ taken up could be released by the calmodulin antagonist W-7 and by AA. Both compounds have been shown to be potent regulators of the acidic Ca2+-stores [23,24]. In addition, Ca2+-uptake was sensitive to CMA, an inhibitor of V-type H+-ATPases. Moreover, the V-type proton pump was concentrated in P0E. A similar enrichment was also obtained for the ER using calnexin-GFP-labeled cells. These Ca2+-stores differed from those of the CV by a greater sensitivity to BHQ, a lower responsiveness to AA and a higher basal extravesicular Ca2+-concentration following Ca2+-uptake.
###end p 41
###begin p 42
###xml 34 36 34 36 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">2+</sup>
###xml 99 101 99 101 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">2+</sup>
###xml 490 492 490 492 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">2+</sup>
###xml 628 630 626 628 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">2+</sup>
###xml 812 814 809 811 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">2+</sup>
###xml 825 839 822 836 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">Dictyostelium </italic>
###xml 948 949 945 946 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="B7">7</xref>
###xml 990 992 987 989 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">2+</sup>
###xml 1036 1038 1033 1035 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">2+</sup>
###xml 1123 1125 1120 1122 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">2+</sup>
In order to examine whether the Ca2+-transport occurring in CV is also involved in cAMP-mediated Ca2+-influx we made use of a mutant, LvsA minus. In this mutant the CV becomes disorganized during the discharge phase of the vacuole and calmodulin dissociates from the CV-membranes. The CV can swell but seems unable to discharge. The LvsA-protein that belongs to the family of BEACH-proteins transiently binds to the CV. We found that the mutant was strongly impaired in receptor-mediated Ca2+-influx when physiological cAMP-concentrations of 0.1-1 muM and even higher concentrations of 10 muM cAMP were applied. A significant Ca2+-influx was apparent only at elevated cAMP concentrations of 20 to 50 muM. Even so, influx was inhibited by about 75% as compared to the parent strain. Other compounds that induce Ca2+-influx in Dictyostelium are long chain fatty acids like AA. They act independently of the cAMP-receptor and of cell differentiation [7]. Similarly to cAMP, AA did not cause Ca2+-influx in the LvsA-minus-strain, instead Ca2+-efflux occurred. These results demonstrate that in order to activate capacitative Ca2+-influx an intact CV-system is absolutely necessary.
###end p 42
###begin title 43
###xml 42 44 42 44 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">2+</sup>
What is the role of the LvsA-protein in Ca2+-influx?
###end title 43
###begin p 44
###xml 36 50 36 50 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">Dictyostelium </italic>
###xml 226 228 226 228 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="B25">25</xref>
###xml 315 317 315 317 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="B26">26</xref>
###xml 418 420 418 420 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">2+</sup>
###xml 435 448 435 448 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">Dictyostelium</italic>
###xml 474 488 474 488 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">Dictyostelium </italic>
###xml 524 526 524 526 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">2+</sup>
###xml 527 529 527 529 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">i </sub>
###xml 553 555 553 555 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">2+</sup>
###xml 556 557 556 557 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">i</sub>
###xml 570 572 570 572 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="B27">27</xref>
The function of the LvsA-protein in Dictyostelium is not yet known. BEACH-proteins belong to several classes and only a few proteins have been characterized. The FAN-protein mediates TNF-activation via the MAP-kinase pathway [25]. Neurobeachin conveys anchoring of the regulatory subunit of protein kinase A (PKA) [26]. In both cases, the BEACH-protein participates in signal transduction that could lead to altered Ca2+-regulation in Dictyostelium. Elevated cAMP-levels in Dictyostelium were shown to affect both, basal [Ca2+]i and stimulus-induced [Ca2+]i-transients [27].
###end p 44
###begin p 45
###xml 192 194 192 194 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">2+</sup>
###xml 217 219 217 219 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">2+</sup>
###xml 228 230 228 230 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="B24">24</xref>
###xml 251 253 251 253 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">2+</sup>
###xml 386 388 386 388 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">2+</sup>
###xml 416 417 416 417 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">-</sup>
Apart from this, it was shown that the loss of the LvsA-protein was accompanied by a loss of calmodulin from the CV-membranes. Since we have shown that the calmodulin antagonist W7 inhibits Ca2+-transport of acidic Ca2+-stores [24] we conclude that Ca2+-uptake by the CV is inhibited in the absence of calmodulin. This could be an explanation of the severe reduction of cAMP-mediated Ca2+-influx measured in the LvsA--mutant strain.
###end p 45
###begin title 46
Conclusion
###end title 46
###begin p 47
###xml 19 21 19 21 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">2+</sup>
###xml 79 82 79 82 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">2+ </sup>
###xml 120 123 120 123 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">2+ </sup>
###xml 147 150 147 150 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">2+ </sup>
###xml 180 183 180 183 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">2+ </sup>
###xml 287 290 287 290 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">2+ </sup>
Among the acidic Ca2+-stores the contractile vacuole represents an efficient Ca2+ transport organelle that sequesters Ca2+ to low extravesicular Ca2+ concentrations and releases Ca2+ in response to arachidonic acid. Furthermore, it is essential for the occurrence of receptor-mediated Ca2+ influx to physiological concentrations of cAMP.
###end p 47

###begin title 51
Cells and culture
###end title 51
###begin p 52
###xml 75 77 75 77 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="B28">28</xref>
###xml 97 99 97 99 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="B14">14</xref>
###xml 119 121 119 121 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="B29">29</xref>
###xml 201 203 200 202 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="B16">16</xref>
###xml 433 434 429 430 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">2</sub>
###xml 437 438 433 434 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">2</sub>
###xml 443 444 439 440 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">4</sub>
###xml 524 526 514 516 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">7 </sup>
The strains were grown in liquid culture as discribed by Sonnemann et al. [28]. The dajumin-GFP [14] and calnexin-GFP [29] strains were raised in the presence of 50 mug/ml G418. The LvsA-minus-strain [16] was grown with 100 mug/ml ampicillin and 60 mug/ml streptomycin and its parent strain DH1 additionally with 20 mug/ml uracil. Cells were shaken on a rotary shaker at 23degreesC, 150 rpm at 2 x 107 cells/ml until use.
###end p 52
###begin title 53
Preparation of vesicles
###end title 53
###begin p 54
###xml 12 13 12 13 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">7</sup>
###xml 168 170 168 170 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">8 </sup>
###xml 273 274 273 274 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">2</sub>
###xml 551 552 549 550 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">0</sub>
###xml 637 638 635 636 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">2</sub>
###xml 770 771 766 767 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">1</sub>
###xml 958 959 954 955 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">2</sub>
20 ml 2 x 107cells/ml were shaken in Sorensen phosphate buffer containing 5 mM EGTA for 2-4 h, washed once in ice-cold 20 mM Hepes buffer, pH 7.2, resuspended at 2 x 108 cells/ml, and lysed by passage through nuclepore filters. Immediately, 3% sucrose, 50 mM KCl, 1 mM MgCl2, 10 mug/ml leupeptin, 1 mug/ml aprotinin and 1.25 mM dithiothreitol were added (final concentration). After centrifugation for 5 min at 150 x g in order to remove unbroken cells, the supernatant was further fractionated by centrifugation for 5 min at 3800 x g. The sediment (P0) was resuspended in 1 ml 10 mM Tris buffer, pH 7.8, 3% sucrose, 50 mM KCl, 1 mM MgCl2, 20 mug/ml leupeptin and 2 mug/ml aprotinin (TKS-buffer). The supernatant was centrifuged at 12.000 x g for 20 min. The sediment (P1) was resuspended in 0.9 ml TKS-buffer. The supernatant was further centrifugated at 40.000 x g for 30 min. The fluffy sediment was resuspended in about 0.6 ml TKS-buffer and designated P2.
###end p 54
###begin title 55
Purification of vesicles with anti-GFP-magnetic microbeads
###end title 55
###begin p 56
###xml 9 11 8 10 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">0 </sub>
###xml 449 450 437 438 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">0</sub>
###xml 1127 1128 1110 1111 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">0</sub>
400 mul P0 suspended in 10 mM Tris-buffer, pH 7.8, 50 mM KCl, 20 mug/ml leupeptin and 2 mug/ml aprotinin (TK-buffer) was incubated for 30 min at 4degreesC with 100 mul anti-GFP-microbeads and then transferred to a Mini MACS-separation column exposed to a magnetic field. The column had been rinsed with 500 mul TK-buffer. The flow through was centrifuged for 5 min at 3.800 x g. The sediment was resuspended in 150 mul of TKS-buffer and designated P0F. In the meantime the column was washed with 500 mul TK-buffer, 500 mul TK-buffer containing 0.1 M NaCl and 500 mul TK-buffer containing 0.3 M NaCl. In case the column was running inefficiently the contents were transferred to a new column by pressing 500 mul TK-buffer through the old column in the absence of a magnetic field onto the new column positioned in the field. The final elution was performed in the same way after the column had been washed free of salt with TK-buffer. The washing solutions usually contained negligible amounts of the GFP-label. The purified eluate was centrifuged for 5 min at 3.800 x g, resuspended in about 350 mul TKS-buffer and designated P0E.
###end p 56
###begin p 57
###xml 246 248 245 247 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">0 </sub>
In most experiments the cells were treated with 8 muM latrunculin B for 20 min before lysis to disrupt F-actin filaments. Under this condition the cells rounded up and became immotile. Nonetheless, the dajumin-GFP-label remained associated with P0 and purification did not change significantly.

###begin title 58
###xml 2 4 2 4 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">2+</sup>

###end title 58
###begin p 59
###xml 2 4 2 4 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">2+</sup>
###xml 54 56 54 56 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="B23">23</xref>
###xml 204 205 200 201 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">3</sub>

###xml 273 275 268 270 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">2+</sup>
###xml 473 475 467 469 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">2+</sup>
###xml 476 479 470 473 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">ev </sub>
###xml 533 535 527 529 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">2+</sup>
###xml 584 586 578 580 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">2+</sup>
###xml 629 630 623 624 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="table" rid="T2">2</xref>
###xml 670 672 664 666 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">2+</sup>
###xml 673 676 667 670 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">ev </sub>
###xml 806 808 800 802 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">2+</sup>
###xml 809 812 803 806 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">ev </sub>
###xml 834 836 828 830 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">0 </sub>
###xml 840 841 834 835 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">1</sub>
###xml 898 900 892 894 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">2+</sup>
###xml 901 904 895 898 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">ev </sub>
###xml 920 922 913 915 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">2+</sup>
###xml 951 953 944 946 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">0 </sub>
###xml 958 959 951 952 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">1</sub>
###xml 1023 1025 1014 1016 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">2+</sup>
###xml 1026 1028 1017 1019 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">ev</sub>
Ca2+-transport was measured essentially as described [23]. In brief, about 60 mul fraction was added to 10 mM Hepes, pH 7.2, 50 mM KCl, 3% sucrose, 6 mug/ml antimycin A, 6 mug/ml oligomycin A, 100 muM NaN3, 2 mM MgCl2, and about 0.5 muM Fluo-3 in a total volume of 1 ml. Ca2+-uptake was initiated by addition of 400 muM ATP. Fluo-3 fluorescence was monitored at 505 nm excitation and 526 nm emission with a fluorimeter (Perkin Elmer 650-10S, Uberlingen, Germany). Since [Ca2+]ev at the beginning of the experiment is not known and Ca2+-ATPase activity is increasing with increasing Ca2+-concentrations the results shown in Table 2 were corrected for the same initial [Ca2+]ev in order to relate the outcome of different experiments to each other. This was done by using a calibration curve at different [Ca2+]ev obtained with either P0 or P1, respectively, and adjusting the measured value to a [Ca2+]ev of 1 muM. The Ca2+-ATPase activities of both, P0 and P1, increased by a factor of about 3.3 from 0.5 muM to 1.5 muM [Ca2+]ev. Statistical data are expressed throughout as the mean +/- S.D.
