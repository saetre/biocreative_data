###begin article-title 0
The CXCL12gamma Chemokine Displays Unprecedented Structural and Functional Properties that Make It a Paradigm of Chemoattractant Proteins
###end article-title 0

###begin p 3
###xml 62 68 58 64 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">Cxcl12</italic>
###xml 1107 1108 1083 1084 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">d</sub>
###xml 1462 1469 1434 1441 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">in vivo</italic>
###xml 1699 1706 1663 1670 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">in vivo</italic>
###xml 714 719 <span type="species:ncbi:10090">mouse</span>
###xml 724 729 <span type="species:ncbi:9606">human</span>
The CXCL12gamma chemokine arises by alternative splicing from Cxcl12, an essential gene during development. This protein binds CXCR4 and displays an exceptional degree of conservation (99%) in mammals. CXCL12gamma is formed by a protein core shared by all CXCL12 isoforms, extended by a highly cationic carboxy-terminal (C-ter) domain that encompass four overlapped BBXB heparan sulfate (HS)-binding motifs. We hypothesize that this unusual domain could critically determine the biological properties of CXCL12gamma through its interaction to, and regulation by extracellular glycosaminoglycans (GAG) and HS in particular. By both RT-PCR and immunohistochemistry, we mapped the localization of CXCL12gamma both in mouse and human tissues, where it showed discrete differential expression. As an unprecedented feature among chemokines, the secreted CXCL12gamma strongly interacted with cell membrane GAG, thus remaining mostly adsorbed on the plasmatic membrane upon secretion. Affinity chromatography and surface plasmon resonance allowed us to determine for CXCL12gamma one of the higher affinity for HS (Kd = 0.9 nM) ever reported for a protein. This property relies in the presence of four canonical HS-binding sites located at the C-ter domain but requires the collaboration of a HS-binding site located in the core of the protein. Interestingly, and despite reduced agonist potency on CXCR4, the sustained binding of CXCL12gamma to HS enabled it to promote in vivo intraperitoneal leukocyte accumulation and angiogenesis in matrigel plugs with much higher efficiency than CXCL12alpha. In good agreement, mutant CXCL12gamma chemokines selectively devoid of HS-binding capacity failed to promote in vivo significant cell recruitment. We conclude that CXCL12gamma features unique structural and functional properties among chemokines which rely on the presence of a distinctive C-ter domain. The unsurpassed capacity to bind to HS on the extracellular matrix would make CXCL12gamma the paradigm of haptotactic proteins, which regulate essential homeostatic functions by promoting directional migration and selective tissue homing of cells.

###xml 1149 1153 1145 1149 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0002543-Aiuti1">[16]</xref>
###xml 1243 1247 1239 1243 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0002543-Nanki1">[17]</xref>
###xml 1280 1284 1276 1280 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0002543-Gallagher1">[18]</xref>
###xml 1286 1290 1282 1286 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0002543-Ceradini1">[19]</xref>
###xml 1402 1406 1398 1402 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0002543-Burger1">[20]</xref>
###xml 274 279 <span type="species:ncbi:10090">Mouse</span>
###xml 284 289 <span type="species:ncbi:9606">human</span>
The CXC chemokine, stromal cell-derived factor 1/CXCL12 [1] is a constitutive and broadly expressed chemokine that exerts its functions through the G-protein coupled receptor (GPCR) CXCR4 [2]. Recently, a novel receptor for CXCL12, RDC-1/CXCR7, has been identified [3]-[5]. Mouse and human CXCL12alpha, the major CXCL12 isoform, differ by a single, homologous substitution (Val18 to Ile18) [1], [6] and each protein owns the capacity to bind and activate the orthologue CXCR4 receptor. The exceptional conservation of both CXCR4 and CXCL12 structure and function in mammalians announces the essential roles played by this singular couple. CXCL12 is unique among the family of chemokines as it plays non-redundant roles during embryo life in the development of both cardiovascular [7] and central nervous system [8], [9], hematopoiesis [10] and colonization of the gonads by primordial germ cells [11]. In the post-natal life, CXCL12 is involved in trans-endothelial migration of leukocytes [12]-[15] and regulates critically both the homing and egress of CD34+CXCR4+progenitor cells from the bone marrow, and their migration into peripheral tissues [16]. CXCL12 also plays a prominent role in physiopathological processes such as inflammation [17], angiogenesis and wound healing [18], [19]. Moreover, CXCL12 is a critical factor for growth, survival and metastatic dissemination of a number of tumors [20].

###begin p 6
###xml 258 265 247 254 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">In vivo</italic>
###xml 727 734 716 723 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">in vivo</italic>
###xml 796 800 785 789 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0002543-Esko1">[21]</xref>
###xml 801 805 790 794 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0002543-Sweeney1">[24]</xref>
The engagement of CXCR4 by CXCL12 triggers the activation of heterotrimeric Galphabetagamma-proteins, which ultimately promote the directional migration of cells towards a concentration gradient of ligand that defines the haptotactic function of chemokines. In vivo, chemokines are believed to form gradient concentrations by binding to glycosaminoglycans (GAG), the glycanic moieties of proteoglycans, and in particular to heparan sulfate (HS). Electrostatic contacts between the negatively charged HS and basic residues exposed at the surface of chemokines, along with structural features of the oligosaccharide, determine both the affinity and the specificity of the molecular interactions that are supposed to modulate the in vivo biological activity of chemokines complexed to proteoglycans [21]-[24].
###end p 6
###begin p 7
###xml 322 326 315 319 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0002543-ValenzuelaFernandez1">[25]</xref>
###xml 799 802 780 783 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0002543-Shirozu1">[6]</xref>

###xml 913 917 890 894 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0002543-Laguri1">[28]</xref>
###xml 989 996 966 973 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">in vivo</italic>
###xml 1234 1242 1207 1215 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">in vitro</italic>
###xml 1315 1323 1284 1292 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">in vitro</italic>
###xml 1406 1413 1375 1382 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">in vivo</italic>
###xml 1534 1541 1499 1506 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">in vivo</italic>
###xml 770 775 <span type="species:ncbi:9606">human</span>
###xml 777 780 <span type="species:ncbi:10116">rat</span>
###xml 785 790 <span type="species:ncbi:10090">mouse</span>
The study of the well characterized CXCL12alpha isoform provided most of the knowledge of CXCL12 biological properties including interaction with GAG, which is essentially accounted for by a canonical BBXB (B for basic amino-acids, X any other amino-acid) HS-binding motif, located in the first beta-strand of the protein [25]. In contrast, the novel CXCL12gamma isoform remains largely unexplored regarding protein expression and biological function. CXCL12gamma is formed by a core domain encompassing the 68 amino-acids of the major CXCL12alpha isoform shared with all CXCL12 proteins, which is extended by a carboxy-terminal (C-ter) domain. This region, highly-enriched in basic amino-acids, encodes four overlapped HS-binding motifs and shows identical sequence in human, rat and mouse species [6], [26], [27]. This positively charged domain enables CXCL12gamma with an amazing capacity to interact with GAG [28]. We speculated that this property might be determinant in defining the in vivo capacity of this peculiar chemokine to promote both migration and homing of cells in tissues. In this work we characterized CXCL12gamma tissue expression and the capacity of this isoform to interact with CXCR4 and promote cell migration in vitro. Moreover, we investigated the interaction of CXCL12gamma with GAG both in vitro and on intact cells. Finally, we assessed the functionality of this novel isoform in vivo. Our findings indicate that CXCL12gamma displays a sustained binding on GAG and exhibits a prolonged chemokine activity in vivo that makes it a paradigm among haptotactic proteins. The intacteness of the BBXB sites in the distinctive CXCL12gamma C-ter domain critically determine the biological activity of the chemokine.

The Cxcl12gamma isoform cDNA was obtained from BALB/c mouse brain mRNA. The isolated cDNA nucleotide sequence was identical to the previously reported murine Cxcl12gamma isoform (GenBank NCBI accession number NM_001012477) that encodes the CXCL12gamma protein (thereafter called gamma-wt for the recombinant and chemically synthesized proteins, GenPept NCBI accession number NP_001012495). The expression of the gamma-wt mRNA and protein in embryo and adult mouse tissues and in human adult tissues was investigated by RT-PCR and immunohistochemistry (Figure 1C-E) using a novel monoclonal antibody (mAb) (6E9) that recognizes selectively a gamma-wt C-ter epitope encompassing the sequence K78/K80 (Figure 1A-B). The gamma-wt protein expression was compared to these of other isoforms detected by the well characterized K15C mAb, which recognizes an amino-terminal (N-ter) -encoded epitope shared by all the CXCL12 isoforms [29], [30].

Recognition of CXCL12 proteins by the K15C mAb is not masked by their interaction with GAG [23]. ###xml 1558 1567 1490 1499 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="supplementary-material" rid="pone.0002543.s002">Figure S2</xref>
###xml 1628 1630 1552 1554 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">50</sub>
The pharmacological properties of gamma-wt regarding its interaction with CXCR4 were investigated on transformed A3.01 T cells and primary unstimulated CD4+ T lymphocytes (Figure 5). Both lymphoid cell types lack detectable levels of HS as assessed by immunostaining with the specific 10E4 anti-HS mAb (data not shown) and permits the strict analysis of CXCL12/CXCR4 interaction per se. The capacity of gamma-wt to set in motion CXCR4-dependent activation cell pathways was first assessed by measuring the amount of the non-hydrolysable [S35]-GTPgamma associated to activated Galpha subunits, the earliest cell-signal event induced by GPCR agonists. We observed that gamma-wt was less potent than alpha-wt to activate CXCR4 (Figure 5A), which is in full agreement with the reduced binding affinity (one order of magnitude) shown by gamma-wt for CXCR4 as compared to alpha-wt [28]. When concentration of gamma-wt was raised and the occupancy of CXCR4 was enhanced, G protein activation increased to levels comparable to those measured with alpha-wt. However, presumably due to the sustained reduced potency of gamma-wt to induce GTPgammaS binding, we were unable ro reach saturation for gamma-wt, thus precluding determination of Emax and EC50 values for this chemokine in ths assay. For alpha-wt isoform, we deduced an EC50 value equal to 19.65 nM. CXCL12alpha has been shown to bind to- and activates CXCR7 [3], [4], [33]. We thus analysed the ability of gamma-wt to compete with a C-ter biotinylated CXCL12alpha chemokine for binding to CXCR7. As shown in Figure S2, alpha-wt and gamma-wt isoforms similarly bound to CXCR7 (IC50 = 6.56 nM and 10.37 nM for alpha-wt and gamma-wt, respectively).

We next investigated the capacity of gamma-wt to promote CXCR4-mediated lymphocyte migration, the hallmark of chemokine-promoted responses (Figure 5B). ###begin p 32
###xml 245 249 225 229 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0002543-Amara1">[31]</xref>
###xml 807 811 771 775 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0002543-Jones1">[34]</xref>
###xml 813 822 777 786 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="supplementary-material" rid="pone.0002543.s004">Figure S4</xref>
###xml 681 686 <span type="species:ncbi:9606">human</span>
Invalidation of the C-ter BBXB motifs in gamma-wt (gamma-m1 and gamma-m2) restored the potency of the chemokine to the levels showed by alpha-wt and alpha-m, two chemokines that have been previously shown to bind to and activate CXCR4 similarly [31]. Comparable results were found regarding GTPgammaS binding, as the gamma-m1 mutant proved to be consistently a better agonist than gamma-wt, displaying a similar potency than this observed for alpha-wt (data not shown). Additionally, these findings indicated that the mutations introduced in the C-ter domain did not affect the overall structure of the chemokine. The biological relevance of these results was further confirmed in human blood leukocytes activated with phytohemagglutinin and IL-2, a process known to enhance GAG expression on primary cells [34] (Figure S4).

###begin title 33
In vivo biological activity of gamma-wt
###end title 33
###begin p 34
###xml 182 189 174 181 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">in vivo</italic>
###xml 279 287 271 279 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">in vitro</italic>
###xml 499 508 483 492 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="fig" rid="pone-0002543-g006">Figure 6A</xref>
###xml 520 529 504 513 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="fig" rid="pone-0002543-g006">Figure 6B</xref>
###xml 390 394 <span type="species:ncbi:10090">mice</span>
The singular structural and functional features that distinguish gamma-wt from alpha-wt prompted us to compare their respective capacities to promote haptotactic attraction of cells in vivo using chemokine concentrations in the range of these that consistently induce chemotaxis in vitro. To this purpose, we first evaluated the migration of leukocytes into the peritoneal cavity of BALB/c mice following administration of an endotoxin-free, 30 nM solution, of gamma-wt or alpha-wt at 6 hours (hr) (Figure 6A) or 15 hr (Figure 6B) post-injection. After 6 hr of treatment, both alpha-wt and gamma-wt induced a significant and equivalent increase of the absolute number of cells (fold increase 2.99+/-0.18 and 3+/-0.18, for alpha-wt and gamma-wt respectively, as compared to control PBS injected animals), that was accounted for by the recruitment of myeloid cells, including both neutrophils and macrophages (Gr-1+CD11b+CD19-, 6.27+/-1.45 for alpha-wt and 8.72+/-4.95 for gamma-wt). The situation was radically different at 15 hr post-injection as solely gamma-wt promoted a sustained accumulation of leukocytes (fold increase 4.96+/-1.35 as compared to PBS-injected animals). At this time point, cell increase was basically accounted for by T lymphocytes (CD3+, 4.38+/-1.65) and B lymphocytes corresponding to B1 (CD19+CD11b+, 5.09+/-2.16) and B2 (CD19+CD11b-, 3.9+/-1) subpopulations. Importantly, both alpha-m and gamma-m2, that totally lack HS-binding activity, failed to attract leukocytes either at 6 hr or 15 hr time points.

###xml 1198 1202 1166 1170 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0002543-Passaniti1">[36]</xref>

CXCL12alpha has the capacity to promote de novo formation of vessels, a property related to the ability of this chemokine to regulate both the traffic and survival of stem and progenitor cells [19], [35]. Of note, both alpha-m and gamma-m2 display a reduced capacity to promote cell infiltration and angiogenesis in Matrigel implants, demonstrating the importance of GAG binding for this process.

###begin title 40
Discussion
###end title 40
###begin p 41
###xml 0 11 0 7 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">Cxcl12&#947;</italic>
###xml 272 276 265 269 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0002543-Gleichmann1">[26]</xref>
###xml 278 289 271 278 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">Cxcl12&#947;</italic>
###xml 373 377 362 366 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0002543-Segret1">[37]</xref>
###xml 416 420 405 409 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0002543-Stumm1">[38]</xref>
###xml 469 473 458 462 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0002543-Yu1">[27]</xref>
###xml 605 616 590 597 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">Cxcl12&#947;</italic>
###xml 84 88 <span type="species:ncbi:10116">rats</span>
###xml 363 366 <span type="species:ncbi:10116">rat</span>
###xml 411 415 <span type="species:ncbi:10090">mice</span>
###xml 449 454 <span type="species:ncbi:9606">human</span>
Cxcl12gamma mRNA has been primarily detected in the central nervous system of adult rats, where this isoform is supposed to undergo inverse regulation as compared to the beta isoform both during brain development and in pathophysiological events like sciatic nerve lesion [26]. Cxcl12gamma mRNA is also differentially expressed in normal and myocardial infarcted rat heart [37], in normal and ischemic brain of mice [38], and is broadly detected in human adult tissues [27]. Here, we have characterized for the first time the expression of CXCL12gamma at the protein level. The apparent exclusion of both Cxcl12gamma mRNA and protein from discrete places suggests that the expression of this isoform is tightly regulated by a RNA-splicing regulatory mechanism. Remarkably, CXCL12gamma seems to be expressed in anatomical sites, such as small vessels and lower respiratory tract, where it could be involved in the diapedesis of inflammatory leukocytes and other cells from hematopoietic origin. In embryo, its enhanced capacity to form haptotactic gradients could be critical for guiding discrete cell precursors into their final localization during organogenesis.
###end p 41
###begin p 42
###xml 428 432 424 428 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0002543-Laguri1">[28]</xref>
###xml 732 735 724 727 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">off</sub>
###xml 804 806 792 794 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">&#8722;1</sup>
###xml 808 810 796 798 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">&#8722;1</sup>
###xml 884 887 868 871 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">off</sub>
###xml 895 897 879 881 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">&#8722;1</sup>
###xml 899 901 883 885 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">&#8722;1</sup>
###xml 902 906 886 890 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0002543-Laguri1">[28]</xref>
The tight array of BBXB motifs in the CXCL12gamma C-ter domain, that distinguish this protein from other CXCL12 isoforms, is unprecedented among HS-binding proteins. The C-ter domain has on its own a marked affinity for heparin that decreases dramatically when HS-binding motifs are mutated. This observation is in keeping with our results issued from a Nuclear Magnetic Resonance analysis of the soluble form of this chemokine [28], which revealed that the C-ter peptide is unfolded and could offer an accessible, highly cationic surface for the molecular recognition in the interaction with GAG. Our interpretation of SPR findings is that the high affinity for the oligosaccharide displayed by gamma-wt largely relies in the low koff of the HP/gamma-wt complexes which has been estimated to be 0.0019 M-1 s-1, contrasting with the rapid dissociation from HP observed for alpha-wt (koff 0.111 M-1 s-1)[28]. This is well exemplified by the SPR profile obtained with the mutant gamma-m1. This mutant dissociates more rapidly from HP and shows a marked, reduced interaction with HP as compared to the wild type counterpart. However, it retains a substantial affinity for HP that might result from the stabilization of the complex through the collaboration between the conserved BBXB motif in the core of the chemokine and the remaining positive charges in the yet highly cationic C-ter domain. Collectively, these data underline the important contribution of the C-ter BBXB motifs to the formation of high-affinity and stable HP/gamma-wt complexes.
###end p 42
###begin p 43
###xml 191 199 187 195 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">in vitro</italic>
###xml 771 775 751 755 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0002543-Nickel1">[39]</xref>
The chemokine-binding experiments carried out in intact cells proved the specificity and high affinity of gamma-wt for cellular HS structures, thus validating the biological relevance of the in vitro analysis. The astounding strong interaction of gamma-wt with cell GAG was also observed in an alternative assay. Indeed, our findings prove that gamma-wt is massively adsorbed at the cell surface following secretion. The simplest explanation for this phenomenon is that the secreted gamma-wt could be rapidly trapped on cell-surface HS structures. Alternatively, the high affinity of gamma-wt for HS might result in the formation of an intracellular complex before being expressed at the cell surface, a phenomenon previously described for the Fibroblast Growth Factor-2 [39]. On view of the low dissociation rate of HS-gamma-wt complexes, it can be speculated that the secreted, free form of the chemokine hardly would reach the equilibrium of interaction with immobilized HS and that under physiological conditions, the binding of natural CXCL12gamma to extracellular HS structures is tight and long-lasting.
###end p 43
###begin p 44
###xml 231 235 219 223 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0002543-Laguri1">[28]</xref>
###xml 414 418 402 406 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0002543-Brelot1">[40]</xref>
Using lymphoid T cells, we confirm that gamma-wt signals through CXCR4 with diminished agonist potency as compared to alpha-wt. This can be accounted for by the decreased affinity of gamma-wt for CXCR4 that was previously reported [28]. It can be hypothesized that, either the electrostatic interactions of the highly cationic C-ter domain with the negatively charged N-ter domain and extracellular loops of CXCR4 [40], or the steric hindrance promoted by the bulky basic residues in the gamma-wt C-ter domain, impair the specific interaction with CXCR4 and therefore reduce the agonist potency of gamma-wt.
###end p 44
###begin p 45
###xml 138 142 126 130 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0002543-Laguri1">[28]</xref>
###xml 305 309 285 289 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0002543-Amara1">[31]</xref>
Importantly, neutralization of positive charges in the BBXB motifs of gamma-wt (gamma-m1 and gamma-m2) leads to an increased affinity for [28] and activation of CXCR4 comparable to this achieved either by alpha-wt or alpha-m, two proteins that preserves similar overstructure and efficiency towards CXCR4 [31]. Collectively, these findings conclusively identify the charged C-ter domain as responsible for the distinctive structural and cell-signaling properties showed by gamma-wt. In contrast to the situation observed for CXCR4, gamma-wt binding to CXCR7 is comparable to this of alpha-wt, suggesting that molecular determinants of CXCL12 for binding to CXCR4 and CXCR7 are different.
###end p 45
###begin p 46
###xml 21 28 21 28 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">in vivo</italic>
###xml 273 277 273 277 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0002543-Proudfoot1">[22]</xref>
###xml 461 468 457 464 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">in vivo</italic>
The demonstration of in vivo consequences of chemokine/GAG interactions have been hampered by conformational changes consecutive to the mutagenesis of BBXB consensus sites that leads frequently to an overall reduced affinity of the chemokine for the corresponding receptor [22]. The naturally occurring CXCL12gamma protein is free of this bias and offers an unprecedented opportunity to ascertain the importance of chemokine/GAG complexing in the regulation of in vivo cell migration in adult life.
###end p 46
###begin p 47
###xml 116 120 112 116 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0002543-Soriano1">[41]</xref>
###xml 208 212 204 208 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0002543-Ceradini1">[19]</xref>
###xml 241 245 237 241 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0002543-Orimo1">[35]</xref>
###xml 487 491 479 483 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0002543-Ferrara1">[42]</xref>
###xml 493 499 485 491 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">Vegf-A</italic>
###xml 565 569 557 561 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0002543-Tischer1">[43]</xref>
###xml 664 668 656 660 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0002543-Neufeld1">[44]</xref>
###xml 946 950 938 942 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0002543-Houck1">[45]</xref>
###xml 1249 1253 1241 1245 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0002543-Ruhrberg1">[46]</xref>
###xml 1366 1373 1354 1361 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">in vivo</italic>
###xml 1594 1601 1574 1581 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">in vivo</italic>
###xml 1649 1657 1629 1637 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">in vitro</italic>
The capacity of endogenous CXCL12alpha to promote leukocyte attraction in the peritoneum has been proved previously [41]. Similarly, it has been demonstrated that the formation of vessels under physiological [19] and pathological conditions [35] is induced by CXCL12alpha and is related to the regulation of the traffic and survival of CD34+ progenitor cells. The secreted signalling protein Vascular Endothelial Growth Factor-A (VEGF-A) is another potent and specific angiogenic factor [42]. Vegf-A isoforms are produced by alternative splicing from a single gene [43] and their levels are exquisitely regulated through transcriptional control and mRNA stability [44]. Similarly to CXCL12, these isoforms differ by the absence or presence of protein domains that confer the ability to bind heparin and therefore could mediate their capacity to interact differentially with GAG components in the extracellular environment of VEGF-secreting cells [45]. In keeping with this assumption, it has been shown that the absence of the heparin-binding isoforms of VEGF-A alters the distribution and gradients of VEGF-A protein without heparin-binding capacity in tissues and leads to a reduced vascular branching complexity and increased microvessel calibre [46]. This situation is reminiscent of our observation showing that gamma-wt displays an enhanced capacity to induce in vivo recruitment of cells as compared to alpha-wt, which is due to its enhanced affinity for HS. This conclusion is strongly supported by the fact that the HS-binding disabled mutant gamma-m2 is devoid of detectable activity in vivo despite it shows full agonist potency on CXCR4 in vitro.
###end p 47
###begin p 48
###xml 196 200 192 196 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0002543-Sadir1">[23]</xref>
###xml 327 331 319 323 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0002543-Ferrara2">[47]</xref>
It has been shown that the association of HS with CXCL12alpha prevents the proteolytic attack of its N-ter domain by the endopeptidase CD26/DPP, thus preserving the functionality of the chemokine [23]. CXCL12gamma encodes several serine-protease cleavage sites in its distinctive C-ter and is conceivable that, like for VEGF-A [47], the interaction of gamma-wt with HS also protects this domain from proteolytic attack, thus contributing to the prolonged immobilization and increased half-life of CXCL12gamma in tissues.
###end p 48
###begin p 49
###xml 238 245 234 241 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">in vivo</italic>
Overall, from our findings, we conclude that, despite its reduced agonist potency on CXCR4, both the prolonged immobilization and increased stability of gamma-wt would determine the superior capacity of this isoform to promote chemotaxis in vivo as compared to alpha-wt.
###end p 49
###begin p 50
The conserved structure and differential expression of CXCL12gamma both during development and in homeostatic and pathological conditions in the adult, herald the important and specific role that it might play both in embryogenesis and in adult life. Its localization in vascular endothelia where leukocyte diapedesis occurs and pathogen host defense are initiated, suggests that this chemokine is key in the fine-tuning of immune responses. CXCL12gamma would represent the paradigm of haptotactic proteins that critically promote the directional migration and tissue homing of cells and regulate important homeostatic and physiopathological functions.
###end p 50
