###xml 135 140 <span type="species:ncbi:4932">Yeast</span>
The CDK-Activating Kinase (CAK) Csk1 Is Required for Normal Levels of Homologous Recombination and Resistance to DNA Damage in Fission Yeast
###end article-title 0
###begin p 1
Conceived and designed the experiments: RF HG YP. Performed the experiments: HG YP. Analyzed the data: HG YP. Wrote the paper: RF HG.
###end p 1
###begin title 2
Background
###end title 2
###begin p 3
###xml 225 250 225 250 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">Schizosaccharomyces pombe</italic>
###xml 700 708 700 708 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">S. pombe</italic>
###xml 211 224 <span type="species:ncbi:4896">fission yeast</span>
###xml 225 250 <span type="species:ncbi:4896">Schizosaccharomyces pombe</span>
###xml 644 649 <span type="species:ncbi:4932">yeast</span>
###xml 700 708 <span type="species:ncbi:4896">S. pombe</span>
Cyclin-dependent kinases (CDKs) perform essential roles in cell division and gene expression in all eukaryotes. The requirement for an upstream CDK-activating kinase (CAK) is also universally conserved, but the fission yeast Schizosaccharomyces pombe appears to be unique in having two CAKs with both overlapping and specialized functions that can be dissected genetically. The Mcs6 complex-orthologous to metazoan Cdk7/cyclin H/Mat1-activates the cell-cycle CDK, Cdk1, but its non-redundant essential function appears to be in regulation of gene expression, as part of transcription factor TFIIH. The other CAK is Csk1, an ortholog of budding yeast Cak1, which activates all three essential CDKs in S. pombe-Cdk1, Mcs6 and Cdk9, the catalytic subunit of positive transcription elongation factor b (P-TEFb)-but is not itself essential.
###end p 3
###begin title 4
Methodology/Principal Findings
###end title 4
###begin p 5
###xml 18 19 18 19 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">+</sup>
###xml 14 19 14 19 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">csk1<sup>+</sup></italic>
###xml 259 263 259 263 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">csk1</italic>
###xml 265 269 265 269 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">mcs6</italic>
###xml 274 278 274 278 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">cdk9</italic>
Cells lacking csk1+ are viable but hypersensitive to agents that damage DNA or block replication. Csk1 is required for normal levels of homologous recombination (HR), and interacts genetically with components of the HR pathway. Tests of damage sensitivity in csk1, mcs6 and cdk9 mutants indicate that Csk1 acts pleiotropically, through Cdk9 and at least one other target (but not through Mcs6) to preserve genomic integrity.
###end p 5
###begin title 6
Conclusions/Significance
###end title 6
###begin p 7
###xml 16 29 <span type="species:ncbi:4896">fission yeast</span>
The two CAKs in fission yeast, which differ with respect to their substrate range and preferences for monomeric CDKs versus CDK/cyclin complexes as substrates, also support different functions of the CDK network in vivo. Csk1 plays a non-redundant role in safeguarding genomic integrity. We propose that specialized activation pathways dependent on different CAKs might insulate CDK functions important in DNA damage responses from those capable of triggering mitosis.
###end p 7

Introduction
###end title 8
###begin p 9
###xml 297 300 297 300 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0001492-Morgan1">[1]</xref>
###xml 469 472 469 472 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0001492-Fisher1">[2]</xref>
###xml 496 520 496 520 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">Saccharomyces cerevisiae</italic>
###xml 632 635 632 635 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0001492-Cismowski1">[3]</xref>
###xml 637 640 637 640 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0001492-Valay1">[4]</xref>
###xml 730 733 730 733 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0001492-Kaldis1">[5]</xref>
###xml 734 737 734 737 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0001492-Espinoza1">[7]</xref>
###xml 757 782 757 782 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">Schizosaccharomyces pombe</italic>
###xml 862 865 862 865 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0001492-Bamps1">[8]</xref>
###xml 866 870 866 870 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0001492-Lee2">[12]</xref>
###xml 905 909 905 909 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0001492-Lee2">[12]</xref>
###xml 910 914 910 914 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0001492-Molz1">[14]</xref>
###xml 490 495 <span type="species:ncbi:4932">yeast</span>
###xml 496 520 <span type="species:ncbi:4932">Saccharomyces cerevisiae</span>
###xml 743 756 <span type="species:ncbi:4896">fission yeast</span>
###xml 757 782 <span type="species:ncbi:4896">Schizosaccharomyces pombe</span>
A network of cyclin-dependent kinases (CDKs) coordinates eukaryotic cell division with duplication, maintenance and regulated expression of the genome. To attain full activity, CDKs require cyclin-binding and phosphorylation within the activation segment (T-loop) by a CDK-activating kinase (CAK) [1]. In metazoans, the CAK is itself a CDK, the heterotrimeric Cdk7/cyclin H/Mat1 complex, which is also a component of the general transcription factor TFIIH [reviewed in [2]]. In the budding yeast Saccharomyces cerevisiae, however, the Cdk7 ortholog Kin28 is a dedicated component of the transcription machinery with no CAK activity [3], [4]. CAK function instead resides in Cak1, a monomeric kinase only distantly related to CDKs [5]-[7]. The fission yeast Schizosaccharomyces pombe has two CAKs: 1) the Mcs6/Mcs2/Pmh1 complex, orthologous to Cdk7/cyclin H/Mat1 [8]-[12]; and 2) Csk1, an ortholog of Cak1 [12]-[14].
###end p 9
###begin p 10
###xml 0 8 0 8 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">S. pombe</italic>
###xml 471 475 471 475 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0001492-Lee1">[11]</xref>
###xml 575 579 575 579 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0001492-Lee2">[12]</xref>
###xml 581 585 581 585 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0001492-Saiz1">[15]</xref>
###xml 699 703 699 703 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0001492-Molz1">[14]</xref>
###xml 705 709 705 709 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0001492-Molz2">[16]</xref>
###xml 805 809 805 809 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0001492-Hermand1">[17]</xref>
###xml 843 847 843 847 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">mcs6</italic>
###xml 849 853 849 853 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">mcs2</italic>
###xml 858 862 858 862 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">pmh1</italic>
###xml 976 979 976 979 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0001492-Bamps1">[8]</xref>
###xml 980 984 980 984 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0001492-Lee1">[11]</xref>
###xml 986 990 986 990 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0001492-Molz1">[14]</xref>
###xml 992 996 992 996 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0001492-Saiz1">[15]</xref>
###xml 1285 1289 1285 1289 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0001492-Lee2">[12]</xref>
###xml 1291 1295 1291 1295 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0001492-Saiz1">[15]</xref>
###xml 1297 1301 1297 1301 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0001492-Bimbo1">[18]</xref>
###xml 1302 1306 1302 1306 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0001492-Pei1">[20]</xref>
###xml 0 8 <span type="species:ncbi:4896">S. pombe</span>
###xml 256 269 <span type="species:ncbi:4896">fission yeast</span>
S. pombe affords a unique opportunity to dissect functions of the CDK network genetically, with the goal of understanding the coordination of cell division, growth and the DNA damage response in all eukaryotes. Separate ablation of each of the two CAKs in fission yeast can reveal how signaling through CDKs is coordinated and how different subpathways are insulated from one another. The Mcs6 complex is essential, but that might be because of a role in gene expression [11]; its ability to activate Cdk1 (the major cell cycle CDK) appears to be redundant with that of Csk1 [12], [15]. Components of the Mcs6 complex were initially isolated in genetic screens for regulators of the G2/M transition [14], [16], and it has been suggested that the CAK activity of Csk1 cannot normally support mitotic entry [17]. Phenotypic analysis of multiple mcs6, mcs2 and pmh1 mutants, however, failed to uncover a non-redundant role for the complex in activating Cdk1 or promoting mitosis [8]-[11], [14], [15]. Csk1, although not essential for viability, is required for growth in suboptimal conditions or in the absence of normal Mcs6 complex function. It is a general CAK that activates Cdk1, Mcs6 and Cdk9-an essential homolog of positive transcription elongation factor b (P-TEFb) in metazoans [12], [15], [18]-[20].
###end p 10
###begin p 11
###xml 141 145 141 145 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0001492-Hartwell1">[21]</xref>
###xml 291 295 291 295 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0001492-OConnell1">[22]</xref>
###xml 350 358 350 358 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">activity</italic>
###xml 367 371 367 371 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0001492-Caspari1">[23]</xref>
###xml 372 376 372 376 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0001492-Ferreira1">[25]</xref>
###xml 701 709 701 709 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">S. pombe</italic>
###xml 150 163 <span type="species:ncbi:4896">fission yeast</span>
###xml 701 709 <span type="species:ncbi:4896">S. pombe</span>
When proliferating cells encounter DNA damage, they must pause cell cycle progression in order to allow repair of the lesion(s), if possible [21]. In fission yeast and metazoans, the normal checkpoint response to damage during G2 is a cell-cycle arrest due to inhibition of CDK [reviewed in [22]]. How repair or signaling pathways that depend on the activity of CDKs [23]-[25] can operate under these conditions remains a mystery. One possible solution to this paradox is the existence of multiple CDK activation pathways that can be insulated from one another, to permit proper control of the DNA damage response without triggering mitosis prematurely. We sought to determine whether the presence in S. pombe of two distinct CDK-activating enzymes might provide such insulation.
###end p 11
###begin p 12
###xml 38 39 38 39 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">+</sup>
###xml 34 39 34 39 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">csk1<sup>+</sup></italic>
###xml 252 256 252 256 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">cdk9</italic>
###xml 577 586 577 582 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">csk1&#916;</italic>
###xml 796 809 <span type="species:ncbi:4896">fission yeast</span>
Here we show that strains lacking csk1+ are hypersensitive to DNA-damaging agents and defective in homologous recombination (HR), suggesting that normal CDK activation-by a full complement of CAKs-is needed to maintain genomic integrity. A hypomorphic cdk9 mutant in which the kinase is refractory to activation by Csk1 was also hypersensitive to DNA damage, indicating a role for Cdk9, possibly dependent on activating phosphorylation, in the normal DNA damage response. A T-loop mutation that activates Cdk9 constitutively, however, did not suppress UV-hypersensitivity of a csk1Delta strain. Bypassing the CAK requirement for Cdk9 thus uncovered Csk1-dependent functions presumably mediated by other CDKs. Loss of the non-essential, single-subunit CAK therefore has pleiotropic effects on the fission yeast CDK network and its functions in response to DNA damage.
###end p 12
###begin title 13
Results
###end title 13
###begin title 14
Csk1 is required for normal resistance to DNA damage
###end title 14
###begin p 15
###xml 129 133 129 133 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0001492-Lee2">[12]</xref>
###xml 141 158 141 154 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">mcs6-13 csk1&#916;</italic>
###xml 570 574 566 570 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0001492-Saiz1">[15]</xref>
###xml 617 621 613 617 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">mcs6</italic>
###xml 625 629 621 625 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">pmh1</italic>
###xml 762 766 758 762 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0001492-Lee1">[11]</xref>
###xml 768 772 764 768 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0001492-Saiz1">[15]</xref>
###xml 48 61 <span type="species:ncbi:4896">fission yeast</span>
Only when both Mcs6 and Csk1 are compromised do fission yeast cells arrest their division cycle due to insufficient CAK activity [12]. In an mcs6-13 csk1Delta strain, progression through both G1/S and G2/M transitions was blocked at restrictive temperature. Phosphorylation of Cdk1 on T-loop residue Thr-167 decreased whereas phosphorylation of another Mcs6 target-the carboxyl-terminal domain (CTD) of the RNA Polymerase (Pol) II large subunit-was maintained, suggesting that inactivation of Mcs6 in this setting was incomplete and preferentially affected CAK function [15]. Tight temperature-sensitive mutations in mcs6 or pmh1 arrested at a different point in the cell cycle with a hypophosphorylated CTD but near-normal levels of Cdk1 T-loop phosphorylation [11], [15].
###end p 15
###begin p 16
###xml 183 192 183 188 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">csk1&#916;</italic>
###xml 280 287 276 283 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="fig" rid="pone-0001492-g001">Fig. 1A</xref>
###xml 338 345 334 341 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="fig" rid="pone-0001492-g001">Fig. 1B</xref>
###xml 371 380 367 372 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">csk1&#916;</italic>
###xml 569 576 561 568 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="fig" rid="pone-0001492-g001">Fig. 1C</xref>
###xml 612 621 604 609 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">csk1&#916;</italic>
###xml 794 798 782 786 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0001492-Bimbo1">[18]</xref>
###xml 846 855 834 839 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">csk1&#916;</italic>
###xml 749 762 <span type="species:ncbi:4896">fission yeast</span>
Just as functional overlap between the two CAKs helped reveal essential functions of Mcs6 in gene expression, it allowed detection of specific defects in cells living without Csk1. A csk1Delta strain was more sensitive than a wild-type strain to killing by ultraviolet light (UV; Fig. 1A) and, to a lesser extent, ionizing radiation (IR; Fig. 1B). In addition, growth of csk1Delta cells was more severely impaired by continuous exposure to the alkylating agent methylmethanesulfonate (MMS) and the replication inhibitor hydroxyurea (HU) than that of wild-type strains (Fig. 1C). Similarly, hypersensitivity of a csk1Delta strain to HU and the DNA-damaging agents ethylmethanesulfonate and bleomycin was detected in a systematic deletion analysis of fission yeast genes encoding protein kinases [18]. Together, the impaired survival and growth of csk1Delta cells under a variety of conditions suggest a generalized hypersensitivity to genotoxic stress.

###begin p 19
###xml 172 176 172 176 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0001492-Hartwell1">[21]</xref>
###xml 178 182 178 182 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0001492-OConnell1">[22]</xref>
###xml 253 262 253 258 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">csk1&#916;</italic>
###xml 396 397 392 393 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">2</sup>
###xml 495 502 491 498 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="fig" rid="pone-0001492-g002">Fig. 2A</xref>
###xml 514 523 510 515 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">rad3&#916;</italic>
###xml 591 595 583 587 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0001492-Walworth1">[26]</xref>
###xml 601 610 593 598 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">csk1&#916;</italic>
###xml 709 710 697 698 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">+</sup>
###xml 705 710 693 698 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">rad3<sup>+</sup></italic>
###xml 785 794 773 778 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">csk1&#916;</italic>
Increased sensitivity to DNA lesions could be due to a defect in damage repair or in the cellular response to damage, i.e. the proper function of the DNA damage checkpoint [21], [22]. To test the latter possibility, we monitored checkpoint integrity in csk1Delta cells exposed to DNA damage. We obtained cells synchronized in G2 by centrifugation in lactose gradients, irradiated them with 40 J/m2 UV light and recorded the total number of cells that passed through mitosis at 30-min intervals (Fig. 2A). Unlike a rad3Delta strain that lacks the checkpoint kinase (and ATR/ATM homolog) Rad3 [26], the csk1Delta mutant could arrest the cell cycle in response to UV irradiation, and that arrest depended on rad3+. However, re-entry into the cell cycle was slower and less synchronous in csk1Delta than in wild type cells, suggesting a defect in DNA damage repair and/or checkpoint recovery.
###end p 19
###begin title 20
Csk1 is not required for activation of the DNA damage checkpoint or for NER.

###begin title 22
The NER pathway is functional in the absence of Csk1
###end title 22
###begin p 23
###xml 57 66 57 62 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">csk1&#916;</italic>
###xml 75 85 71 81 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="fig" rid="pone-0001492-g001">Fig. 1A, B</xref>
###xml 278 282 274 278 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0001492-Lehmann1">[27]</xref>
###xml 323 327 319 323 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0001492-Drapkin1">[28]</xref>
###xml 329 333 325 329 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0001492-Wang1">[29]</xref>
###xml 543 548 539 544 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">rad13</italic>
###xml 568 572 564 568 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0001492-Lehmann1">[27]</xref>
###xml 580 600 576 588 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">rad13&#916; csk1&#916;</italic>
###xml 660 669 648 653 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">csk1&#916;</italic>
###xml 673 683 657 663 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">rad13&#916;</italic>
###xml 706 713 686 693 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="fig" rid="pone-0001492-g002">Fig. 2B</xref>
The differential effects of UV and IR on survival of the csk1Delta strain (Fig. 1A, B) indicate a specific problem in handling DNA damage caused by the former, and suggest a defect in repair. The major pathway for removing UV-induced lesions is nucleotide excision repair (NER) [27], in which TFIIH plays an essential role [28], [29]. We therefore investigated a possible function for Csk1 (and perhaps, by extension, its target the Mcs6 complex) in NER, by testing for genetic epistasis with a known component of the pathway. Deletion of the rad13 gene abolishes NER [27], but a rad13Delta csk1Delta double mutant strain was more UV-sensitive than either the csk1Delta or rad13Delta single mutant parent (Fig. 2B). Therefore NER confers UV-resistance in the absence of Csk1, suggesting a role for the CAK in another repair pathway.

###xml 21 25 21 25 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">csk1</italic>
Interactions between csk1 and genes in the homologous recombination pathway
###end title 24
###begin p 25
###xml 129 133 129 133 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0001492-Krogh1">[30]</xref>
###xml 261 265 261 265 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0001492-Lehmann1">[27]</xref>
###xml 484 488 484 488 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0001492-Paques1">[31]</xref>
###xml 617 621 617 621 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0001492-Paques1">[31]</xref>
###xml 623 627 623 627 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0001492-Peterson1">[32]</xref>
###xml 632 645 632 645 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">S. cerevisiae</italic>
###xml 738 742 738 742 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0001492-Sugawara1">[33]</xref>
###xml 62 67 <span type="species:ncbi:4932">yeast</span>
###xml 136 149 <span type="species:ncbi:4896">Fission yeast</span>
###xml 212 217 <span type="species:ncbi:4932">yeast</span>
###xml 591 596 <span type="species:ncbi:4932">yeast</span>
###xml 632 645 <span type="species:ncbi:4932">S. cerevisiae</span>
Homologous Recombination (HR) is a primary mechanism by which yeast cells repair double strand breaks (DSBs) in DNA [reviewed in [30]]. Fission yeast mutants defective in HR, unlike their counterparts in budding yeast, are very sensitive to killing by UV light [27]. Rhp51, Rad51 and the homologous bacterial RecA protein play a conserved role at a DSB, forming a nucleoprotein filament on single-stranded DNA (ssDNA), which can invade a double-stranded region on a sister or homolog [31]. Rhp54/Rad54 assists in the process, as does a complex of Rhp55 and Rhp57 (Rad55 and Rad57 in budding yeast), paralogs of Rad51 [31], [32]. In S. cerevisiae, Rad55/57 acts in Rad51 filament assembly, whereas Rad54 acts later, during strand invasion [33].
###end p 25

###xml 13 17 13 17 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">csk1</italic>

###xml 233 243 221 227 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">rhp54&#916;</italic>
###xml 269 278 253 258 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">csk1&#916;</italic>
###xml 280 287 260 267 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="fig" rid="pone-0001492-g003">Fig. 3B</xref>
###xml 400 409 380 385 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">csk1&#916;</italic>
###xml 414 424 390 396 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">rhp57&#916;</italic>
###xml 430 450 402 414 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">csk1&#916; rhp57&#916;</italic>
###xml 484 491 448 455 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="fig" rid="pone-0001492-g003">Fig. 3C</xref>
We tested if csk1 interacts with genes important for HR repair by epistasis analysis. The rhp51Delta csk1Delta double mutant was very slightly more sensitive to both UV and IR than was the rhp51Delta single mutant (Fig. 3A), whereas rhp54Delta was clearly epistatic to csk1Delta (Fig. 3B), suggesting a role for Csk1 in the HR repair pathway. In contrast, we observed a synthetic interaction between csk1Delta and rhp57Delta. The csk1Delta rhp57Delta double mutants grew very slowly (Fig. 3C and data not shown), suggesting compromised genomic stability even in unchallenged cells.

The severe effect on growth in the absence of DNA damaging agents suggests a requirement for either Csk1 or Rhp57 in order to deal with DSBs that occur normally, perhaps during replication. Taken together, the genetic data suggest that Csk1 acts upstream of Rhp51 and Rhp54, in a pathway separate from the one including Rhp55/57, to promote HR. We observed complex genetic interactions between csk1 and sfr1 (Fig. 3D): at low UV doses, deletion of sfr1+ suppressed hypersensitivity due to csk1Delta; at an intermediate dose, csk1Delta was epistatic to sfr1Delta; and at high doses, there was an additive effect on sensitivity (see Discussion for a possible interpretation).

We also asked if csk1+ and srs2+ have opposite effects on HR in vivo. The srs2+ gene encodes a DNA helicase that negatively regulates recombination [38]-[40]. In maintaining viability after UV irradiation (Fig. 4D), csk1Delta was epistatic to srs2Delta at low doses, but the two mutations caused synthetic hypersensitivity at higher doses (see Discussion). In budding yeast, Srs2 suppresses recombination leading to crossovers [41] through its ability to disrupt Rad51-ssDNA filaments [40], [42], [43]; deletion of srs2+ in S. pombe causes elevated levels of spontaneous HR, presumably by a similar mechanism [38]. Deletion of csk1+ suppressed hyper-recombination in an srs2Delta strain (Fig. 4B), consistent with Csk1 working in opposition to Srs2, possibly to promote Rhp51 function.

###xml 259 267 <span type="species:ncbi:4896">S. pombe</span>
Csk1 promotes survival of DNA damage, presumably through one or more downstream CDKs. To ascertain which kinase(s) might require activation by Csk1 to perform a function in the DNA damage response, we mutated the sites phosphorylated by Csk1 in two essential S. pombe CDKs, Mcs6 and Cdk9, and tested the resulting mutants for DNA damage-sensitivity. The addition of a hemagglutinin (HA) epitope to the carboxyl terminus of Mcs6 caused a mild UV-hypersensitivity phenotype, which was not further exacerbated by mutation of the T-loop phosphorylation site, Ser165, to Ala; the mcs6S165A-HA strain had a UV sensitivity similar to that of tagged but otherwise wild-type mcs6-HA (Fig. 5A). This suggests the mcs6S165A mutation by itself did not increase UV-sensitivity, and that failure to activate Mcs6 fully is unlikely to contribute to the DNA damage-sensitivity of a csk1Delta strain.

The cdk9T212A mutation rendered the kinase insensitive to stimulation by Csk1 in vitro, and phenocopied csk1Delta in both its cold-sensitivity and poor growth on the minimal medium EMM [20]. In contrast, a cdk9T212E mutation mimicked constitutive T-loop phosphorylation, resulting in a CAK-independent kinase with nearly wild-type activity in vitro [20]. In vivo, a cdk9T212E strain was slightly more UV-sensitive than a wild-type strain (Fig. 5C). This strain grew normally on minimal media, and the cdk9T212E mutation suppressed poor growth on EMM due to csk1Delta (Fig. 5D). The csk1Delta cdk9T212E double mutant was just as UV-sensitive as a csk1Delta single mutant, however (Fig. 5C), indicating that: 1) functions of Csk1 and Cdk9 in growth and DNA damage responses are genetically separable; and 2) much of the UV-sensitivity in a csk1Delta strain is due to impaired function of another CAK target, such as Cdk1 (encoded by cdc2+), which is known to regulate HR in both budding and fission yeast [23], [44], [45].

###begin title 41
Discussion
###end title 41
###begin p 42
###xml 123 131 123 131 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">S. pombe</italic>
###xml 203 206 203 206 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0001492-Fisher1">[2]</xref>
###xml 208 212 208 212 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0001492-Lee1">[11]</xref>
###xml 240 249 240 245 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">csk1&#916;</italic>
###xml 259 265 255 261 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="fig" rid="pone-0001492-g005">Fig. 5</xref>
###xml 123 131 <span type="species:ncbi:4896">S. pombe</span>
We have uncovered a role for CAK in the DNA damage response. Mutants with defects in the Mcs6 complex-the essential CAK in S. pombe, which also participates in the control of transcription by RNA Pol II [2], [11]-are not as UV-sensitive as csk1Delta strains (Fig. 5 and data not shown), suggesting a specialized function for Csk1, not redundant with Mcs6, in protecting genome integrity. There are several possible explanations for this specificity, which are not mutually exclusive.
###end p 42

###xml 371 376 367 372 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">T212E</sup>
###xml 367 376 363 372 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">cdk9<sup>T212E</sup></italic>
###xml 522 526 518 522 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0001492-Lee2">[12]</xref>
###xml 528 532 524 528 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0001492-Hermand2">[19]</xref>
###xml 691 699 687 695 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">S. pombe</italic>
###xml 699 703 695 699 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0001492-Karagiannis1">[46]</xref>
###xml 717 721 713 717 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">lsk1</italic>
###xml 802 811 798 803 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">csk1&#916;</italic>
###xml 811 815 803 807 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0001492-Bimbo1">[18]</xref>
###xml 896 900 888 892 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0001492-Ostapenko1">[47]</xref>
###xml 43 56 <span type="species:ncbi:4896">fission yeast</span>
###xml 138 146 <span type="species:ncbi:4896">S. pombe</span>
###xml 691 699 <span type="species:ncbi:4896">S. pombe</span>
###xml 829 834 <span type="species:ncbi:4932">yeast</span>
Csk1 is a general CAK able to activate all fission yeast and mammalian CDKs with which it has been tested, whereas Mcs6 can only activate S. pombe Cdk1 [12], [19], [20]. The specific requirement for Csk1 in response to DNA damage could-and, to a degree, probably does-reflect its unique ability to activate Cdk9 [20]. The epistatic relationship between csk1Delta and cdk9T212E, however, implicates additional Csk1 targets. Mcs6, the other essential CDK activated by Csk1, requires T-loop phosphorylation for full activity [12], [19], but preventing that phosphorylation did not impair survival of UV-irradiation. The CDK/cyclin complex Lsk1/Lsc1 is a second, non-essential P-TEFb homolog in S. pombe[46]. Deletion of lsk1 produced sensitivities to HU and DNA-damaging agents similar to those caused by csk1Delta[18]. The budding yeast ortholog of Lsk1 is Ctk1, which is activated in vivo by Cak1 [47]. Lsk1 is therefore another likely (and possibly exclusive) target of Csk1, likely to be important for normal resistance to DNA damage.
###end p 43
###begin p 44
###xml 165 169 165 169 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0001492-Lee2">[12]</xref>
###xml 202 206 202 206 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0001492-Saiz1">[15]</xref>
###xml 498 502 498 502 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0001492-Molz1">[14]</xref>
###xml 504 508 504 508 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0001492-Hermand2">[19]</xref>
###xml 614 618 614 618 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0001492-Lee2">[12]</xref>
###xml 911 915 911 915 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0001492-Kaldis2">[48]</xref>
###xml 970 974 970 974 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0001492-Darbon1">[49]</xref>
###xml 976 980 976 980 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0001492-Tassan1">[50]</xref>
###xml 1058 1062 1058 1062 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0001492-Chen1">[51]</xref>
###xml 876 881 <span type="species:ncbi:4932">yeast</span>
Csk1-dependent functions in the DNA damage response need not be restricted to ones performed by its exclusive targets. Csk1 and Mcs6 can both activate Cdk1 in vitro [12] and are likely to do so in vivo [15], raising the possibility of two separate activation pathways to support distinct Cdk1-dependent functions. Insulation of those pathways could be achieved temporally, spatially or kinetically. Neither Mcs6/Mcs2 nor Csk1 fluctuates significantly with respect to activity during the cell cycle [14], [19], and neither CAK appears to discriminate between Cdk1 complexes containing interphase or mitotic cyclins [12], making simple temporal regulation seem unlikely. Specific regulation of either Mcs6 or Csk1 in response to damaged DNA or stalled replication remains to be investigated. Spatial separation of CDK activation pathways is another possibility. Cak1 in budding yeast is predominantly cytoplasmic [48], whereas Cdk7 in higher eukaryotes is largely nuclear [49], [50], although it has also been reported to shuttle between nucleus and cytoplasm [51].
###end p 44
###begin p 45
###xml 309 313 309 313 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0001492-Fisher2">[52]</xref>
###xml 357 361 357 361 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0001492-Kaldis3">[53]</xref>
###xml 363 367 363 367 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0001492-Tsakraklides1">[54]</xref>
###xml 428 433 428 433 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">CDC28</italic>
###xml 535 539 535 539 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0001492-Ross1">[55]</xref>
###xml 558 566 558 566 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">S. pombe</italic>
###xml 724 728 724 728 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0001492-Saiz1">[15]</xref>
###xml 1034 1038 1034 1038 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0001492-Parker1">[56]</xref>
###xml 1040 1044 1040 1044 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0001492-Parker2">[57]</xref>
###xml 234 239 <span type="species:ncbi:9606">Human</span>
###xml 380 385 <span type="species:ncbi:4932">yeast</span>
###xml 558 566 <span type="species:ncbi:4896">S. pombe</span>
###xml 761 774 <span type="species:ncbi:4896">fission yeast</span>
Another potential explanation of the specific requirement for Csk1 is kinetically distinct activation pathways driven by the two types of CAK. In vitro, the Cdk7 and Cak1/Csk1 classes are distinguished by their substrate preferences. Human Cdk7 recognizes the mitotic CDK, Cdk1, only in a complex with cyclin [52], whereas Cak1 and Csk1 prefer CDK monomers [53], [54]. In budding yeast, the cell-cycle CDK (Cdk1, product of the CDC28 gene) is phosphorylated on its T-loop in vivo while in monomeric form, and throughout the cell cycle [55]. Co-expression of S. pombe Csk1 and Cdk1 in insect cells likewise generated monomeric Cdk1 that was phosphorylated on Thr167 and could be activated by cyclin in a single step in vitro [15]. A similar pathway operating in fission yeast could generate active CDK even in cells arrested in response to DNA damage, because the inhibitory kinases that phosphorylate Tyr15 of Cdk1-and which are terminal effectors of negative signaling by the G2 checkpoint-act preferentially on CDK/cyclin complexes [56], [57].
###end p 45
###begin p 46
###xml 324 329 324 329 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">T212E</sup>
###xml 320 329 320 329 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">cdk9<sup>T212E</sup></italic>
###xml 570 574 570 574 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0001492-Gould1">[58]</xref>
###xml 636 641 636 641 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">T167A</sup>
###xml 632 641 632 641 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">cdc2<sup>T167A</sup></italic>
Tracing the connections between Csk1 and defined repair pathways through individual CDK intermediaries is difficult, because CAK function is pleiotropic. A case in point is the unexpected involvement of Cdk9 in the response to UV-induced damage. Bypassing the requirement for T-loop phosphorylation to activate Cdk9, in cdk9T212E mutant strains, could permit a more direct test of Cdk1's role, but might also uncover added complexity: e.g. functions of other, as-yet-unconfirmed Csk1 targets such as Lsk1. Cdk1 requires T-loop phosphorylation for its essential function [58], precluding a simple, direct test of UV-sensitivity in a cdc2T167A mutant.
###end p 46
###begin p 47
###xml 204 213 204 209 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">csk1&#916;</italic>
###xml 222 229 218 225 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="fig" rid="pone-0001492-g004">Fig. 4D</xref>
###xml 388 392 384 388 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0001492-Akamatsu1">[34]</xref>
###xml 393 397 389 393 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0001492-Haruta1">[36]</xref>
###xml 465 474 461 470 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">csk1 sfr1</italic>
###xml 490 497 486 493 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="fig" rid="pone-0001492-g003">Fig. 3D</xref>
###xml 591 592 587 588 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">+</sup>
###xml 587 592 583 588 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">sfr1<sup>+</sup></italic>
###xml 626 635 622 627 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">csk1&#916;</italic>
###xml 780 789 772 777 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">csk1&#916;</italic>
###xml 806 815 794 799 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">sfr1&#916;</italic>
###xml 1044 1048 1028 1032 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">srs2</italic>
###xml 1325 1329 1309 1313 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0001492-Chiolo1">[59]</xref>
###xml 1331 1335 1315 1319 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="pone.0001492-Liberi1">[60]</xref>
###xml 1319 1324 <span type="species:ncbi:4932">yeast</span>
Also complicating matters is the likelihood that more than one DNA damage response pathway depends on CDKs, as suggested by the suppression of both conversion- and deletion-type recombination events in a csk1Delta strain (Fig. 4D). Csk1 and Rhp55/57 appear to act in independent pathways that converge on Rhp51 recombinase-a relationship similar to the one between Rhp55/57 and Swi5/Sfr1 [34]-[36]. By one possible interpretation, the complex UV-dose response of a csk1 sfr1 double mutant (Fig. 3D) is consistent with both genes acting in the same pathway. In this scenario, deletion of sfr1+ suppresses hypersensitivity of a csk1Delta strain at low doses because it favors efficient processing of HR intermediates by Rhp55/57, lessening the need for Csk1. At intermediate doses, csk1Delta is epistatic to sfr1Delta, perhaps because the Rhp55/57 pathway has been saturated. The synthetic phenotype at still higher doses might reflect additional defects due to impaired function by other CDK targets. Likewise, complex genetic interactions with srs2-epistasis at low, and synthetic hypersensitivity at higher doses-could be explained by Csk1 loss impinging on multiple pathways. Both Sfr1 and Srs2 contain sites matching the consensus sequence for phosphorylation by Cdk1, and Srs2 is a suspected Cdk1 target in budding yeast [59], [60], suggesting that Csk1 could act at least in part through the cell-cycle CDK to influence DNA damage repair.
