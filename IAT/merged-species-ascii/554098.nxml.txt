A novel replication-independent histone H2a gene in mouse
###end article-title 0
###begin p 1

###end p 1
###begin title 2
Background
###end title 2
###begin p 3
###xml 211 218 211 218 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">Hist4h4</italic>
###xml 274 280 274 280 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">H2afj </italic>
###xml 85 90 <span type="species:ncbi:10090">mouse</span>
###xml 268 273 <span type="species:ncbi:9606">human</span>
An uncharacterized histone H2a-coding transcript (E130307C13) has been cloned from a mouse full-length cDNA library. This transcript is encoded on chromosome 6, approximately 4 kb upstream of a histone H4 gene, Hist4h4. The proteins encoded by this transcript and the human H2afj mRNA isoform-2 have the highest amino acid similarity. In this paper, we characterize it from the expression pattern given by quantitative RT-PCR.
###end p 3
###begin title 4
Results
###end title 4
###begin p 5
###xml 69 79 69 79 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">E130307C13</italic>
###xml 151 156 151 156 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">H2afj</italic>
###xml 169 175 169 175 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">H2afj </italic>
Quantitative RT-PCR indicated that the gene that encodes E130307C13 (E130307C13) is regulated in a replication-independent manner, and therefore it is H2afj. Certainly, H2afj transcript lacks a stem-loop structure at the 3'-UTR but contains a poly (A) signal. In addition, its promoter region has a different structure from those of the replication-dependent histone H2a genes.
###end p 5
###begin title 6
Conclusion
###end title 6
###begin p 7
###xml 30 41 30 41 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">E130307C13 </italic>
###xml 176 181 176 181 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">H2afj</italic>
###xml 227 232 <span type="species:ncbi:10090">mouse</span>
The bioinformatics imply that E130307C13 is a replication-independent H2a gene. In addition, quantitative RT-PCR analysis shows that it is replication-independent. Thus, it is H2afj, a novel replication-independent H2a gene in mouse.
###end p 7
###begin title 8
Background
###end title 8
###begin p 9
###xml 229 230 229 230 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="B1">1</xref>
###xml 287 288 287 288 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="B2">2</xref>
###xml 341 347 341 347 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">Hist1 </italic>
###xml 382 388 382 388 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">Hist2 </italic>
###xml 425 431 425 431 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">Hist3 </italic>
###xml 457 458 457 458 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="B2">2</xref>
###xml 592 593 592 593 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="B3">3</xref>
###xml 657 658 657 658 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="B4">4</xref>
###xml 887 888 887 888 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="B5">5</xref>
###xml 889 890 889 890 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="B7">7</xref>
###xml 233 238 <span type="species:ncbi:10090">Mouse</span>
Replication of the eukaryotic chromosomes requires the synthesis of histones to package the newly replicated DNA into chromatin. Control of the level of histone mRNA accounts for much of the control of histone protein synthesis [1]. Mouse has 18 replication-dependent histone H2a genes [2]. Among these 18 genes, 13 genes are located in the Hist1 cluster on chromosome 13, 4 in the Hist2 cluster on chromosome 3 and 1 in the Hist3 cluster on chromosome 11 [2]. The histone mRNAs that are cell-cycle-regulated increase 35-fold as cells progress from mitosis through G1-phase and into S-phase [3]. The promoters of histone genes contain CCAAT and TATA boxes [4]. The replication-dependent histone H2a genes lack introns and a poly (A) signal. They have a conserved stem-loop structure (5'-GGCTCTTTTCAGAGCC-3') at the 3'-UTR, which plays an important role in mRNA processing and stability [5-7].
###end p 9
###begin p 10
###xml 62 68 62 68 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">H2afx </italic>
###xml 88 94 88 94 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">H2afz </italic>
###xml 153 159 153 159 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">H2afx </italic>
###xml 222 223 222 223 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="B8">8</xref>
###xml 276 282 276 282 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">H2afx </italic>
###xml 446 447 446 447 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="B9">9</xref>
###xml 0 5 <span type="species:ncbi:10090">Mouse</span>
###xml 226 231 <span type="species:ncbi:10090">Mouse</span>
Mouse also has two replication-independent histone H2a genes, H2afx on chromosome 9 and H2afz chromosome 3. These two genes encode polyadenylated mRNAs. H2afx mRNA has both a polyadenylated tail and a stem-loop structure [8]. Mouse replication-dependent histone H2a mRNAs and H2afx mRNA have a nuclear export element (5'-ACAACAAGAAGACGCGCATCAT-3') in the protein-coding region that functions to export the mRNA from the nucleus to the cytoplasm [9].
###end p 10
###begin p 11
###xml 172 183 172 183 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">E130307C13 </italic>
###xml 236 243 236 243 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">Hist4h4</italic>
###xml 140 145 <span type="species:ncbi:10090">mouse</span>
An uncharacterized histone H2a-coding transcript (E130307C13, FANTOM clone ID; NM_177688, Genebank accession number) has been cloned from a mouse full-length cDNA library. E130307C13 lies on chromosome 6, approximately 4 kb upstream of Hist4h4. In this paper, we characterize it from the expression pattern given by quantitative RT-PCR. In addition to this, we compared the structure with the sequences deposited in the international DNA/protein database.
###end p 11
###begin title 12
Results and discussion
###end title 12
###begin p 13
###xml 58 69 58 69 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">E130307C13 </italic>
###xml 218 224 218 224 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">H2afj </italic>
###xml 284 285 284 285 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="fig" rid="F1">1</xref>
###xml 287 288 287 288 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="fig" rid="F2">2</xref>
###xml 317 323 317 323 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">H2afj </italic>
###xml 324 326 324 326 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="B10">10</xref>
###xml 327 329 327 329 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="B11">11</xref>
###xml 495 501 495 501 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">H2afj </italic>
###xml 607 608 607 608 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="B2">2</xref>
###xml 614 625 614 625 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">E130307C13 </italic>
###xml 693 701 693 701 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">Hist4h2a</italic>
###xml 783 788 783 788 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">H2afj</italic>
###xml 212 217 <span type="species:ncbi:9606">human</span>
###xml 291 296 <span type="species:ncbi:9606">Human</span>
###xml 538 543 <span type="species:ncbi:9606">human</span>
Comparison of the putative amino acid sequence encoded by E130307C13 with the amino acid sequences deposited in the international DNA/protein database showed that it has the highest similarity to that encoded by human H2afj mRNA isoform-2 (NM_177925, Genebank accession number; Figs. 1, 2). Human has two isoforms of H2afj [10,11]. Isoform-1 (NM_018267, Genebank accession number) is produced after splicing of two introns; isoform-2 does not need intron splicing for maturation. Interestingly, H2afj also lies near a histone H4 gene, on human chromosome 12. According to the nomenclature of histone genes [2], if E130307C13 is regulated in a replication-dependent manner, it is recognized as Hist4h2a. But if it is regulated in a replication-independent manner, it is recognized as H2afj.

###xml 595 606 595 606 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">E130307C13 </italic>
###xml 610 616 610 616 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">H2afz </italic>
###xml 648 649 648 649 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="fig" rid="F3">3</xref>
###xml 679 690 679 690 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">E130307C13 </italic>
###xml 794 795 794 795 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="B2">2</xref>
###xml 798 809 798 809 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">E130307C13 </italic>
###xml 830 839 830 839 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">Hist4h2a </italic>
###xml 846 851 846 851 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">H2afj</italic>
###xml 739 744 <span type="species:ncbi:10090">mouse</span>
Each product of the quantitative RT-PCR gave a single band on the agarose gel of the expected size (Fig. 3). Observation using the quantitative RT-PCR showed that the expression pattern of E130307C13 is typical of replication-independent histone gene (Table 1, Fig. 3). The expression pattern of E130307C13 is more similar to that of the replication-independent H2afz than to that of the replication-dependent Hist2h2aa2. The expression of Hist2h2aa2 increased along with cell cycle progression from the beginning of S-phase (0 h), peaked at 2 h, and then decreased (Fig. 3). On the other hand, E130307C13 and H2afz were expressed constantly (Fig. 3). These results suggest that E130307C13 is a replication-independent histone H2a gene in mouse. According to the nomenclature of histone genes [2], E130307C13 is recognized not as Hist4h2a but as H2afj.

###xml 338 344 338 344 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">H2afx </italic>
###xml 348 354 348 354 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">H2afz </italic>
###xml 381 382 381 382 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="fig" rid="F4">4</xref>
###xml 454 464 454 464 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">E130307C13</italic>
###xml 497 503 497 503 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">H2afz </italic>
###xml 569 580 569 580 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">E130307C13 </italic>
###xml 624 625 624 625 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="fig" rid="F4">4</xref>
Upstream of the 5'-end of E130307C13, no TATA box was found. In addition, the first CCAAT box lies 230 bases upstream of the translation start codon (Fig. 4). The other replication-dependent H2a genes have the first CCAAT and TATA boxes within 100 bases upstream of the translation start codon (Fig. 4). The replication-independent genes H2afx and H2afz also have a TATA box (Fig. 4). Thus, histone H2a genes have a TATA box in the promoters, except for E130307C13. Interestingly, the promoter of H2afz lacks CCAAT box but includes TATA box, on the other hand, that of E130307C13 lacks TATA box but includes CCAAT box (Fig. 4).
###end p 19

###xml 31 42 31 42 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">E130307C13 </italic>

###xml 434 443 434 443 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">Hist1h2aj</italic>
###xml 535 541 535 541 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">H2afx </italic>

###xml 709 710 709 710 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="B8">8</xref>
###xml 713 719 713 719 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">H2afz </italic>
###xml 780 786 780 786 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">H2afz </italic>
###xml 911 922 911 922 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">E130307C13 </italic>
###xml 975 986 975 986 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">E130307C13 </italic>
###xml 1042 1053 1042 1053 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">E130307C13 </italic>
In addition, the 3'-UTR of the E130307C13 mRNA does not include the conserved stem-loop structure (Fig. 5). But the E130307C13 mRNA has two poly (A) signals at the middle and near the 3'-end. It indicates that the E130307C13 gives rise to two differentially polyadenylated mRNA transcripts. Considering the position at the 3'-UTR, it has a possibility that the poly (A) signal near the 3'-end is functional. Except for the pseudogene Hist1h2aj, the replication-dependent H2a mRNAs have the conserved stem-loop structure at the 3'-UTR. H2afx gives rise to a cell-cycle-regulated mRNA ending in the stem-loop when it is transcribed during S-phase, and a polyadenylated mRNA that is present in G1-phase cells [2,8]. H2afz mRNA lacks the stem-loop structure and has poly (A) signals. H2afz contains four introns in the protein-coding region and needs a splicing mechanism to produce the mature mRNA. Interestingly, E130307C13 lacks introns. Comparing the transcript structure of E130307C13 with those of the other histone H2a genes suggests that E130307C13 has replication-independent characteristics.

###begin title 25
Methods
###end title 25
###begin title 26
Phylogenetic tree construction
###end title 26
###begin p 27
###xml 133 135 133 135 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="B12">12</xref>
###xml 321 323 321 323 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="B13">13</xref>
###xml 84 89 <span type="species:ncbi:9606">human</span>
###xml 94 99 <span type="species:ncbi:10090">mouse</span>
Multiple alignment of 44 amino acids sequences of histone H2a related proteins from human and mouse was created using the CLUSTAL W [12] on the DNA Databank of Japan. The phylogenetic tree by the neighbor-joining method with 1000 bootstrap analyses was constructed based on the multiple alignment using MEGA version 2.1 [13].
###end p 27
###begin title 28
Cell cycle synchronization
###end title 28
###begin p 29
The cell cycle of Hepa 1-6 cells was synchronized at the end of G1-phase by the addition of thymidine-hydroxyurea. The cell cycle arrest was released by washing out the thymidine-hydroxyurea, then the cells were harvested at intervals of 1 h from 0 h to 11 h.
###end p 29
###begin title 30
RNA extraction
###end title 30
###begin p 31
Total RNA was extracted by using the RNeasy mini kit (Qiagen) according to the manual for the cell line. After that, each sample was treated with DNase I.
###end p 31
###begin title 32
cDNA synthesis
###end title 32
###begin p 33
RNA (approximately 0.5 mug) and random hexamer primers were heated to 70degreesC for 10 min, followed by cooling on ice for 5 min. The cDNA was synthesized using Superscript III First Strand buffer (Invitrogen) according to the manual. The reverse transcriptase was inactivated by a 15-min incubation at 70degreesC.
###end p 33
###begin title 34
Quantitative PCR
###end title 34
###begin p 35
###xml 238 248 238 248 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">Hist2h2aa2</italic>
###xml 312 317 312 317 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">H2afz</italic>
###xml 527 537 527 537 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">E130307C13</italic>
###xml 606 612 606 612 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">GAPDH </italic>
###xml 1001 1005 983 987 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">Taq </italic>
###xml 1104 1105 1086 1087 <sub xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink">T</sub>
###xml 1204 1206 1186 1188 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ref-type="bibr" rid="B14">14</xref>
The following primers were used: 5'-AACTGTAGCCCGGCCCG-3' and 5'-TTCGTCTGTTTGCGCTTT-3' (primer set 1, product size 100 bp) and 5'-CAACAAGCTGCTGGGCAAA-3' and 5'-TCGCCTTATGGTGGCTCTCC-3' (primer set 2, product size 101 bp) for transcripts of Hist2h2aa2; 5'-ACTCCGGAAAGGCCAAGACA-3' and 5'-GTTGTCCTAGATTTCAGGTG-3' for H2afz, product size 100 bp; 5'-CGTCCTGCCCAATATCCAG-3' and 5'-TCTGCACCCGTCTGTCG-3' (primer set 1, product size 90 bp) and 5'-AAGCAGGGCGGTAAGGTG-3' and 5'-TCCGCGTAGTTGCCCTTC-3' (primer set 2, product size 110 bp) for E130307C13; and 5'-TGTGTCCGTCGTGGATCTGA-3' and 5'-CCTGCTTCACCACCTTCTTGA-3' for GAPDH (glyceraldehyde-3-phosphate dehydrogenase), product size 76 bp. Quantification of GAPDH mRNA was used as a control for data normalization. PCR amplification was performed on an ABI PRISM 7700 Sequence Detection System (Applied Biosystems). The PCR conditions were an initial step of 30 s at 95degreesC, followed by 40 cycles of 5 s at 95degreesC and 30 s at 60degreesC. The SYBR premix Ex Taq (Takara) was used according to the manual. Expression was assessed by evaluating threshold cycle (CT) values. The relative amount of expressed RNA was calculated using Livak and Schmittgen's method [14].
