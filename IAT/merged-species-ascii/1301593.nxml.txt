An autoregulatory loop controlling orphan nuclear receptor DAX-1 gene expression by orphan nuclear receptor ERRgamma

###xml 433 438 <span type="species:ncbi:10090">mouse</span>
###xml 863 868 <span type="species:ncbi:9606">human</span>
###xml 1052 1057 <span type="species:ncbi:4932">yeast</span>
The estrogen receptor-related receptor gamma (ERRgamma/ERR3/NR3B3) is a member of the nuclear receptor superfamily that activates transcription in the absence of ligand. However, the detailed mechanism of gene regulation by ERRgamma is not fully understood. In this study we have found that the orphan nuclear receptor ERRgamma activates the DAX-1 promoter, which, in turn, represses transactivation by ERRgamma. Serial deletions of mouse DAX-1 (mDAX-1) gene promoter have revealed that the region responding to ERRgamma is located between -129 and -121 bp and -334 and -326 bp. Gel shift assays and chromatin immunoprecipitation (ChIP) assays demonstrated that ERRgamma binds directly to the mDAX-1 promoter. Site-directed mutagenesis results demonstrated that ERRE1 (-129 to -121 bp) is more important than ERRE2 (-334 to -326 bp) which is not conserved in the human DAX-1 promoter. In addition, adenovirus-mediated overexpression of ERRgamma induced DAX-1 gene expression in MCF-7 breast cancer cells that co-expressed ERRgamma and DAX-1. Moreover, yeast two-hybrid and glutathione S-transferase (GST)-pull down assays demonstrated that DAX-1 physically interacted with ERRgamma and inhibited ERRgamma transactivation, and that this interaction was dependent on the AF-2 domain of ERRgamma. In addition, in vitro competition assays showed that DAX-1 inhibited PGC-1alpha mediated ERRgamma transactivation, via competition between these two factors for the AF-2 binding domain. We thus propose a novel autoregulatory loop that controls DAX-1 gene expression by ERRgamma.

###begin title 3
INTRODUCTION
###end title 3
###begin p 4
###xml 412 413 412 413 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="b1">1</xref>
Nuclear receptors (NRs) are ligand-dependent transcription factors that bind to specific DNA target sequences through their highly conserved DNA-binding domain (DBD) and activate transcription through interaction with coactivators. The latter property is dependent on their moderately conserved ligand-binding domain (LBD) and in particular requires the extreme C-terminal part of it, the so-called AF-2 region (1).
###end p 4
###begin p 5
###xml 191 192 191 192 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="b2">2</xref>
###xml 193 194 193 194 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="b3">3</xref>
###xml 395 396 395 396 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">L</italic>
###xml 401 402 401 402 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">M</italic>
###xml 540 541 540 541 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="b4">4</xref>
###xml 685 686 685 686 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="b5">5</xref>
###xml 941 942 941 942 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="b4">4</xref>
###xml 943 944 943 944 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="b6">6</xref>
###xml 951 956 951 956 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">DAX-1</italic>
###xml 1176 1177 1176 1177 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="b2">2</xref>
###xml 1178 1179 1178 1179 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="b3">3</xref>
###xml 1198 1203 1198 1203 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">DAX-1</italic>
###xml 1327 1332 1327 1332 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">DAX-1</italic>
###xml 1483 1484 1483 1484 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="b7">7</xref>
###xml 1485 1486 1485 1486 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="b8">8</xref>
###xml 1182 1187 <span type="species:ncbi:10090">Mouse</span>
###xml 1192 1197 <span type="species:ncbi:9606">human</span>
###xml 1416 1421 <span type="species:ncbi:10090">mouse</span>
###xml 1467 1472 <span type="species:ncbi:9606">human</span>
Among NRs, DAX-1 (dosage-sensitive sex reversal, adrenal hypoplasia critical region, on chromosome X, gene 1) (NR0B1) is an atypical orphan nuclear receptor, since it does not contain a DBD (2,3). Instead, the N-terminal half of DAX-1 contains a unique region mainly composed of three repeats, each of which is 65-67 amino acids in length. This region contains LXXLL motif-like sequences (where L and M are leucine and methionine, respectively, and X can be any amino acid) that are necessary for interaction with estrogen receptors (ERs) (4). Since the C-terminal half of DAX-1 containing the putative LBD functions as a repressor domain through interaction with corepressors (NCoR) (5), two mechanisms have been proposed to function cooperatively in the repression of transcription exerted by DAX-1. According to the proposition, DAX-1 would both block the binding of coactivators to NRs and recruit corepressors with its C-terminal half (4-6). The DAX-1 gene was identified through a search for a gene linked to adrenal hypoplasia congenita, a disease affecting the normal development of the adrenal cortex and which is often associated with hypogonadotropic hypogonadism (2,3). Mouse and human DAX-1 promoters have been cloned and characterized. For example, orphan nuclear receptor steroidogenic factor-1 (SF-1) regulates DAX-1 promoter, and the SF-1 response element is located between -129 and -121 bp on the mouse promoter and between -135 and -143 bp on the human promoter (7,8).
###end p 5
###begin p 6
###xml 182 183 182 183 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="b9">9</xref>
###xml 184 186 184 186 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="b10">10</xref>
###xml 286 290 286 290 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">MCAD</italic>
###xml 292 295 292 295 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">pS2</italic>
###xml 300 305 300 305 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">cyp19</italic>
###xml 331 333 327 329 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="b11">11</xref>
###xml 334 336 330 332 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="b13">13</xref>
###xml 482 489 474 481 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">in vivo</italic>
###xml 501 502 493 494 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="b9">9</xref>
###xml 503 505 495 497 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="b10">10</xref>
###xml 676 678 660 662 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="b14">14</xref>
###xml 679 681 663 665 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="b15">15</xref>
###xml 914 916 886 888 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="b16">16</xref>
###xml 917 919 889 891 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="b17">17</xref>
###xml 1218 1220 1170 1172 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="b18">18</xref>
###xml 1518 1520 1466 1468 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="b19">19</xref>
###xml 1884 1886 1824 1826 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="b16">16</xref>
###xml 1887 1889 1827 1829 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="b20">20</xref>
Estrogen-related receptors (ERRs-alpha, -beta and -gamma), are orphan NRs closely related to ERs, with which they share identical target response elements and coregulatory proteins (9,10). However, ERRs do not respond to the classical ER ligand. Previous studies have demonstrated that MCAD, pS2 and cyp19 are targets of ERRalpha (11-13). Among the ERR family, ERRgamma, the newest member of the subfamily, is a constitutively active nuclear receptor, and little is known about its in vivo functions (9,10). Previous studies have demonstrated that 4-hydroxytamoxifen (4-OHT) directly binds to and deactivates ERRgamma, suggesting that 4-OHT is an inverse agonist of ERRgamma (14,15). However, the deactivation mechanism of ERRgamma by 4-OHT requires further elucidation. It has been reported that coactivators such as GRIP1 or PGC-1alpha, interact directly with ERRgamma and activate its transcriptional activity (16,17). ERRgamma acts as a monomer and activates transcription constitutively. It has also been suggested in recent reports, that ERRgamma forms dimers through its LBD. In contrast to this, the related receptor, ERRalpha, inhibits the activities of both ERRalpha and ERRgamma through heterodimerization (18). ERRgamma exhibits significant affinities for binding to a wide spectrum of sequences, including inverted and direct repeat sequences composed of AGGTCA half-sites with differing spacings, as well as a monovalent motif of the same sequence carrying an extra T(C/G)A trinucleotide on the 5' side (19). It has been reported that ERRgamma binds to and transactivates both estrogen response elements and SF-1 response elements (SF-1RE). Previous reports have suggested that several monomeric binding partners, such as SF-1, liver receptor homolog-1 (LRH-1) and ERRgamma, can activate and bind directly to the small heterodimer partner (SHP) promoter through SF-1RE (16,20).
###end p 6
###begin p 7
###xml 109 110 109 110 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="b2">2</xref>
###xml 111 112 111 112 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="b3">3</xref>
###xml 137 142 137 142 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">DAX-1</italic>
###xml 147 150 147 150 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">SHP</italic>
###xml 228 233 228 233 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">DAX-1</italic>
###xml 319 324 315 320 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">DAX-1</italic>
###xml 426 431 418 423 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">DAX-1</italic>
###xml 611 616 599 604 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">DAX-1</italic>
###xml 787 792 771 776 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">DAX-1</italic>
###xml 919 924 895 900 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">DAX-1</italic>
###xml 1307 1312 1263 1268 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">DAX-1</italic>
DAX-1 is closely related to SHP, which neither contains a DNA-binding domain nor functions as a corepressor (2,3). In addition, both the DAX-1 and SHP promoters are regulated by the monomer binding partner SF-1. Thus, since the DAX-1 promoter contains several SF-1RE, we predicted that ERRgamma could also activate the DAX-1 promoter. Transient transfection studies demonstrated that ERRgamma had a preferential effect on the DAX-1 promoter. The results of deletion and site-directed mutagenesis have revealed that two ERR regulatory elements (ERREs) are responsible for the ERRgamma-mediated activation of the DAX-1 promoter. The results of electrophoretic mobility shift assay (EMSA) and chromatin immunoprecipitation (ChIP) assays strongly suggest that ERRgamma binds directly to the DAX-1 promoter. In addition, the results of overexpression and ERRgamma knock-down experiments demonstrated that ERRgamma regulates DAX-1 gene expression in MCF-7 breast cancer cells and that ERRgamma and DAX-1 are co-expressed in breast cancer tissue. The expression of DAX-1 inhibits ERRgamma transactivation of its own promoter and DAX-1 competes directly with the coactivator, PGC-1alpha, for binding to ERRgamma. Taken together, these results suggest that ERRgamma belongs to a new autoregulatory loop that governs DAX-1 gene expression.

###begin title 11
Cell culture and transient transfection assay
###end title 11
###begin p 12
###xml 221 222 221 222 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">2</sup>

Human embryonic kidney (293T), mouse fibriblast (NIH3T3) and human breast cancer (MCF-7) cells were maintained with DMEM in the presence of 10% fetal bovine serum and antibiotics (Gibco) in humidified air containing 5% CO2 at 37degreesC. Cells were transfected, using Superfect and Effectene reagents (Qiagen Inc., Germany), according to the manufacturer's instructions. The total DNA used in each transfection was adjusted to 1 microg by adding the appropriate amount of pcDNA3 vector. Approximately 40-48 h post-transfection, cells were harvested and luciferase activity was measured and normalized to beta-galactosidase activity. Experiments were performed three times in duplicate.

Glutathione S-transferase (GST)-pull down assay
###end title 13
###begin p 14
###xml 73 89 73 89 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">Escherichia coli</italic>
###xml 147 148 144 145 <sc xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">d</sc>

###xml 595 603 582 590 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">in vitro</italic>
###xml 73 94 <span type="species:ncbi:511693">Escherichia coli BL21</span>
The indicated GST-fusion proteins or GST protein alone were expressed in Escherichia coli BL21 (DE3) pLys cultures by adding 0.2 mM isopropyl-beta-d-thiogalactopyranoside (IPTG), and the cells were extracted. GST-fusion proteins were pre-bound with a 30 microl aliquot of glutathione-Sepharose beads. The beads were incubated with transcribed and translated [35S]methionine-labeled proteins for 3-4 h at 4degreesC. The beads were then washed three times with the washing buffer, and analyzed by SDS-PAGE and protein bands were visualized by using a phosphorimaging analyzer. Translated proteins in vitro (10%) were used as input.

###begin title 19
Gel mobility shift assays
###end title 19
###begin p 20
###xml 77 79 73 75 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">32</sup>
###xml 180 189 176 185 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="fig" rid="fig3">Figure 3A</xref>
Double-stranded ERRE oligonucleotides were labeled by filling-in with [alpha-32P]dCTP, using the Klenow fragment of DNA polymerase I. The oligonucleotide sequences are depicted in Figure 3A. ###begin title 23
Preparation of recombinant adenovirus
###end title 23
###begin p 24
###xml 65 67 65 67 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="b24">24</xref>

###xml 403 408 <span type="species:ncbi:9606">human</span>
###xml 879 885 <span type="species:ncbi:9913">bovine</span>
The recombinant adenovirus was prepared as described previously (24). In brief, the cDNA encoding ERRgamma was cloned into a pAd-YC2 shuttle vector, which (under the control of the cytomegalovirus promoter) contains a bovine growth hormone polyadenylation signal sequence. For homologous recombination, a pAd-YC2 shuttle vector (5 microg) and a rescue vector, pJM17 (5 microg), were co-transfected into human embryonic kidney 293 cells. To purify pure plaques, cell culture supernatant was serially diluted into serum-free media and incubated with 293 cells at 37degreesC for 1 h. An equal volume mixture of 2x medium and 1% agarose was overlaid on the 293 cells. After 7 days, plaques that were well isolated were purified further and propagated in 293 cells and screened by PCR, using upstream primers derived from the cytomegalovirus promoter, and downstream primers from the bovine growth hormone polyadenylation sequence. Then, the recombinants were amplified in 293 cells and were purified and isolated using CsCl2 (Sigma). The preparations were collected and desalted, and titers were determined by counting the number of plaques.

###begin title 32
RESULTS
###end title 32
###begin title 33
###xml 65 70 61 66 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">DAX-1</italic>
Identification of ERRgamma as a transcriptional activator of the DAX-1 promoter
###end title 33
###begin p 34
###xml 48 53 48 53 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">DAX-1</italic>

###xml 66 67 66 67 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="b8">8</xref>
###xml 186 191 182 187 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">DAX-1</italic>
###xml 214 223 210 219 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="fig" rid="fig1">Figure 1A</xref>
###xml 256 261 252 257 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">DAX-1</italic>
###xml 496 505 492 501 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="fig" rid="fig1">Figure 1B</xref>
###xml 420 425 <span type="species:ncbi:10090">mouse</span>
###xml 430 435 <span type="species:ncbi:9606">human</span>
###xml 480 485 <span type="species:ncbi:10090">mouse</span>
SF-1 has been identified as an activator of the DAX-1 promoter (7,8). Since SFRE is also a DNA-binding site for ERRgamma, we speculated that an ERR response element (ERRE) exists in the DAX-1 promoter. As shown in Figure 1A, two ERREs were located in the mDAX-1 promoter; ERRE1 (-129 to -121 bp), which was previously identified as an SF-1RE, and a new ERRE2 (-334 to -326 bp). Interestingly, ERRE1 is conserved in both mouse and human promoters, whereas ERRE2 only exists in the mouse promoter (Figure 1B).
###end p 34
###begin p 35
###xml 48 53 48 53 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">DAX-1</italic>

###xml 453 458 442 447 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">DAX-1</italic>
###xml 521 526 510 515 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">DAX-1</italic>
###xml 721 726 695 700 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">DAX-1</italic>
###xml 766 775 740 749 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="fig" rid="fig2">Figure 2C</xref>
###xml 787 792 761 766 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">DAX-1</italic>
###xml 931 936 901 906 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">DAX-1</italic>
###xml 992 1001 962 971 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="fig" rid="fig2">Figure 2D</xref>
###xml 1038 1043 1008 1013 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">DAX-1</italic>
###xml 1099 1104 1065 1070 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">DAX-1</italic>
###xml 174 179 <span type="species:ncbi:10090">mouse</span>
In order to determine whether ERRs regulate the DAX-1 promoter, we performed transient transfection assays using the luciferase reporter gene driven by a -467 bp region from mouse DAX-1 promoter. As shown in Figure 2A and B, there was strong activation by ERRgamma in 293T cells and NIH3T3 cells, whereas ERRalpha and ERRbeta did not demonstrate any significant effect. As expected, SF-1, the positive control for the transfection assays, activated the DAX-1 gene promoter significantly. These results indicated that the DAX-1 gene is a specific target of ERRgamma, but not of ERRalpha or ERRbeta. In order to investigate the sequences that were required for ERRgamma mediated activation, a series of 5' deletions of the DAX-1 promoter were constructed, as shown in Figure 2C. Since the DAX-1 promoter contains two ERREs, we designed deletion constructs both with and without an ERRE. Activation by ERRgamma was decreased when the DAX-1 promoter was deleted up to -325 bp, as demonstrated in Figure 2D. Furthermore, the activation of the DAX-1 promoter by ERRgamma, was almost lost with the -121 bp DAX-1 construct. These results suggest that ERREs are required for ERRgamma response.

###begin title 36
Determination of ERRgamma binding regions
###end title 36
###begin p 37
###xml 124 133 120 129 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="fig" rid="fig3">Figure 3A</xref>

###xml 804 809 788 793 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">DAX-1</italic>

###xml 1163 1168 1135 1140 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">DAX-1</italic>

In order to clarify the specific binding of ERRgamma to ERREs, gel mobility shift assays were performed. As demonstrated in Figure 3A, we designed both wild-type and mutant probes that corresponded to ERRE1 and ERRE2. Previously, the TNAAGGTCA sequence was shown to be a bona fide ERRE. Within the sequence, the GG dimer is pivotal for ERRgamma protein binding. Thus, we substituted GG for the TT sequence. Figure 3B demonstrates that ERRgamma formed specific complexes with ERRE1 and ERRE2; a 50- or 100-fold molar excess of unlabeled ERRE1 and ERRE2 competed strongly with the DNA-protein complex. In order to verify that ERRgamma binds to the DAX-1 promoter in vivo, we performed a ChIP assay using PCR primers that flanked both ERRE1 and ERRE2 (-350 to -100 bp). 293T cells were transfected with the DAX-1 promoter, either with hemagglutinin (HA) only or with HA-tagged ERRgamma. As demonstrated in Figure 3C, a 250 bp PCR product (-350 to -100 bp) was observed in cells that were transfected with the expression vector for HA-ERRgamma but not with the vector expressing only the HA epitope. This indicated that HA-ERRgamma formed a specific complex with the DAX-1 promoter in vivo. Taken together, this suggests that ERRgamma regulates the DAX-1 promoter by direct binding to ERRE.

In order to verify the functional significance of ERRgamma binding sites in the DAX-1 promoter, site-directed mutagenesis was performed on the -467 bp DAX-1 promoter using the primers as shown in Figure 4A. Wild-type -467 bp/Luc and several mutant constructs were transiently transfected with ERRgamma into 293T cells. The ERRE1 mutation (mtERRE1/Luc) and double ERRE1 and ERRE2 mutations (mtERRE1&2/Luc) completely abolished the ERRgamma-mediated transactivation of the DAX-1 promoter whereas the ERRE2 mutation (mtERRE2/Luc) had a slight effect (Figure 4B). Thus, we conclude that ERRE1 plays a major role in the activation of the DAX-1 promoter by ERRgamma.

###end title 40
###begin p 41

###xml 1431 1440 1359 1368 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="fig" rid="fig5">Figure 5D</xref>
###xml 1657 1666 1577 1586 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="fig" rid="fig5">Figure 5D</xref>

###xml 1374 1379 <span type="species:ncbi:9606">human</span>
###xml 1832 1838 <span type="species:ncbi:9986">rabbit</span>
###xml 1974 1979 <span type="species:ncbi:9606">human</span>
Previous reports have demonstrated that ERRgamma is a favorable biomarker and, possibly, an indicator of hormonal sensitivity in breast cancer. In addition, DAX-1 has also been detected in breast cancer tissue (25,26). In order to investigate whether ERRgamma can directly regulate DAX-1 gene expression in breast cancer cells, we introduced ERRgamma by adenovirus infection into MCF-7 cells. As demonstrated in Figure 5A, a basal expression of DAX-1 and ERRgamma was detected in MCF-7 cells, and DAX-1 mRNA was induced by overexpression of Ad-ERRgamma 24 h after infection. Since ERRgamma is basally expressed in MCF-7 cells, we further tested whether DAX-1 gene expression is down-regulated by performing knock-down of endogenous ERRgamma expression using small interfering RNA (si-ERRgamma/I or II) in MCF-7 cells. si-ERRgamma/I, but not si-ERRgamma/II significantly reduced endogenous ERRgamma expression (Figure 5B). All of the ERRgamma-positive cancers co-expressed the DAX-1 protein (Figure 5D, upper part). Two cancer tissues that were DAX-1-negative were also negative for ERRgamma. No staining was observed in the negative controls using either pre-immune rabbit serum or by omitting the primary antibody (data not shown). These results suggest that ERRgamma can stimulate DAX-1 gene expression in human breast cancer cells.

###xml 902 911 870 879 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="fig" rid="fig6">Figure 6A</xref>
###xml 1129 1144 1089 1104 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="fig" rid="fig7">Figure 7A and B</xref>
###xml 1440 1449 1380 1389 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="fig" rid="fig7">Figure 7A</xref>
###xml 1540 1549 1476 1485 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="fig" rid="fig7">Figure 7B</xref>
###xml 1073 1078 <span type="species:ncbi:4932">yeast</span>
DAX-1 interacts with several orphan NRs (ER, AR, LRH-1, SF-1 and Nur77) and represses their transcriptional activity (4-6,21,27). We investigated whether DAX-1 can also repress ERRgamma mediated transactivation of its own promoter. The results of transient transfections demonstrated that DAX-1 inhibits ERRgamma activity of the DAX-1 promoter in a dose-dependant manner (Figure 6A). Since ERRgamma also activates the SHP promoter, and SHP represses ERRgamma transactivation of its own promoter, we examined whether DAX-1 represses the ERRgamma-mediated transactivation of the SHP promoter or vice versa. As demonstrated in Figure 6B and C, DAX-1 and SHP repressed ERRgamma transactivation of both the SHP promoter and the DAX-1 promoter. These results suggest that both DAX-1 and SHP are regulated by ERRgamma and repress the transcriptional activity of ERRgamma through an autoregulatory loop. Since Figure 6A shows that DAX-1 repressed the transcriptional activity of ERRgamma, we examined whether DAX-1 can directly interact with ERRgamma, by using a GST-pull down and yeast two-hybrid interaction assays. As demonstrated in Figure 7A and B, DAX-1 interacted with ERRgamma. Since repression or activation is dependant on the AF-2 domain, we deleted this domain in ERRgamma. As expected, DAX-1 could not interact with ERRgamma DeltaAF-2, which demonstrates that the AF-2 domain of ERRgamma is crucial for the repressive effect of DAX-1 (Figure 7A). In the case of DAX-1, an LBD-like region was involved in the interaction with ERRgamma (Figure 7B). These results suggest that DAX-1 interacts directly with the AF-2 surface of ERRgamma.
###end p 43
###begin p 44
###xml 185 194 177 186 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="fig" rid="fig8">Figure 8A</xref>
###xml 325 330 305 310 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">DAX-1</italic>
###xml 695 703 655 663 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">in vitro</italic>
###xml 855 857 807 809 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="b17">17</xref>
###xml 874 876 826 828 <sup xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">35</sup>
###xml 992 1001 928 937 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="fig" rid="fig8">Figure 8B</xref>
Since the AF-2 surface of ERRgamma is involved in the interaction with DAX-1, we explored the role of coactivator competition in the repression of ERRgamma activity. As demonstrated in Figure 8A, PGC-1alpha, which was described previously as a coactivator of ERRgamma, significantly increased the activity of ERRgamma on the DAX-1 promoter and the co-expression of DAX-1 with PGC-1alpha repressed this induction in a dose-dependant manner. This result suggests that both PGC-1alpha and DAX-1 compete for the binding of the AF-2 pocket of ERRgamma, as demonstrated for other receptors. In order to confirm the direct competition between DAX-1 and PGC-1alpha for binding to ERRgamma, we performed in vitro competition binding assays, using PGC-1alpha and DAX-1 with GST fused ERRgamma. The results of this study confirmed the findings of a previous report (17). Specifically, 35S-labeled PGC-1alpha interacted with ERRgamma, and DAX-1 inhibited the interaction between PGC-1alpha and ERRgamma (Figure 8B). In addition, no interaction between DAX-1 and PGC-1alpha occurred. Taken together, these observations suggest that DAX-1 physically inhibits the transcriptional activity of ERRgamma by competing with PGC-1alpha for interaction with ERRgamma.
###end p 44
###begin title 45
DISCUSSION
###end title 45
###begin p 46
###xml 252 257 240 245 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">DAX-1</italic>
ERRgamma is one of the newest members of the orphan nuclear receptor family. Therefore, the target and molecular mechanisms of ERRgamma have not yet been clearly identified. In this study, we demonstrated that ERRgamma acts as a potential regulator of DAX-1, which, in turn represses ERRgamma at a molecular level. This suggests the existence of an autoregulatory loop.
###end p 46
###begin p 47
###xml 88 93 88 93 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">DAX-1</italic>
###xml 183 188 183 188 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">DAX-1</italic>
###xml 212 213 212 213 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="b7">7</xref>
###xml 214 215 214 215 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="b8">8</xref>
###xml 263 268 263 268 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">DAX-1</italic>
###xml 318 323 318 323 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">DAX-1</italic>
###xml 445 446 445 446 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="b7">7</xref>
###xml 468 476 468 476 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="fig" rid="fig1">Figure 1</xref>
###xml 812 821 804 813 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="fig" rid="fig3">Figures 3</xref>
###xml 826 827 818 819 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="fig" rid="fig4">4</xref>
###xml 843 848 835 840 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">DAX-1</italic>
###xml 919 924 911 916 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">DAX-1</italic>
###xml 997 998 989 990 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="b7">7</xref>
###xml 1166 1171 1143 1148 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">DAX-1</italic>
###xml 1182 1190 1159 1167 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="fig" rid="fig2">Figure 2</xref>
###xml 428 433 <span type="species:ncbi:10090">mouse</span>
###xml 438 443 <span type="species:ncbi:9606">human</span>
###xml 837 842 <span type="species:ncbi:9606">human</span>
Although the physiological role of DAX-1 has been clearly identified, regulation of the DAX-1 promoter is not fully understood. A previous report demonstrated that SF-1 regulates the DAX-1 promoter through SFRE (7,8). Even though several SFREs are located on the DAX-1 promoter, the functional property of SFRE on the DAX-1 promoter has been characterized only in the region between -129 and -121 bp, which is conserved in both mouse and human (7). As demonstrated in Figure 1, ERRE1 is identical to sequences previously identified as the SFRE. We also characterized a new ERRE2 that was not previously identified as an SF-1RE. Although both ERRE1 and ERRE2 are required for the binding of ERRgamma, the data from mutation analysis suggest that ERRE1 is more responsible for ERRgamma transactivation than ERRE2 (Figures 3 and 4). In the human DAX-1 promoter, only ERRE1 is present. However, SF-1 can still activate the DAX-1 promoter. This demonstrates that ERRE1 is critical for transactivarion (7). Although all three members (alpha, beta and gamma) of the ERR family bound to the same DNA response element (data not shown), only ERRgamma was able to activate the DAX-1 promoter (Figure 2). This suggests that DNA-binding is not completely responsible for transactivation.
###end p 47
###begin p 48
###xml 377 379 347 349 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="b28">28</xref>
###xml 629 631 599 601 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="b29">29</xref>
###xml 632 634 602 604 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="b30">30</xref>
###xml 685 690 651 656 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">DAX-1</italic>
The basal transcriptional activity of ERRalpha and beta is relatively low compared to the high activity of ERRgamma. This suggests that the basal transcriptional mechanism governing ERRalpha and beta may be distinct from that of ERRgamma. A previous report demonstrated that ERRgamma is constitutively active, because ERRgamma forms a complex with coactivators, such as SRC-1 (28). It has been suggested that, depending on the specific co-regulator recruitment, transcriptional activity can be very different. Furthermore, since NRs can differentially modulate target promoters through the recruitment of different coregulators (29,30), ERRgamma may regulate target promoters, such as DAX-1, in a distinctly unique manner, compared to ERRalpha and beta. Further investigation is necessary to determine whether ERRgamma regulation of target promoters differs with co-regulator recruitment.
###end p 48
###begin p 49
###xml 119 121 111 113 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="b14">14</xref>
###xml 122 124 114 116 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="b15">15</xref>
###xml 358 360 336 338 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="b31">31</xref>
###xml 511 513 485 487 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="b25">25</xref>
###xml 535 543 509 517 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="fig" rid="fig5">Figure 5</xref>
###xml 674 679 640 645 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">DAX-1</italic>
###xml 725 730 691 696 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">DAX-1</italic>
###xml 774 781 736 743 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">in vivo</italic>
###xml 1055 1060 1005 1010 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">DAX-1</italic>
###xml 1284 1286 1222 1224 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="b32">32</xref>
###xml 1287 1289 1225 1227 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="b33">33</xref>
###xml 490 495 <span type="species:ncbi:9606">human</span>
Until recently, the ligand for ERRgamma was unknown, although 4-OHT was reported to be an inverse agonist of ERRgamma (14,15). Despite a recent report demonstrating that phenolic acyl hydrazone is a selective agonist of ERRbeta and ERRgamma, further study will be required to prove that phenolic acyl hydrazone is a bona fide activator of ERRbeta and gamma (31). 4-OHT is widely used in the treatment of breast cancer and deactivates ERRgamma which was proposed as a favorable biomarker in human breast cancer (25). As demonstrated in Figure 5, both ERRgamma and DAX-1 are expressed in breast cancer cells and tissues. Furthermore, ERRgamma regulated the gene expression of DAX-1 in a positive manner, demonstrating that the DAX-1 promoter is a potential target of ERRgamma in vivo. Although the function of ERRgamma and DAX-1 in breast cancer is not completely understood, we speculate that there is both a positive and negative relationship between DAX-1 and ERRgamma, since DAX-1 acts as both a corepressor of ER and ERRgamma and as a regulator of the DAX-1 promoter. Recent reports indicated that ERRgamma regulates the ERRalpha promoter via a conserved hormone response element and epidermal growth factor signaling which affects ERRalpha transactivation in breast cancer cells (32,33). Since ERRalpha, ERRgamma and PGC-1alpha are co-expressed in breast cancer, their physiological function may be significantly correlated in breast cancer tissues.
###end p 49
###begin p 50
###xml 114 115 114 115 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="b4">4</xref>
###xml 116 117 116 117 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="b6">6</xref>
###xml 118 120 118 120 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="b21">21</xref>
###xml 121 123 121 123 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="b27">27</xref>
###xml 288 289 288 289 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="b6">6</xref>
###xml 609 617 590 598 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="fig" rid="fig7">Figure 7</xref>
###xml 923 925 904 906 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="b34">34</xref>
###xml 1123 1125 1104 1106 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="b35">35</xref>
###xml 1269 1277 1242 1250 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="fig" rid="fig8">Figure 8</xref>
It has been reported that DAX-1 interacts directly with various NRs and represses their transcriptional activity (4-6,21,27). Previous studies have demonstrated that DAX-1 uses its N-terminal repeating region, defined as the LXXLL-containing interaction domain, for interaction with NRs (6). In this study, we demonstrated that the LBD-like region of DAX-1 interacts with ERRgamma. We also found that DAX-1 interacts with ERRalpha and beta (data not shown). The AF-2 domain of ERRgamma was crucial for the interaction with DAX-1 and acted as a binding surface for competition with the coactivator PGC-1alpha (Figure 7). Depending on the binding region of the nuclear receptor with the corepressor, the repressive mechanism of the corepressor can be quite different. When the DBD of the nuclear receptor is involved in interaction with the corepressor, the DNA-binding of the nuclear receptor is blocked by the corepressor (34). Inversely, if the AF-2 domain of the nuclear receptor interacts with the corepressor, the competition between coactivators and the corepressor occurs on the AF-2 surface of the nuclear receptor (35). In the case of ERRgamma, the AF-2 domain interacts with DAX-1 and acts as a binding surface for interaction with the coactivator PGC-1alpha (Figure 8).
###end p 50
###begin p 51
###xml 33 36 33 36 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">SHP</italic>
###xml 66 68 66 68 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="b16">16</xref>
###xml 159 167 155 163 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="fig" rid="fig6">Figure 6</xref>
###xml 233 238 225 230 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">DAX-1</italic>
###xml 243 246 235 238 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">SHP</italic>
###xml 306 311 294 299 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">DAX-1</italic>
###xml 316 319 304 307 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">SHP</italic>
###xml 720 721 704 705 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="bibr" rid="b1">1</xref>
###xml 819 827 799 807 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="fig" rid="fig5">Figure 5</xref>
###xml 904 907 880 883 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">SHP</italic>
###xml 1196 1203 1160 1167 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">in vivo</italic>
Previously, we reported that the SHP promoter is a target of ERR (16). In the current study, we suggest that DAX-1 is a target of ERRgamma. As demonstrated in Figure 6, DAX-1 repressed the transcriptional activity of ERRgamma on the DAX-1 and SHP promoters. In addition, SHP also inhibited ERRgamma on the DAX-1 and SHP promoters. It is very interesting that ERRgamma regulates corepressor proteins, such DAX-1 and SHP, which act as repression modulators. Although both SHP and DAX-1 are involved in the regulation of the corepressor-mediated transactivation of NRs, their physiological functions are quite different from each other. While DAX-1 plays a role in steroidogenic tissues, SHP functions mainly in the liver (1). We demonstrated that there is a correlation between ERRgamma and DAX-1 in breast cancer cells (Figure 5). In the case of SHP, we confirmed using adenovirus that ERRgamma activated SHP gene expression in the pancreas, but not in the liver (data not shown). This demonstrated that tissue-specific target gene expression is modulated by ERRgamma. DAX-1 is also differentially regulated by ERRgamma, depending on the target tissue. The modulation of DAX-1 and SHP by ERRgamma in vivo needs to be examined further, in order to identify the physiological roles of ERRgamma, SHP and DAX-1.
###end p 51
###begin p 52
###xml 29 37 29 37 <xref xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" ref-type="fig" rid="fig9">Figure 9</xref>
###xml 82 87 78 83 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">DAX-1</italic>
###xml 199 204 191 196 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">DAX-1</italic>
Collectively, as depicted in Figure 9, we hypothesize that ERRgamma activates the DAX-1 promoter, which in turn inhibits ERRgamma through coactivator competition. In this study, we demonstrated that DAX-1 is a potential target of ERRgamma. Our study provides a new insight into understanding the basic mechanisms of DAX-1 and ERRgamma, and is expected to provide clues for the identification of the functions of ERRgamma.

###begin p 100
###xml 76 81 68 73 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">DAX-1</italic>
###xml 83 86 75 78 <italic xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">SHP</italic>
Proposed model of ERRgamma and DAX-1 action. ERRgamma directly binds to the DAX-1 (SHP) promoter, resulting in an increase in DAX-1 (SHP) gene expression. In turn, DAX-1 (SHP) represses ERRgamma through a direct interaction with its AF-2 domain. DAX-1 (SHP) competes with the coactivator for binding to the AF-2 surface of ERRgamma and homeostasis between ERRgamma and DAX-1 (SHP) is maintained through this autoregulatory loop.
###end p 100
